שמע קולנו — The Culmination of Our Petitions

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 20 July 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

שמע קולנו as the closing ברכה of the twelve בקשות, the place where the gathered תפילה of every יהודי rises. Two senses of "שמע" — the warm hearing and the cold; the chilling גמרא in תענית כ"ה about שמואל הקטן and the rain that arrived too quickly. Personal additions to the ברכה — the ט"ז's caution, the ערוך השלחן's sharp protest against the printers, and the יעב"ץ's counsel to add in your own words. "ריקם אל תשיבנו" with Rav Schwab's WWI memory of his mother and the loaf of black bread.

Sponsored לעילוי נשמת מורינו ורבינו. Today is the יארצייט of Rabbi Yaakov Yosef[15], the first and last Chief Rabbi of New York. The Satmar Rebbe once said that every good מחשבה a יהודי has in New York is בזכות Rabbi Yaakov Yosef. He is buried in Cypress Hills. A תלמיד of the Salant school, he faced enormous opposition; New York in his day was a מדבר in terms of תורה observance, and his צרות were great.

I. The Place of שמע קולנו

שמע קולנו is a critically important ברכה — the culmination of the middle section of שמונה עשרה. The first three ברכות are שבח; the middle twelve are בקשה; the last three הודאה. The twelve themselves split into the personal six and the communal six. שמע קולנו closes the entire arc.

People do not always realize: the אמן after שמע קולנו requires extra כוונה. Just as the אמן after קדושת השם seals the opening unit, the אמן after שמע קולנו elevates the אמנים and בקשות of the entire central section.

Why It Follows צמח דוד

The גמרא in מגילה[1]: the פסוק connects the rebuilt ירושלים to בית תפילתי — the בית המקדש as the בית התפילה. The שלימות of ירושלים under מלכות בית דוד opens directly into the ברכה in which we ask הקדוש ברוך הוא to listen to our תפילות.

II. The Original Moment

The מדרש: when כלל ישראל in שעבוד מצרים cried out — "ויאנחו ויזעקו ותעל שועתם אל האלקים" — and הקדוש ברוך הוא heard them, the מלאכי השרת proclaimed: "ברוך אתה ה' שומע תפילה". We daven daily that הקדוש ברוך הוא hear us as He heard us then.

III. "אב הרחמן" or Not?

A נוסח question. נוסח ספרד: "אב הרחמן שמע קולנו". Most נוסח אשכנז סידורים drop "אב הרחמן" and open with "שמע קולנו".

The טור[2] goes into uncharacteristic detail on גימטריא and word counts in שמונה עשרה. Take the opening letter of every ברכה in שמונה עשרה — they sum to 1,800, mirroring the זוהר הקדוש's 1,800 מלאכים appointed to receive תפילות. The טור: open with "שמע", not "אב הרחמן" — you need the ש' (300), not the א' (1), to hit the count.

The אריז"ל[3], however, has "אב הרחמן שמע קולנו" in his נוסח; he was not מקפיד on the ראשי תיבות. Some נוסחאות from various חסידות place "אב הרחמן" mid-ברכה, preserving the ש' at the opening.

IV. The Two Senses of Hearing — A גמרא in תענית

The גמרא in תענית כ"ה ע"ב[4] tells two stories about שמואל הקטן — the same תנא who composed ברכת המינים — that complicate the simple reading of "שמע".

The First Year

A drought. שמואל הקטן declared a תענית ציבור. The morning the fast was to begin — before they davened, before the fast started — it began pouring. The קהילה rejoiced and came to שמואל הקטן: "Even before we fasted, even before we davened, הקדוש ברוך הוא sent us so much rain! He loves us! It is praise for the ציבור!"

שמואל הקטן: "No, no, no. משל למה הדבר דומה — a servant who keeps coming to his master's door, asking and asking. The master cannot stand the sight of him. He says to a friend: 'Give him whatever he wants. I do not want him knocking at my door anymore.'"

Sometimes הקדוש ברוך הוא gives, gives, gives — "just don't bother Me." A frightening thought.

The Second Year

Another drought; another fast. They davened the entire day. Right after שקיעת החמה, as everyone was leaving the בית כנסת to break the fast with coffee and cake — it began to pour. They came to שמואל הקטן: "This year you cannot say anything negative! We davened, He listened, we have rain!"

שמואל הקטן: "Not necessarily. משל למה הדבר דומה — a servant who comes to his master begging. The master, displeased with him, tells a friend: 'Let him beg until he is exhausted. Then I will give him what he wants.'"

The משל: הקדוש ברוך הוא sometimes wants us to beg until we are exhausted, and only then to grant. Both stories — the rain before the fast and the rain at sundown — are not the שמע we want.

V. The Lesson — Pleasant תפילה

This is what we daven for at שמע קולנו. Not the cold "שמע" of the master who cannot bear the servant, not the wearying "שמע" of the master who waits for exhaustion. The warm "שמע" — that our תפילה be pleasant in the ears of הקדוש ברוך הוא, not a burden.

If a person carries חטאים and עברות, the words have a hard time rising. The Baal Shem Tov[17] once entered a בית מדרש and said: "This shul is full of תורה and תפילה!" The חזנים, the רב, and the ראשי קהל swelled with pride. The Baal Shem Tov clarified: "That is not what I meant. תפילות and תורה are supposed to ascend. If they are weighted with עברות and lack יראת שמים, they remain in the room. Hopefully one day someone will break through the ceiling and lift them all up. Your בית מדרש is full of תורה and תפילה because the תורה and תפילה never left."

We want the opposite — a shul empty of תפילה because the תפילה rose. "שמע קולנו" is the תפילה for that very lift.

VI. Adding Personal בקשות

שמע קולנו is the place in שמונה עשרה for the broadest range of personal בקשות. The גמרא in עבודה זרה ח'[5]: one may add specific needs in the relevant ברכה (רפואה in רפאנו, פרנסה in ברכת השנים). שמע קולנו is general — anything goes here.

The אריז"ל on וידוי

The first addition many סידורים place at שמע קולנו is a וידוי. The אריז"ל said one should add a וידוי on his חטאים. Some say a וידוי at every תפילה. The אריז"ל's instruction: specify the חטא — not generic, but particular. The אריז"ל draws this from the זוהר הקדוש[14].

The תפילה for פרנסה

The next standard insert is a תפילה for פרנסה. The שולחן ערוך[6] permits it; many אחרונים say one may add it daily.

The ט"ז's Caution

The ט"ז[7] protests: "You are seriously going to add something בקביעות — to make it a permanent fixture of your daily שמונה עשרה? You are adding to what the אנשי כנסת הגדולה instituted? Who do you think you are?" The גמרא in עבודה זרה permits adding בעראי — when the need arises that day, that week, that month — not בקביעות.

The משנה ברורה[8] records the ט"ז's position; most אחרונים permit daily recitation. If you say it daily, you are not following the ט"ז; הקדוש ברוך הוא certainly listens.

The ערוך השלחן — Sharper

The ערוך השלחן[9] goes much further. The תקנה of the אנשי כנסת הגדולה is קודש קודשים; every word and letter is קדוש; the permission to add applies only בעראי, not בקביעות. To add daily is חוצפה.

The ערוך השלחן's polemic: "All the printers — they are guilty! They sought to insert these additions in שמונה עשרה every day. It is their fault. Without them no one would say it. We just spoke about this in פרקי אבות — the printers." He continues: "Who are you to insert these additions? It is all the fault of the printers. Many עמי הארץ do so regularly."

The world says the ערוך השלחן is the opposite of the שולחן ערוך — many times he rules differently from most פוסקים. He was a great מחדש; we do rely on him in many places, though most of כלל ישראל, especially those who follow the משנה ברורה, accepted the משנה ברורה more. But for clarity in any סוגיא — open the ערוך השלחן. "Fantastic" is the only word.

The ערוך השלחן: "I mourn over the corruption of our סידורים. One of the גדולי הדור made a great protest about this. The world has been handed over to fools who decide what to put in the סידור; the printers do what they want; כלל ישראל is corrupted." Strong words.

No one else holds this way למעשה. Continue to say the וידוי daily; this is shared as לידיעה.

VII. The יעב"ץ — In Your Own Words

Let us not forget the יעב"ץ's teaching from a דרשה in Prague (long pages of it; people in those generations had patience)[10].

The גמרא permits adding. People have printed תפילות they add. Forget the printed תפילות — add your own words.

This is a חידוש. שמע קולנו is general. If you need a שידוך for your child — three hundred and fifty years ago, the יעב"ץ already speaks of the שידוך crisis of his generation; "we suffer greatly, we have great difficulty finding שידוכים for our children or grandchildren" — add the תפילה in your own words, your own language, your own כוונה.

If you need פרנסה, add your own words. We do not even know the source of many of the printed תפילות — one appears in מסכת סופרים; the second only in the גאוני period. We do not know who wrote them. Use your own language. "ממעמקים קראתיך ה'" — תפילות from the depths of the heart are more powerful than what is printed. Although the printed תפילות are also powerful — the אנשי כנסת הגדולה wove every כוונה into them.

Stop and Speak From the Heart

Stop at שמע קולנו and say: "רבונו של עולם — I need פרנסה; I need this business deal to come through today." The Brisker Rav[16]: if you have a major business meeting that day, do it — one line. The יעב"ץ: הקדוש ברוך הוא will listen because it comes from the heart. If you need a שידוך, a רפואה, anything — say it briefly, not at length, just before the close of the ברכה. The חיד"א says the same.

One Caution — Do Not Miss קדושה

The משנה ברורה: if your שמונה עשרה is going to run so long that the שליח ציבור will already be in חזרת הש"ץ and you will miss קדושה — do not add at length here. Wait until after שמונה עשרה. קדושה is too important to lose. Always maintain that balance.

VIII. The זוהר on Constant תפילה — רב יבא סבא

The זוהר הקדוש records: there was a תנא named רב יבא סבא. He never sat down to eat — breakfast, lunch, or dinner — without first stopping to daven: "Just as You gave me food for this meal, give me food for the next meal." A יהודי who lived with הקדוש ברוך הוא in every moment, taking nothing for granted. We do not stop before every meal. But three times a day, in שמע קולנו, we should add a תפילה for פרנסה — even if we are ברוך השם doing fine. In your own words, as רבי יונה לנדסופר taught.

IX. The Whole Point of Studying תפילה

The Rebbe steps back: this is why we have spent every Sunday morning since שמחת תורה after October 7 on תפילה. We needed something on Sunday morning. The community agreed: תפילה is what we needed to learn. Every ברכה of שמונה עשרה in turn — to develop appreciation for what we say each day.

תפילה is a meeting with הקדוש ברוך הוא. The essence of שמע קולנו is exactly this: תפילה as חיבור. נפתלי is חיבור, says רש"י: we connect. We are the only species capable of intellectual communication with our Creator. Birds chirp; פרק שירה is full of songs; trees rustle "ירננו כל עצי היער". We speak. We do not always hear an answer; sometimes there is silence. But we speak, and we are certain He listens.

X. "ריקם אל תשיבנו"

An expression in this ברכה the Rebbe shares his personal כוונה on. There are no more beautiful words in תפילה than "כי א-ל שומע תפילות ותחנונים אתה, מלפניך מלכנו ריקם אל תשיבנו".

The Simple פשט

"מלפניך מלכנו ריקם אל תשיבנו": רבונו של עולם, I asked for so much. From דעת to רפואה, from פרנסה to גאולה. Don't let me leave empty-handed. "Make a deal — give me a third, half, something." Whoever can shed a tear at these words, his תפילה is being accepted.

A Deeper פשט

Several מפרשים read "ריקם אל תשיבנו" differently. Even if הקדוש ברוך הוא for our own benefit does not grant the request, at least the feeling that I just spent four minutes connected to Him should remain. Don't send me away empty in that sense. We take three steps back and within minutes we have lost the connection. We daven: don't let me feel empty. Whether You give me what I asked or not — let me leave still feeling connected.

XI. Rav Schwab's פשט — A Loaf of Black Bread

The Rebbe shares from Rav Shimshon Schwab in ספר עיון תפילה[11] a פשט of unusual depth. The Rav writes that when he says "ריקם אל תשיבנו", this is what he has in mind.

He was six years old in Germany during World War I. There was a severe shortage of food. Weekly government rations — Kommissar Brot, commissary bread. A family of nine got nine loaves a week. The bread was black, with potato peels and whatever could be ground in. Terrible bread; on a normal day a person would not touch it; in those days it was everything.

Each child had his own loaf — Sunday a piece, Monday a piece, paced for the week. One day his mother sliced him a piece for his meal. He finished it; he was hungry; he asked for another piece. There was none to give — it had to last the week. She told him sharply: "Enough. Save it. We need it for tomorrow." She raised her voice at the six-year-old; he was hurt.

But when she turned her face away, he saw a single tear escape from her eye, fall onto her cheek, and drip onto the loaf of bread she was holding. And the six-year-old understood, in that moment, that everything was for his benefit. She was raising her voice at him because she so wanted to give him more bread; she so wanted to give him more bread that she could not give him.

The פסוק in תהלים ל"ז:כ"ה

דוד המלך[12]: "נער הייתי גם זקנתי, ולא ראיתי צדיק נעזב וזרעו מבקש לחם". "I was young, I have aged — I never saw a צדיק abandoned, his children begging bread." Surely there are צדיקים whose children beg bread? How can דוד המלך say this?

Rav Schwab's פשט: I have never seen a צדיק who felt abandoned — never seen a צדיק whose children carried that abandonment within them. The צדיק remains connected to הקדוש ברוך הוא regardless of the bread on the table.

Rav Schwab: "I am one hundred percent certain my mother — the צדקת — even at the moment that tear fell on the black bread because she could not give her child another piece, she did not feel abandoned by הקדוש ברוך הוא. She had to do what she had to do. This is the situation now. הקדוש ברוך הוא arranged it."

This Is "ריקם אל תשיבנו"

Even when the piece of bread does not arrive, רבונו של עולם, we want to remain connected. We want to feel You did not abandon us. That is the deeper request — and a very high מדרגה if one can hold it daily in תפילה.

XII. The Power of Tears

There is nothing wrong with crying. We forgot how to cry on a regular weekday.

The Rebbe shares about his father ע"ה: Rabbi Avraham Schorr[18] — the Flatbush רב, with whom the Rebbe's father davened in his later years on the East Side — said publicly after the Rebbe's father passed away: "There was no other person in the בית כנסת who cried during a regular weekday שמונה עשרה. Your father cried."

As a child, the Rebbe was embarrassed. No one else's father cried on a regular Tuesday. "You're a weakling if you cry." But his father would shed a tear at קריאת שמע — he really meant it. Perhaps more emotional than others. That makes no difference. You are allowed to cry. When the words come out — "השיבנו בחיים", "בא נשלום" — you should have something to cry for. The tear can take you to far places, and you can get what you need.

XIII. The תפילה for גשמים in שמע קולנו

One additional insert in שמע קולנו: a תפילה for גשמים when there is a drought. Not normally said. Some make the mistake of saying it without a תענית; the פוסקים debate.

The ב"ח's Warning

The ב"ח[13]: requesting rain in any ברכה other than ברכת השנים is forbidden בציבור. ביחיד, one may insert it at שמע קולנו. But for the ציבור to add it together, even in שמע קולנו, is a serious problem.

The ב"ח records that two גדולים instituted exactly this in their קהילות — when they needed rain and did not declare a formal תענית, they had the entire ציבור say the תפילה for rain at שמע קולנו. Both גדולים died within the year. A דבר חמור to add to שמונה עשרה beyond what חז"ל instituted, particularly to insert שאלת גשמים outside its proper place.

הלכה למעשה: when there is communal need for rain, consult a רב; do not improvise.

Timing

These inserts are typically said at שחרית, not מנחה or מעריב. Some סידורים include them at all three; some don't. The ערוך השלחן warns against them at מנחה and מעריב; the שערי ציון and משנה ברורה are more permissive.

XIV. Closing

שמע קולנו is the place to bring your week, your year, your life. Do not be a bear licking honey through the page. Add a וידוי in the אריז"ל's spirit — specify the חטא. Add a תפילה for פרנסה in the זוהר הקדוש's spirit — like רב יבא סבא at every meal. Add for your שידוכים and your children in the יעב"ץ's spirit — in your own words. And, at the very close, mean "ריקם אל תשיבנו": do not let me leave empty, and if You will not give me bread today, do not let me feel abandoned. Stay connected to me. We will keep coming.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. גמרא מגילה י"ז ע"ב — סדר of the ברכות; בית תפילתי after מלכות בית דוד. — The סוגיא on the order of ברכות שמונה עשרה places שומע תפילה immediately after צמח דוד. The structural reading: ירושלים rebuilt under מלכות בית דוד is, in חז"ל's framing, בית תפילתי — the future בית המקדש as "a house of prayer for all peoples" (ישעיהו נ"ו:ז'); שמע קולנו follows naturally as the ברכה in which we ask הקדוש ברוך הוא to listen. ↩
  2. טור אורח חיים סימן קי"ח — גימטריא of 1,800 from opening letters; the ש' of "שמע". — The טור uniquely engages in detailed גימטריא arguments around the structure of שמונה עשרה in his treatment of these ברכות. Summing the opening letters of every ברכה yields 1,800 — corresponding to the זוהר הקדוש's 1,800 מלאכים appointed to receive the תפילות of כלל ישראל. The ש' (300) of "שמע" is structurally required at the opening of שמע קולנו; substituting "אב הרחמן" with its leading א' (1) would break the count. ↩
  3. שער הכוונות for the אריז"ל — "אב הרחמן שמע קולנו"; not מקפיד on ראשי תיבות. — The אריז"ל in שער הכוונות (transmitted by רב חיים ויטאל) preserves "אב הרחמן שמע קולנו" in his נוסח; his כוונות here rest on the kabbalistic structure of the ברכה rather than on the טור's גימטריא framework. Various חסידי נוסחאות compromise by placing "אב הרחמן" mid-ברכה, preserving the structural ש' at the opening while incorporating the אריז"ל's לשון. ↩
  4. גמרא תענית כ"ה ע"ב — שמואל הקטן's two תעניות; משלים of master and servant. — The סוגיא in תענית records two consecutive years in which שמואל הקטן declared a תענית ציבור for rain. In the first year rain came before the fast began; שמואל הקטן gave the משל of the master who tells a friend "give the servant whatever he wants — I cannot stand the sight of him knocking at my door". In the second year rain came at שקיעת החמה, just as the קהל was about to break the fast; שמואל הקטן gave the משל of the master who tells a friend "let him beg until he is exhausted, then give". Both משלים register that תפילה נשמעת can take forms that are not the חיבה we want. ↩
  5. גמרא עבודה זרה ז' ע"ב — ח' ע"א — adding personal בקשות; מעין הברכה. — The סוגיא in עבודה זרה establishes the framework for personal בקשות within שמונה עשרה: one may add specific needs in the ברכה whose theme they fit (רפואה in רפאנו, פרנסה in ברכת השנים, etc.) — "מעין הברכה". שמע קולנו is general (since its theme is תפילה as such); פוסקים read this as license to add any personal בקשה there. The סוגיא further distinguishes between בעראי (occasional, when need arises) and בקביעות (daily fixed), with the ט"ז and ערוך השלחן reading the permission as restricted to the former. ↩
  6. שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן קי"ט — adding personal בקשות in שמע קולנו. — The שולחן ערוך codifies the פסק from the סוגיא in עבודה זרה: a יחיד may add personal בקשות in the relevant ברכה; שמע קולנו functions as the catch-all. משנה ברורה there develops the practical applications, including the framework for וידוי additions and בקשות פרנסה. Most contemporary פוסקים permit daily recitation of standard inserts; the ט"ז and ערוך השלחן are minority voices on this. ↩
  7. ט"ז על שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן קי"ט — caution against בקביעות additions. — רבי דוד הלוי סגל (the ט"ז, 1586–1667), in his commentary on שולחן ערוך, argues that the סוגיא in עבודה זרה permits adding only בעראי (when the need arises in real time), not בקביעות (daily fixed). The תקנה of the אנשי כנסת הגדולה is the structure of שמונה עשרה; daily fixed additions effectively expand the structure beyond what was instituted. The משנה ברורה records the ט"ז's position; most אחרונים, while preserving the ט"ז's framing of the distinction, permit daily recitation of the standard inserts in practice. ↩
  8. משנה ברורה סימן קי"ט — recording the ט"ז's caution; permissive פסק in practice. — The משנה ברורה (חפץ חיים) on סימן קי"ט presents both sides of the מחלוקת — the ט"ז's caution against daily additions and the majority position permitting them — and rules in line with the majority that one may say the standard וידוי and בקשות daily. Practical guidance is included, particularly the warning that personal additions should not extend the silent שמונה עשרה so long that one misses קדושה in the חזרת הש"ץ. ↩
  9. ערוך השלחן אורח חיים סימן קי"ט — sharp protest on adding בקביעות; the printers; corruption of סידורים. — רבי יחיאל מיכל עפשטיין (the ערוך השלחן, 1829–1908), in his treatment of סימן קי"ט, escalates the ט"ז's caution into an explicit polemic against the printers of סידורים: by inserting personal תפילות as standard daily text, they have, in his judgment, corrupted the נוסח instituted by the אנשי כנסת הגדולה. He frames the תקנה as קודש קודשים and treats fixed daily additions as חוצפה. The position is sharp and stands largely alone among אחרונים; למעשה no קהילה follows it, and the standard וידוי and בקשות פרנסה are recited daily by virtually everyone. The Rebbe brings the ערוך השלחן as a window into how seriously earlier generations weighed structural fidelity to the תקנה. ↩
  10. יעב"ץ (רבי יעקב עמדן, 1697–1776, Altona; son of the חכם צבי) in his סידור בית יעקב / סידור היעב"ץ — and parallel teachings of רבי יונה לנדסופר (1678–1712, Prague; author of בני יונה and the דרשות from his Prague pulpit) — develop the principle that the most powerful additions to שמע קולנו are those formulated in one's own words rather than printed insertions. The יעב"ץ writes openly of his own generation's צרות in finding שידוכים for children and grandchildren and recommends specific in-the-moment בקשות for the צרכים of one's own household. The חיד"א in his דרשות on תפילה echoes the same teaching. Precise published references not specified in the שיעור.

  11. Rav Shimshon Schwab זצ"ל, עיון תפילה / Rav Schwab on Prayer — the WWI bread story; "ריקם אל תשיבנו" as the תפילה not to feel abandoned. — רב שמשון רפאל שוואב זצ"ל (1908–1995, רב of קהל עדת ישורון in Washington Heights), in his עיון תפילה / Rav Schwab on Prayer, develops a profound personal reading of "ריקם אל תשיבנו": the request is not only that הקדוש ברוך הוא grant the substance of our בקשות but that we not feel abandoned even when the substance is not granted. He grounds the reading in his own childhood memory of his mother in WWI Germany — the moment of the tear falling on the loaf of black bread because she could not give him another piece, and the dawning recognition that her sharpness with him was love, not abandonment. He pairs the reading with a fresh פשט on תהלים ל"ז:כ"ה: "לא ראיתי צדיק נעזב" as "I never saw a צדיק who felt abandoned." ↩
  12. תהלים ל"ז:כ"ה — "נער הייתי גם זקנתי, ולא ראיתי צדיק נעזב, וזרעו מבקש לחם". — דוד המלך's declaration in מזמור ל"ז: "I was young and I have aged, and I have never seen a צדיק abandoned and his children begging bread." The פסוק is recited at the close of ברכת המזון and stands as one of the foundational פסוקים of בטחון in תפילה. Rav Schwab's פשט resolves the apparent empirical difficulty (we have all seen צדיקים whose families have struggled materially) by reading "נעזב" as experiencing oneself as abandoned rather than as objective material status. The צדיק retains חיבור with הקדוש ברוך הוא regardless of circumstance. ↩
  13. ב"ח על טור אורח חיים — שאלת גשמים in שמע קולנו; the two גדולים who died within the year. — רבי יואל סירקיש (the ב"ח, 1561–1640, Krakow), in his commentary on the טור, takes up the question of inserting שאלת גשמים in שמע קולנו outside its proper place in ברכת השנים. His ruling: a יחיד may insert it; the ציבור may not — instituting it as a communal practice constitutes a סטיה from the תקנה. He records that two גדולים who instituted exactly this in their קהילות, when rain was needed and a תענית was not formally declared, both died within the year — read as a סימן of the gravity of structural deviation in תפילה. The פסק grounds the practical הלכה: communal need for rain requires a רב's direction, not improvisation in שמע קולנו. ↩
  14. זוהר הקדוש on the 1,800 מלאכים; on וידוי as a daily addition in שמע קולנו; on רב יבא סבא davening for פרנסה at every meal. — The זוהר הקדוש across multiple passages develops three relevant teachings. First, the structural correspondence between the opening letters of שמונה עשרה and the 1,800 מלאכים appointed to receive תפילות כלל ישראל — the foundation of the טור's גימטריא argument. Second, the practice of inserting וידוי in שמע קולנו with specification of the חטא rather than generic confession — adopted by the אריז"ל and codified by later פוסקים. Third, the מעשה of רב יבא סבא (a תנא appearing in זוהר passages) who would not begin any meal without first davening that the next meal be granted — a model of constant תפילה that the Rebbe brings as the spirit behind daily personal additions in שמע קולנו. ↩
  15. Rabbi Yaakov Yosef of New York (רבי יעקב יוסף הלוי זצ"ל, 1840–1902; תלמיד of the Salant school; רב of Vilna and Slutsk before emigrating to America in 1888) — the first and only Chief Rabbi of New York, appointed by the Association of American Hebrew Orthodox Congregations with the support of fifteen Eastern European immigrant קהילות. His tenure was marked by the deep difficulty of imposing centralized halachic authority on a fragmented immigrant community in turn-of-the-century New York, and he faced significant opposition from competing factions. He suffered a debilitating stroke in 1895 and was largely incapacitated for the remaining seven years of his life; his צרות were great and his פטירה in 1902 (כ"ד תמוז) drew tens of thousands of mourners. He is buried in Union Field Cemetery in Cypress Hills, Queens. The Satmar Rebbe's tribute that "every good מחשבה a יהודי has in New York is בזכות Rabbi Yaakov Yosef" circulates in oral מסורה as recognition that the failed effort itself sanctified the soil of New York Jewish life.

Baal Shem Tov tradition (רבי ישראל בן אליעזר, c. 1698–1760, founder of חסידות) — the מעשה of his entering a בית מדרש and remarking that the room was "full of תורה and תפילה", then clarifying that this was a problem rather than a compliment: the תפילות had not ascended. The story circulates in חסידות sources (דגל מחנה אפרים, תולדות יעקב יוסף, and parallel קבצים) as a paradigmatic Baal Shem Tov teaching on the relationship between the moral state of the מתפלל and the upward trajectory of his תפילה. The image grounds the כוונה of "שמע קולנו" as a request that one's words actually rise — that they not be left circling the ceiling.
