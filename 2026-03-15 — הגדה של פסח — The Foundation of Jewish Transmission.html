Rabbi Ephrati · Pre-פסח שיעור

הגדה של פסח — The Foundation of Jewish Transmission

A Nation of Books, the Most-Printed ספר Ever, and the Mission of ליל הסדר
Recorded 15 March 2026 · Reworked from the original שיעור
Sponsored by Yehuda in honor of the birth of his granddaughter Bella
מזל טוב to Yehuda and the entire family — may we all share only שמחות ומצוות

We welcome everyone this morning, and we welcome the חשוב Reb Shmuel Sneed who is with us today — what an honor. We deviate from our regular סדר תפילה this week to learn about the ימים טובים and הגדה של פסח. In two and a half weeks every yid here will be standing behind one of the most important מצוות in the תורה: to bequeath to the next generation our story, what we are all about, יציאת מצרים. Today is an introduction — what is the הגדה all about, and what is our role on ליל הסדר?

I. A Story of the Steipler — The Power of ברכות

When I grew up in בני ברק there was a famous story about the Steipler. A yid came in troubled because his daughter could not find a שידוך. The Steipler asked an unexpected question: "Did you make a קידוש for this girl when she was born?" The man answered that they had planned to but never did. The Steipler said: "Make a קידוש now" — even though she was already twenty-five or twenty-six. He made the קידוש. Shortly afterwards she was a כלה.

What was the Steipler's understanding? When a yid makes a קידוש for a daughter — just as we make a ברית for a son — that is a ברכה. We tend to think the ברכות we give at simchas are pleasantries, things we have to say. They are not. When you give a ברכה, even if you are not fully focused, the ברכה works לאמיתו. When one hundred or two hundred people give you a ברכה — מזל טוב, מזל טוב — those ברכות work. The man's daughter needed those ברכות, and the קידוש brought them. (Some say the story never happened. We grew up with it; either way it makes sense and is logical.) So Reb Yehuda — after breakfast and the שיעור, every yid here should wish him מזל טוב for this אייניקל and for all his other grandchildren.

II. עם הספר — A Nation of Books

The Rav זצ״ל said something striking. We are called עם הספר — the nation of the book. Yidden and books are one. Did you know that 500 years ago, when the printing press was invented by Gutenberg in the late 1400s and early 1500s, more than half of all the books printed in Italy were Jewish books? This is mind-boggling: we are a tiny percentage of people worldwide, and yet this little nation was producing more than half of Italian printing.

Until 150 years ago, do you know how marriage certificates were signed in England? With fingerprints. Most people could not read or write. We — from the times of the משנה onward — taught our children to read and write immediately.

III. The הגדה — The Most-Printed Book in History

The most successful book ever printed in our history — and I mean human-written, not the תורה which is from שמים — is the הגדה של פסח. Anyone who knows anything about books will tell you the הגדה has been reprinted more than the סידור, more than תהלים, more than ש״ס, מסכת, רמב״ם. It is also the book with the most commentaries. Go to the great libraries — not the seforim stores where every Tom, Dick, and Harry now puts out a הגדה — and you will see how many editions and how many מפרשים have been published over the centuries.

What is the secret? Why did the הגדה become the book that defines יהדות, that defines the מסורה passing hand to hand, generation to generation, father to son?

Who Wrote It?

By the way, do you know that no one knows who wrote the הגדה? It is anonymous. The most printed and most commented-on Jewish book — and we do not know its author. It seems that כנסת הגדולה, רב, then תנאים, אמוראים, and ראשונים all added pieces. Until the twelfth century things were still being added. The פיוטים at the end of the הגדה — חד גדיא, אחד מי יודע, אדיר הוא[1] — come from later generations. The הגדה is a compilation of many centuries.

Why was the book so successful? Open the libraries: every רב, every גדול, every תלמיד חכם, every ראשון felt compelled to write about the הגדה, to share his thinking, his interpretation. They are all דברים נחמדים, all true. Go to Zindel Berman's now and you will see tables and tables of new הגדות for this year. I am not telling anyone to buy them all — many are לקוטים — but each carries something.

IV. The מסורה From אב to בן

When you talk about this you become emotional. We all remember our father's seder. We all try — if we are still זוכה to attend our father's seder, or if we are now the ones leading it for the next generation — to mimic our father's seder. Some in this generation think we should change. Do not change anything. Try to remember what your father said. Try to remember how he gave it over. Try to remember the emotions around the table. Pass those on. That is the most powerful tool we have for חינוך.

V. אליהו הנביא — The Eternal Witness

Two Events

Why פסח? What is its importance? Who is the most important guest at the seder? We open the door for אליהו הנביא. (My father convinced us that אליהו really did drink from the cup. I used to measure it after the seder, and we always woke up the next morning to find a little wine missing. As children it was a real moment.)

אליהו הנביא comes to two Jewish events. What other event? ברית מילה. What is the connection between ברית and פסח, between אליהו at a ברית and אליהו at the seder? It is דם — and not one דם but two. The קבלה[2] calls them דם פסח and דם מילה.

The First פסח

When כלל ישראל saw the קרבן פסח that very first time — פסח מצרים — the מדרש[3] describes that the קרבן פסח smelled delicious. Like a barbecue. Everyone smelled it and wanted to participate. But the תורה[4] says you must have a ברית מילה — anyone who is an ערל cannot eat the קרבן. So משה רבינו went around that day and was מל everyone who wanted to participate. Two streams of דם at once: דם המילה and דם הקרבן.

And אליהו הנביא, to this day, shows up at every ברית and at every seder in every Jewish home on ליל הסדר. Why? One פירוש: since an ערל cannot participate in the קרבן, when you gather your family on פסח night someone has to testify that no one is an ערל. Who comes to testify? אליהו, who was there at every ברית. He can confirm: he is good for the קרבן פסח, I remember his ברית.

The Deeper Reason — אליהו's Punishment

But there is something deeper. Remember the story of אליהו הנביא and נביאי הבעל? אליהו came before הקדוש ברוך הוא and said: everyone is doing עבודה זרה, everyone has gone after נביאי הבעל. He said something very difficult: ואותר אני לבדי[5] — I am the only one left. Close it down. I am the only true yid; everyone else is fake, doing עבודה זרה while pretending to serve הקדוש ברוך הוא.

Look at the מפרשים there. חז״ל understand that אליהו opened his mouth against כלל ישראל — our great נביא somehow lost faith in our nation. Be careful before you open your mouth speaking of yidden. You do not know the כח of yidden, the נשמה of yidden, the secret of Jewish resilience. Even a yid who is far from religion — a mother takes her eight-day-old son for a ברית. A yid vacationing in Thailand with no formal connection to religion finds his way to a Chabad seder, because it is in our blood. We want to hear the מה נשתנה. We want to taste the crunch of the מצה and the bitter of the מרור.

This is why אליהו הנביא is bound to these two events of דם ברית and דם קרבן פסח — and why הקדוש ברוך הוא, כביכול, sends him to every one of them. You said yidden were lost? Look at them — thousands of years later they are still doing ברית. Look at them — thirty-three hundred years later they still sit around the seder table. אליהו is, so to speak, forced to come and watch us, so that he sees: נצח ישראל לא ישקר[6]. עם ישראל חי, forever.

VI. The Children at the Center

Now to the heart of things. If you look at the הגדה, I know — it can get boring to us. Our attention spans are not what they were. Scientifically, the average attention span of a human being years ago was about ninety minutes. Today, six or seven seconds. We have to learn how to make the הגדה of פסח interesting.

Someone here had a wonderful idea — Reb Zachary Olsen sent out information about a מחנך, Rabbi Webberman, who travels to קהילות before פסח to teach parents and grandparents how to make the הגדה engaging so the children do not disappear in the middle of the night. חז״ל[7] already said you give קליות ואגוזים — give them treats. Today my kids do not want קליות ואגוזים; they want other things. Whatever it is, give them something. Make it interesting.

Why? Because they are the most important people at the seder.

VII. Pedagogy in the הגדה

If you want to know pedagogy — if you want a textbook on educating children — open the הגדה של פסח. It contains every secret, every technique, every tool of חינוך. The first and primary concern of חז״ל, of whoever compiled the הגדה, was the children. The single most important thing for a parent to internalize: the הגדה has to be relevant.

We cannot sit there with the whole family and just start saying דברי תורה. I am telling you, ליל הסדר has been diminished over the generations because it became, in some homes, a שיעור with a meal. The seder is not the time to just give a ווארט. If your father had a דבר תורה he said every year — that is הייליג, that is part of your family's tradition; preserve it. But that is not the עיקר. The עיקר is the story, and the story has to be relevant to the children.

How do you make it relevant? The הגדה teaches us: do not turn the children into an audience. They are participants. Their story.

VIII. יציאת מצרים — Foundation of Jewish Identity

It Happened to Us

The story of יציאת מצרים is not just history; it happened to us. One asks: what do you mean? We were not in מצרים; we were not redeemed from מצרים. The מהר״ל[8] addresses this with one of the most fundamental ideas we must internalize before the seder begins. He explains that יציאת מצרים changed our מהות — our very essence. We were transformed from a nation of enslaved spirits into a nation of liberated spirits — eternally so.

The liberals think they are the true liberals. We are the real liberals — the truly free people. Why? Because יציאת מצרים changed our מהות. Even though we are in גלות today, even after all the צרות that have come after יציאת מצרים, in our essence we are a nation that will never accept enslavement again.

הקדוש ברוך הוא tells us that every one of us was at מצרים. We must transmit this to our children. You must tell your child what makes him a yid, what forges his identity. You are different from other nations. You are a free spirit, free to serve הקדוש ברוך הוא no matter what circumstances you face.

Why "סדר"?

You are free to transcend the natural order — to live למעלה מן הסדר. Why is ליל הסדר called סדר? It looks like the greatest בלאגאן! Wine and מצה all over the table. Children running around: "my turn — your turn — I want to say מה נשתנה first!" Especially as a grandfather hosting multiple families, every household with its parsha sheets, everyone vying for attention. It should be called ליל הבלאגאן, not ליל הסדר.

The simple answer: it is called סדר because of the established order — קדש, ורחץ, כרפס, יחץ, מגיד, רחצה. That is the סדר.

But on a deeper level, ליל הסדר is the night when we recognize that there is a סדר in the world. There is טבע (nature) and there is למעלה מן הסדר — תורה. There are the nations of the world; and there is כלל ישראל. There are seventy wolves; and one small lonely sheep. We are still here to tell the story. The same drama plays out today: there is איראן, there is חמאס. Same plot, same protagonists.

IX. בכל דור ודור — The Secret of Our שמחה

Have you ever wondered about the famous niggun? Long before שפוך חמתך, we sang: בכל דור ודור עומדים עלינו לכלותנו, והקדוש ברוך הוא מצילנו מידם[9]. Listen to those words. In every generation they rise up to destroy us — and we are singing it joyfully? Why are we celebrating that?

It is one of the most amazing phenomena in יהדות. Why are they after us? We have a tiny strip of land in the Middle East. We are a fraction of a percent of the world. There are billions of others. Why does every מלכות, every empire, become so obsessed with us? Why are they constantly trying to eliminate us?

This is, in fact, the source of our greatest שמחה. Consider this analogy: if thieves break into your home every night and you call the police repeatedly, eventually the police will say: what do you have that they keep trying to steal? You must have something extraordinary — gold, silver, cryptocurrency. We have a treasure. We do not always recognize it: our nation, our תורה, our מצוות, our connection with הקדוש ברוך הוא, the fact that we communicate directly with Him. We have something the nations of the world cannot stop staring at. They sense in us something we sometimes do not even realize about ourselves. That is the source of our שמחה. We sing בכל דור ודור with joy because on ליל הסדר we acknowledge: what they pursue, what drives them to obsess about us, exists because we possess something truly special.

X. Including Every Child — The ארבעה בנים

The Unique Mitzvah of פסח

When you run your seder, ensure it is relevant. Make sure each child, according to his age and his הבנה, understands that he is part of the story.

Compare to other מצוות. On פורים — there is no halachic obligation to require everyone to be there. We want everyone to come, but if a child has a short attention span and cannot sit through forty-five minutes of מגילה, fine — there is no absolute חיוב. On חנוכה — there is no מצוה in the תורה that requires you to bring the רשע, the תם, and the שאינו יודע לשאול. Only on פסח is there such a מצוה, and it is the עיקר מצוה.

The תורה repeats והגדת לבנך — "you shall tell your son" — four separate times[10]. All four sons must be brought back to הקדוש ברוך הוא. A father cannot excuse himself: "this child? I have no patience for him. The other three can sit at my table — the חכם, the תם, even the שאינו יודע לשאול. But the רשע? Whatever I say, he won't listen." No. No father may exempt himself from inviting every single child to the seder.

Of course we apply the חנוכה story to today, and we activate the פורים story for our times. That is the work of the מפרשים. But the עיקר מצוה of פסח, as הקדוש ברוך הוא gave it, is to include everyone around the table. The message must be relevant to every child — even the בן רשע — because the בן רשע is also part of the story. According to his level, according to his age, you must explain it and let him experience יציאת מצרים.

XI. From שכל to רגש — Reaching the Heart

The Leader Sets the Tone

Everything depends on the person leading the seder. If you feel the joy, if you feel genuinely Jewish — wake up in the morning, put on תפילין, daven, return to basic Jewish identity, a free nation that connects to הקדוש ברוך הוא — that transmits. If you feel it deeply, the children will feel it.

The חתם סופר — שכל and the Heart

The חתם סופר[11] has a beautiful דבר תורה on this. A sharp קושיא, a beautiful דבר תורה — those engage the שכל. We need them. But they must reach the heart. By ליל הסדר, שכל is important; רגש is more important. Emotions are a survival mechanism. Psychology teaches that emotions are tools to motivate action. When something touches you emotionally, you are moved to do. Pure intellect — interesting, but it stays abstract.

A Personal Example — Cryptocurrency

Recently I was listening to a presentation about cryptocurrency. Most of it I followed intellectually — pure שכל. Then near the end, the speaker said something that changed everything: "How you, the listener, could make money through crypto." Suddenly it became נוגע to me. The discussion shifted from my head to my heart. It was no longer about developers and experts; even I could participate. (I did not invest, by the way. But the moment of shift was real.) You can tell your child the entire סדר חכמה of the seder — fine. But the moment you tell a story in which he is a character, in which the narrative includes him, you have reached the רגש.

XII. The Power of Personal History

This is a critical point — people do not give it enough חשיבות. I am speaking from personal experience. People think, "just say what is in the הגדה, that is enough." No. Do you remember when you sat at your father's seder? Somehow — I do not know if this happened to all of you — somehow the story of your grandfather or father came up. How he survived the שואה. How he came to America. How he made it onto the boat, the plane.

My grandfather, my mother's father, did not speak about it during the year. Some yidden spoke about the שואה; some never did. It depended on the person, on how he absorbed the experience. (Psychologically it is complicated. Our generation is still byproducts of the שואה generation. The wreckage on our national psyche is still being studied.)

But my grandfather spoke about it once a year — at his seder in Chicago. Once a year he told us. That is who I am. That is what made me. We were glued to him. Suddenly יציאת מצרים was not something that happened thirty-three hundred years ago. It was something our nation, in every generation, is continuously coming out of — מצרים repeating itself. My own grandfather, the reason I am here, was telling me what he went through in the שואה.

When we went on our trip to Europe, I saw his name in the Glass House in Budapest. Suddenly everything became real on Seder night. The fact that I am here as a yid, as a continuity of the previous generation — all alive in that moment. That is what makes me feel Jewish to this day.

I guarantee that the עולם sitting here has its own version of this — כל אחד כפי מדרגתו. So it is important to share your grandfather's story. And if your grandfather is no longer with us, share his story — why we are still yidden. Two nights for בני חוץ לארץ; we have time. Find the moment to make your family story and the continuation of כלל ישראל real to your children. The story does not only travel to the שכל; it travels down to the heart.

XIII. The Elements of Effective סיפור יציאת מצרים

First and most important: do not be an audience, and do not let your children be an audience. Make sure they realize they are part of the story, and make sure they understand the relevance.

Several elements stand out when you study the הגדה:

First — בדרך סיפור. Not just recitation: tell a story. Narrative. Plot. Build it.

Second — שאלות ותשובות. Every מחנך knows the secret to opening a class: ask a question. Provoke thought. Engage. The whole structure of the הגדה is built around questions and answers.

Third — Many activities. The seder itself is choreographed to keep the children present. The four cups, the כרפס, the dipping, the pinkies during עשר מכות, the breaking of the matzah, the searching for the afikoman. Every מנהג, every gesture, is designed to bring children back into the room when their attention drifts.

Some families act out עבדים היינו. It may look childish or silly. It is vitally important. You take a piece of מצה, walk around the table with it on a stick, dramatize. Yes, it is theater. Yes, it is a game. But it is real, because it enters the children's blood. It becomes part of their DNA. We are the nation that הקדוש ברוך הוא chose. That is why it is so crucial to make the story real for them. Hand puppets, props, costumes — whatever it takes.

XIV. The Obligation to Prepare

But this is our obligation as parents and grandparents. Before סוכות we look for the four מינים. Before we buy תפילין for our children we go to the סופר and check. We prepare for מצוות. Yidden know you cannot just walk into the time of a מצוה and execute it cold. You prepare.

I said this last year: there is no excuse for a parent — a father, a grandfather — about to lead the seder, with a whole family waiting around the table to hear his סיפור יציאת מצרים, not to prepare. I am not saying every yid must work through every הגדה in the seforim store. But prepare. Get yourself in the mood. Open a הגדה. How can it be that we walk into ליל הסדר without having looked at the הגדה in advance?

I remember my father — a busy man, who worked very hard for parnasah and struggled. The night before פסח is etched in my memory. My mother was in the kitchen cooking. My father was at the dining room table with a stack of הגדות, looking through them. Why? Because he took it that seriously. That is what our parents and grandparents did.

We are busy. But prepare. There are wonderful editions today — every flavor, every approach. Each yid can find one that speaks to him. Beyond saying over what your parents and grandparents said, find one new thing each year that resonates with you, that you can give over. You have two and a half weeks until פסח. Take out the הגדות. (Just keep them out of the chametz!) Prepare. You are the main player. You hold the role of giving over Jewish identity to the next generation.

XV. The Stakes — Transmitting אמונה

If you succeed on פסח night in bequeathing אמונה to your children, the rest of the year will be better. If you fail — if a yid fails to give over even a spark of אמונה — you have failed your job. You have failed as a Jewish father, as a Jewish grandfather. The mission of ליל הסדר is not merely to inform; it is to transform — to reach the heart and make יציאת מצרים not just our history but our living identity.

XVI. הלכה — When You Go to Your Father-in-Law

The Question

Halachically, many yidden go to their grandparents for the seder; many sons go to their fathers; many sons-in-law go to their fathers-in-law. The question always comes up: I am going to my שווער. The מצוה of והגדת לבנך is on me — I have to tell my children. But my שווער is sitting and saying the הגדה his way, and I am silent. How am I being מקיים והגדת?

The גמרא[12] says בשעה שמצה ומרור מנחים לפניך — all the מצוות we have around the story have to be performed on פסח night specifically. Yes, every day of the year we mention יציאת מצרים. But the זכר יציאת מצרים of פסח is different: it must be סיפור — with detail. שאלה ותשובה. דווקא over the לחם עוני — the bread שעונין עליו דברים הרבה, the bread over which many things are said. So how does a son fulfill the מצוה when sitting in his father-in-law's seat?

The Answer — Different Forms of Participation

והגדת does not necessarily mean only that you must open your mouth and lecture. Some מפרשים say: just by bringing your children to your father or your father-in-law's seder, that is real. But the issue is delicate. Some ראשונים say: a yid who goes to his father should bring his children to their grandfather's seder — and still stick something in. Make sure your children ask you. Make sure you answer them. Know the main parts of סיפור יציאת מצרים. Say what we are supposed to say about the גאולה. The rest they can listen to from their grandfather.

It is sensitive — grandparents get insulted. It is their seder. This night brings up real emotions because it is the most important night of the year. The גמרא says: even if you are sitting alone, ask yourself the questions and answer them yourself. And of course daughters are part of the מצוה of שאלה ותשובה — tell it to them too.

Make sure to do some והגדת לבנך. Do not rely entirely on your father or grandfather. But I would recommend: be somewhat silent, allow the previous generation to give the story their way — even if it differs from how your father did it. Listen to their story, even if you have learned more in yeshiva than your father did. That is the whole point of והגדת לבנך — receiving from those before you.

XVII. The צורת המצוה — רב אשר וייס

רב אשר וייס explains this beautifully in his מנחת אשר on הגדה של פסח[13]. He says the צורת המצוה of the seder is that there should be an audience listening to someone saying it. According to a מדרש, originally only one person should say the הגדה; everyone else listens.

There is a great מחלוקת among the אחרונים: should you follow along with the one saying the הגדה? Some yidden say it בלחש along with the leader. That is a beautiful מנהג, probably the best — saying it together. Better to say it on your own, so you are מקיים it actively.

But when the leader pauses and starts saying the story in his own language — that is the עיקר. The ראשונים say: do not stay locked into לשון הקודש; tell it in your language. Why are parts of the הגדה written in Aramaic? Why are we saying חד גדיא, parts of the זוהר, certain other pieces in Aramaic?

Simple. The אבודרהם[14] explained: when כלל ישראל was in בבל and stopped speaking לשון הקודש, they spoke Aramaic. So the leaders said the central pieces in Aramaic so the children could understand. The same principle applies today. How many of our children fully understand לשון הקודש? Tell the story in English, in their language. A grandfather his way; a father his way. Explain in their language to make them feel: this is our story, this is what makes us.

XVIII. A Personal Story — Don't Put Them to Sleep

I give a daily שיעור to a group of very חשוב retired yidden. One time, in the middle of the שיעור, one of the men fell asleep. Some yidden snore loudly when they sleep. He started snoring; it was distracting. So I asked the yid sitting next to him, in front of forty-five people: "Give him a little nudge." And the yid sitting next to him — in front of all forty-five people — answered: "The one who put him to sleep should wake him up."

The point: we cannot put our kids to sleep at the seder. Our job is to keep them awake. If it requires preparation, if it requires taking off time from work the week before פסח, that is our מצוה, our opportunity, our chance.

סיכום

בעזרת השם, in the coming weeks we will go through pieces of the הגדה — to understand why each is so relevant, who composed which parts, where they come from, how they fit. May we be זוכה to transmit the מסורה faithfully.

The הגדה של פסח is our greatest pedagogical tool. It teaches that education is not about lecturing; it is about engaging. Not about intellectual knowledge alone; about touching the heart. And most importantly, not about turning our children into an audience to our history; about making them participants in our eternal story. When we sit at the seder table on April 12-13, may we be זוכה to transmit not just information but inspiration — not just knowledge but connection — not just the story of יציאת מצרים but the living experience of being part of עם ישראל, a nation freed from bondage to serve הקדוש ברוך הוא forever.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. Late Ashkenazic פיוטים at the close of the הגדה: חד גדיא, אחד מי יודע, אדיר הוא, כי לו נאה. — These four פיוטים are absent from the earliest הגדות (e.g., סדר רב עמרם גאון, סדור רש"י) and entered Ashkenazic הגדות in the 14th–15th centuries. חד גדיא first appears in print in the Prague הגדה of 1590; the others in earlier 14th–15th-century Ashkenazic manuscripts. חיד"א in סנסן ליאיר traces them through earlier manuscripts; modern liturgical scholarship dates them to the late ראשונים period. ספרדים and תימנים historically did not include them; they have been adopted in many communities today. ↩
  2. זוהר on פרשת בא; זוהר חלק ב' מ' ע"א. — The זוהר's development of the dual דם: דם פסח and דם מילה together at פסח מצרים, fulfilling the פסוק "ואומר לך בדמיך חיי" (יחזקאל ט"ז:ו') — the two bloods that gave כלל ישראל the זכות to be redeemed. חז"ל in מכילתא on פרשת בא develop the same theme. ↩
  3. מכילתא, פרשת בא, פרשה ה'; cf. שמות רבה י"ז:ג'. — The מדרש's description of the smell of the קרבן פסח in מצרים and the rush of those who wanted to participate. The מדרש develops משה רבינו's circuit of mass מילה on the day before פסח מצרים — known in חז"ל as מצות מילת פסחים. ↩
  4. שמות י"ב:מ"ח — "וכל ערל לא יאכל בו". — The תורה's prohibition: an ערל may not eat from the קרבן פסח. The פסוק establishes מילה as a prerequisite for קרבן פסח — both for the original פסח מצרים and for all subsequent generations of קרבן פסח. The רמב"ם codifies the דין in הלכות קרבן פסח פ"ה. ↩
  5. מלכים א' י"ט:י', י"ד — "ואותר אני לבדי ויבקשו את נפשי לקחתה". — אליהו's lament after the confrontation with נביאי הבעל and איזבל's death threat. חז"ל in פרקי דרבי אליעזר read אליהו's words as a קטרוג against כלל ישראל — for which הקב"ה obligated him to attend every ברית מילה and every סדר and witness the eternal אמונה of כלל ישראל. ↩
  6. שמואל א' ט"ו:כ"ט — "וגם נצח ישראל לא ישקר ולא ינחם". — שמואל הנביא's declaration: ה''s eternal commitment to כלל ישראל will not deceive. The פסוק is one of the central scriptural anchors for the principle of the eternity of the Jewish people; the שיעור reads אליהו's פסח-night attendance as continuous fulfillment of this נצח. ↩
  7. פסחים ק"ט ע"א — "מחטפינן מצות בלילי פסח בשביל תינוקות שלא ישנו"; פסחים קי"ד ע"ב — "קליות ואגוזים". — חז"ל's pedagogical practice of "snatching" מצות on ליל הסדר and distributing roasted grains and nuts to keep the children awake and engaged. The גמרא develops the principle that the entire structure of the סדר is choreographed around the children — every gesture and irregularity is designed to provoke their question and engagement. ↩
  8. גבורות ה', מהר"ל, ch. 4 and ch. 61. — The מהר"ל of Prague (ר' יהודה ליווא בן בצלאל, c. 1520–1609) develops the foundational principle that יציאת מצרים changed the מהות of כלל ישראל from a slave-spirit to a free-spirit, eternally. גבורות ה' is his comprehensive philosophical treatment of יציאת מצרים; chapters 60–61 develop the מצוה of סיפור יציאת מצרים on this metaphysical foundation. The שיעור's conceptual frame is the מהר"ל's. ↩
  9. הגדה של פסח — "בכל דור ודור עומדים עלינו לכלותנו, והקב"ה מצילנו מידם". — Liturgical formulation in the הגדה, immediately preceding שפוך חמתך. Built on תהלים קכ"ד:ו'-ח': "לולי ה' שהיה לנו... הברוך ה' שלא נתננו טרף לשניהם". The line is among the most quoted in the entire הגדה; the שיעור's reading — that we sing it joyfully because the very persistence of the threat testifies to the value of what is being threatened — is widely cited in חסידי and מוסר literature. ↩
  10. Four "והגדת לבנך" in the תורה: שמות י"ג:ח'; שמות י"ג:י"ד; שמות י"ב:כ"ו-כ"ז; דברים ו':כ'-כ"א. — The four scriptural references to telling the son about יציאת מצרים — read by חז"ל in the הגדה as the textual source for the ארבעה בנים. Each פסוק uses different language and addresses a different style of son: חכם, רשע, תם, and שאינו יודע לשאול. The מכילתא on פרשת בא and the ירושלמי פסחים פ"י ה"ד develop the connection. ↩
  11. חתם סופר על הגדה של פסח; תורת משה on פרשת בא. — The חתם סופר's דרשות on the הגדה develop the principle that סיפור יציאת מצרים must reach the לב and not stop at the שכל. The חתם סופר's teaching that רגש outweighs שכל on ליל הסדר is widely cited in pre-פסח derashos in his school and in subsequent חסידי-influenced halachic-pedagogical literature. ↩
  12. פסחים קט"ז ע"ב; פסחים ל"ו ע"ב. — "בעבור זה — בשעה שמצה ומרור מונחים לפניך": the תורה's חיוב of סיפור יציאת מצרים applies specifically when מצה and מרור are physically present on the table — not at any other time. The גמרא develops the special character of the פסח-night סיפור over and against the daily מצוה of זכירת יציאת מצרים: פסח requires sippur with question and answer, not mere mention. ↩
  13. מנחת אשר על הגדה של פסח, ר' אשר וייס (Jerusalem). — ר' אשר וייס שליט"א, ראש כולל of דרכי תורה and a leading contemporary פוסק. מנחת אשר על הגדה של פסח develops the צורת המצוה of סיפור יציאת מצרים: an audience listening to a teller, with the מצוה framed by the question-answer structure that distinguishes סיפור from mere זכירה. ↩
  14. אבודרהם on הא לחמא עניא. — ר' דוד אבודרהם (Seville, mid-14th century) explains why parts of the הגדה — most notably הא לחמא עניא and חד גדיא — are in ארמית rather than לשון הקודש: in בבל, when לשון הקודש was no longer the spoken language, the central pieces of the הגדה were rendered in ארמית so that women, children, and עמי הארץ could understand. The principle: tell the story in the language the family understands. ↩
