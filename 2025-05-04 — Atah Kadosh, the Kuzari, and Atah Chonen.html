אתה קדוש, the כוזרי, and אתה חונן

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 4 May 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

Returning to the running track of שמונה עשרה. The סדר of the first three ברכות rooted in הבו לה' בני אלים; the unique double "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש"; the meaning of קדוש as מובדל ומופרש; the כוזרי of רבי יהודה הלוי and the Khazar story corroborated by the Cairo Geniza; the original נוסח of לדור ודור versus today's אתה קדוש and why חז"ל needed three mentions of קדוש; רב אבלי פוסוועלר's rejected חידוש on המלך הקדוש on ראש השנה night; and the opening of אתה חונן with the גמרא in נדרים: "אי דעת קנית, מה חסרת? אם דעת חסרת, מה קנית?"

I. Where We Are

Last year before פסח we worked through קדושה, taking the detour because in חזרת הש"ץ קדושה precedes the third ברכה. We now move forward to ברכת אתה קדוש and the body of שמונה עשרה that follows. The question always: where does each ברכה come from, and why did חז"ל arrange them in this סדר?

II. The סדר from "הבו לה' בני אלים"

The famous גמרא in מגילה י"ז ע"ב[2] gives the סדר from a single פסוק in תהלים כ"ט[1]:

הבו לה' בני אלים, הבו לה' כבוד ועוז.
  • "בני אלים" — the powerful, the strong; the אבות הקדושים. Hence the first ברכה: אבות.
  • "הבו לה' עוז" — גבורה. Hence the second: גבורות.
  • "הבו לה' כבוד שמו" — קדושת השם. Hence the third: קדושה.

III. The Double "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש"

Strikingly, this ברכה contains a doubled praise: "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש". No other ברכה in שמונה עשרה doubles the praise. Every other one expresses one concept — אתה חונן, השיבנו, סלח לנו. Why double it here?

The זוהר הקדוש

The זוהר הקדוש[4] goes deep — depths beyond the Rebbe's השגה — but draws this surface point: the moment a finite, limited human being speaks the words "קדוש" upon הקדוש ברוך הוא, the very utterance is presumptuous. Who are we to be מקדיש? So we add — "ושמך קדוש" — pushing the recognition of separation further. "You are so far above us that we must add more קדושה."

IV. The Meaning of קדוש

The English translation "holy" is shallow. The פשט is מובדל ומופרש — separated, set apart, transcendent.

The פסוק "קדושים תהיו" in פרשת קדושים[3]: רש"י immediately glosses "פרושים תהיו מן העריות" — separate yourselves. Man has the capacity to step out of his finite being and his תאוות and יצר הרע and become מובדל, just as הקדוש ברוך הוא, despite His communication with us, is ultimately separate from this world. We can imitate that.

Different Faces of קדושה

  • Consecration of a קרבן — מקדיש means separating it from your possession; it is no longer yours.
  • מקדש את האשה — קידושין. The ראב"ד in הלכות גירושין[5]: קידושין is a קנין איסור; she is now separated, not available to others. Before חרם דרבינו גרשום a man could marry many — like דוד המלך; today ברוך השם we have the חרם.
  • נזיר — the פסוק calls him "קדוש": separated from society — no wine, no contact with מת, hair grown out.

Geographic קדש

תנ"ך contains place-names קדש ברנע (southern border of ארץ ישראל) and קדש נפתלי (northern border, ספר שופטים). The Rebbe only learned this recently: every place named קדש sits on a border between regions. קדש separates one nation from another — the very meaning of the word in space.

V. ספר הכוזרי

Author and תקופה

רבי יהודה הלוי[6] was one of the great ראשונים of ספרד — a major פייטן (we sing his פיוטים in our סידור), a philosophical תיאולוג, and a תלמיד חכם. He wrote the כוזרי in 1139.

Why It Is Not Standard in Our ישיבות

The כוזרי is one of the foundational ספרי מחשבה of כלל ישראל — and you will not find it on the standard ישיבה bookshelf. Show me a Lithuanian ישיבה that learns מורה נבוכים or כוזרי. The one place: ישיבת מרכז הרב. When רב קוק opened the ישיבה in ארץ ישראל, he deliberately added כוזרי to the curriculum, and from there it became central in the דתי לאומי world. רב קוק drew on the גר"א[8], who said כוזרי for יודעי דעת is קדוש וטהור.

In standard Litvish ישיבות the משגיח would intercept a בחור learning כוזרי: "You're going to start philosophizing!" Today, in any case, very few בחורים are bothered by the philosophical questions the כוזרי answers; the ספר survives in another part of the תורה world.

VI. The Khazar Story

The כוזרי opens with what reads like legend. In the 800s, in the Caucasus region (today the area of Uzbekistan and the surrounding mountains — the Kavkaz), a king named יהוסף (or יוסף) ruled the kingdom of Khazar. The כוזרי presents him as a pious, righteous גוי גמור seeking אמת.

(Many יהודים in ארץ ישראל today come from the Caucasus — Kavkazi Jews. Some historians argue that a portion of אשכנזי Jewry descends from the converted Khazars; for all we know, some of you trace back this way.)

The Dream and the Search

The king dreamt of a מלאך who told him: "Your intent is good and righteous; your deeds are wrong." He woke understanding: he had to find a different religion. He met a philosopher — unimpressed. A Muslim scholar — unimpressed. He went to the יהודים and met a חכם: רב יצחק הסנגרי. The כוזרי records the dialogue between king and חכם — the חכם's answers eventually convinced the king to convert. He returned to his nation, retreated first to a cave with hidden Jewish חסידים for his own conversion with his chief of staff, then convinced his entire kingdom.

Verification — The Cairo Geniza Letters

For centuries the story was treated as legend. The Cairo Geniza[7] changed the picture. Among the manuscripts Solomon Schechter recovered: three letters from the eighth or ninth century either by the king of Khazar himself or by his court, written to רב חסדאי אבן שפרוט — a major figure in ninth-century Spanish Jewry, after whom a street in ארץ ישראל is named. He had heard the story and sent שלוחים to verify; the letters are the responses he received. They contain the Khazar story with variations — the conversion may have been partial (king and noble families) rather than total — but something happened. Islamic historical sources from the same period record a similar story: a Caucasus tribe that experimented with Islam and then turned to Judaism. The Khazar episode is real.

The ספר Itself

רבי יהודה הלוי wrote in Arabic; יהודה אבן תיבון, the great translator of the ראשונים' Arabic works, rendered it into Hebrew. The original Arabic title — translated literally — is "The Book of Disputations, Refutations, and Proofs for the Despised Religion." (Ours.) רבי יהודה הלוי himself, in a letter recovered from the Cairo Geniza, calls the work "דבר קטן"; he was unsatisfied with it. The world disagreed. By the רמב"ם's time the חכמים in Lunel were writing to him, "We've read the כוזרי; we've read the חובות הלבבות; now send us מורה נבוכים." The כוזרי stands as one of the pillars of Jewish מחשבה.

VII. The כוזרי on the סדר of the First Three ברכות

The כוזרי's reading of שמונה עשרה made a deep impression on the king and is foundational for our ברכה.

The second ברכה — גבורות — speaks of רופא חולים, מתיר אסורים, מחיה מתים. Each of these declares an intimate connection between הקדוש ברוך הוא and the world: when one is sick He is involved; when one is downtrodden He is involved; we acknowledge it in תפילה.

One could conclude from גבורות that הקדוש ברוך הוא is, so to speak, our buddy — close, accessible, immanent. Reachable like a friend. The כוזרי says חז"ל immediately follow with אתה קדוש to correct the mistake. Yes — He is close. And He is also infinitely separate. Even in His closeness, do not collapse the distance. The פסוק in קדושים says "קדושתי למעלה מקדושתכם"; before any בקשה, we acknowledge that קדושתו is למעלה.

VIII. The יהודי's Capacity for קדושה

Beneath the כוזרי's point lies another — that the אחרונים draw out: when we say "אתה קדוש" we are not only praising. We are asserting our own capacity. The משנה in אבות[12]: "חביבין ישראל שנקראו בנים למקום"; we have צלם אלוקים in a way גוים do not. הקדוש ברוך הוא implanted in us the same כח He has — separation. Just as He is קדוש, we have the כח to be קדוש: "וקדושים בכל יום יהללוך סלה".

Each morning, when we say "אתה קדוש", we are also saying — I have that capacity. No matter how difficult the יצר הרע or the daily תאוות, כלל ישראל has the כח built in by the same Hand that built us.

The ריזשינער on "קדושים תהיו"

The ריזשינער רבי[13] reads רש"י on "קדושים תהיו כי קדוש אני" with a twist. The standard רש"י: "יכול כמוני? תלמוד לומר 'כי קדוש אני' — קדושתי למעלה מקדושתכם". The ריזשינער reads "תלמוד לומר" not as a derivation but as an instruction: "Teach yourself to say it" — talk to yourself, every day, "I am a קדוש". Convince yourself. Self-affirmation crystallizes into self-becoming.

The מעשה with רב יונה מווישניץ

The רבי Rav Yonah of Vizhnitz[18], in Prague: a member who had not received the עליה he wanted made a public scene on שבת. רב יונה approached him: "I do not understand. You sit by מזרח; I just heard you saying ישתבח aloud. You said 'ונפשי כעפר לכל תהיה' — let my soul be like dust to all. How can it be that you make a fuss over an עליה?" The work of "אתה קדוש" is to close the gap between the words on our lips and the state of our נשמה.

IX. ברכת הקדוש as the Close of the Opening Unit

The third ברכה closes the first unit of שמונה עשרה — the unit of שבח. להלכה: if you make a mistake mid-way through the first three ברכות and need to return, you cannot simply restart from the beginning of הא-ל הקדוש; you must go back to the very beginning of שמונה עשרה. The three are one unit.

A Lesser-Known פסק on Late Arrival

If you arrive at שול while חזרת הש"ץ is in progress and cannot start with the חזן, the רמ"א[14] rules: wait. Say קדושה with the ציבור; then continue waiting until the close of "הא-ל הקדוש"; answer אמן to that ברכה specifically; only then begin your שמונה עשרה. That אמן — closing the unit of the first three ברכות — is more important than every other אמן in חזרת הש"ץ. The בית יוסף registers some hesitation; the רמ"א's point stands: it is not only the קדושה that matters here but the אמן that seals the unit.

Inside קריאת שמע

We know one cannot interrupt קריאת שמע for ordinary אמנים. The exceptions: קדיש, קדושה — and, on the same logic, the אמן after "הא-ל הקדוש". הלכה למעשה: not many people know this.

X. לדור ודור versus אתה קדוש — A נוסח Story

A מחלוקת: in the silent שמונה עשרה of a יחיד, do you say "לדור ודור נגיד גדלך" (נוסח אשכנז) or "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש" (נוסח ספרד)?

The Original נוסח

The רמב"ם in ספר אהבה[9] records his סדר תפילה with the לדור ודור formulation — fascinating, since the רמב"ם is Sephardic and our current לדור ודור sits in נוסח אשכנז. The ארחות חיים and the כל בו[10], both cited extensively by the בית יוסף, confirm that the original נוסח המסורה ran "לדור ודור" — flowing directly from קדושה.

The Reason for the Change

Why was the נוסח later modified to begin "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש" in the silent שמונה עשרה? Because the ברכה requires three mentions of קדוש — mirroring the מלאכים' triple "קדוש קדוש קדוש".

In the ציבור's קדושה, the three are explicit. But the יחיד davening alone has no קדושה. חז"ל reorganized his נוסח to insert three קדוש's: "אתה קדוש, ושמך קדוש, וקדושים בכל יום יהללוך סלה". Now the count is satisfied.

So the ספרדי "אתה קדוש" is not the original formulation; it is a later structural תקנה. לדור ודור is the original. By חזרת הש"ץ, both נוסחים say "לדור ודור"; only in the silent תפילה of the יחיד do they diverge.

XI. המלך הקדוש on עשרת ימי תשובה

During the ten days, we substitute "המלך הקדוש" for "הא-ל הקדוש". The זוהר הקדוש grounds it: "הא-ל" is associated with חסד; "המלך" with דין and גבורה. When המלך יושב בדין, we say "המלך". The גמרא in ברכות[15]: forgetting "המלך הקדוש" is מעכב — one must return.

Rav Avli Posweller's חידוש

Rav Avli Posweller[16] is largely unknown today; his fame rests on a single חידוש that the חיי אדם brings, and around which the אחרונים debate. לומדישע תפילה — the kind of פלפול they bring up before ראש השנה.

His חידוש: if one says "הא-ל הקדוש" on ראש השנה day, he must repeat. But if one says "הא-ל הקדוש" on ראש השנה night, he is יוצא and need not repeat.

The parallel: forgetting יעלה ויבוא on ראש חודש day requires repetition; on ראש חודש night it does not — because "אין מקדשין את החודש בלילה". Rav Avli applies the same logic: עשרת ימי תשובה begins ראש השנה, which is bound to חודש תשרי — and at night the חודש is not yet formally מקודש. So "המלך הקדוש" at night should not be מעכב.

The Rejection

The משנה ברורה and most אחרונים reject this. Their objection: on a weekday when one forgets יעלה ויבוא at the previous מעריב, the rest of the תפילה is a regular weekday תפילה; nothing else conflicts. But on ראש השנה night, the entire נוסח of תפילה is the full יום טוב davening — אתה בחרתנו, המלך מוחל, all of it. Saying "הא-ל הקדוש" in the middle is תרתי דסתרי — a head-on collision with the rest of the תפילה you are saying. Therefore it remains מעכב.

XII. ברכת אתה חונן — Why It Begins With "אתה"

We move now into the body of בקשות. Notice: ברכת אתה חונן opens with "אתה" — no other ברכה in the middle twelve does. Why?

The ראשונים: the first three ברכות are שבח; we are about to begin בקשות. חז"ל wove a transition — let the first ברכה of בקשה still open in the register of שבח. "אתה חונן לאדם דעת" is praise — acknowledging that הקדוש ברוך הוא is the source of דעת. Only then the request: "חננו מאתך דעה בינה והשכל".

XIII. Why דעת First?

Of all the things one might ask הקדוש ברוך הוא for, why דעת first? The גמרא in נדרים מ"א ע"א[17] has the answer:

אי דעת קנית, מה חסרת? אי דעת חסרת, מה קנית?
If you have דעת, what are you lacking? If you lack דעת, what do you have?

And further: "אין עני אלא בדעת". The truly impoverished is not the man at the door collecting צדקה; the impoverished is the man without שכל. Avraham Fried has made it a song; we knew the גמרא long before. דעת is the foundation. Everything else — פרנסה, רפואה, שלום בית — only matters if דעת is in place.

XIV. The Tragedy of Lost דעת

If you have ever sat with someone who has lost their דעת — Alzheimer's, dementia, severe amnesia — the loss is total. The body remains; the person, in a sense, does not. חז"ל in this ברכה are asking that we not take for granted the gift of waking each morning with a thinking, understanding, intellectual mind.

The פסוק: "ואל תשליכנו לעת זקנה". הקדוש ברוך הוא, do not cast us off when our years grow long; let us retain דעת until the end.

The Rebbe shares: my grandfather עליו השלום was sharp as a knife to his last moment. He passed away from COVID at 94 in a Queens hospital this past Thursday, 1 May 2025. I visited him three days before. The infection was terminal; the mind was completely there.

My other grandfather — a brilliant תלמיד חכם, professor of תלמוד at Bar-Ilan, author of ספרים — spent his last six years being fed by hand. The contrast is what we daven about each morning.

It is never too early or too late: "אתה חונן לאדם דעת... חננו מאתך דעה בינה והשכל" — give me דעת until the end of my days, that I should function as the human being You created me to be.

XV. Modern Adaptations of the כוזרי

Two notable later adaptations of the כוזרי[11]:

  • Rabbi David Nieto (1654–1728), חכם of the Spanish and Portuguese congregation in London, wrote "כוזרי השני" — updating the questions of the כוזרי for his own era.
  • Rabbi Dr. Isaac Breuer (1883–1946), grandson of רב שמשון רפאל הירש, leader in אגודת ישראל, wrote "ספר כוזרי חדש" — a twentieth-century version. (He lived for some time in Washington Heights.)

Both are barely read today, for the same reason mainstream ישיבות do not learn כוזרי. The כוזרי's genius — what makes it different from a typical philosophy book — is that it is structured as narrative: a sustained conversation between king and חכם, with give-and-take. It pulls the reader in. The Rebbe is not pushing us all to start learning כוזרי; but the idea drawn from it on the סדר of the first three ברכות entered the mainstream ראשונים and אחרונים who explain סדר התפילה.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. תהלים כ"ט:א-ב — "הבו לה' בני אלים, הבו לה' כבוד ועוז; הבו לה' כבוד שמו, השתחוו לה' בהדרת קדש"; סדר of the first three ברכות. — The opening פסוקים of מזמור כ"ט in תהלים — said as the closing מזמור of קבלת שבת and as part of the הוצאת ספר תורה — are read by חז"ל as the textual ground for the structure of שמונה עשרה's opening unit. The three clauses ("בני אלים", "עוז", "כבוד שמו") map onto אבות, גבורות, and קדושה respectively. ↩
  2. גמרא מגילה י"ז ע"ב — סדר ברכות שמונה עשרה derived from תהלים כ"ט. — The גמרא in מגילה presents the famous דרשה deriving the סדר of שמונה עשרה's eighteen (now nineteen) ברכות from פסוקים in תנ"ך. The opening triad is grounded in הבו לה' בני אלים; the middle בקשות are tied to פסוקי תהלים and דברי הימים in turn; the closing three (עבודה, הודאה, שלום) rest on the closing structure of the קרבן תמיד. The סוגיא is the principal source for understanding why חז"ל ordered the ברכות as they did. ↩
  3. ויקרא י"ט:ב — "קדושים תהיו, כי קדוש אני ה' אלקיכם"; רש"י: "פרושים תהיו מן העריות". — The opening פסוק of פרשת קדושים. רש"י, drawing on ספרא, glosses "קדושים" as "פרושים" — separated, particularly from forbidden relations — establishing the lexical equation קדוש = מובדל that runs through the rest of the Rebbe's discussion. The same פסוק closes with "כי קדוש אני" — the סיפא on which the ריזשינער רבי builds his reading of "תלמוד לומר". ↩
  4. זוהר הקדוש on "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש"; on "המלך" (דין) versus "הא-ל" (חסד). — The זוהר develops two relevant points across multiple passages. First, on the doubled "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש": a finite, embodied מתפלל presuming to declare הקדוש ברוך הוא "קדוש" must immediately deepen the recognition of separation — pushing the קדושה further out by adding "ושמך קדוש". Second, on the substitution during עשרת ימי תשובה: "הא-ל" is associated kabbalistically with חסד (the open hand of giving), while "המלך" sits in גבורה and דין — when המלך יושב בדין, the formulation shifts to "המלך הקדוש". ↩
  5. השגות הראב"ד on רמב"ם הלכות גירושין — קידושין as a קנין איסור. — רבי אברהם בן דוד (the ראב"ד, c. 1125–1198, Posquières) in his השגות on the רמב"ם's הלכות גירושין develops the analytical position that קידושין works as a קנין איסור — not (or not only) a property-style קנין ממון in the wife, but a חלות of forbidden status excluding her from all other men. The Rebbe brings this to illustrate the lexical sense of קדושה as מובדל: קידושין separates the אשה from the rest of the world, just as הקדש separates a קרבן from חולין. ↩
  6. ספר הכוזרי (רבי יהודה הלוי, c. 1075–1141) — composed in Judeo-Arabic in 1139 in Spain (full Arabic title: Kitāb al-Radd wa-'l-Dalīl fī al-Dīn al-Dhalīl, "The Book of Refutation and Proof on Behalf of the Despised Religion"); translated into Hebrew by יהודה אבן תיבון in 1167 in Provence. Structured as a sustained dialogue between the king of Khazar and the חכם רבי יצחק הסנגרי, the work develops Jewish theology, the unique covenantal status of כלל ישראל, the structure of תפילה, and a sustained reading of the סדר of שמונה עשרה's first three ברכות. רבי יהודה הלוי, in a letter recovered from the גניזת קהיר, calls the work "דבר קטן"; the world has held it as one of the foundational ספרי מחשבה of כלל ישראל.

The Cairo Geniza (Ben Ezra Synagogue, Fustat, Old Cairo) — recovered to Cambridge University Library in 1896–97 by Solomon Schechter with the prior reconnaissance of Margaret Gibson and Agnes Smith Lewis. Among the גניזה's recovered manuscripts: three letters between the Khazar court and רב חסדאי אבן שפרוט (c. 915–970, Cordoba; physician and diplomat to Caliph Abd al-Rahman III; major patron of רבי דונש and רבי מנחם בן סרוק). The Khazar correspondence — written in Hebrew, including a letter purporting to be from King Joseph (יוסף) himself — provides independent corroboration of the conversion narrative that opens the כוזרי. Islamic chroniclers of the same period (al-Mas'udi, Ibn Fadlan, Ibn Rusta) describe a Caucasus tribal kingdom that had adopted Judaism. While the historical scope of the conversion (royal court versus full population) remains scholarly debated, the basic episode is corroborated.

  9. רמב"ם, ספר אהבה, סדר תפילה — the original נוסח of the third ברכה with "לדור ודור". — The רמב"ם in משנה תורה, at the close of ספר אהבה, prints his נוסח התפילה in full. The third ברכה in his נוסח reads "לדור ודור נגיד גדלך" — what is now identified as נוסח אשכנז. The fact that the Sephardic רמב"ם recorded this formulation is the strongest evidence for the Rebbe's claim that "לדור ודור" is the original נוסח המסורה of the יחיד's silent שמונה עשרה; the "אתה קדוש ושמך קדוש" formulation now standard in נוסח ספרד represents a later structural תקנה to insert three קדוש mentions where the יחיד has no קדושה. ↩
  10. ארחות חיים (רבי אהרן הכהן מלוניל) and כל בו — both cited extensively in the בית יוסף; confirm "לדור ודור" as the original נוסח. — רבי אהרן הכהן מלוניל (late thirteenth–early fourteenth century, Provence and Mallorca), in his halachic compendium ארחות חיים, and the closely-related anonymous כל בו (likely an abridgment of the same work or by a closely related author), preserve the early Provencal-Sephardic נוסח and confirm "לדור ודור" as the original silent-שמונה עשרה formulation. בית יוסף on סימן קי"ב (and parallel discussions) cites both works heavily; their joint testimony alongside the רמב"ם establishes the historical priority of the לדור ודור נוסח. ↩

  12. משנה אבות ג':י"ד (רבי עקיבא) — "חביב אדם שנברא בצלם... חביבין ישראל שנקראו בנים למקום". — The משנה of רבי עקיבא in the third פרק of פרקי אבות establishes the structural חיבה hierarchy: every human being is beloved as צלם אלוקים; כלל ישראל is uniquely beloved as "בנים למקום". The משנה grounds the אחרונים' reading that "אתה קדוש" is not only a declaration about הקדוש ברוך הוא's separation but also an assertion of כלל ישראל's capacity to be "קדושים בכל יום יהללוך סלה" — the same כח קדושה implanted in us by virtue of our being His "בנים". ↩
  13. ריזשינער רבי (רבי ישראל מריזשין, 1796–1850, Ukraine; great-grandson of the מגיד ממעזריטש; founder of the ריזשין-סאדיגורא dynasty) on "קדושים תהיו כי קדוש אני": the standard רש"י read "תלמוד לומר 'כי קדוש אני'" as the דרשה's answer to the suggestion that we be as קדוש as הקדוש ברוך הוא — implying limitation. The ריזשינער reads "תלמוד לומר" imperatively: "teach yourself to say" — practice declaring "אני קדוש", talk to yourself this way; the act of self-affirmation crystallizes into עבודה. Recorded in the oral מסורה of his descendants and in collected דרשות; precise published source not given in the שיעור.

  14. רמ"א, שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן ק"ט סעיף א' — wait for the אמן after "הא-ל הקדוש"; בית יוסף's discussion. — The רמ"א in his glosses on שולחן ערוך on the late-arrival סוגיא rules: a יחיד arriving while חזרת הש"ץ is in progress should delay starting his own שמונה עשרה through both the קדושה and the close of "הא-ל הקדוש" — answering אמן to that ברכה specifically — before beginning. The בית יוסף records hesitation about extending the wait this far; the רמ"א insists, on the principle that the third ברכה closes the unit of שבח and the אמן on its חתימה is structurally weighty. משנה ברורה there elaborates the practical applications, including the parallel פסק that this אמן may be answered mid-קריאת שמע. ↩
  15. גמרא ברכות י"ב ע"ב — "המלך הקדוש" as מעכב on עשרת ימי תשובה. — The סוגיא in ברכות establishes that the substitution of "המלך הקדוש" for "הא-ל הקדוש" during עשרת ימי תשובה is not merely מנהג but מעכב — one who said "הא-ל הקדוש" in error must return to the beginning of שמונה עשרה and re-daven. The same דין applies (in תפילה at מנחה) to "המלך המשפט" in the eleventh ברכה. The severity stems from the זוהר's reading: "המלך" at this season is the proper formulation of קדושה when המלך יושב בדין, and the substitution structurally cannot be elided. ↩

Rav Avli (Aybele) Posweller (רבי אבלי פוסוועלר, c. 1764–1836, Vilna) — Lithuanian פוסק known principally for the חידוש brought in his name by the חיי אדם (רבי אברהם דנציג, 1748–1820, Vilna; author of the חיי אדם compendium on אורח חיים): the חקירה whether forgetting "המלך הקדוש" on the night of ראש השנה is מעכב like the day, given the parallel דין with יעלה ויבוא on ליל ראש חודש (where forgetting is not מעכב because "אין מקדשין את החודש בלילה"). The משנה ברורה and most אחרונים reject the חידוש: on a regular weekday מעריב after ראש חודש, the rest of the תפילה is a standard weekday davening, so a missing יעלה ויבוא creates no internal contradiction; on ליל ראש השנה, the entire נוסח is the full יום טוב davening, and a missing "המלך הקדוש" creates תרתי דסתרי with the surrounding text. The חידוש circulates as a classic לומדישע דבר תורה before ראש השנה.

  17. גמרא נדרים מ"א ע"א — "אי דעת קנית, מה חסרת? אי דעת חסרת, מה קנית?"; "אין עני אלא בדעת". — The סוגיא in נדרים on the comprehensive value of דעת: if a person possesses דעת, what could he be lacking?
