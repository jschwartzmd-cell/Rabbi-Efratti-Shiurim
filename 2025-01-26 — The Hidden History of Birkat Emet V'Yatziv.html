The Hidden History of ברכת אמת ויציב

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded כ"ו טבת תשפ"ה · 26 January 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026  ·  Mareh Mekomos upgraded 5 May 2026

A unique ברכה, with no opening "ברוך", longer than any other in the סידור. Through the שבולי הלקט we discover that the language of "אמת ויציב ונכון וקיים..." preserves an actual letter of acceptance from the diaspora communities to ארץ ישראל — and that the secret was carried into הלכה by an extraordinary, controversial encounter in Rome in 1280.

I. A Loose End from Two Weeks Ago — Women and the רמ"ח אברים

Two weeks ago we discussed the רמ"ח תיבות in קריאת שמע and, following the זרע קודש,[1] that they correspond to the רמ"ח אברים of the body. A participant raised a sharp question: women say קריאת שמע, and the גמרא states that women have more than רמ"ח אברים. How does the count work for them?

Searching the ספרים, this question was apparently not addressed for nearly a thousand years — until the מנחת אלעזר,[2] who answers: "אדרבה — יישר כוח!" The essential אברים are the רמ"ח; the additional אברים noted by חז"ל in women do not enter into this calculation. There is no concern with one's wife's קריאת שמע.

II. Introducing ברכת אמת ויציב

קריאת שמע in שחרית is bracketed by three ברכות: two before — יוצר אור and אהבה רבה (or אהבת עולם, depending on נוסח) — and one after, the ברכה that opens with "אמת ויציב" and closes with "ברוך אתה ה' גאל ישראל."

This is an extraordinary ברכה. It is the longest in the סידור — there is, to my knowledge, no longer one. And it carries a fundamental structural problem.

Where Is the Opening "ברוך"?

Every long ברכה opens with "ברוך אתה ה'" and closes with "ברוך אתה ה'...". "אמת ויציב" closes with the standard חתימה — but where is its opening "ברוך"? The answer requires us first to understand what the ברכה actually is.

III. The גמרא in ברכות י"ב ע"א — The Obligation

The גמרא[3] rules:

כל מי שלא אמר "אמת ויציב" בשחרית ו"אמת ואמונה" בערבית — לא יצא ידי חובתו.
Whoever did not say "אמת ויציב" in שחרית or "אמת ואמונה" in ערבית has not fulfilled his obligation.

Which obligation? The מצוה of זכירת יציאת מצרים.

Why "אמת" Is Attached

The פסוק at the close of פרשת ציצית in פרשת שלח[4] reads "אני ה' אלקיכם" — without אמת. חז"ל add אמת based on the פסוק in ירמיהו[5]: "וה' אלקים אמת." Functionally, אמת is attached to the close of קריאת שמע, but in truth it is the first word of the next ברכה: קריאת שמע and אמת ויציב flow as one continuous declaration.

IV. Verbal זכירת יציאת מצרים — The ספר החינוך

The ספר החינוך[6] teaches that of all the זכירות the תורה commands — the "שש זכירות" we recite at the end of the סידור: עמלק, what הקדוש ברוך הוא did to מרים, שבת קודש, and the rest — only one requires verbal expression. The זכירות can otherwise be discharged in the heart, by thought alone. זכירת יציאת מצרים is the unique exception: חז"ל require it to be articulated verbally, morning and night. Hence אמת ויציב and אמת ואמונה; without them, the מצוה is not fulfilled.

The פסוק of "להגיד"

The גמרא grounds this in תהלים צ"ב:[7]

להגיד בבקר חסדך, ואמונתך בלילות.

"להגיד בבקר חסדך" we fulfill through אמת ויציב — a ברכה saturated with the חסדים of הקדוש ברוך הוא: the יציאת מצרים, קריעת ים סוף, "ידיד נעוריו". "ואמונתך בלילות" we fulfill through אמת ואמונה — declaring our faith that just as הקדוש ברוך הוא rescued us from every enemy through history, so will He bring the final גאולה. The primary מצוה remains זכירת יציאת מצרים; חסד and אמונה are the further dimensions layered on top.

V. The Five Required Components — The ירושלמי

The ירושלמי in מסכת ברכות[8] rules that ברכת אמת ויציב must contain five components; omission of any one means the ברכה is not יוצא. The טור, citing the רא"ש with the relevant פסוקים, brings them; the משנה ברורה rules להלכה:[9]

  1. זכירת יציאת מצרים — the essence.
  2. מלכות — repeatedly: מלכנו, מלך אבותינו, גואלנו, גואל אבותינו.
  3. קריעת ים סוף.
  4. מכת בכורות — the climax of the revelations on מצרים; we are in the season of פרשת וארא and בשלח.
  5. שירת ישראל and their גאולה — "שירה חדשה שבחו גאולים..."

VI. Who Composed "אמת ויציב"? — The שבולי הלקט

What stands out at the very opening: "אמת ויציב ונכון וקיים..." — fifteen descriptive terms in a single chain (sixteen if one counts through "טוב ויפה"). No other ברכה from חז"ל reads like this — the cascade of conjunctions, the long historical narrative, the explicit acceptance language. This unusual style is what set the ראשונים hunting for the source.

The ספר שבולי הלקט

The decisive ראשון for our תפילה's history is the שבולי הלקט,[10] written by רבי צדקיה בן אברהם הרופא, who lived in Rome in the twelfth and thirteenth centuries. The בית יוסף and the שולחן ערוך[11] — particularly in אורח חיים, the שבולי הלקט's focus — cite him every few pages. An astonishing number of our daily practices rest on him alone; among the ראשונים shaping הלכה in this period, he is one of the most influential.

VII. The Four Noble Roman Families

The שבולי הלקט belongs to one of the most remarkable communities in our history. After the חורבן הבית, טיטוס exiled many Jewish families to Rome. Among them were four aristocratic families — the most distinguished families of ירושלים — who settled there together, intermarried, and preserved across centuries the מנהגים, תפילות, ברכות, and formulas they had brought from ירושלים. These are the ארבע משפחות מיוחסות בישראל, with a יוחס traceable directly to בית שני:

  1. משפחת הענבים — the Anavim, the "humble"
  2. משפחת האדומים — Min Ha'Adumim
  3. משפחת התפוחים — the Tappuchim
  4. משפחת הבחורים — the Bachurim

Each family had its own seal. They produced the most consequential פוסקים, דרשנים, and ראשי ישיבות of Italian Jewry, and shared a single בית כנסת in Rome called The Synagogue of the Four Heads, where the heads of the four families davened together.

Among them: רבינו נתן בעל הערוך[12] — author of the original Talmudic dictionary, a major ראשון cited constantly by תוספות — was of משפחת הענבים (the Ravivon family); רבי עזריה מן האדומים, the controversial ראשון, was of משפחת האדומים; the שבולי הלקט himself was of משפחת הענבים.

The Roman קהילה still preserves a unique נוסח — they recite ברכות different from ours and trace every detail back to בית המקדש. The פוסקים have ruled that one whose family מנהג follows the Roman tradition is to be left undisturbed.

VIII. רבי אברהם אבולעפיא — A ראשון Lost for Five Centuries

To understand how the שבולי הלקט arrived at the secret of אמת ויציב, we have to spend time with one of the most extraordinary — and most misunderstood — figures of the ראשונים: רבי אברהם אבולעפיא (c. 1240 – c. 1291).

An Ordinary תלמיד חכם — Until 31

For his first thirty years, רבי אברהם אבולעפיא was a conventional תלמיד חכם. He had studied with the בעלי התוספות, including רבינו שם משאנץ, one of the major בעלי התוספות. He learned the standard תורה of his day.

The Vision and the Mission

At age 31, he reports having received a prophetic vision — recorded in his autobiographical ספר העדות[13] — directing him to travel to Rome and meet the Pope, that some kind of reconciliation might be effected between Judaism and the Christian world. He spent ten years preparing himself for this encounter.

The Ten Lost Tribes — The Mongol Hypothesis

Before the Roman trip, he traveled toward ארץ ישראל because he believed he had found the ten lost tribes. His identification: the Mongols. The Mongol expansion of the early-mid 1200s — they had begun moving toward the Middle East and Turkey, ultimately clashing with the Mamluks (in our literature, הממלוכים) over ארץ ישראל — fit, in his mind, the גמרא's account of the סנהדרין[14] of the נהר סמבטיון — the river that throws stones all week and rests on שבת. He reached עכו in northern ארץ ישראל, but the wars made onward travel impossible; he turned back to Europe.

Meditation-Based קבלה

Back in Europe, אבולעפיא began to teach his particular method of קבלה. It was experiential — a קבלה not only studied but practiced, through meditative entry into יחודים using שמות הקדוש ברוך הוא, letters, and numbers. (Meditation, of course, is not foreign to תורה; the assumption that it belongs only to Eastern religions is mistaken.) He opened ישיבות in ספרד and Italy and drew students.

The Rebbe's aside: search YouTube for "whirling dervishes" — the Sufi Muslim mystics who pursue דבקות through whirling movement. Not, of course, that anyone in this שיעור is going to become a Sufi from a video, but it gives a sense of the experiential-mystical texture אבולעפיא was working in. He authored over fifty ספרים; most still remain in manuscript.

The רשב"א's חרם

The קהילה approached the רשב"א — the גדול הדור and the supreme halachic authority of Spain. The רשב"א, in שו"ת חלק א' סימן תקמ"ח,[15] issued a scathing חרם: he forbade printing, learning, or spreading אבולעפיא's תורה, and instructed כלל ישראל to distance themselves from him.

From roughly 1270 to 1291, אבולעפיא wandered city to city, refused everywhere on the רשב"א's authority. He kept writing; almost nothing was copied or learned.

The חיד"א's Rehabilitation

For nearly five centuries he was effectively unknown. רבי חיים ויטאל cites him once or twice; the רמ"ק mentions him[16]; otherwise his תורה vanished from the bookshelves. Then the חיד"א, in ספר שם הגדולים[17] (his great encyclopedia of ראשונים and אחרונים), wrote: "I know the רשב"א placed him in חרם; I know his method was a novelty rejected in his time. But I have looked into his ספרים — he was a giant in קבלה, and we should learn his ספרים." Slowly, publication began. Today there is a publisher in בני ברק who issues one ספר a year; some thirty or forty are now in print.

IX. The Trip to the Pope — Av–Elul 5040 (1280)

In the summer of 1280, אבולעפיא traveled to Rome to fulfill what he had understood as his mission ten years earlier. The cardinals refused his request for an audience. The Pope at the time, Nicholas III, had retired for the summer to the papal estate outside Rome — the same estate, Castel Gandolfo, that the popes still use.

אבולעפיא resolved to circumvent protocol and travel to the summer palace, where security would be lighter. He was warned: "If you are caught approaching the Pope, you will be burned alive." He was מוסר נפש — this story I do not know from Google; he writes it himself in ספר העדות — and went anyway. He records the date: י"ח אלול, four days before ראש השנה.

He reached the palace and knocked. The reply: The Pope died last night, suddenly, at 55, of a heart attack. אבולעפיא read this as סימן מן השמים, a sign that the קליפות had tried to obstruct his mission and that השם had stood with him. He returned to Rome for ראש השנה; on יום הכפורים he was arrested. Instead of being executed he was imprisoned for four weeks, then released and ordered to leave. He fled to Sicily, where he lived his last five years. Where and when he died, where he is buried — none of this is known.

X. The Roman Encounter — שבולי הלקט, ריא"ז, and אבולעפיא

While in Rome, אבולעפיא met the גדולים of the city. One was the שבולי הלקט, רבי צדקיה בן אברהם הרופא, of משפחת הענבים. The שבולי הלקט was struck by him: "This person knows so much. I want to learn with him."

The חברותא: מורה נבוכים.[18] The רמב"ם's controversial work on philosophy — itself, in earlier generations, the cause of חרם. The שבולי הלקט had not yet learned it.

A third חברותא joined them: רבינו ישעיה בן מלי טראני — the ריא"ז,[19] whose פירוש on the רי"ף every בחור and בן כולל learns; he was a cousin of the שבולי הלקט, also of the four families, and a nephew of the רי"ד of תוספות.[20]

The three of them — the most celebrated פוסק of אורח חיים, the most celebrated commentator on the רי"ף, and the most controversial מקובל of the era — sat together for roughly a month and learned מורה נבוכים. The tradition records that shortly after they finished, both the שבולי הלקט and the ריא"ז were נפטר.

That month or two with אבולעפיא changed the שבולי הלקט. He absorbed kabbalistic ideas he had not known before, and they made their way into his halachic ספר. For five centuries no one knew that אבולעפיא's תורה had quietly survived inside the שבולי הלקט's פסקים — and through them, inside our daily תפילה.

XI. The Secret of "אמת ויציב"

The שבולי הלקט, in his הלכות קריאת שמע, reveals what he received in Rome.

The Question

The ברכה says "ויציב ונכון וקיים וישר ונאמן ואהוב וחביב..." — but what is אמת ויציב? "This thing is אמת ויציב ונכון ..." — but which thing? קריאת שמע we just recited? "ה' אלקיכם אמת"? The coming חתימה "גואל ישראל"?

The Answer — Through קבלה

The שבולי הלקט answers — drawing, presumably, on אבולעפיא's קבלה — that the long chain refers back to what we just said: קריאת שמע, sealed by "ה' אלקיכם אמת". We affirm: this declaration is true and upright and accepted upon us.

The גימטריא

And how is this hinted in the very letters? Count the ו's in "ויציב ונכון וקיים וישר ונאמן ואהוב וחביב ונחמד ונעים ונורא ואדיר ומתוקן ומקובל וטוב ויפה":

  • Fifteen ו's.
  • 15 × 6 (the value of ו) = 90.
  • Add the two ה's of "הדבר הזה" (5 + 5 = 10): 100.

The שבולי הלקט calls this "דבר נפלא עד מאד."

XII. The Hundred ברכות — דוד המלך's תקנה

The טור at the opening of הלכות ברכות, citing רב נטרונאי גאון,[21] records: in the days of דוד המלך there was a מגפה taking one hundred lives a day. דוד inquired in heaven; the answer came that כלל ישראל had to bring more ברכות into daily life — one hundred ברכות a day. דוד המלך instituted the תקנה; the ברכות bracketing קריאת שמע in שחרית and ערבית are part of that count.

The Letter of Acceptance

The שבולי הלקט continues — in the era of the אנשי כנסת הגדולה, when the תקנה of one hundred ברכות was being cemented, an inquiry was sent from ארץ ישראל to the diaspora communities: "Do you accept upon yourselves the תקנה of one hundred ברכות daily?"

The communities of the diaspora wrote back. The reply contained, precisely, the language we now recite each morning:

אמת ויציב ונכון וקיים וישר ונאמן ואהוב וחביב ונחמד ונעים ונורא ואדיר ומתוקן ומקובל וטוב ויפה הדבר הזה.

מקובל — accepted. נחמד, נעים — delightful, pleasing. "This thing," the hundred ברכות, "is true upon us, and we receive it forever."

Fifteen ו's + two ה's = one hundred — encoded into the very ברכה the diaspora was being asked to add. חז"ל took that letter of acceptance and made it the opening of the ברכה after קריאת שמע, so that twice each day כלל ישראל renews — in the original wording — the original commitment.

XIII. Implications

This explains, structurally, why the ברכה opens without a "ברוך": its opening is not the start of a new ברכה but the continuation of קריאת שמע through "ה' אלקיכם אמת" into a long historical chain — ברכה ה'סמוכה ל'חברתה, ultimately closing with "ברוך אתה ה' גאל ישראל." It also explains the unusual לשון: the cascade of conjunctions and adjectives is the language of an actual letter, preserved verbatim.

And it carries a quiet historical lesson. The תורה of a ראשון placed in חרם by the רשב"א, lost for five hundred years, traveled into our daily תפילה through one Roman summer in 1280. We have been saying his words every morning of our lives.

XIV. Conclusion

This is one of four approaches the ראשונים develop on what "ויציב ונכון" actually refers to — each rich in its own historical and spiritual texture. בעזרת ה', next week we will take up the other three.

May we merit to daven with כוונה, to discharge the hundred daily ברכות as דוד המלך instituted them, and to serve הקדוש ברוך הוא בשמחה ובטוב לבב.

Together with R' Azariah of משפחת האדומים and the Shibolei HaLeket (also Anavim), part of the same Roman aristocracy descended from the four families exiled by Titus after the חורבן. ↩

R' Avraham Abulafia (c. 1240 – c. 1291), Sefer HaEdus (autobiographical). — The autobiographical record of his prophetic vision at age 31, his ten years of preparation, his identification of the Mongols with the Ten Lost Tribes, his journey to עכו, his return to Europe, and his attempt to meet Pope Nicholas III at Castel Gandolfo on י"ח אלול ה"א מ' (1280). The story is preserved entirely in his own writing; Abulafia is the principal source for his own biography. ↩
  14. סנהדרין (general reference; the סוגיא on נהר סמבטיון). — The גמרא's account of the river that throws stones all week and rests on שבת; the location, in חז"ל, of the Ten Lost Tribes. Abulafia's hypothesis identified the Mongols approaching the Middle East with the Tribes prophesied to return — fitting the Sambatyon's geography in his reading. ↩
  15. Shu"t HaRashba, חלק א' סימן תקמ"ח. — The Rashba's scathing חרם against R' Avraham Abulafia: prohibition on printing, learning, or spreading his תורה; instruction to כלל ישראל to distance themselves. The חרם shaped Abulafia's reception for nearly five centuries; almost nothing of his fifty-plus ספרים was copied or learned during that period. ↩
  16. R' Chaim Vital (general reference); R' Moshe Cordovero (רמ"ק). — The rare bridge-citations through which Abulafia's name survived between the Rashba's חרם and the Chida's rehabilitation. R' Chaim Vital cites him once or twice; the רמ"ק mentions him. Otherwise the תורה vanished from the bookshelves. ↩
  17. Chida (R' Chaim Yosef David Azulai), Shem HaGedolim, entry on R' Avraham Abulafia. — The Chida's rehabilitation: acknowledging the Rashba's חרם and the rejection of the method in its time, while affirming on the basis of his own study that Abulafia "was a giant in קבלה, and we should learn his ספרים." The Chida's authority began the slow reopening of Abulafia's corpus; today perhaps thirty or forty of his ספרים are in print, with one new title released each year by a publisher in בני ברק. ↩
  18. Rambam, Moreh Nevuchim. — The חברותא-text of the trio in Rome (1280): the Shibolei HaLeket, the Riaz, and Abulafia. The Rambam's controversial philosophical work, itself in earlier generations the cause of חרם; the Shibolei HaLeket had not yet learned it before this Roman summer. ↩
  19. R' Yeshayah ben Mali da Trani (Riaz), Pesakim on the Rif. — The third חברותא in Rome; cousin of the Shibolei HaLeket; nephew of the Rid (his uncle, of Tosfos Rid). The Riaz's פירוש on the Rif is standard learning material in ישיבות and כוללים. Tradition: he and the Shibolei HaLeket were both נפטר shortly after completing the Moreh Nevuchim with Abulafia. ↩
  20. R' Yeshayah da Trani the Elder (Rid), Tosfos Rid. — Uncle of the Riaz; major ראשון of southern Italy; of the same Roman aristocratic context as the four families. The Tosfos Rid is among the standard ראשונים printed alongside the גמרא. ↩
  21. Tur, או"ח, opening of הלכות ברכות; citing Rav Natronai Gaon. — The institutional account of the hundred daily ברכות: a מגפה in the days of David HaMelech taking 100 lives a day; the heavenly response demanding 100 ברכות daily; David HaMelech's תקנה; the ברכות bracketing קריאת שמע as part of the count. The framework into which the diaspora's letter of acceptance — preserved as the opening of אמת ויציב — fits as the original communal commitment. ↩
