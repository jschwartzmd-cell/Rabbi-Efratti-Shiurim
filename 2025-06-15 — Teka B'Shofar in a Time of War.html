תקע בשופר — In a Time of War

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 15 June 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

An abbreviated שיעור two days into the war with Iran. The streets of בני ברק empty, missiles falling on בת ים, the קהילה beginning אבינו מלכנו. Then into ברכת תקע בשופר as the hinge of שמונה עשרה — where the personal בקשות close and the communal בקשות begin. The מדרש on the שבטים's reunion in מצרים as the original "ברוך מקבץ נדחי עמו ישראל"; the מדרש in פרקי דרבי אליעזר on the two horns of איל העקידה; and Rav Schwab's metaphorical reading — that the שופר גדול is the moment when the world's leaders themselves declare that all power is from הקדוש ברוך הוא.

I. The Hour

The situation in ארץ ישראל is frightening. Someone sent me a photograph from בני ברק — the עיר ואם בישראל[1], the city that learns and davens day and night, the city that does not sleep — empty. The streets empty. ברוך השם, the קהילה is following the instructions of פיקוד העורף, but it is frightening to see those streets empty. People are sheltering at home.

I just heard: three children killed in Bat Yam. Missiles falling everywhere. Devastation[2].

Trust and השתדלות

The גאון רבי חיים קנייבסקי זצ"ל, during the first Gulf War when Scuds were flying toward בני ברק, said: "כל טיל יש לו כתובת"[3] — every missile has its address. We believe that absolutely. And the חיוב of השתדלות for פיקוח נפש is not suspended; one goes to the מקלט.

Several shuls in our community — Young Israel of Lawrence and Cedars among them — began saying אבינו מלכנו this morning. The הלכות of אבינו מלכנו need to be checked, but if כלל ישראל is reaching for אבינו מלכנו, the moment is real.

Seriousness

I saw a picture of three חיילים davening at the כותל — heartbreaking. Michael's son was at the כותל Friday night. Tell him: when there is an aza'akah, go to the מקלט. This is not a moment for heroics. We dedicate today's דברי תורה to the welfare of אחינו בני ישראל in ארץ ישראל, especially to the חייל killed today in עזה — we forget there is still a war ongoing in עזה alongside everything else.

רבונו של עולם, we need רחמי שמים. We need to feel the צער. Tell fewer jokes today; carry less cheer; enter a register of seriousness. They are laughing less when they run to the מקלטים.

For Israel's Leadership

Someone sent a video of יהודים singing קבלת שבת in a bomb shelter. The decision-making at the head of the State of Israel is unbearably weighty — when to bomb, whom, how. השם ירחם. הקדוש ברוך הוא is with us; we will be victorious; perhaps we are about to see the גאולה שלמה.

Reb Kozak's Organization

An abbreviated שיעור today — the רבנים of Lawrence (Rabbi Asher Stern, Rabbi Aharon, Rabbi Masri, Rabbi Orlian) are co-hosting a breakfast for an extraordinary חסיד from גור, Rabbi Kozak, who has built an organization providing mental-health therapy in ארץ ישראל for those uncovered by ביטוח לאומי — חיילים returning from עזה with PTSD, families needing therapy for their children, anyone in spiritual or psychological crisis. הצלת נפשות ממש. I checked the organization carefully before lending my name. צה"ל invites returning חיילים to one therapy session and is done; people fly to Thailand to escape what they saw, or come home shattered. This יהודי built the bridge.

II. ברכת תקע בשופר — The Hinge

There is no ברכה more meaningful at this moment in תקופת כלל ישראל. We are in a difficult תקופה; every יהודי who says תקע בשופר wants to hear that שופר.

The סדר in מגילה י"ז

The גמרא[4]: after ברך עלינו we close the segment of personal בקשות — דעת, תשובה, סליחה, גאולה פרטית, רפואה, פרנסה — and turn to the communal. The סדר: a person first asks for רפואה; then פרנסה (the basis of מנוחת הנפש and יישוב הדעת); then the ultimate מנוחת הנפש a יהודי can ask for — "וקבצנו יחד מארבע כנפות הארץ". קיבוץ נדחים is the deepest שלום.

III. The מדרש — The Reunion in מצרים

Each ברכה in שמונה עשרה was first uttered by the מלאכי השרת at one moment of history[11]. "ברוך מקבץ נדחי עמו ישראל" was first proclaimed when יעקב אבינו and the שבטים arrived in מצרים and were finally reunited — after יוסף's years in גלות מצרים and שמעון's imprisonment. The first time the family — the nucleus of כלל ישראל, the twelve שבטים, complete — stood as one. The מלאכים, watching, opened: "ברוך אתה ה' מקבץ נדחי עמו ישראל".

May we be זוכה to see the unification of all כלל ישראל soon, and join the מלאכים in saying it.

IV. What Is the שופר?

The פסוקים in תנ"ך we read on ראש השנה seem to indicate we will literally hear a שופר — an actual blast that grows and grows until the entire world hears it and gathers to ארץ ישראל. Is the שופר physical or metaphor?

The מדרש in פרקי דרבי אליעזר — Physical

The מדרש[5]: the שופר is physical, and traceable. When אברהם אבינו found the איל נאחז בסבך בקרניו — the ram caught in the thicket — and offered it in place of יצחק, the two קרנות of that ram entered Jewish history.

  • The left horn sounded קול שופר at מתן תורה. We just read about it on שבועות: "וקול שופר חזק מאד".
  • The right horn is the שופר של משיח — held in reserve since the עקידה, awaiting the moment.

The connection is dense and beautiful: the צער of the עקידה, אברהם's and יצחק's מסירת נפש, the ram caught in the thicket — that very horn closes our story. Another מדרש says אליהו הנביא will be the one to blow it.

The Connection to חצוצרות

This week's פרשה[6] mentions the חצוצרות: "חצוצרות על עולותיכם ועל זבחי שלמיכם". The טור connects תקע בשופר to the moment we return to ארץ ישראל with קרבנות in the בית המקדש — both are תקיעות.

V. Rav Schwab — The Metaphor

Rabbi Yossi Popack שליט"א[13] recently gave the Rebbe the ספר עיון תפילה from Rav Shimshon Schwab זצ"ל (now also published in English as Rav Schwab on Prayer; Meir Weiss, the מחותן of Reb Meir, made a bar mitzvah for one of the Schwab איניקלעך, Yisrael Schwab — an איניקל of רב שמשון שוואב). The ספר is remarkable; the more one reads, the more one appreciates the יהודי behind it.

Rav Schwab proposes a different reading of the שופר גדול[7]: it need not be physical. It is a משל.

The Misconception

Rav Schwab observed something in his day. In the 1980s, when beepers entered Jewish life — and now WhatsApp and the rest — the technology offered an unprecedented thing: at the press of a button, the entire globe could receive a single message. Various circles in כלל ישראל began circulating: "משיח is coming now," "We want משיח now." Bumper stickers. Street-corner posters. "משיח is here."

Rav Schwab said clearly: "I don't like it."

The Rebbe's framing: I am presenting Rav Schwab's שיטה without mocking other approaches. The Lubavitcher Rebbe זצ"ל had a deliberate שיטה here — to dissolve the תודעת גלות we had been stuck in for two thousand years and bring the consciousness of משיח to the front of the mind. To make people aware. The Rebbe (myself) is also a Lubavitcher חסיד by lineage; we study every שיטה, learn from everyone, and think critically. Rav Schwab was opposed to that מהלך. Both have על מי לסמוך.

Rav Schwab's claim: this kind of public campaign risks turning משיח and גאולה into a slogan, and the slogan into a mockery of one of the holiest concepts in יהדות. משיח is not a man on a white donkey saying "come back home." Those are משלים. What משיח represents is something far more profound.

The קושיא Posed to Rav Schwab

People came to him: "Rabbi, if we don't do something to prepare the world for גאולה, how will we be able to look משיח in the face? What will we say to him when he asks what we did to bring him?"

Rav Schwab's answer: "Even with all your bumper stickers and beeper messages, do you think you will be able to look at משיח? Do you know how afraid we will be? חז"ל describe משיח בן יוסף and משיח בן דוד; the נביא ישעיהו describes the world at that moment — the יראת שמים so intense that כלל ישראל will be hiding in נקיקי הסלעים[8], in the crevices of the rocks, unable to gaze upward."

VI. Rav Chaim of Brisk and the חזן

Two stories from Rav Chaim Soloveitchik of Brisk[12] illuminate the same point.

The הלל on פסח Night

Rav Chaim as a child loved music deeply. (He himself could not sing — he had no voice — but he understood music, perhaps mathematically.) In Brisk they followed נוסח אשכנז per the רמ"א and did not say הלל on ליל פסח in shul[9]. The Beis HaLevi (Rav Chaim's father, Rav of Brisk) would head straight home from מעריב to begin the סדר.

The חסידים in Brisk — Lechovitch, Kabrina, Slonim — kept their own שטיבל where they did say הלל at night, and sang it beautifully. One פסח night, walking home with his father, young Rav Chaim heard the הלל through the windows. He stopped. He wanted to listen. The Beis HaLevi pulled his hand to keep walking.

Rav Chaim protested: "I want to stay for the beautiful music, the beautiful הלל!"

The Beis HaLevi answered, in characteristically Brisker form: "It is not הלכה to say הלל now. If it is not the proper זמן הלכתית, what is beautiful about it? Beautiful music is meaningful only when it fulfills רצון ה'."

The חזן's ברכה

Years later, Rav Chaim attended a function with a choir led by a head חזן who sang like a מלאך. Rav Chaim approached him: "You moved me deeply. I want to give you a ברכה. What would you like — long life? Success? Children?"

The חזן's answer was unusual. Not wealth, not fame, not Carnegie Hall. "Rebbe, I want a ברכה that when משיח comes, I should be part of the choir that greets him — to be מקבל פני משיח with song."

Rav Chaim began trembling with יראת שמים. He answered: "Who do you think you are? Even the greatest צדיקים will be hiding their faces. משיח is a complete גילוי of כבוד שמים in this world. We will be hiding in the crevices of rocks, as ישעיהו describes. You think you have the audacity to imagine yourself singing in משיח's choir? It is beautiful you think of such things — but that ברכה I will not give."

Both stories — the child wanting to listen to הלל at the wrong time, the חזן wanting to sing for משיח — point to the same Brisker frame: spiritual experience is real, but always subordinate to the structures הקדוש ברוך הוא built. משיח is not a venue for self-expression. It is overwhelming קדושה.

VII. Different שיטות in כלל ישראל

Other communities have helped bring משיח and גאולה into our collective consciousness. Today there is no יהודי who does not recognize the signs or know the conviction that there will be a משיח and a גאולה. Each קהילה has על מי לסמוך; we should learn the best from everyone.

Many צדיקים kept a special בגד ready — a בעקישע, a frock coat, depending on the מסורה — for שבת or for משיח. Being prepared is appropriate. The texture of preparation differs across communities; each draws on real sources.

VIII. Rav Schwab on the שופר גדול

Rav Schwab returns to the שופר גדול with this frame: the שופר is the metaphor for the moment when the world recognizes that הקדוש ברוך הוא is המלך — that no power exists except through Him.

Imagine when the President of the United States, the kings and presidents and ministers of every country — secular or religious, makes no difference — stand and declare: "I am in this position only through the grace of God. I was elected, I was appointed; I hold this office because הקדוש ברוך הוא placed me here. Everything is מלכות הקדוש ברוך הוא." Imagine the United Nations — all the leaders of the nations — proclaiming together that everything belongs to הקדוש ברוך הוא, that recognition reverberating through every corner of creation.

That is the שופר גדול. The גאולה, on this reading, is fundamentally a transformation of consciousness — a universal recognition of "ה' מלך, ה' מלך, ה' ימלוך לעולם ועד"[10].

Glimpses

We see fragments today. World leaders sometimes acknowledge divine providence in their survival or success. Brief, partial — but seeds of awareness entering the collective consciousness of humanity. The full גאולה is when the seeds bloom into a chorus.

IX. Closing

May we hear בשורות טובות from ארץ ישראל speedily. May the חיילים in עזה and on every front return home safely. May the leadership of מדינת ישראל have סייעתא דשמיא in every decision they face. May the שופר גדול be heard — physical or metaphorical, however הקדוש ברוך הוא chooses — and may the world know ה' מלך, ה' מלך, ה' ימלוך לעולם ועד.
