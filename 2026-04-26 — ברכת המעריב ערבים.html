Rabbi Ephrati · Sunday Morning שיעור on תפילה

ברכת המעריב ערבים

A Family מחלוקת Between the רא״ש and רבי יחיאל, and the Essence of תפילת ערבית
Recorded 26 April 2026 · Reworked from the original שיעור

Today's שיעור focuses on תפילת מעריב, particularly the first ברכה before קריאת שמע — המעריב ערבים. We have been learning תפילת שבת, but paused to journey through מעריב, since we never properly discussed it in depth. The path will take us from a גמרא in ברכות, through a fascinating father-and-son מחלוקת in the family of the רא״ש, to a question that shakes the very ground of why מעריב was originally only רשות.

I. The Structure of ברכות קריאת שמע

There is a משנה in מסכת ברכות[1] that establishes the framework. In the morning we make two ברכות before קריאת שמע and one ברכה after. At night, by מעריב, we make two ברכות before קריאת שמע and two after. The first ברכה of מעריב is המעריב ערבים.

II. The גמרא's Question — Day in Night, Night in Day

The גמרא in ברכות דף ט״ו ע״א[2] raises a fundamental question. Why does the morning ברכה of יוצר אור also mention חושך — darkness? And why does the evening ברכה mention both day and night, light and dark? The morning ברכה should focus on יוצר אור — why include ובורא חושך? And the night ברכה speaks of the transition from day to night — why mix it all together, day and night, light and dark, in one breath?

The גמרא's answer is foundational: when חז״ל were מתקן the ברכות of מעריב and שחרית around קריאת שמע, they instituted that a yid must mention יום בלילה ולילה ביום — day at night and night at day.

III. רבינו יונה — Rejecting Dualism

The ראשונים — specifically the תלמידי רבינו יונה[3] in their commentary on מסכת ברכות, written by his disciples in his ישיבה — explain why. They cite the גמרא in חולין דף פ״ז[4], which references times of antiquity when המינים והאפיקורסים claimed there were two gods — one the creator of day and light, the other the creator of night and darkness. They tried to bring a פסוק in ישעיה as a "proof" for this dualistic theology.

To negate the position of the מינים and האפיקורסים, חז״ל built into our brachot that when we discuss the Creator of morning, we must not forget He is the same One who created night. Conversely, at night, when we recite the ברכה, it is our way of declaring that הקדוש ברוך הוא is one — the אחדות הבורא is absolute. Whether day or night, it is all the same אלוקים. That is the straightforward explanation for why we mention day at night and night at day.

IV. The Correct Reading — A Common Error

There is another נוסח of this ברכה in the גאונים, but our נוסח is what we say today. I want to address a common mistake almost all of us make — myself included.

How do we read the ברכה? Most yidden read: "בחכמה פותח שערים, בתבונה משנה עתים, ומחליף את הזמנים, ומסדר את הכוכבים…" — pausing as if "with wisdom He opens the gates of heaven, with understanding He changes times…"

The האריז״ל, quoted in the שערי תשובה סימן רל״ו[5], says no — we read it incorrectly. The phrase is really one continuous thought about הקדוש ברוך הוא changing the times, with the comma in a different place. The שערי תשובה writes a clever line: רוב העולם מערבבים ערבים — most yidden mix up המעריב ערבים. מערבבים means "mix up" — wordplay on our mistake.

The האריז״ל's position appears to be singular. כלל ישראל and all the printed סידורים, when they punctuated the נוסח, follow the common reading. (Apart from a סידור האריז״ל that adjusts the commas accordingly.) I do not fully grasp the difference between the two readings — it may be a קבלי matter, more esoteric and mystical. Perhaps another time.

V. The טור's Family Debate — A Window Into the Essence of מעריב

The Words at the End of the ברכה

At the end of the ברכה we say: המעריב ערבים, ומבדיל בין יום ובין לילה, ה' צבאות שמו. אל חי וקים תמיד ימלוך עלינו לעולם ועד. ברוך אתה ה' המעריב ערבים. He separates day and night; ה' צבאות is His name; the living and existing אלוקים should always reign over us — לעולם ועד.

The Disagreement in the Family

The טור in הלכות מעריב[6] brings a fascinating debate between his father the רא״ש and his older brother רבינו יחיאל. To appreciate it, we must know who they were.

The טור was written by רבינו יעקב, son of the רא״ש. The רא״ש — רבינו אשר בן יחיאל — was one of the greatest ראשונים we ever had. He had three famous sons among others. The oldest was named after his own father — רבי יחיאל בן אשר. The טור quotes him often. רבי יחיאל took over his father's ישיבה in Toledo, ספרד, after the רא״ש was נפטר.

A Father Standing for His Son Even though the more famous son of the רא״ש is the טור — רבינו יעקב, the younger one — רבי יחיאל was the eldest and a tremendous תלמיד חכם. The famous halachic question in מסכת קידושין[7]: if your son is a great תלמיד חכם and you are his father, he is חייב to be מכבד you; but because he is a great תלמיד חכם, you are obligated to be מכבד him. When he walks into the room, do you stand?

The רמב״ם in הלכות תלמוד תורה paskens that both stand for one another — we stand for the תורה itself. The חיד״א[8] brings a tradition: the רא״ש, when his son רבי יחיאל — the גדול הדור — entered the room, stood up entirely for him. Imagine how רבי יחיאל must have felt — the רא״ש standing for him.

We should all be זוכה to have the שקלא וטריא of standing for our children and grandchildren. I give every yid here a ברכה: we should be זוכה to have children and grandchildren who are better than us. אין אדם מקנא בבנו — a father is never jealous of his son. הלוואי, we should be זוכה to have this wonderful problem: should we stand for them, or should they stand for us?

VI. Who the רא״ש Was — A Bridge Between Two Worlds

תלמיד of the מהר״ם מרוטנבורג

We have to appreciate who the רא״ש was. He stands out from all the ראשונים. He lived first in אשכנז — Germany — as a תלמיד מובהק of the מהר״ם מרוטנבורג.

The Death of the מהר״ם and the Question of Ransom

In 1286 the מהר״ם מרוטנבורג was imprisoned. The רא״ש, his most beloved תלמיד, decided to gather the twenty thousand francs needed to secure his freedom. According to the famous tradition, the מהר״ם himself forbade this. The government would not have permitted such a transaction in any case, and the מהר״ם invoked a משנה in מסכת גיטין[9]: paying such a ransom would create an incentive for גוים to keep kidnapping yidden and demanding payment — a tragic pattern that has played out in our history again and again, and that we ourselves witnessed in עזה for five hundred and six days.

The רא״ש accepted his רבי's ruling and did not pay the ransom. (The מהר״ש in יום טוב ליפמן on מסכת גיטין פרק ה' argues that the legend is not historically accurate. Whatever the precise details, the מהר״ם died in prison.) Retrieving his body from the jail became its own saga; eventually a גביר committed to paying the substantial sum, on condition that he be buried next to the מהר״ם. Two years ago, with siyata di-shmaya, our group visited that cemetery and saw the מהר״ם's grave with the tomb of that בעל הבית beside him — a yid who chose to honor the תורה and bring his רבי to קבורת ישראל. One of the most moving stories from the era of the ראשונים.

The Rindfleisch Pogroms and the Flight from אשכנז

After the מהר״ם was נפטר, the רא״ש became the גדול הדור and chief רב of Germany. In 1298 — on כ״ב ניסן, which actually fell on April 20 of this year, six days before today's שיעור — one of the most horrific pogroms in our history broke out in that region of Germany: the Rindfleisch Pogroms[10].

Why "Rindfleisch"? It means cattle meat in Yiddish/German. The perpetrator who started the massacres bore that name (whether actual or a nickname is unclear), and he slaughtered yidden like cattle, like בהמות. The תקנה we discussed on שבת morning partially stems from this tragedy, among other similar events.

During the Rindfleisch Pogroms, in the very region where the רא״ש lived, his closest friend, רבינו מרדכי בן הלל — the בעל המרדכי who appears in the back of every גמרא, one of our great ראשונים — was killed together with his wife Zelda and their five children, ה' ינקום דמם. Between twenty and forty thousand yidden were slaughtered. One hundred forty-six קהילות in every village, town, and city in that region of Germany were completely destroyed. Christians came to shulen and burned them to the ground with the people inside. We have records of women and children jumping into bonfires with song and dance. We endured it. Look where we are today, ברוך השם — still here, thriving, still learning the תורה of the מהר״ם, the תורה of the מרדכי, the תורה of the רא״ש. עם ישראל חי, נצח ישראל לא ישקר.

The רא״ש's Livelihood

As a result of the pogroms, the רא״ש — being the leading rav in the area — was pursued by the government. He was a wealthy man who lost considerable money in this period. How do we know what the רא״ש did for a living? Because the רא״ש tells us. The טור brings it in הלכות אבלות in יורה דעה[11], in the discussion of what work is permitted on חול המועד. The רא״ש says: "I worked on חול המועד." His son the טור says: "My father worked on חול המועד." What did the רא״ש do? He was a moneylender.

In those days Christians were not permitted to engage in הלוואה ברבית — lending with interest. The yidden, however, had a special dispensation; they lent to גוים, and the Church acknowledged the practice. The רא״ש held an official license to lend money with רבית to גוים. He writes that he was מלוה ברבית on חול המועד, because if he did not conduct business on חול המועד, customers would go elsewhere and stop coming to him. So the גדול הדור sat at home — gentile customers came throughout the day; he ate his סעודה, learned תורה, conducted his lending business. He lost it all in the pogroms; not just lives but the whole Jewish economy was destroyed. (Yidden, characteristically, always managed to rebuild.) During this era, the רא״ש lost everything and found himself fleeing the authorities.

From אשכנז to ספרד — The Integration

The רא״ש first went to France, then traveled to ספרד. This was unprecedented — that the גדול הדור of אשכנז should relocate to ספרד. He did not become a ספרדי; he remained an אשכנזי. But when he traveled through Barcelona he met the רשב״א, the גדול הדור of the ספרדים. The רשב״א declared, "this man is one of the most special scholars I have ever met," and told the ספרדים — despite the limited connection between אשכנזים and ספרדים in that era — to receive him. The אשכנזי קהילה of Toledo (תוליטולא in the writings of the ראשונים) took the רא״ש as their רב, and he became a leading authority for the ספרדים as well.

What makes this so significant? The רא״ש took everything he had learned in אשכנז and integrated it with what he learned from the ספרדי גדולים. He demonstrated a unity in הלכה. While we have different מנהגים and נוסחאות, the רא״ש was crucial in the evolution of הלכה because he integrated both traditions. He took the best from the אשכנזים and synthesized it with what he later learned in ספרד. That is exactly why the טור — which became the basis of the שלחן ערוך — so successfully bridged both worlds. The טור was written by the son of the רא״ש, who learned from his father how to integrate both. That is כלל ישראל's הלכה today. We are not as far apart as ספרדים and אשכנזים as one might think; we are actually quite close. We owe the רא״ש a tremendous debt for bringing the two worlds — once separate — together.

VII. The Debate Over the נוסח of המעריב ערבים

רבי יחיאל's Position

Now we can appreciate the מחלוקת in the family. רבי יחיאל argued that the closing words of the ברכה — אל חי וקים תמיד ימלוך עלינו לעולם ועד — were a mistake. רבי יחיאל had been with his father in אשכנז, then moved with him to ספרד. When he came to shul in ספרד, he noticed that the ספרדים did not say these extra words; they simply concluded with ברוך אתה ה' המעריב ערבים.

Back in אשכנז, he remembered, they said the additional phrase. רבי יחיאל said to his father, "The אשכנזים are wrong. We should do like the ספרדים."

The Halachic Argument

Why? There is a הלכה in הלכות ברכות[12] that when you have a long ברכה, you must end it מעין החתימה — the immediate words before "ברוך אתה ה'" must directly relate to the ברכה's main theme. The phrase המעריב ערבים opens the ברכה. What does אל חי וקים תמיד ימלוך עלינו לעולם ועד have to do with המעריב ערבים? These additional words seem to separate the opening from the closing. There is "day and night" — then we should jump straight to ברוך אתה ה' המעריב ערבים. רבי יחיאל concluded: הספרדים הקדושים are correct.

The רא״ש's Defense

His father, the רא״ש, responded beautifully. Although he said, "now we are davening נוסח ספרד and we will daven their נוסח," he declared: "I am telling you that I can defend the נוסח of אשכנז that we used until today."

Why make a big deal of one sentence? Because it touches the very essence of the ברכה. According to רבי יחיאל, these words are not essential — that is exactly what bothers him. According to the רא״ש, however, these words absolutely relate to the ברכה — and his defense throws light on the essence of תפילת מעריב.

The Deeper Reading

The רא״ש explains: אל חי וקים תמיד ימלוך עלינו לעולם ועד is indeed part of המעריב ערבים. When we say המעריב ערבים, we are declaring that הקדוש ברוך הוא — the same One who is our King during the day — is the same One who is our King at night.

But the רא״ש offers a deeper meaning. Day is the time of גאולה. Night is the time of גלות. Every night is, in miniature, a beginning of גלות. As we transition from day to night, we ask הקדוש ברוך הוא: the same אלוקים who was with us by day and gave us חסד — חסד is like יום — should remain with us as night descends. It is one continuous unity.

In other words, if a yid has the right כוונה when he says these words at מעריב, he is asking הקדוש ברוך הוא: "You gave חסד during the day. There was sunlight, the birds were chirping, the world was alive. Now it is quiet. Now there is doom and gloom, darkness. But we want You to be — and this is the very essence of the ברכה — we want You to remain with us even into the night."

A beautiful פשט from the רא״ש in defense of נוסח אשכנז.

VIII. The ב״ח's Addition — מעריב as תפילת רשות

The ב״ח[13] builds on this and says it captures the essence of מעריב. The גמרא teaches that שחרית and מנחה are חובה — obligatory. מעריב, everyone agrees, began as תפילת רשות — optional.

The זוהר הקדוש[14] teaches that יעקב אבינו's image is engraved on the מרכבה. The מרכבה bears four images: שור, נשר, ארי, and אדם. Whose image is the אדם? The זוהר says it is יעקב אבינו. יעקב merited this more than אברהם (who had ישמעאל) or יצחק (who had עשו). יעקב's posterity was complete — שנים עשר שבטי קה. יעקב was the greatest צדיק. That is why his image is on the מרכבה.

Why, then, was the תפילה he established — מעריב — only רשות? Today we accept it as חובה — the שלחן ערוך records that כלל ישראל took it on as חובה, as we explained when we introduced מעריב. It is one of the very few halachic rulings classed this way. But why did יעקב אבינו's תפילה begin as רשות?

IX. The Fourteen Years of יעקב in the ישיבה of שם ועבר

What Did They Learn?

רבי צדוק[15] and others give a beautiful answer. Picture the scene of יעקב אבינו. He leaves his parents and makes a detour. Where did he go? For fourteen years, to the ישיבה של שם ועבר.

What was the ישיבה של שם ועבר? שם was the son of נח. עבר was his great-grandson. What did they learn? There was no תורה yet. The גמרא in מסכת יומא[16] says האבות הקדושים קיימו את כל התורה כולה קודם שניתנה — they had some revelation of what the תורה would be. אברהם אבינו ate מצה; he blew שופר. But שם ועבר were entirely before מתן תורה — yet they established the foundation. The אבות themselves were בני נח, and there is minimal discussion of the שבע מצוות בני נח in the גמרא — about three pages in סנהדרין דף נ״ח[17].

The question: what did יעקב אבינו learn for fourteen years by שם ועבר? If the curriculum took fourteen years, what was its content? The answer is that those ישיבות were not like our contemporary institutions focused on the legalities of הלכה and technical learning. They were spiritual centers — places of meditation where they spoke about mysticism and contemplated existence. Places of spirituality.

Why שם and עבר After Learning From אברהם and יצחק?

Everyone asks: יעקב learned by אברהם אבינו until age fifteen, and by יצחק his father. Why did he need fourteen years more by שם ועבר?

The famous answer: יעקב was about to enter גלות. The תורה learned in the ישיבה of אברהם and יצחק did not necessarily provide the survival skills a yid would need in the world, in גלות. אברהם and יצחק had a holy community, an enclave of קדושה inside a sea of גוים. The תורה they taught was not תורה you could carry into גלות — it would not help in the darkness of exile. To survive spiritually outside an enclave, יעקב needed to learn from שם ועבר, צדיקים who themselves had been righteous yidden — so to speak — among the גוים. He learned from them תורת הגלות.

This applies today as well. Sometimes ישיבות do not provide sufficient skills. If half the בחורים will go on to corporate America and the workforce, they need skills — not for parnasah alone, but for spiritual survival. Just learning תורה does not necessarily give a yid the tools to deal with the גוישע world without being מושפע. ישיבות should spend more time teaching Jewish children to value their identity, their religion, their תורה — and not to give it up when they enter the world. ברוך השם, in our community we live in something of a bubble; we may not feel the urgency. But I have seen friends who were the best בחורים in ישיבה — חס ושלום, I am not advocating against working if you need פרנסה — but the moment they went out and made money, they lost their connection. יהדות exists on Fifth Avenue too, but it must be maintained and sustained even there.

X. יעקב's Journey to חרן and the First מעריב

The Detour Back to הר המוריה

When יעקב arrived at חרן, the גמרא — and רש״י at the beginning of פרשת ויצא[18] — explain that he realized he had passed the place where his father had been bound on the מזבח, the הר המוריה. How could I not daven there? He turned to go back. הקדוש ברוך הוא responded by miraculously bringing הר המוריה to him, near לוז. יעקב fell asleep there.

The Vision of the Ladder

He dreamed of מלאכים עולים ויורדים. This is where ערבית begins. יעקב davened ערבית for the very first time. What were these מלאכים עולים ויורדים? רש״י explains: מלאך ארץ ישראל and מלאך חוץ לארץ. But the מדרש[19] offers another reading.

יעקב had a prophetic vision. He saw angels ascending, representing the four גלויות in our history: גלות בבל, גלות פרס, גלות יון, and גלות אדום (Rome, the West, our current exile).

  • גלות בבל: the angel climbed seventy rungs and came back down — seventy years, then return.
  • גלות פרס: fifty-two rungs — fifty-two years under פרס and אחשורוש.
  • גלות יון: one hundred and one steps — the era of חנוכה.
  • גלות אדום: the angel of עשו, the greatest שונא ישראל, the רודף — from whom comes Rome and אדום — kept ascending and did not come down.

יעקב fell into יאוש. What will become of me and my descendants? They will survive the others — but this final exile, what will be? Then he had the dream where הקדוש ברוך הוא said to him: do not be afraid. And הקדוש ברוך הוא was not speaking only to יעקב אבינו. He was speaking to every one of us. Every one of us is the יעקב in that vision, receiving the promise: even in the deepest גלות, even in the darkest time — I will be with you. You will be all right. You will survive. וזרעך כעפר הארץ — כלל ישראל will live and survive.

XI. The Essence of תפילת ערבית — תפילת אמונה

Three תפילות, Three States

This is מעריב. ערבית is the תפילה of אמונה.

שחרית is the morning תפילה — joy, gratitude, hope. Even if you were depressed last night, every yid wakes up to a new day. The birds chirp. The sun rises. New hope — maybe today will be better.

מנחה, the תפילה of יצחק, has a different posture — a small panic as the sun retreats toward the horizon. You want to grasp the last rays. You feel the day slipping away: "please be with me!"

ערבית is when it is dark, when there is nothing visible. You are יעקב without money, without family, not knowing if you will see your parents again. He was זוכה to see יצחק; not רבקה — she died before his return. You are יעקב with אליפז chasing him, going out into the big world, into לבן's home — the most morally depraved place imaginable, ממש a fraud, a fraudster. The environment is toxic, and that is where you are heading. What will be?

יעקב davened ערבית because ערבית is the תפילה of אמונה. Just as there is day, even at night when there is no hope, when there is nothing visible — הקדוש ברוך הוא is with me. I do not see it. I do not feel it on the surface. But I have the conviction. I am a yid of אמונה, a yid who has faith that הקדוש ברוך הוא is with me.

Why ערבית Started as רשות

רבי צדוק הכהן says: תפילת ערבית is the תפילה of אמונה. Therefore you have to aspire to acquire it. It does not come naturally.

In the morning, every child understands: a new morning, thank הקדוש ברוך הוא, gratitude. מנחה — the day is slipping away. (Electricity ruined this for us — we no longer experience the transition between day and night. In the old days night was a time of stillness; you shut down. Today night is when you "go out on the town" because of electric light. We no longer feel המעריב ערבים בחכמה — that night is the same as day, day as night. Imagine yourself upstate in the country where night is truly night — no light pollution. You feel the night. Night is a different mode of feeling, of existence. That is what יעקב experienced.)

יעקב said, "I am going to daven ערבית" — but that is not simple. It is not simple to acquire אמונה like that. It was not, originally, a חיוב. They did not incorporate it into the obligatory liturgy because it is something a yid must acquire. You must acquire a taste for אמונה. You must understand it. The גמרא's harsh language about a yid who does not say ערבית should be a lesson for us. We assume אמונה is easy. The Brisker Rav used to say: אמונה is easy when you have a million dollars in the bank. When you have nothing — that is where real אמונה begins.

So do not think, "I daven שחרית like אברהם, I daven מנחה like יצחק, ערבית will come easily." It is רשות, not חובה — and only later did כלל ישראל take it on as חובה.

XII. Returning to the נוסח

Now we can understand why אשכנז defended their נוסח. Part of the ברכה — and now that we have explained the essence of the תפילה — is exactly this: by day הקדוש ברוך הוא is with us; by night He will also save us. That is why right after שמע we say אמת ואמונה. This is אמונה. In the morning the emphasis is not specifically אמונה; at night it is. You must know how to have אמונה.

סיכום

May הקדוש ברוך הוא help us daven ערבית with the right כוונה, possess that beautiful מידה of אמונה, and be זוכה to a week full of הצלחה, ברכה, and ישועה. אמן.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. משנה ברכות פ"א מ"ד. — "בשחר מברך שתים לפניה ואחת לאחריה, ובערב מברך שתים לפניה ושתים לאחריה". The opening משנה establishing the structure of ברכות קר"ש: שחרית has יוצר אור and אהבה רבה before, אמת ויציב after. ערבית has המעריב ערבים and אהבת עולם before, אמת ואמונה and השכיבנו after. ↩
  2. ברכות י"א ע"ב – י"ב ע"א. — (Note: the שיעור referenced ברכות ט"ו ע"א; the principal סוגיא is at י"א-י"ב.) The גמרא's structural rule: "מזכירין מדת יום בלילה ומדת לילה ביום" — one mentions the attribute of day at night and the attribute of night at day. The morning ברכה includes "יוצר אור ובורא חושך"; the evening ברכה mentions both day and night. The reasoning: to negate dualistic theology and affirm that the same הקב"ה creates both light and darkness. ↩
  3. תלמידי רבינו יונה על מסכת ברכות (printed in רי"ף on ברכות). — Commentary on ברכות by the תלמידים of רבינו יונה in his ישיבה in Toledo, transmitted in his name. Anchors the גמרא's structural rule in the polemic against the מינים who claimed dualistic creation. Foundational source for the contemporary halachic framing of המעריב ערבים and יוצר אור. ↩
  4. חולין פ"ז ע"א. — The גמרא's exchange with the מינים who attempted to derive dualistic theology from biblical verses. חז"ל's response — that the same הקב"ה creates both light and darkness — became the structural basis for the ברכות קר"ש's mention of day-at-night and night-at-day. תלמידי רבינו יונה cite this סוגיא as the polemical anchor for the ברכה. ↩
  5. שערי תשובה, או"ח רל"ו, citing the אריז"ל. — שערי תשובה (ר' חיים מרדכי מרגליות, 18th century, son of ר' אפרים זלמן מרגליות) is one of the standard שו"ת-style commentaries printed at the back of the שלחן ערוך. He cites the אריז"ל's סדור on the proper punctuation of המעריב ערבים. The wordplay "רוב העולם מערבבים ערבים" ("most people mix up the evening ברכה") is widely cited. ↩
  6. טור, אורח חיים סימן רל"ו. — The טור (ר' יעקב בן אשר, son of the רא"ש, c. 1270–1340) records the family מחלוקת on the closing words of המעריב ערבים: his older brother ר' יחיאל בן הרא"ש argued the Sephardic נוסח (without "אל חי וקים") was halachically preferable; their father the רא"ש defended the Ashkenazic נוסח (with the additional phrase) on theological grounds related to the essence of תפילת ערבית. The סימן is foundational for the family's halachic-historical place at the bridge between אשכנז and ספרד. ↩
  7. קידושין ל"ג ע"ב; רמב"ם, הלכות תלמוד תורה פ"ה. — The classical סוגיא on כיבוד אב and כבוד תלמיד חכם: when one's son is a תלמיד חכם greater than oneself in תורה, both stand for one another (in honor of the תורה). The רמב"ם codifies the principle in הלכות תלמוד תורה. The חיד"א records that the רא"ש stood for his son ר' יחיאל when he entered the room — a vivid illustration of the principle in practice. ↩
  8. חיד"א on the רא"ש standing for ר' יחיאל.
    Note: The biographical anecdote of the רא"ש standing for his son ר' יחיאל is preserved in חיד"א's biographical/halachic compilations (likely שם הגדולים); verify exact citation. The story is a frequently cited illustration of the halachic principle in קידושין on parent-child כבוד dynamics where the son is the greater תלמיד חכם.
    ↩
  9. משנה גיטין פ"ד מ"ו; גיטין מ"ה ע"א. — "אין פודין את השבויים יותר מכדי דמיהן מפני תיקון העולם". The משנה's rule that one does not redeem captives for more than their value, lest גוים develop incentives to keep kidnapping yidden. The מהר"ם מרוטנבורג's reported invocation of this משנה in his refusal to be ransomed (1286) is one of the most famous applications of the principle in halachic history. The שיעור links it explicitly to the modern Gaza hostage crisis. ↩
  10. Rindfleisch Pogroms (1298) — קדושי קהילות אשכנז ובוואריה. — Wave of pogroms led by Rintfleisch (German "ox-flesh") of Röttingen, sweeping Franconia, Bavaria, and Austria from April 20, 1298 (כ"ב ניסן). Death toll estimates range from 5,000 to 100,000, with 146 communities documented as wiped out by Hebrew chronicles. ר' מרדכי בן הלל הכהן, the בעל המרדכי, was killed at Nuremberg with his wife and children. The רא"ש fled west to France and ultimately south to Toledo as a result. Modern scholarship: F. Lotter, Der Rintfleisch-Aufstand (1988); V. Stollberg-Rilinger. ↩
  11. טור, יורה דעה הלכות אבלות; או"ח תקל"ט on חול המועד. — The טור on permissible work during חול המועד: דבר האבד may be performed. He records his father the רא"ש's personal practice of conducting his moneylending business with non-Jews on חול המועד, classifying it as דבר האבד: if not done, the customers would shift to other lenders permanently. The story illustrates both the רא"ש's personal livelihood as a moneylender and the application of the דבר האבד principle. ↩
  12. ברכות מ"ט ע"א — מעין החתימה. — "כל ברכה שאין בה הזכרת השם ומלכות אינה ברכה" and "כל הברכות חותם בהן מעין החתימה סמוך לחתימה": every long ברכה must close with content that flows directly into the closing ברוך אתה ה' and matches the חתימה. ר' יחיאל's halachic concern: "אל חי וקים תמיד ימלוך עלינו לעולם ועד" seems to interrupt the flow from "מבדיל בין יום ובין לילה" to "המעריב ערבים". The רא"ש's defense reads the entire phrase as integral to the theme. ↩
  13. ב"ח, או"ח רל"ו. — The ב"ח (ר' יואל סירקיש) builds on the רא"ש's defense and develops the principle that the closing words of המעריב ערבים capture the very essence of תפילת ערבית: a continuous declaration that the הקב"ה who reigned during the day continues to reign at night, in גלות as in גאולה. This essence-based reading of המעריב ערבים sets up the שיעור's account of ערבית as תפילת אמונה. ↩
  14. זוהר חלק א' פרשת ויצא קמ"ז ע"א; בראשית רבה ס"ח. — The זוהר on the מרכבה (drawing on יחזקאל פרק א') and the four faces — שור, נשר, ארי, אדם — with the human face being that of יעקב אבינו. The מדרש רבה on פרשת ויצא develops the same: יעקב's דמות חקוקה על המרכבה. The שיעור's question — why was the תפילה founded by such a צדיק originally only רשות? — sets up ר' צדוק הכהן's answer about אמונה. ↩
  15. פרי צדיק, ר' צדוק הכהן מלובלין (1823–1900). — ר' צדוק הכהן רבינוביץ of Lublin, the פרי צדיק, one of the great חסידי-הלכתי thinkers of the late 19th century, originally a Litvish תלמיד חכם who became a חסיד of the קוצקער רבי and the איזביצער. His treatment of תפילת ערבית as תפילת אמונה — a תפילה that must be acquired rather than given naturally — appears in פרי צדיק on פרשת ויצא and in his treatment of יעקב אבינו's ladder vision. ↩
  16. יומא כ"ח ע"ב. — "קיים אברהם אבינו את כל התורה כולה עד שלא ניתנה". חז"ל derive that the אבות הקדושים kept the entire תורה before it was given at סיני, through divine inspiration and prophetic revelation. The שיעור invokes this to clarify what יעקב would have learned at the ישיבה של שם ועבר: not the תורה as later given, but spiritual disciplines for survival in גלות. ↩
  17. סנהדרין נ"ו ע"א – נ"ט ע"ב — שבע מצוות בני נח. — (Note: the שיעור referenced סנהדרין נ"ח; the principal סוגיא spans נ"ו-נ"ט.) The גמרא's discussion of the seven מצוות בני נח: idolatry, blasphemy, murder, sexual immorality, theft, eating from a living animal, and establishing courts of justice. The שיעור's point: the שבע מצוות are remarkably brief in גמרא coverage, suggesting that the curriculum of ישיבת שם ועבר was not legalistic but רוחני/meditative. ↩
  18. בראשית כ"ח:י"א; רש"י ad loc.; חולין צ"א ע"ב. — The opening of פרשת ויצא: יעקב's detour back to הר המוריה, the miraculous קפיצת הדרך, and the dream of the ladder. רש"י brings the חז"ל in חולין that יעקב instituted ערבית at this moment, and the related מדרשים on the מלאכים עולים ויורדים. Foundational פשט + מדרש for the שיעור's framework. ↩
  19. בראשית רבה ס"ח:י"ב – ס"ט:ב'; פסיקתא דרב כהנא. — The מדרש on יעקב's ladder vision developing the four גלויות reading: each angel's ascent represents one of the four exiles — בבל (70 rungs), פרס/מדי (52 rungs), יון (101 rungs), and אדום (the angel that did not descend). יעקב's יאוש and הקב"ה's reassurance — "אל תירא עבדי יעקב" — define the message of תפילת ערבית as the תפילה of אמונה in גלות, the שיעור's culmination. ↩
