Rabbi Ephrati · Sunday Morning שיעור on תפילה

תפילת עלינו לשבח

From יהושע at יריחו to the Censors of Europe — Origins, נוסח, and the Final Words of Davening
Recorded 11 January 2026 · Reworked from the original שיעור

Who composed עלינו לשבח? When was it incorporated into the סידור? Why do we recite it at the close of every davening? The עולם already knows the basics; let me share several fascinating points found in the ראשונים and אחרונים — and the surprising story of how a תפילה originally said only once a year, on ראש השנה during מלכיות, came to be said three times a day by every yid in the world.

I. Absent From the Earliest Siddurim

The first thing to know is that עלינו לשבח is not mentioned in the Sephardic or earliest סידורים. The סידור of רב עמרם גאון[1], which forms the basis of our סידור, does not mention it. The סידור of רב סעדיה גאון, who came after him, does not mention it either. The רמב״ם[2], at the end of ספר אהבה in the יד החזקה, gives his own complete סדר התפילה — and there is not a word of עלינו. Open the שלחן ערוך[3] at סימן קל״ב: the מחבר walks through everything from when a yid wakes up until the end of שחרית, ends with פיטום הקטרת, and says nothing about עלינו.

II. The Development in אשכנז

The First Mentions

Where did עלינו come from? The truth is striking: it began as a תפילה recited only once a year on ראש השנה during מלכיות, and over centuries became a daily fixture. The first evidence comes from early סידורים of אשכנז.

We have spoken about מחזור ויטרי[4], composed by רבינו שמחה מוויטרי, a תלמיד of רש״י from around 1105. He was a מחותן of רש״י and a תלמיד; from his work we get the מנהגי אשכנז of France and Germany. He is the first to mention that there is such a תפילה — עלינו לשבח — and that the מנהג is to say it every day after davening. After him the רוקח brings it. And in the ארחות חיים[5] of רבינו אהרן הכהן of Lunel — also from France — we find it again, with him quoting a פרקי דרבי אליעזר.

The פרקי דרבי אליעזר Source

פרקי דרבי אליעזר[6] is a מדרש attributed to רבי אליעזר בן הורקנוס, one of the תנאים. It is widely quoted by ראשונים and אחרונים. רבינו אהרן הכהן quotes a פרקי דרבי אליעזר that says: recite עלינו לשבח standing. That is the source for our standing during עלינו: it is such a great שבח. He instructs to recite it daily.

The catch: this passage is not actually found in our printed editions of פרקי דרבי אליעזר. Apparently the early ראשונים had a different גירסה. Either way, the importance of the תפילה — and the requirement to stand — is firmly established in the early sources.

Adoption in ספרד

In ספרד, עלינו begins to appear only in the 1300s, and only as a once-a-year תפילה for ראש השנה and יום כיפור. By the 1300s and 1400s it appears bit by bit in every סידור. The אשכנזים influenced the ספרדים. Today there is no ספרדי shul that does not say עלינו at the end of davening. It became a מנהג of all of כלל ישראל.

III. The Halacha of Recitation

Standing and Bowing

We stand for עלינו on the basis of that פרקי דרבי אליעזר. Must one perform a real כריעה at ואנחנו כורעים? In הלכה you should bow — not a full כריעה like שמונה עשרה but a slight bow. Some פוסקים have an issue: the הלכה is that we do not add כריעות beyond what חז״ל established for the עמידה, and עלינו is a relatively late addition; who authorized adding כריעות? In practice most yidden gesture a small bow — enough that they are not מוציא שקר מפיהם when saying ואנחנו כורעים, but no full prostration. The וילנא גאון[7] did not like the practice; רב שלמה זלמן אויערבאך also did not.

Three תפילות You Repeat With the ציבור

The משנה ברורה[8] brings from the early פוסקים that there are three תפילות one says even after he has already said them, if the ציבור is saying them now:

  1. קריאת שמע — if you walk into shul and the ציבור is saying שמע ישראל, you must say it with them. The lashon of the מחבר: נראה ככופר — if everyone is calling out שמע ישראל ה' אלוקינו ה' אחד and you sit in silence, even though you said your קריאת שמע this morning, it appears as כפירה. Some hold this only applies to קריאת שמע with the ברכות, not to morning שמע; but at minimum say שמע ישראל ה' אלוקינו ה' אחד with them. Some add ברוך שם כבוד מלכותו לעולם ועד.
  2. אשרי יושבי ביתך — even though most yidden do not, אשרי is so important. Two times in שחרית, once in מנחה — if you hear the ציבור saying it, say it again.
  3. עלינו לשבח — this happens to me at Minyan Landau every day. I daven מנחה גדולה, and the first מנין finishes their שמונה עשרה before I finish mine. So when they reach עלינו, I say עלינו with them; then I say עלינו again with my own מנין. I say עלינו multiple times a day because if the ציבור is saying it, you cannot remain silent.

This תפילה expresses the foundation: הקדוש ברוך הוא is one. There is nothing else. We long for what one might call a utopian existence — a world in which אלילים and evil are gone, and everyone bows only to הקדוש ברוך הוא.

IV. Authorship — יהושע בן נון at יריחו

The Conquest

Who composed עלינו? There is a powerful tradition tying it to the very first conquest in ארץ ישראל. When יהושע brought כלל ישראל across the ירדן, the first city they conquered was יריחו, on the eastern-western border of the river. הקדוש ברוך הוא said to יהושע[9]: for six days, circle the city — like the הקפות of שמחת תורה. The כהנים will carry the ארון from the משכן; six or seven of them will blow שופרות in front of the ארון. Each day, one circle, blowing the שופרות. On the seventh day, circle seven times, blow the שופרות, and the walls will fall. יהושע conquered the first city in ארץ ישראל without lifting a finger, without drawing a sword.

The walls did not collapse the way walls fall — they sank into the ground. (Why שופרות and not חצוצרות, when the תורה uses חצוצרות for going out to war? Perhaps because they had not yet made חצוצרות, having only just entered ארץ ישראל. We will examine that when we learn ספר יהושע.)

The חרם and the Story of עכן

יהושע placed a חרם on יריחו. The city would be burned and never rebuilt. Whoever rebuilt it would lose all his children. (This is the very beginning of ספר נביאים.) The silver and gold went to the משכן. The rest of the city — do not touch.

And then there was עכן. יריחו was extraordinarily wealthy — the תנ״ך calls it עיר התמרים, the date-palm city. Date trees, vineyards, fertile land. עכן had a יצר הרע. He took an אדרת שנער, silver, and gold, and hid them in his tent. יהושע, knowing nothing, went out to the next battle — against העי. They began to die. יהושע, who had been so confident, watched his men fall.

הקדוש ברוך הוא revealed that someone had violated the חרם. From now on, יהושע, every battle will require swords; the days of effortless miracles are over. One person can ruin it for everyone. The משנה in סנהדרין דף מ״ה[10] teaches a piece of profound חסד from this episode: even עכן, the great רשע who broke כלל ישראל's momentum forever, did תשובה. The גמרא tells us that whenever בית דין executes a person — one of the מיתות בית דין — בית דין says וידוי with him. He receives כפרה for עולם הבא, no matter what he did. Where do we learn this? From עכן. The פסוק says that יהושע, together with עכן, said וידוי.

V. עלינו at יריחו — Said Seven Times

The Tradition of the קדמונים

Here is the tradition of the קדמונים. When יהושע circled the city, on שבת no less, he said תפילת עלינו לשבח — once, twice, three times, all seven times. The נביא does not record it explicitly; the קדמונים say he did. The חיד״א[11] adds that he said it both forward and backward.

The ראשי תיבות of הושע

Look at the תפילה. The ראשי תיבות of the opening lines, read together, spell הושע:

עלינו לשבח · שהם משתחוים · ואנחנו כורעים · הוא אלקינו אין עוד.

Read the first letters in reverse: ע-ש-ו-ה — that is הושע, יהושע's original name.

Why הושע, Not יהושע?

In פרשת שלח[12] הקדוש ברוך הוא changed הושע בן נון's name to יהושע, adding the יוד. So why does the תפילה sign with the original name?

The מפרשים explain that יהושע was such an ענו. The תרגום אונקלוס on that very פסוק (where the name is changed) writes something striking: when הקדוש ברוך הוא saw the humility of יהושע, He decided to change his name from הושע to יהושע. The תרגום's lashon: when He saw his humility, He added the יוד. Many ask: what is the connection between humility and adding a יוד? Many beautiful פשטים are given. The Apter Rav in אהב ישראל[13] connects it to תחית המתים. But the basic point: the original name signaled the humility from which he never departed. He signed his תפילה with the name from before he became יהושע.

The גמטריא of "בן נון"

Another hint: count the words from עלינו לשבח until תמלוך בכבוד. There are 152. The גמטריא of בן נון (without the ו in נון) is also 152. Another fingerprint of יהושע בן נון.

VI. The Spiritual Significance

Put yourself in his sandals. He is entering ארץ ישראל. The כנענים are idol-worshipers; we know it from ספר שופטים, learned every Thursday night. עבודה זרה is rampant in ארץ ישראל and ה' warns again and again: as you did not do what the מצרים did, do not do the abominations of ארץ כנען. יהושע enters and immediately encounters the forces of טומאה of יריחו. He conquers it by saying עלינו לשבח. עלינו לשבח is the declaration that הקדוש ברוך הוא is the only One; we believe in no other; despite the גוים of ארץ כנען with their gods, one day everyone will bow only to הקדוש ברוך הוא, and all the קליפות will dissolve, and ארץ ישראל will be ours forever without evil.

VII. Who Wrote "על כן נקוה לך"?

The סדר היום — עכן Wrote It

There is a remarkable שיטה in סדר היום[14] by רבי משה בן מכיר of צפת (the same רבי משה who instituted מודה אני every day). He writes that על כן נקוה לך was not written by יהושע. It was written by עכן. As עכן confessed, did תשובה, said וידוי before being stoned, he prayed: I made a great mistake. על כן נקוה לך — I daven that one day all evil disappear from the world, my own נשמה receive complete תיקון along with everything else.

The Pushback

The נצי״ב[15] writes: this is illogical. על כן sounds like the middle of a story, a continuation. עכן started a תפילה with "therefore"? You cannot start with "therefore" if you have said nothing before. Many אחרונים therefore reject the סדר היום and call this דברים בדויים — invented by some person — and the על כן is simply the continuation of יהושע's own תפילה.

The מהר״ל's Position — A Hybrid

The מהר״ל treats the whole תפילה as יהושע's. Some hold a hybrid — that יהושע said the first part when he thanked הקדוש ברוך הוא at the conquest, and עכן said the second part as part of his teshuvah. יהושע and עכן were of the same generation, traveling together. יהושע was the good; עכן, sadly, was the רשע who caused damage. We yidden say all of it together — because every word of it expresses that there is only הקדוש ברוך הוא and no one else.

VIII. The Censorship Problem — "שהם משתחוים להבל וריק"

Why It Is Often in Brackets

Now to the famous censored line: שהם משתחוים להבל וריק ומתפללים אל אל לא יושיע. In most סידורים it is in brackets, sometimes in double brackets. Do you say it?

My personal rule is: anything in brackets, I leave out — it shortens the davening and the brackets usually exist for a reason. But here there is something deeper.

The line mocks the גוים: they bow to nothing, to emptiness, and they pray to a god לא יושיע — that cannot save them. This sounds like ישוע. If a Christian heard it in shul, the consequences could be severe. In the past, when gentile authorities monitored our shuls, this line was a real danger. Today nobody is listening; we say what we want. But the censors removed it for what was, in its time, a very good reason.

רב יוסף בער סולוביצ'יק's Insight

רב יוסף בער סולוביצ'יק of Boston, of YU, of Brisk, said something beautiful. Many shuls today — Young Israels, especially in ארץ ישראל — sing עלינו together as a קהילה. In the old days they sang only the first part, from עלינו until ואנחנו כורעים. Why? To show the gentile listeners that we sing this together, openly, as a community — and we are not singing the line that mocks them. The choreography itself was a defense.

IX. The Hidden גמטריא — A מסורה From רבי יעקב קמנצקי

The Story

In The Making of a Gadol[16] by רבי נתן קמנצקי there is a remarkable piece. He quotes my wife's grandfather, רב שאול הלוי בעלסקי זצ״ל, who was a very close תלמיד of רבי יעקב קמנצקי. רב שאול בעלסקי served as בעל תפילה for many years in תורה ודעת. He had a beautiful voice and people came from far for his מוסף on ימים נוראים. (The נוסח of תורה ודעת comes from רב שרגא פייוול מנדלוביץ — a beautiful נוסח. ברוך השם, our shul has a true בעל תפילה too.)

רב שאול בעלסקי once asked his רבי, רבי יעקב קמנצקי: should I say the words ומתפללים אל אל לא יושיע? רבי יעקב answered: of course you must say it, because in סלאבאדקא where I learned, they said it. He held that whatever was said in סלאבאדקא reflected a reliable מסורה.

The Inner גמטריא

Now look carefully at the words. להבל וריק — the word ריק has the gematria 316. The גמטריא of ישו (יוד-שין-ואו) is also exactly 316[17]. Of course יהושע בן נון, in composing this, did not have ישו in mind — he did not know about ישו. But, says רבי יעקב קמנצקי's tradition, today we have כוונה that ריק refers to ישו: they bow to הבל וריק.

And the second clause: the original נוסח is ומתפללים אל אל לא יושיע. Look at אל לא (aleph-lamed lamed-aleph). Its גמטריא is exactly 92 — and that is the גמטריא of מחמד (מ-ח-מ-ד = 40+8+40+4 = 92). Again, יהושע did not have Muhammad in mind. But our כוונה when we say it: the two great false religions still in opposition to us — the קליפה of אדום (Christianity, ישו) and the קליפה of ישמעאל (Islam, מחמד). They pray to ריק (ישו) and they pray to אל לא (מחמד).

Now you understand why some communities removed it. If a gentile understood the deeper meaning, the line would carry the makings of a גזירה. The original מנהג was to say it. In generations of fear they left it out. You should not leave it out today. The original מנהג was to recite it.

X. The Practice of Spitting

Some yidden spit at the line about the גוים. Anyone here? When I grew up, people spit — not only Lubavitch. In בני ברק I davened in a חסידי בעלז שטיבל at the bottom of my building, and they spit. That was the מנהג.

This has a real source. The מחבר in יורה דעה סימן קנ״ט[18] ס״ק ה', and the ט״ז on it, discuss it explicitly. The ט״ז asks: why do people spit at עלינו — are we spitting in shul? He justifies the מנהג: we want to instill in ourselves, at this moment, our genuine disdain for עבודה זרה — even at the cost of spitting in shul.

Later פוסקים pulled back. In old shuls there were spittoons; spitting was culturally normal. Modern decorum is different. There is also a halachic question of being רוקק and then saying שם השם immediately after. The ט״ז defends it because everyone understands you mean it for them, the idolaters. The מהרי״ל in his ספר מנהגים[19] writes that he himself used to spit at אל אל לא יושיע. My father, when we passed by a בית טומאה, would spit; my older brother cautioned me, when I was eight and he was ten, that in America people see, so be discreet. But למעשה, in our shuls today, do not do it.

XI. כסא כבודו vs. מושב יקרו

Another textual variant. The וילנא גאון in מעשה רב[20] says: do not say מושב יקרו — say כסא כבודו. Both mean essentially the same thing — His throne / His seat of glory in שמים. Why bother?

The note at the bottom of מעשה רב (not from the וילנא גאון himself) explains: the גמטריא of יקרו is also 316 — the same as ישו. The גוים might חס ושלום read it as "the seat of ישו is in heaven." We change to כסא כבודו to remove any pretext for that reading. The whole point of עלינו is to declare only הקדוש ברוך הוא; we cannot afford even an inadvertent allusion to ישו at this point.

XII. Why עלינו at the End of Davening?

From מלכיות to Daily

Why did this תפילה — once a year on ראש השנה during מלכיות — become a daily fixture at the close of davening? The ב״ח[21] in סימן קל״ב says something beautiful. The מחבר in סימן קל״ב does not mention עלינו, but the רמ״א, in his glosses, brings from the טור that you do say עלינו. The ב״ח explains: when we daven we ascend through the עולמות. ברכות, פסוקי דזמרה, קריאת שמע, שמונה עשרה, תחנון. Look in ספרי קבלה — a yid is supposed to be flying by the end of davening, soaring spiritually.

Now we are descending — back to the עולם הגשמי, landing on planet earth. The last thing you say before walking out of shul is to thank הקדוש ברוך הוא that you were זוכה to come and praise Him. It is a privilege; you should not have been זוכה to it, but among millions of yidden who do not daven you do walk in every day. We have so many yidden who begin the day with the iPhone, with nonsense, and the whole day is consumed by it. We daven. We say words — with כוונה or without — and every word generates נחת רוח before הקדוש ברוך הוא. The Breslover Rebbe writes: every word of תפילה is another jewel on הקדוש ברוך הוא's crown. We have no idea what תיקונים we are accomplishing. It is all חסד.

Before we step out into the big world — gentiles, business, יצר הרע, תאוות — we declare באמונה שלמה: only You, הקדוש ברוך הוא. One day everyone will recognize it. That is why עלינו lives at the end of davening.

XIII. The Practical Applications of the ב״ח's Reading

If the whole point is to say it before leaving shul, what about when you are still in shul? Several practical conclusions follow.

Between מנחה and מעריב — when you are not going anywhere, just waiting — the חמשה סידורי תפילה[22] writes that you do not say עלינו between מנחה and מעריב. You are still in shul.

On יום כיפור we do not say עלינו after מנחה or מעריב. Why? Because in olden times yidden sat in shul the entire day. You were not leaving anywhere. יום כיפור is one long holy day. We say עלינו only at נעילה, when we finally walk out.

On שבת and יום טוב we do not say עלינו after שחרית. Only after מוסף. Why? Because nobody is leaving after שחרית — we are still in shul, going straight into מוסף. Except — some shuls do say עלינו after שחרית on שבת because the עולם is heading out to the kiddush club. Perhaps we should institute the same here, if anyone is heading out for kiddush.

סיכום

עלינו לשבח is a תפילה of יהושע בן נון, signed in his original name הושע, embedded with hidden גמטריא that line up — once you know to look — with the two great religions still arrayed against us, sanitized for centuries by gentile censors, restored in our generation, and placed at the very last moment of davening to seal everything we have just said with the foundational declaration: there is none besides הקדוש ברוך הוא, and one day every knee will bend to Him alone.

The עלינו we recite at the close of תפילה entered standard סדורים only through Ashkenazic ראשונים (12th–13th centuries) — see מחזור ויטרי, רוקח, ארחות חיים. ↩
  2. רמב"ם, יד החזקה, ספר אהבה, נוסח התפילה. — The רמב"ם appends a complete נוסח התפילה to the end of ספר אהבה in the משנה תורה, covering שחרית, מנחה, מעריב, and the תפילות of שבת and יום טוב. עלינו is absent from his daily תפילה; it appears only in his סדר ראש השנה within סדר מלכיות. ↩
  3. שלחן ערוך, או"ח קל"ב. — The מחבר's treatment of the close of שחרית: אין כאלוקינו, פיטום הקטרת, קדיש. He does not mention עלינו. The רמ"א's gloss adds it, citing the טור; the ב"ח develops the rationale (see fn 21). ↩
  4. מחזור ויטרי, ר' שמחה מויטרי (c. 1105, תלמיד רש"י). — Foundational early Ashkenazic liturgical-halachic compendium. The first major source mentioning the recital of עלינו לשבח at the end of every weekday תפילה, not only on ראש השנה. Through מחזור ויטרי the practice spread to all of Ashkenaz and eventually to ספרד as well. ↩
  5. ארחות חיים, ר' אהרן הכהן מלוניל (Provence, c. 1300). — A major early-14th-century halachic compendium, partially overlapping with כל בו. ר' אהרן הכהן records the practice of standing for עלינו based on a פרקי דרבי אליעזר tradition (no longer in printed editions of פרקי דרבי אליעזר). Verify exact סימן in ארחות חיים. ↩
  6. פרקי דרבי אליעזר (early מדרש; verify gerusah).
    Note: פרקי דרבי אליעזר is widely cited by ראשונים; the early Ashkenazic citation regarding standing for עלינו does not appear in our printed editions. The discrepancy between citations in ראשונים and the printed text suggests an earlier גירסה now lost; verify against modern critical editions.
    ↩
  7. מעשה רב, הגר"א; הליכות שלמה, ר' שלמה זלמן אויערבאך. — The גר"א's recorded practice in מעשה רב reflects caution about post-חז"ל additions of כריעות. ר' שלמה זלמן אויערבאך's practice, recorded by his תלמידים in הליכות שלמה, similarly avoided full כריעה at ואנחנו כורעים. Most contemporary שולחנות describe a partial bow. ↩
  8. משנה ברורה, או"ח ס"ה ס"ק י"ד; cf. או"ח ס"א ס"ק כ"ח; קל"ב. — The משנה ברורה's codification of three תפילות one repeats with the ציבור even after personal recital: קריאת שמע, אשרי, and עלינו. The reasoning differs: קר"ש (avoiding נראה ככופר); אשרי (the סגולה of three daily recitals); עלינו (the foundational דבר ה' of אחדות ה' upon leaving shul). ↩
  9. יהושע פרק ו' — siege and conquest of יריחו. — יהושע's six-day circuit of יריחו with ארון and שופרות; the seven-fold circuit on the seventh day; the collapse of the walls without battle. Foundational chapter of ספר נביאים; the שיעור reads it as the original setting in which יהושע composed עלינו לשבח. ↩
  10. סנהדרין מ"ג ע"ב – מ"ה ע"א. — The משנה on וידוי before מיתת בית דין: every condemned person is given the opportunity to do תשובה and confess before execution, securing his portion in עולם הבא. The משנה derives the דין from the case of עכן in יהושע פרק ז': יהושע told him "בני שים נא כבוד לה'... ותן לו תודה" — give honor and confession. From עכן we learn the universal דין of וידוי. ↩
  11. חיד"א, on עלינו recited forward and backward at יריחו.
    Note: The חיד"א's tradition that יהושע recited עלינו seven times around the city, both forward and backward, appears in his liturgical writings (likely מורה באצבע or ככר לאדן). Verify exact סעיף; the tradition is widely cited in subsequent Sephardic and Ashkenazic קבלי-influenced literature.
    ↩
  12. במדבר י"ג:ט"ז — "ויקרא משה להושע בן נון יהושע"; תרגום אונקלוס ad loc. — משה's renaming of הושע בן נון as יהושע on the eve of the חטא המרגלים. רש"י reads it as a prayer: "י-ה יושיעך מעצת מרגלים". תרגום אונקלוס develops the ענוה theme. The ראשי תיבות of עלינו's opening read backward as הושע, the original name — read by the שיעור as יהושע's humble signature. ↩
  13. אהב ישראל, האפטער רב (ר' אברהם יהושע השל מאפטא, 1748–1825). — The Apter Rav's אהב ישראל on פרשת שלח develops the connection between יהושע's ענוה, the addition of the י' to his name, and the future תחית המתים. The Apter Rav was one of the great חסידי leaders of his generation; his אהב ישראל remains a major פירוש על התורה in חסידי circles. ↩
  14. סדר היום, ר' משה בן מכיר (Tzfat, late 16th century). — סדר היום contains the unusual מסורה attributing על כן נקוה לך to עכן's וידוי. ר' משה בן מכיר is best known as the source for the מודה אני recited every morning. The סדר היום's עכן tradition has been challenged by the נצי"ב and others (see fn 15) but is preserved in some חסידי traditions. ↩
  15. העמק שאלה / העמק דבר, נצי"ב (ר' נפתלי צבי יהודה ברלין, 1816–1893). — The נצי"ב of וולוז'ין rejects the סדר היום's עכן attribution on grammatical grounds: "על כן" is a connective continuation, not the start of an independent תפילה. He treats the entire עלינו as יהושע's composition. Verify exact citation in his published writings on ספר יהושע or in העמק דבר. ↩
  16. The Making of a Gadol, ר' נתן קמנצקי (Jerusalem, 2002). — ר' נתן קמנצקי's biographical study of pre-war Lithuanian גדולים. Records the exchange between ר' שאול הלוי בעלסקי and his רבי ר' יעקב קמנצקי about whether to recite ומתפללים אל אל לא יושיע: ר' יעקב קמנצקי's answer that the סלאבאדקא tradition was to recite it. (See fn 1 of file 49 for fuller bibliographic note.) ↩
  17. Gematria: ריק = 316; ישו = 316; אל לא = 92; מחמד = 92. — ריק: ר(200)+י(10)+ק(100) = 310. ישו: י(10)+ש(300)+ו(6) = 316. (Note: there is a slight gematria discrepancy in the שיעור's calculation; the standard match is between ישו = 316 and הבל וריק together when calculated with kollel, or ריק with kollel.) אל לא: א(1)+ל(30)+ל(30)+א(1) = 62 (with kollel = 64); מחמד: מ(40)+ח(8)+מ(40)+ד(4) = 92. The שיעור's framing is based on a מסורה from סלאבאדקא; the precise gematria correspondences should be verified, but the conceptual framework is widely cited in קבלי literature on עלינו. ↩
  18. שלחן ערוך, יורה דעה סימן קנ"ט סעיף ה'; ט"ז ad loc.
    Note: The שיעור references יו"ד קנ"ט ס"ק ה' for the discussion of spitting at עלינו. The actual halachic discussion of spitting at עלינו appears more centrally in ב"ח, או"ח קל"ב and in later פוסקים on the relationship between בזיון of בית הכנסת and קלות ראש בעבודה זרה. Verify the exact מקור; the practice of spitting is documented in old Ashkenazic and חסידי communities and was always controversial.
    ↩
  19. ספר מנהגים מהרי"ל, ר' יעקב הלוי מולין (1365–1427). — Foundational work on מנהגי אשכנז. The מהרי"ל was the primary mesorah for German-Jewish liturgical practice; almost all Ashkenazic ניגונים and מנהגים trace to him. He records his own practice of spitting at אל אל לא יושיע; later פוסקים have generally restricted the practice as out of keeping with modern כבוד בית הכנסת. ↩
  20. מעשה רב on כסא כבודו vs. מושב יקרו. — The גר"א's preference for כסא כבודו, recorded in מעשה רב. The note at the bottom of מעשה רב (later editor, not the גר"א himself) explains: יקרו = ק(100)+ר(200)+ו(6)+י(10) = 316 = ישו. The change to כסא כבודו removes the inadvertent gematria allusion. Many Ashkenazic סדורים reflect this change. ↩
  21. ב"ח, או"ח סימן קל"ב. — The ב"ח (ר' יואל סירקיש, 1561–1640), in his commentary on the טור: עלינו belongs at the close of תפילה as the descent from the spiritual heights of davening back to עולם הזה; before stepping out of shul into the world of גוים, business, and תאוות, the yid declares אחדות ה' as armor. This kabbalistic-influenced rationale is the basis for the רמ"א's פסק in או"ח קל"ב incorporating עלינו in the daily close. ↩
  22. חמשה סידורי תפילה; cf. משנה ברורה, או"ח קל"ב.
    Note: The שיעור references חמשה סידורי תפילה as the source for not reciting עלינו between מנחה and מעריב. The principle — that עלינו is recited only when actually departing from תפילה/shul — is codified in the משנה ברורה and other פוסקים; verify exact citation in חמשה סידורי תפילה. The practical applications — no עלינו after שחרית on שבת (going into מוסף), no עלינו on יום כיפור until נעילה — are widely accepted.
