תפילת מעריב של חול

From the Removal of רבן גמליאל to the Three Words of והוא רחום
Recorded 8 March 2026 · Reworked from the original שיעור
Sponsored by the Klaus family לעילוי נשמת their יארצייט this week

As we work toward תפילת שבת, I realized we never properly addressed מנחה and מעריב of חול. There is not much new to say about מנחה, but מעריב deserves its own session — because the entire structure of weekday מעריב emerges from one of the most dramatic stories in all of ש״ס, and the תפילה itself is shaped by halachic decisions, by שיטות in the זוהר, and by manuscripts dating back to רש״י's son-in-law. We cannot fully understand the שבת תפילה until we have established the weekday foundation.

I. The Great מחלוקת — רשות or חובה?

The Story

The entire question of מעריב emerges from גמרא ברכות דף כ״ז ע״ב[1]. This is not just a halachic discussion; it is a Talmudic event with profound consequences. A תלמיד walked into the בית מדרש one day when the נשיא was רבן גמליאל. The תלמיד asked a straightforward question: is תפילת מעריב רשות or חובה?

רבן גמליאל answered with finality: חובה — it is an obligation, just like שחרית and מנחה. You must daven מעריב. The תלמיד then revealed: the other day, I asked רבי יהושע, who is also a גדול הדור, and רבי יהושע said תפילת מעריב is רשות.

The Public Confrontation

רבן גמליאל told him: wait until everyone is gathered in the בית מדרש, and we will address this publicly. The גמרא reveals at the end of the story that the תלמיד was none other than שמעון בר יוחאי — the very same yid who would later cause such upheaval. But at this moment, he was simply a student.

When the בית מדרש filled, רבן גמליאל told the תלמיד to ask the question again. שמעון בר יוחאי stood and asked: is תפילת מעריב רשות or חובה? רבן גמליאל turned to the assembly: does anyone here disagree with me? Nobody stood. Did anyone say otherwise? Then, turning directly to רבי יהושע: I heard you said differently — that מעריב is optional, that if a yid wants to be a בעל תפילה he can, but he is not obligated.

רבי יהושע admitted: yes, I am the one who said this. One must appreciate the חוצפה this required. רבן גמליאל was the נשיא, the most important halachic authority of his generation, the ראש מתיבתא as the גמרא calls him.

The Humiliation and the Coup

רבן גמליאל declared: "Ah, you are the one" — and continued his שיעור. רבי יהושע remained standing. And standing. And standing — while everyone else sat. A public זילזול of one of the גדולי הדור, in front of all the תלמידים.

The חכמים could not tolerate it. They turned to the מתורגמן — the translator who relayed the words of the ראש ישיבה to the masses; his name was חוצפית המתורגמן — and said: stop translating what רבן גמליאל is saying. We do not want to hear from him anymore. This was a coup. Right then and there, רבן גמליאל was fired. The first ראש ישיבה in our history to be dismissed.

The New Leadership and Reinstatement

The גמרא tells at length how they searched for a new נשיא and chose רבי אלעזר בן עזריה, who was young — but whose beard miraculously turned white overnight. Eventually רבן גמליאל realized his error, went to רבי יהושע, was מפייס him, asked מחילה, and was reinstated. The exact arrangement of leadership afterward is for another time.

II. The הלכה — We Pasken Like רבן גמליאל

The Ruling

The הלכה[2] is that we follow רבן גמליאל. תפילת מעריב is חובה. This has practical implications even though everyone today davens מעריב.

Why Do We Generally Pasken Like רבא Over אביי?

This raises a broader question. When אביי and רבא argue, whom do we generally follow? There is the famous principle: in six specific places throughout ש״ס we pasken like אביי against רבא, captured in the mnemonic יע״ל קג״ם[3]. Everywhere else we pasken like רבא. Why?

The general principle of פסק is that we follow the בתרא — the later authority. The later אמוראים had access to all the סברות of the earlier ones and still ruled their way. Since רבא was ראש ישיבה after אביי, we follow רבא.

III. The Million-Dollar רבינו גרשום

אביי's Humility

רבינו גרשום מאור הגולה writes something extraordinary in his פירוש on בבא בתרא דף כ״ב[4]. The גמרא in בבא מציעא דף ל״ג[5] records that אביי used to tell תלמידים who came to learn from him: "why are you coming to me? I am giving you only dry bones. Go to רבא — רבא has the steak, the fatty meat." That was אביי's humility; he was sending his תלמידים to רבא.

רבינו גרשום makes an astounding statement: this is precisely why הלכה is always like רבא and not like אביי. Why would אביי send people to רבא? Because אביי did not learn as much as רבא.

Was אביי Lazy?

How can we say אביי "did not learn as much"? Was he a businessman? The explanation is that אביי was a יתום from a young age — that is why his name is אביי, an endearment meaning "my father." The גמרא tells us that both אביי and רבה (not רבא; a different אמורא) were descendants of עלי הכהן. עלי's children were wicked, and שמואל הנביא delivered a curse on עלי's descendants that they would die young[6].

אביי and רבה both descended from this lineage. Both indeed died young. But the גמרא explains: רבה, who learned תורה all day, died at age forty. אביי, who learned תורה and was עוסק בגמילות חסדים, lived an extra twenty years and died at age sixty.

Says רבינו גרשום: because אביי was busy with גמילות חסדים, he did not learn as much as רבא. רבא sat and learned the entire day; אביי spent considerable time in חסד.

A Difficult חידוש This is a difficult רבינו גרשום to absorb. We are taught that without גמילות חסדים, learning תורה is incomplete. The Brisker Rav used to say: if you learn תורה but are not engaged in חסד — if you do not close the gemara when somebody needs you — even when your gemara is open, it is as if it is closed. And if you do close the gemara to do חסד, even when it is closed, it is as if it is open.

Yet for paskening הלכה למעשה — says רבינו גרשום — we follow רבא, who learned תורה all day without the heavy עוסק of חסד. גמילות חסדים brings אריכות ימים; learning תורה all day produces the man whose pesakim are followed forever. This is a חידוש worth keeping in your pocket. Anyone who has been taught that חסד is the highest thing in the world should also know that for halachic authority, total dedication to לימוד is what counts.

IV. The Second Reason — כבוד רבו

רבא and רב יוסף

There is another reason we pasken like רבא. רבינו חננאל in מסכת נדרים דף נ״ה[7] writes a famous principle: it is essential to give כבוד to one's רבי. רבא's רבי was רב יוסף — and רב יוסף was blind.

Even though רב יוסף could not see, every time רבא left his רבי's בית מדרש, he walked out backwards. (In earlier times, when leaving a רבי you walked backwards to show כבוד. The גמרא in many places says this is not the רבי's גאוה; it is the תלמיד's recognition of the כבוד התורה his רבי represents.)

The Bloodied Threshold

The point was not what רב יוסף saw. The point was what the practice did to רבא — it forced him to internalize the כבוד. Because רבא walked backwards while concentrating on his learning, not looking at the floor, he tripped constantly on the threshold. The גמרא[8] says the מפתן of רב יוסף's doorway became full of blood. רבא was so focused on his כבוד and his learning that he did not even notice he was bleeding — a level of concentration we cannot reach.

The ברכה and Its True Meaning

When they finally told רב יוסף that the floor was full of blood, the גמרא says רב יוסף gave רבא a ברכה: תהא רישא כולא כרעא. The simple פשט: you should become the ראש ישיבה. In those days, to be ראש ישיבה of the great בית מדרש was the ultimate position.

But רבינו חננאל explains: that is not the deeper meaning. The real ברכה was that everywhere in ש״ס they should pasken halacha like you. And indeed, on every page of ש״ס, perhaps six times a page, the halacha falls like רבא — because of the כבוד he gave his רבי.

יע״ל קג״ם — A Hidden Connection

Look at the mnemonic for the six exceptions: יע״ל קג״ם. The first three letters — יע״ל (יוד-עין-למד) — are the same letters as עלי (עין-למד-יוד). The other three — קג״ם — are the acronym we know from learning קרבנות: קמיצה, גלילה, מערכה. Why? Because the children of עלי corrupted the עבודה in the משכן in שילה. As a punishment, their descendants — אביי, of עלי's lineage — had a special עבודה of being מתקן what was ruined. In those six exceptional cases, where אביי is followed against רבא, the rule traces back to a tikkun for עלי's children. Everywhere else, רבא prevails because of the כבוד he gave his rebbe.

V. Why Is מעריב רשות in the First Place?

The Question

Now back to מעריב. We pasken like רבן גמליאל that מעריב is חובה. But why was there ever a question? אברהם was מתקן שחרית; יצחק was מתקן מנחה; יעקב was מתקן מעריב[9]. Why is יעקב's תפילה less? Why is it רשות?

The קרבן of Night

Simple answer: there was a קרבן תמיד של שחר in the morning, a קרבן תמיד של בין הערבים in the afternoon. What קרבן is brought at night that corresponds to מעריב? The גמרא[10] explains: sometimes the schedule in the בית המקדש was so full that the כהנים could not bring all the קרבנות and complete the הקטרת איברים during the day. Throughout the night, they would burn the leftover איברים from earlier קרבנות.

But this was not necessary on a regular day. On a regular day there were enough כהנים, enough time. Only on יום טוב, or when there were unusually many קרבנות ציבור, did the מזבח continue burning into the night. So the corresponding tefillah — מעריב — also has the character of an "extra" rather than a daily fixed obligation. Therefore the ראשונים explained, יעקב's תפילה was originally רשות, not חובה. Once כלל ישראל accepted מעריב upon themselves, however, it became binding — like the principle of אינו מצווה ועושה elsewhere in the גמרא.

VI. The סדר היום in the Times of the גמרא

The גמרא at the beginning of מסכת ברכות[11] describes the original schedule. A yid finished his work in the field and came back into town. In the old days they did not go straight home for supper. They went straight to the בית הכנסת, to the בית מדרש. Some תנאים held this was the highest level — before going home to your wife, your children, your supper, you go to learn and to daven. And before he davened שחרית the next morning, he davened מעריב tonight.

Once at shul: if he was a yid who could read חומש, he read חומש. If he was more of a שונה משניות, he learned משניות. (The גמרא is here describing the era during which it itself was being written.) Then קריאת שמע. Then מעריב. Only afterward did he go home to eat and greet his family. That was the סדר. It is hard for us today, with our different schedules, but that was the practice during the time of the גמרא.

VII. The Riaz of Trani — and a Detour Through Venice

Who He Was

The שלטי גיבורים[12] at the back of מסכת ברכות brings extensively from the ריא״ז — רבינו ישעיה אחרון זכרונו לברכה — one of the great ראשונים. Just as some people know the names of every president of the United States, we should know our גדולים — we are alive because of every word they wrote.

Why "אחרון"? Because his grandfather was also רבינו ישעיה. The grandfather is the famous ר״י of תוספות ר״י — רבינו ישעיה דטראני. (Has anyone been to southern Italy? There is a city called Trani.) רבינו ישעיה came from Trani, hence the name. He had a grandson, also from Trani, also named רבינו ישעיה — so to distinguish the two, the grandson is called the "אחרון," giving us the acronym ריא״ז.

The ריא״ז came from Italy, traveled to אשכנז to learn by the בעלי התוספות in the 1200s, and wrote a ספר of הלכות on the entire גמרא with many great חידושים — that is why he is so frequently cited.

אבולעפיא in Rome

An interesting aside: the ריא״ז had a remarkable encounter. רבי אברהם אבולעפיא[13] was one of the most controversial figures of the times of the ראשונים. He came from Spain and introduced what is called ecstatic קבלה — קבלה pursued through meditation, ecstasy, transcending the body to reach a spiritual high. This was new and dangerous. The רשב״א — one of the great ראשונים, known for his נובלות and תשובות — could not tolerate it; he wrote a famous תשובה strongly against אבולעפיא. There was a major clash.

אבולעפיא traveled the world. He went to Rome and met both the ריא״ז and the שיבולי הלקט (another great Roman ראשון), and taught them קבלה. אבולעפיא writes himself that several days after he taught them קבלה, they both were נפטר. You have to be careful with these things.

The Gondola on שבת

The ריא״ז is also famous in the world of הלכה for a particular היתר. He lived in Italy. Have you ever been to Venice? In Venice there are no cars — only gondolas. Could you ride a gondola on שבת that a גוי is driving for his own purposes — a kind of ancient water taxi? The ריא״ז addressed this in the 1200s. Yes — when the gondola travels up and down the canals, you may step on for the ride. The היתר became famous among the פוסקים.

VIII. Why We Need Learning Before מעריב

The ריטב״א

The ריטב״א in מסכת ברכות דף ד'[14] establishes a foundational principle. Why does the גמרא require that when you go to shul at night you should first learn — חומש, משניות, תורה of some kind — before davening מעריב? You already learned during the day. Why must you learn before מעריב specifically?

The ריטב״א answers: you cannot daven מעריב without first having said ברכות התורה — just as you cannot say a דבר תורה without ברכות התורה first. So we say a few פסוקים of תורה before מעריב to establish that the davening sits on top of a fresh ברכת התורה.

שיר המעלות in נוסח אשכנז

This is why every yid davening נוסח אשכנז starts מעריב with שיר המעלות הנה ברכו את ה' כל עבדי ה' העומדים בבית ה' בלילות[15]. We are symbolizing the עומדים בבית ה' בלילות. We have come to בית ה'; we are about to say ברכות. The line שאו ידיכם קדש follows. We then say ברכו, then the ברכות, then קריאת שמע.

Why נוסח ספרד Skips Straight to ברכו

נוסח ספרד does not say שיר המעלות; it goes straight to ברכו. Why? Because נוסח ספרד reflects the מנהג of davening מנחה and מעריב together back-to-back. If you have just davened מנחה, you have already said תורה (in קדושה דסידרא) — there is no need for an extra preface. Even today, when ספרדים daven מעריב alone, they preserve the older מנהג.

A Personal סגולה — The ירושלמי By the way, there is a ירושלמי in מסכת ברכות[16] that says: there are פסוקים that should never leave a yid's mouth. Mumble them constantly. When something happens and you need help right away, recite these three פסוקים, three times each:
ה' צבאות עמנו, משגב לנו אלוקי יעקב סלה · ה' צבאות, אשרי אדם בוטח בך · ה' הושיעה, המלך יעננו ביום קראנו.

This is not from a Rebbe, not from חסידים, not from קבלה — from the ירושלמי. I was once learning this and something came up. I tried it. I said the three pesukim three times each. בדרך נס — it worked.

IX. והוא רחום — Two Reasons

What It Says

Now the central question of the second half of the שיעור: why do we say והוא רחום every night of the week, including מוצאי שבת, but not on ליל שבת? The phrase is from תהלים[17]: והוא רחום יכפר עון ולא ישחית, והרבה להשיב אפו ולא יעיר כל חמתו.

Reason One — The מלאכים of גהינום

Three forces are mentioned in this פסוק: משחית (destruction), אף (anger), חמה (fury). The ראשונים say there are three מלאכים in heaven who execute the punishments of רשעים in גהינום, named for these three: משחית, אף, and חמה.

Every night, גהינום is real. We make a big mistake today — it is a personal pet peeve — that we do not tell our children enough about גהינום. Where is שכר ועונש in our discourse? Young yidden today do not know that there is גן עדן and there is גהינום. According to the זוהר הקדוש[18], at night the רשעים receive double punishment. During the day the punishment is one measure; at night it doubles. The lashon of the זוהר is clear: nighttime is a time of דינים, of משחית and פורעניות.

So we beg הקדוש ברוך הוא: והוא רחום יכפר עון — please diminish the punishment. Take away the קטרוג, the אף, the חמה. Be מכפר עון every night before מעריב. If a yid would think about this when he says והוא רחום, if גהינום became a reality more vivid in our minds, יראה and תשובה would follow.

This is also why we do not say והוא רחום on ליל שבת. There is no גהינום on שבת. The fires of גהינום are extinguished — they keep שבת up there. Just as we do not light fire on שבת down here, the heavenly fires of פורענות are silent. So והוא רחום would be unnecessary.

Reason Two — A Memory of מלקות

There is a second reason that I came across when learning מסכת מכות. At the end of מכות, in the second-to-last דף, there is a torah from the מהרי״ק[19] brought down in הלכות מעריב in שלחן ערוך.

The torah says — and it is shocking — that the מנהג in earlier generations was that between מנחה and מעריב, when yidden gathered in shul, they administered מלקות. Lashes in shul. Why? To be מכפר for whatever a yid might have done that he could have received מלקות for. (Many of us know the מנהג of receiving מלקות on ערב יום כיפור; this was a daily version of the same idea.)

והוא רחום contains thirteen words. We say it three times. Three times thirteen equals thirty-nine — exactly the number of מלקות[20]. This is to commemorate the מלקות. Even though we no longer give מלקות, when you say והוא רחום, imagine that you are receiving מלקות now — as כפרה.

The שלחן גבוה — A Textual Correction

This was hard to digest. There is no other source in Jewish history I have found that yidden gathered nightly to receive literal מלקות. Until I found a ספר called שלחן גבוה[21], written in Greece by one of the great חכמים, רבי יוסף מולכו. He writes that this is a שיבוש — a copyist's error in the manuscripts of the מהרי״ק that was carried into the printed שלחן ערוך. The original torah did not mean literal מלקות.

What it really meant: at night a yid receives spiritual מלקות — heavier דינים and פורעניות than during the day. Since at night a yid metaphorically receives מלקות from heaven, we say והוא רחום — three times thirteen, the count of those spiritual מלקות. Not literal lashes between מנחה and מעריב; spiritual ones at night that we ask הקדוש ברוך הוא to be מכפר.

Either way — whether the literal-מלקות reading or the שלחן גבוה's correction — the message of והוא רחום is the same: at the gateway from day to night, ask for רחמים, ask for כפרה, before stepping into the dangers of the night.

סיכום

Weekday מעריב is built on the dramatic mahalokes between רבן גמליאל and רבי יהושע — and we follow רבן גמליאל that it is חובה. The principle that we follow רבא over אביי traces back, says רבינו גרשום, to the fact that רבא learned more — and, says רבינו חננאל, to the bloody threshold of his רבי's house. מעריב was originally רשות because the קרבן at night was not always; כלל ישראל accepted it as חובה. Before saying מעריב we say פסוקי תורה — שיר המעלות in אשכנז — to establish ברכות התורה. And והוא רחום, said three times for thirteen words each — thirty-nine — answers either the literal מלקות in shul or the spiritual מלקות of night, and in either case begs הקדוש ברוך הוא: be מכפר עון.

The mnemonic of six exceptions where הלכה follows אביי against רבא: יאוש שלא מדעת (ב"מ כ"א), עד זומם למפרע (ב"ק ע"ב), לחי העומד מאליו (ערובין ט"ו), קדושין שלא נמסרו לביאה (קידושין נ"א), גלוי דעתא בגיטא (גיטין ל"ד), מומר אוכל נבילות להכעיס (סנהדרין כ"ז). ↩
  4. פירוש רבינו גרשום מאור הגולה, בבא בתרא כ"ב. — רבינו גרשום (c. 960–1028, Mainz), the great early Ashkenazi ראשון who issued the famous חרם against polygamy and against opening another's mail. His commentary on בבא בתרא, preserved at the back of standard editions, develops the principle that הלכה כרבא because רבא dedicated more time to learning than אביי, who was occupied also with גמילות חסדים. ↩
  5. בבא מציעא ל"ג ע"ב. — "אמר ליה אביי לרב יוסף: רבא קרי לי גרמא דעצם" — אביי's humble self-assessment that he gave only "dry bones" while רבא gave the substantial meat. רבינו גרשום uses this גמרא as the textual anchor for his pesak that we follow רבא. ↩
  6. שמואל א פרק ב':כ"ז-ל"ו; ראש השנה י"ח ע"א. — שמואל הנביא's prophetic curse on the descendants of עלי הכהן: they would die young. The גמרא in ראש השנה identifies אביי and רבה as descendants of עלי and explains the differential mortality: רבה, who learned only תורה, died at 40; אביי, who added גמילות חסדים, lived to 60. The גמרא's point: חסד extends life beyond the curse, even as תורה alone cannot. ↩
  7. פירוש רבינו חננאל, נדרים נ"ה ע"א; cf. הוריות י"ד ע"א. — רבינו חננאל בן חושיאל of Kairouan (c. 990–1055), the foundational North African ראשון on ש"ס. His commentary on נדרים develops the principle of כבוד רבו through רבא's practice toward his blind רבי רב יוסף: רבא walked backward leaving רב יוסף's presence even though רב יוסף could not see, and the ברכה "תהא רישא כולא כרעא" meant: may your פסקים become the ראש (the prevailing rulings) on every page of ש"ס. ↩
  8. קידושין ל"ב ע"א – ל"ב ע"ב; cf. סנהדרין ק"ד ע"ב. — רבא walking backwards from רב יוסף's house, the bloody threshold, רב יוסף's ברכה. The סוגיא develops the broader question of whether a רבי may forego the כבוד due him (רב יוסף's blindness made the issue acute, since he could not perceive the כבוד); רבא's practice answers that the תלמיד's internalization of כבוד is the heart of the matter, regardless of the רבי's perception. ↩
  9. ברכות כ"ו ע"ב. — "אברהם תיקן תפילת שחרית... יצחק תיקן תפילת מנחה... יעקב תיקן תפילת ערבית". The גמרא's derivation of the three daily תפילות from the אבות based on key פסוקים: בראשית י"ט:כ"ז for אברהם, בראשית כ"ד:ס"ג for יצחק, בראשית כ"ח:י"א for יעקב. The same גמרא brings the alternative derivation from the קרבנות. ↩
  10. ברכות כ"ז ע"א; תוספות ברכות ב' ע"א. — The גמרא's explanation of why מעריב was originally רשות: there is no fixed nighttime קרבן; nighttime burning of leftover איברים from earlier קרבנות is occasional rather than daily. The תוספות develops the implications for סמיכה גאולה לתפילה at night and the rules of תשלומין. ↩
  11. ברכות ב' ע"ב – ד' ע"א. — The משנה opening ש"ס: "מאימתי קורין את שמע בערבין? משעה שהכהנים נכנסים לאכול בתרומתן". The גמרא develops the practical schedule of the תנאים: returning from work, going first to the בית מדרש, learning חומש or משניות, davening מעריב, and only then returning home. The שיעור reads this as the original framing for the structure of weekday מעריב. ↩
  12. שלטי גיבורים, ר' יהושע בועז ברוך; brings the ריא"ז. — שלטי גיבורים at the back of the רי"ף in standard ש"ס editions extensively cites the ריא"ז (ר' ישעיה אחרון of Trani, c. 1230s–1280s), grandson of ר"י דטראני the elder. The grandson's halachic decisions on תפילת מעריב, סמיכה גאולה לתפילה, and the gondola היתר on שבת are widely cited. ↩
  13. שו"ת רשב"א ח"א סימן תקמ"ח on ר' אברהם אבולעפיא (1240 – c. 1291). — ר' אברהם אבולעפיא of Spain, the founder of "ecstatic קבלה" — meditative practices intended to achieve prophetic states. His attempt to meet Pope Nicholas III in 1280 (and his messianic claims) generated fierce opposition. The רשב"א's תשובה against him is one of the most important medieval polemical works against unauthorized קבלה. Verify the שיעור's anecdote about the ריא"ז and שיבולי הלקט's deaths against printed editions of אבולעפיא's autobiographical שבע נתיבות התורה. ↩
  14. חידושי הריטב"א, ברכות ד' ע"ב. — The ריטב"א (ר' יום טוב בן אברהם אשבילי, Spain, c. 1260–1330) on the סוגיא of סמיכת גאולה לתפילה at night and the requirement of לימוד תורה before מעריב. The ריטב"א derives that one cannot daven without first having ברכות התורה in effect; hence the recital of פסוקי תורה before ברכו of מעריב. ↩
  15. תהלים קל"ד — "שיר המעלות הנה ברכו את ה' כל עבדי ה' העומדים בבית ה' בלילות". — The opening of מעריב in נוסח אשכנז. The פסוק celebrates those who stand in the בית המקדש at night — historically the לוים guarding the מקדש overnight. חז"ל appropriated it as a self-identification: those davening מעריב are the modern equivalent of the nightlong משמר. ↩
  16. ירושלמי ברכות פ"ה ה"א — three פסוקים for סגולה.
    Note: The three פסוקים — ה' צבאות עמנו (תהלים מ"ו:ח' and י"ב), ה' צבאות אשרי אדם בוטח בך (תהלים פ"ד:י"ג), and ה' הושיעה המלך יעננו ביום קראנו (תהלים כ':י') — are widely cited in ירושלמי-influenced and kabbalistic סגולה literature. The שיעור attributes them to ירושלמי ברכות; verify exact citation in the printed ירושלמי. Recited three times each as a סגולה at moments of distress.
    ↩
  17. תהלים ע"ח:ל"ח — "והוא רחום יכפר עון ולא ישחית, והרבה להשיב אפו ולא יעיר כל חמתו". — The פסוק from משכיל לאסף recited at the opening of weekday מעריב. The three categories — משחית, אף, חמה — are read by the ראשונים as the three מלאכים who execute punishment in גהינום. Recited three times before ברכו; thirteen words × three = ל"ט, the count of מלקות. ↩
  18. זוהר חלק א', פרשת ויחי רכ"ד ע"א; cf. זוהר ח"ב, פרשת תרומה. — The זוהר's teaching that דינים intensify at night: the gates of גהינום's פורענות are open, the קליפות are active, and משחית/אף/חמה roam. Reciting והוא רחום three times before ברכו of מעריב appeases these forces. The זוהר's framing of nighttime as the realm of דין structures the entire קבלי reading of מעריב. ↩
  19. שו"ת מהרי"ק, ר' יוסף קולון (c. 1420–1480); cited in שלחן ערוך, או"ח רל"ז. — ר' יוסף קולון, the leading 15th-century פוסק of Italy. His תשובה on the מנהג of administering מלקות between מנחה and מעריב is brought in the שלחן ערוך at the close of או"ח רל"ז; later פוסקים debate whether the practice was literal or symbolic. שלחן גבוה argues it is a שיבוש for spiritual מלקות received from heaven at night. ↩
  20. דברים כ"ה:ג'; משנה מכות פ"ג מ"י; מכות כ"ב ע"ב. — "ארבעים יכנו לא יסיף": the תורה's 40 מלקות; חז"ל reduce to 39 (ארבעים חסר אחת, on the principle of "לא יסיף"'s safeguard). The classical 39 מלקות for biblical לאוין. The שיעור's connection: 13 words × 3 recitals of והוא רחום = 39, paralleling the מלקות count. ↩
  21. שלחן גבוה, ר' יוסף מולכו (Salonika/Greece, 18th century). — שלחן גבוה is a major commentary on the שלחן ערוך by ר' יוסף מולכו (1692–1768), רב of Salonika. He argues that the מהרי"ק's reference to מלקות between מנחה and מעריב is a textual corruption — it should not be read as literal corporal lashes administered communally, but rather as a reference to spiritual מלקות received from heaven at night, for which והוא רחום serves as כפרה. ↩
