הלכות תפילה — Repetition of Words in חזנות

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 2 March 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

A halachic detour from the run-through of שמונה עשרה, prompted by the חזן who davened in shul this past שבת. May a שליח ציבור repeat words for the sake of the ניגון? The משנה's strict rule on "מודים מודים"; the ראשונים' debate over its scope; the מהר"ם שיק's five reasons for an absolute prohibition; and a remarkable לימוד זכות from a sixteenth-century gambler-rabbi of Venice — Rabbi Yehuda Aryeh of Modena — whose תשובה almost every modern חזן quietly relies on without realizing.

Today's breakfast and שיעור are sponsored by Reb Tzvi Akiva Gamerman in honor of his grandson David's הנחת תפילין this morning, one month before his בר מצוה. מזל טוב to Reb Tzvi Akiva, his רבצין, and the זיידע with us this morning. Reb Roni notes that today is the fifteenth יארצייט of his mother, אסתר בת יום טוב ע"ה.

I. The Question — Words Repeated by the חזן

This past שבת we had as שליח ציבור the חזן Nissim Sal — a truly exceptional חזן; the davening was exhilarating. The Rebbe: I have been a חזנות enthusiast since boyhood, sitting with my grandmother in the בית הכנסת הגדול of Petach Tikva, and later in the בית הכנסת הגדול of ירושלים for Hershik and Yaffe and the choir.

What struck me was that only one person came over after davening with a halachic concern: the חזן repeated words. ברוך השם, Rav Nachman confirmed for me that the repetition was not in קדושה. I had spoken with the חזן earlier in the week and laid out the הלכה and the מנהג of our בית כנסת: "חס ושלום, do not repeat words in קדושה, in חזרת הש"ץ, or anywhere between ברכו and שמונה עשרה — הפסק is forbidden." He countered that without repetition, the music — Pinchik, Rosenblatt, Koussevitzky — cannot be performed properly. I told him: in אבות, the only word I would consider giving him a היתר for is "הא-ל". He raised "מקימי" — "You want 'מקימי מקימי'?" — in the end I don't think he performed that section.

And before going further: when I thought I heard him repeat in קדושה, I became upset. I immediately stopped to ask myself: is this קנאות לשם שמים, or am I just upset that he is undermining my authority? A question worth holding always.

II. The Foundational משנה — "מודים מודים"

The משנה in ברכות ה':ג'[1]:

האומר "מודים מודים" — משתקין אותו.
One who says "מודים מודים" — we silence him.

The גמרא's reason: saying "מודים" twice implies one is addressing two entities. חס ושלום, in a בית כנסת that included מינים and אפיקורסים — and during the משנה and גמרא period these were not theoretical, they sat in our shuls — that suggestion of two could be taken at face value.

The גמרא in ברכות ל"ג[2] extends the principle to "שמע שמע", calling it "מגונה". (This is what some Briskers say: "שמע שמע"; the גמרא registers a problem.) There is a long סוגיא distinguishing מגונה from משתקין אותו; we will not enter it here.

III. מחלוקת הראשונים — How Far Does the Rule Reach?

The משנה mentions only "מודים" and "שמע". What about every other word in תפילה?

The ריטב"א — Strict

The ריטב"א in סוכה and ברכות[3]: חז"ל certainly did not mean to limit the rule to those two words alone. The principle reaches any word whose repetition could suggest "speaking to two entities".

רבינו יונה — A קושיא

רבינו יונה raises a difficulty[4]: on יום הכפורים, at נעילה, we say "ה' הוא האלקים" seven times. The מחזורים explain: הקדוש ברוך הוא descended to us during יום הכפורים, and ascends back through the seven רקיעים with each קריאה. How is this not the very repetition we forbid?

The answer: this is not the first time כלל ישראל said "ה' הוא האלקים" more than once. The phrase comes from אליהו הנביא at הר הכרמל:

ויפלו על פניהם, ויאמרו: ה' הוא האלקים, ה' הוא האלקים.
מלכים א' י"ח:ל"ט[5]

When the entire ציבור says it together, we are reenacting הר הכרמל — there is no concern of two entities, only the unified declaration of כלל ישראל. Similarly, "כל הנשמה תהלל י-ה הללוי-ה" at the close of פסוקי דזמרה, and "ה' ימלוך לעולם ועד" at the close of שירת הים, are recited twice as a structural marker of conclusion[6]; this is not the prohibited repetition.

The ב"ח — Lenient

The ב"ח (בית חדש) in טור אורח חיים[7] goes the other way against the בית יוסף and שולחן ערוך. (We visited his קבר in Krakow a few months ago.) Since the משנה only forbids "מודים" and "שמע", the ב"ח is reluctant to extend; especially when the ציבור davens out loud, there is no realistic concern of a מין exploiting the repetition. The ב"ח has a striking, often counter-mainstream דרך — those who learn him know his חידושים.

להלכה, the בית יוסף's position prevails[20]: the prohibition extends well beyond "מודים" and "שמע".

IV. The מהר"ם שיק — Five Reasons to Forbid חזנים from Repeating

Who Was the מהר"ם שיק?

רבי משה שיק, the מהר"ם שיק (1809–1879), was the foremost תלמיד of the holy חתם סופר. The acronym "שיק" stands for "שמו ישראל קודש".

As a בחור, the מהר"ם שיק was "עבה ראש" — slow to grasp לימוד. A single דף גמרא required immense effort. Through sheer התמדה he became the חתם סופר's greatest תלמיד. The story is told that on a wagon journey, the young בחור fell asleep against the חתם סופר's shoulder; the other בחורים moved to wake him — "What חוצפה!" The חתם סופר stopped them: "He is a living ספר תורה; he has the right to rest his head on my shoulder." When the חתם סופר later commissioned a ספר תורה, he called it "ספרי" — my ספר תורה. The lesson: it does not matter what your IQ is, it does not matter when you start. With יראת שמים, desire, and התמדה, like the מהר"ם שיק, you can become a great תלמיד חכם.

He authored שו"ת with over a thousand תשובות, and was ראש ישיבה of eight hundred תלמידים in Hoest, where he is buried.

The Battle Against Reform

The מהר"ם שיק's era was the nineteenth-century battle against the השכלה and Reform. The חתם סופר was the great קנאי; the מהר"ם שיק his standard-bearer. Reform brought the בימה to the front of the בית כנסת, the organ inside, choirs and חזנים with חידושים in תפילה. In Hungarian Jewish history we have the Neologs (Reform), the Orthodoxishe, and the so-called "Status Quo" in between; the מהר"ם שיק wrote in one תשובה to the כתב סופר (the חתם סופר's son) that even the Status Quo should be treated as literally גוים for halachic purposes — one cannot remain neutral when it is good against evil.

Note the irony: the מהר"ם שיק did not oppose Reform's introduction of German for the דרשה. He himself spoke beautiful German and held one cannot stop it. (The חרם against changing the דרשה language, signed by 120 גדולים in 1800, is what stands behind certain Williamsburg shuls' refusal even today to allow English דרשות.)

The Five Reasons

The מהר"ם שיק in שו"ת מהר"ם שיק אורח חיים סימן ס"ח[8] gives five reasons a חזן may not repeat words in שמונה עשרה, קדושה, or any "מטבע שטבעו חכמים":

  1. בל תוסיף — even תפילה is דרבנן, but חז"ל's תקנות are "כעין דאורייתא". To add to the fixed נוסח — even by repetition — violates בל תוסיף. Whether bare repetition counts as adding is itself a question; רב עובדיה יוסף in שו"ת יביע אומר[9] takes it up at length. The Rebbe's פסק: if you missed כוונה, a יחיד may repeat "ברוך"; the שליח ציבור may not.
  2. עמד ניזק / Public Embarrassment — repetition itself broadcasts that the first recitation lacked כוונה. "What were you doing the first time?" as the Rebbe's רבי in seventh grade asked a בחור who said "אמר רבי, אמר רבי..."
  3. הפסק — the central reason. Repeating a word in the middle of a ברכה is structurally an interruption, no different from speaking idle words.
  4. איסור שירה בתפילה — the גמרא in סנהדרין[10] warns that the תורה herself comes to קטרג when תפילות are sung. Today it is widely accepted to sing in תפילה, but the underlying gravity remains: one is speaking before the מלך, not performing.
  5. כבוד מלך מלכי המלכים — would you stand before a president and stutter "please give me, please give me ammunition"? Perhaps if you are Zelensky in unfamiliar English. But standing before the מלך, one speaks clearly. People do not realize before Whom they stand.

The פסק

The מהר"ם שיק rules[11] that one is required to protest a חזן who repeats words and to remove him from the עמוד, even mid-תפילה.

V. Eighteenth- and Nineteenth-Century Authorities

The מחנה חיים records his father's actual practice: a Sephardic בית כנסת where the חזן repeated words in the middle of קדושה at "כתר". The מחנה חיים was מפסיק from קדושה entirely, told the חזן directly "Get off the עמוד; you cannot daven for the ציבור," and physically expelled him[12]. We thus have the פסק from the early 1800s through the late 1800s: forbidden.

VI. רב משה פיינשטיין in America

An old Yiddish saying: "חזנים זענען ערומים" — "חזנים are naked" (of לימוד). One of the great צדיקים defended them: through the כח הניגינה, they ascend to the היכל הניגינה in שמים — and since the היכל הניגינה sits adjacent to the היכל התשובה, all they need is to walk through the next door. But I digress.

רב משה פיינשטיין זצ"ל in שו"ת אגרות משה[13] took up the question sharply when חזנים arrived in pre- and post-war America. The חזנים argued they needed extra words for the ניגון — Kwartin, Pinchik, Rosenblatt, Koussevitzky. רב משה's answer: that is not the חשבון of שולחן ערוך. The בית יוסף rules clearly that one cannot repeat; we do not פסקען like the ב"ח; חס ושלום to repeat words. Most contemporary פוסקים follow.

VII. Why Don't חזנים Care?

One reads through these תשובות and notices the same phenomenon — the פוסקים are screaming, and the חזנים are not listening. להלכה מפורשת, why? Because there is a single, almost solitary לימוד זכות that חזנים rely on. To understand why we should think twice before relying on it, we have to know who he was.

VIII. רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא

The עילוי

רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא (Yehuda Aryeh of Modena, c. 1571–1648, תח"ת) was a גאון עולם in early seventeenth-century Venice. Born in Venice, family from Modena in northern Italy near Milano. He learned under רב שמואל יהודה קצנלנבוגן, the son of the מהר"ם פדואה. At two and a half he stood up in shul and recited an entire הפטרה by heart — a verifiable historical fact. The Rebbe: my own son is exactly that age; the only thing he can articulate is, "טאטי, I want to watch Uncle Moishe on the computer."

The חכמת חוץ Track

He immersed himself in חכמות חיצוניות — music, drama, poetry. He was a playwright on a level with, להבדיל, Shakespeare. The חיד"א in שם הגדולים[14] brings him as a חשוב תלמיד חכם with his ספרים and פסקים.

The Tragic התמכרות

This is the painful part. He became addicted to gambling. From his autobiography we know he was already deeply ensnared by age thirteen. At thirteen he wrote a ספר against gambling — in dialogue form, between אלדד (the addict) and מידד (the friend trying to pull him out)[15]. The figures are from חומש (the seventy זקנים); the dialogue is, in effect, his thirteen-year-old self talking to himself.

From his own autobiographical writing — extraordinary candor for an אחרון: "Once on שבועות I davened in בית כנסת, fasting and exerting myself. Suddenly a רוח שטות entered me, my mind became confused, and somehow my feet carried me to the gambling house. I gambled and gambled and gambled. הקדוש ברוך הוא punished me; I lost everything that day of חג השבועות." He was the gambling רב of Venice. Don't be quick to criticize רבנים of today — there were such מקרים in earlier generations too.

The Family Cost

He destroyed his family. Three children: one died young, one was killed (the Rebbe suspects from gambling debts owed to lenders), one fled to Brazil in the late sixteenth century and did not return until his father's death from shame. His wife suffered from severe mental illness. He writes openly: "What have I done to my family? My wife is ill, I have no relationship with my children, I am destroying my life." And in the same breath, he is writing תשובות and teaching תלמידים.

The דרשן

He was a phenomenal דרשן with hundreds of תלמידים. Christians came to listen to his דרשות; his oratory was that compelling. One of his תלמידים later became the רבי of Spinoza — Baruch Spinoza, the יהודי-turned-apostate philosopher; the apple did not fall far from the tree.

IX. שו"ת זקני יהודה — The היתר

Repetition for the Sake of the ניגון

In שו"ת זקני יהודה כלל א'[16], רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא writes, in summary: "Why is this a problem? I study music. I understand that for the sake of musicology, for the notes and the beauty of the חזנות, sometimes one has to repeat words. There is no problem at all."

His own example: in קדושה, his חזן would say "כתר כתר". "גוואלדיג!" he writes — "two crowns to הקדוש ברוך הוא!" He simply ignores the rest of the פוסקים.

Christian Melodies in the בית כנסת

Later in the same תשובה[17] he goes further: it is fine to take Christian melodies and incorporate them into the חזנות. He writes of himself: before ימים נוראים he would go to church in Venice (להבדיל) to hear what melodies they were composing for their liturgy. "I had a brilliant ear; I memorized them, and on ראש השנה we sang them in בית כנסת." The ב"ח too has a discussion of this — a שיעור of its own.

X. Should We Rely on Him?

This is what the חזנים rely on. Shame. We are speaking of someone who, by his own writing, transgressed real איסורים. He records that during the שלושת השבועות a friend told him there was a concert with live music. "This is forbidden," he replied, "and I hope you will not go. By the way — at what time is it?" Hinting that he himself wished to attend. He writes this in his own ספר. There are deeper איסורים in his life history that I will not detail here. One who spends much time in gambling halls — this is the very world we are commanded to stay far from.

XI. The ערוך השלחן — Realism

The ערוך השלחן, in הלכות שבת[18], captures the situation with characteristic realism.

The Tuning Fork on שבת

He addresses whether a חזן's tuning fork is מוקצה on שבת. He is unenthusiastic, calling it כלי לגנאי, but concludes: "In any event one cannot protest against the חזנים — the קהל wants the חזן and is not interested in הלכות שבת; do not arouse מחלוקת."

Repetition of Words — In Parentheses

And then, in parentheses, the ערוך השלחן writes — and this is the most painful single passage in the סוגיא — that he has another deep grief with these חזנים: they repeat words. He writes that anyone with יראת שמים suffers when he hears it. But — "אינן שומעים בקול דברי חכמים" — they do not listen, the המון will not hear it from the רב ("You want to take from me my עונג שבת?"); "and perhaps in truth there is no real איסור" — invoking, faintly, the היתר of רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא. He concludes with the meta-כלל: once a practice has spread among the ציבור, do not raise a great commotion.

XII. להלכה למעשה — The היתר for הלל

This is why I gave Nissim Sal a היתר on הלל with repetition. I called the Rav before the שבת to verify I was not mistaken.

Two reasons combine:

  1. הלל is its own self-contained unit, with a ברכה before and after. Full הלל would be a serious הפסק question. But on ראש חודש we say חצי הלל, not הלל השלם; the structural concern is reduced.
  2. Repetition is built into הלל itself: "אנא ה' הושיעה נא, אנא ה' הצליחה נא" is recited twice; the closing פסוקים are doubled. This is מנהג נביאים in משנה סוכה נ"ג[19]. Since the תפילה already contains repetition לכתחילה, פוסקים permit further repetition for the sake of the ניגון.

For הלל on ראש חודש, then, the היתר stands. For קדושה, חזרת הש"ץ, and the rest of שמונה עשרה — חס ושלום; the פסק of the בית יוסף, the מהר"ם שיק, the מחנה חיים, and רב משה פיינשטיין stands.

I have another forty-five minutes of תורה on this — but a לויה calls. לחיים ולא למוות. מזל טוב again to David and to the Gamerman family.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. משנה ברכות ה':ג' — "האומר מודים מודים — משתקין אותו." — The opening משנה in the fifth פרק of ברכות. The גמרא (ברכות ל"ג ע"ב) explains the silencing as a guard against the appearance of addressing two entities — a live concern in the בית כנסת of חז"ל's era, where מינים and dualists prayed alongside כלל ישראל. ↩
  2. גמרא ברכות ל"ג ע"ב — "הקורא קריאת שמע, וכופלה — הרי זה מגונה." — The גמרא extends the principle of משנה ה':ג' to one who repeats "שמע", but uses the milder term מגונה rather than משתקין אותו. The סוגיא there opens an extended discussion (the precise distinction between the two categories is debated by the ראשונים); the Rebbe sets that aside in this שיעור. ↩
  3. חידושי הריטב"א on ברכות ל"ג ע"ב (and parallel discussion in סוכה). — רבינו יום טוב בן אברהם אשבילי (c. 1260–1320, Spain), the foremost תלמיד of the רא"ה and the רשב"א, holds that חז"ל never intended to limit the prohibition to the two specific words named in the משנה. Any repetition that can suggest dual address is included. This is the strict reading that the בית יוסף later adopts. ↩
  4. רבינו יונה on the רי"ף, ברכות פרק ה' — the קושיא from ה' הוא האלקים. — רבינו יונה of Gerona (c. 1200–1263) raises the difficulty: if repetition is forbidden, why does the ציבור say "ה' הוא האלקים" seven times at the close of נעילה on יום הכפורים? His resolution — that a communal, structurally fixed repetition modeled on a biblical event is categorically different from a personal interpolation — frames the ראשונים' approach to all the תפילה's deliberate doublings. ↩
  5. מלכים א' י"ח:ל"ט — "וירא כל העם, ויפלו על פניהם, ויאמרו: ה' הוא האלקים, ה' הוא האלקים." — The biblical source for the doubled declaration. After the fire descends at הר הכרמל and consumes אליהו's קרבן, the entire nation — having watched the prophets of Baal fail — falls on its face and proclaims ה''s exclusivity twice. The תפילה at נעילה echoes this collective acclamation. ↩
  6. טור and שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן נ"א (laws of פסוקי דזמרה); cf. מ"א and מ"ב on the closing פסוקים of שירת הים. — The doubled closing פסוקים — "כל הנשמה תהלל י-ה הללוי-ה" at the end of the הללוי-ה chapter, and "ה' ימלוך לעולם ועד" at the end of שירת הים — function as structural seals, not as separate addresses to two entities. Treated as concluding markers in the פוסקים, parallel in form to חזק חזק ונתחזק. ↩
  7. ב"ח (בית חדש) on טור אורח חיים סימן ס"א — lenient view. — רבי יואל סירקיש (1561–1640, Krakow), the ב"ח, argues that the משנה's prohibition is limited to the specific words named in it — "מודים" and "שמע" — and is reluctant to extend a דרבנן by היקש. He further argues that when the entire ציבור is davening aloud the original concern about מינים evaporates. The ב"ח stands famously at odds with the בית יוסף across many סוגיות; this is one of his characteristic counter-readings. ↩
  8. שו"ת מהר"ם שיק אורח חיים סימן ל"א (and parallel תשובות in the same חלק) — five reasons against repetition by a חזן. — רבי משה שיק (1809–1879, Hungary), foremost תלמיד of the חתם סופר and ראש ישיבה in Huszt (Hoest), wrote שו"ת with over a thousand תשובות. In this תשובה on the practice of nineteenth-century חזנים in central Europe he assembles five independent grounds for prohibition: בל תוסיף; public embarrassment / עמד ניזק; הפסק; איסור שירה בתפילה; and the violation of כבוד מלך מלכי המלכים. The Rebbe cited the סימן as ס"ח; readers should verify against the printed edition, as the citation in oral שיעור may have been approximate. ↩
  9. שו"ת יביע אומר (רב עובדיה יוסף) — extended treatment of repetition and בל תוסיף. — רב עובדיה יוסף זצ"ל (1920–2013) takes up the question whether mere repetition of an existing word counts as an addition for purposes of בל תוסיף, or only the introduction of new material. He surveys the ראשונים and rules narrowly: a private יחיד who lost כוונה may repeat in defined circumstances, but a שליח ציבור may not. The תשובה appears in the חלקים on אורח חיים; the precise חלק and סימן were not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  10. סנהדרין ק"א ע"א — "הקורא פסוק של שיר השירים ועושה אותו כמין זמר... תורה חוגרת שק... ואומרת לפני הקדוש ברוך הוא: רבונו של עולם, עשאוני בניך ככינור שמנגנין בו ליצים." — The גמרא's warning against treating פסוקים as material for ordinary musical performance. The תורה personified comes before the כסא הכבוד wrapped in sackcloth and protests that her words have been turned into a singer's instrument. The מהר"ם שיק uses this as the fourth of his five grounds: even where contemporary practice tolerates singing in תפילה, the original חז"ל framework treats תפילה as speech before the מלך, not as performance. ↩
  11. שו"ת מהר"ם שיק שם — פסק requiring removal of the חזן from the עמוד. — Concluding the same תשובה, the מהר"ם שיק rules that one is obligated to protest a חזן who repeats words and to physically remove him from the עמוד, even mid-תפילה. The severity reflects the תשובה's broader context — the nineteenth-century battle against Reform liturgical innovations, in which the introduction of choral and operatic חזנות figured prominently. ↩
  12. שו"ת מחנה חיים (רבי חיים סופר) — incident at a Sephardic בית כנסת at "כתר". — רבי חיים סופר (1821–1886, Hungary; not to be confused with the חתם סופר's family despite the surname) records his father's actual practice. The חזן in a Sephardic בית כנסת began repeating words during "כתר" in קדושת מוסף; the מחנה חיים's father interrupted קדושה, instructed the חזן to step down from the עמוד, and physically expelled him. The episode supplies the פסק from the early-to-mid 1800s and matches the מהר"ם שיק's ruling. ↩
  13. שו"ת אגרות משה (רב משה פיינשטיין), אורח חיים — prohibition of word-repetition by חזנים. — רב משה פיינשטיין זצ"ל (1895–1986) addressed the question multiple times in אגרות משה as the great American חזנים of the early twentieth century — Kwartin, Pinchik, Rosenblatt, Koussevitzky — populated American shuls. He rules sharply with the בית יוסף against the ב"ח: musical convenience cannot override a פסק of the שולחן ערוך. The תשובה appears in אורח חיים חלק ב'; the precise סימן was given orally as כ"ב and should be verified. ↩
  14. שם הגדולים (חיד"א), entry on רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא. — רבי חיים יוסף דוד אזולאי (1724–1806), the חיד"א, in his bibliographic-biographical work שם הגדולים, brings רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא as a חשוב תלמיד חכם with his ספרים and פסקים. The חיד"א's inclusion gives rabbinic legitimacy to a figure whose biography (gambling, attendance at church concerts, controversial פסקים) could otherwise have placed him outside the standard canon. ↩
  15. סור מרע / סוד ישרים — historically referenced as "ספר אלדד ומידד" — the anti-gambling dialogue composed by רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא at age thirteen. The work is structured as a debate between אלדד (the gambler) and מידד (his moral counterweight), the names borrowed from the seventy זקנים in בהעלותך (במדבר י"א:כ"ו). The autobiographical material referenced here — including the שבועות incident at the gambling house — is preserved in his memoir Chayei Yehuda, which circulated in manuscript and was published in modern critical editions in the twentieth century.

  16. שו"ת זקני יהודה כלל א' (רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא) — היתר for word-repetition for the sake of the ניגון. — רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא (c. 1571–1648, Venice), in his שו"ת זקני יהודה, treats the question musicologically: a trained ear understands that the structure of certain ניגונים requires the repetition of words, and there is therefore no איסור. He gives his own חזן's "כתר כתר" as a positive illustration. The תשובה stands almost alone among the פוסקים on the lenient side; חזנים for centuries afterward have leaned on it almost exclusively. ↩
  17. שו"ת זקני יהודה שם — incorporation of Christian melodies into the חזנות. — Continuing in the same תשובה, רבי יהודה אריה defends the practice of taking liturgical melodies heard in non-Jewish religious settings and adapting them for the בית כנסת, citing his own pre-ימים נוראים attendance at Venetian church services. This passage is the central historical text invoked when later פוסקים (and modern חזנים) debate the use of secular or non-Jewish-origin melodies in תפילה; the ב"ח has a separate, parallel discussion. ↩
  18. ערוך השלחן אורח חיים סימן של"ח (הלכות שבת) — חזן's tuning fork; parenthetical lament about word-repetition. — רבי יחיאל מיכל עפשטיין (1829–1908, Novardok), the ערוך השלחן, treats the חזן's tuning fork as כלי לגנאי on שבת but rules that one should not protest its use, since the קהל is invested in their חזן and the issue cannot be won. In a parenthetical aside in the same סימן, he extends the same realism to word-repetition: anyone with יראת שמים suffers when he hears it, but the המון will not accept rebuke, and "אינן שומעים בקול דברי חכמים"; he concludes with a faint invocation of רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא's היתר and the meta-כלל that one does not raise a great commotion against an entrenched practice. ↩
  19. משנה סוכה ג':י"א (דף ל"ח; cf. סוכה נ"ג) — "מקום שנהגו לכפול — יכפול." — The משנה on the מנהגי הלל records that doubled פסוקים in הלל — "אנא ה' הושיעה נא, אנא ה' הצליחה נא", and the closing פסוקים — are מנהג נביאים. Because the structure of הלל contains its own internal repetition לכתחילה, פוסקים permit further repetition for the ניגון, particularly in חצי הלל on ראש חודש where the הפסק concern is reduced. The Rebbe cited סוכה נ"ג; the canonical משנה on this is in the third פרק of סוכה at דף ל"ח; readers should verify against their preferred edition. ↩
  20. בית יוסף and שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן ס"א — main halachic position against the ב"ח. — רבי יוסף קארו (1488–1575, Tzfas) in the בית יוסף on the טור and again in his own שולחן ערוך rules with the ריטב"א: the prohibition of word-repetition is not limited to "מודים" and "שמע". This is the position adopted by the overwhelming majority of later פוסקים — מהר"ם שיק, מחנה חיים, רב משה פיינשטיין — against the ב"ח's narrow reading and against רבי יהודה אריה ממודינא's musicological היתר. ↩
