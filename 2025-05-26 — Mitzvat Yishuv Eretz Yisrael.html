מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל — A Halachic Analysis

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Special יום ירושלים Shiur
Recorded כ"ח אייר תשפ"ה · 26 May 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

A halachic — not ideological — survey of the מצוה of ישוב ארץ ישראל: the פסוק in פרשת מסעי; the major מחלוקת between רש"י and רמב"ן; the רמב"ן's lifelong commitment culminating in the famous ויכוח at Barcelona, his flight to ארץ ישראל in 1267, and his דרשת ראש השנה at עכו; the רמב"ם's puzzling omission with multiple resolutions; תוספות's citation of רבינו חיים הכהן and the תרומת הדשן's account of the dangers in the thirteenth century; רב קוק's letter to the רידב"ז wearing "Zionist" as a badge; רב משה פיינשטיין's framing as מצוה קיומית; and the ציץ אליעזר's ruling that everything changed after 1948.

Dedicated לעילוי נשמת יוסף בן שמואל הלוי, murdered by the Nazis ימ"ש, and לעילוי נשמת זולמה ע"ה, dedicated by a survivor of Bergen-Belsen.

I. Methodological Note

Today is כ"ח אייר תשפ"ה, יום ירושלים. It is fitting to discuss מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל. I must be intellectually honest at the outset: I grew up in Brisk and in Satmar. The ideological approach to ארץ ישראל in those communities differs significantly from much of כלל ישראל. Today is not for ideology. Today we are discussing הלכה.

The fundamental principle: the mind must rule over the heart. In every halachic decision, what determines is הלכה. A פוסק's השקפה sometimes influences his פסק — perhaps we will see exactly that today on this סוגיא — but the bottom line: "הלכה היא הלכה, תורה היא תורה, רצון ה' הוא רצון ה'."

II. The Underlying פסוק

Where does the תורה command us to live in ארץ ישראל? The פסוק is in פרשת מסעי:

והורשתם את הארץ וישבתם בה.
במדבר ל"ג:נ"ג[1]

"והורשתם" — dispossess the seven nations from the land. "וישבתם בה" — and dwell in it. On its face, a clear ציווי.

III. The מחלוקת — רש"י vs. רמב"ן

The שיטת רש"י

רש"י הקדוש[2] reads the פסוק as a promise, not a ציווי: הקדוש ברוך הוא assures עם ישראל that after forty years in the wilderness, they will reach ארץ ישראל, dispossess their enemies, and dwell. There is no חיוב in the פסוק; there is a הבטחה.

The רמב"ן reads רש"י as concluding: there is no חיוב on a Jew to live in ארץ ישראל.

The שיטת הרמב"ן

The רמב"ן[3] was the great champion of מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל among the ראשונים. He challenges רש"י in his פירוש על התורה (אחרי מות and מסעי), in his דרשות, and in his פירוש על הש"ס. He writes sharply: "רש"י made a significant error."

His position:

  1. This is a complete מצוה — a חיוב on every Jew to live in ארץ ישראל.
  2. The פסוק in מסעי is not the only source — many פסוקים in תורה, especially in ספר דברים, command עלייה לארץ.
  3. The מצוה has two components:
    • כיבוש הארץ — removing the גוים.
    • ישיבה בארץ — settling.

The Rebbe's השקפה note for our generation: every soldier today in עזה, removing רשעים from ארץ ישראל — the land given to us as a divine gift, as רש"י teaches in his very first פירוש on the תורה — is fulfilling "והורשתם" according to the רמב"ן. We must be proud of them and daven that they all return home safely.

A historical precedent in תורה: the Girgashites. Of the seven nations, when כלל ישראל arrived, the גרגשי chose to leave — they were offered peace or war, and they took the third option. הקדוש ברוך הוא gave them land in Africa. According to certain historians, a tribe in Africa descends from them to this day.

IV. The רמב"ן's Proofs from ש"ס

The רמב"ן's argument: if there is no מצוה, how does רש"י account for the many הלכות that presuppose one?

The משנה in כתובות

The משנה at the close of כתובות[7]: in a marriage where one spouse wishes to make עלייה and the other refuses, the one who wishes to ascend may compel. A husband may compel his wife: "I am making עלייה; come, or here is your גט." A wife may compel her husband. If there is no מצוה, on what basis does this הלכה stand?

V. The רמב"ם's Omission

The רמב"ם, in his enumeration of the תרי"ג מצות in ספר המצוות, omits מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל entirely. Why?

The רמב"ן's Response

In his השגות on the רמב"ם's ספר המצוות[4], the רמב"ן lists the מצות the רמב"ם forgot to include — and ישוב ארץ ישראל is on the list. He writes:

  1. Both רש"י and the רמב"ם erred and forgot one of the most important מצות in the תורה.
  2. I am restoring it to the count of תרי"ג.
  3. This is a מצוה נוהגת בזמן הזה — even in his thirteenth century, the חיוב stands.

VI. The רמב"ן Lived His פסק

Brief Biography

Born in Girona, Spain, around 1194; נפטר around 1270. רב, פוסק, philosopher, theologian, physician. One of the supreme ראשונים.

The ויכוח at Barcelona

The King of Aragon summoned the רמב"ן to a public debate with Pablo Christiani, the famous apostate, in the presence of king, ministers, and clergy. Four consecutive days. The רמב"ן documented the entire debate in ספר הויכוח[5]. He emerged the absolute victor — and made a public לעג of Christianity, of ישו, and of the messianic claims around him, in front of the king himself.

The king loved the רמב"ן and respected him. After the disputation he told the רמב"ן privately: "Between you and me, you won." The following שבת the king himself came to the main synagogue of Barcelona — featured speaker on a Jewish שבת — and addressed the קהל: "I know your רב is against ישו and Christianity, but he gave his שטיקל תורה." The רמב"ן stood up and respectfully argued back to the king's face: "We have our beliefs; we ask that you let us remain with them." The king embraced him, gave him three hundred dinar — a substantial sum — and said, "You are good." Everything calmed.

Exile

The רמב"ן then had the audacity to write down everything he had said against Christianity, and his תלמידים circulated the ספר הויכוח. The Christians were outraged. Two years later, around 1265, they came to the king demanding the רמב"ן be punished. The king refused, deflected, refused again — "He is a good man, a holy man; I am not putting him in jail." Finally they appealed to Pope Clement IV, who hated יהודים. The Pope wrote officially demanding the רמב"ן's appearance for trial — to be tried for לעג of ישו. Most historians: the רמב"ן understood he would not survive the trial. He fled.

1267 — עכו

The רמב"ן packed his bags and arrived in ארץ ישראל in 1267, landing in עכו. In 1972, archaeologists in ארץ ישראל uncovered an official seal[24] of the רמב"ן — among the most important physical finds from the era of the ראשונים. It is on display in the Israel Museum in ירושלים; the Rebbe has seen it. It reads "רבינו משה בן נחמן". ArtScroll reproduced it on the front of their English רמב"ן on חומש.

From עכו the רמב"ן traveled to ירושלים, where he built a בית כנסת — the famous בית הכנסת הרמב"ן — and gave tremendous כח to the renewal of Jewish settlement there. He is regarded by historians as one of the founders of the renewal of יישוב in ירושלים.

VII. The דרשת ראש השנה at עכו (1269)

Returning to עכו in 1269, the רמב"ן stood up on ראש השנה and gave a דרשה[6]. (For shuls that complain about ראש השנה davening running long: the רמב"ן's דרשה on that ראש השנה runs seventy to eighty pages. Apparently they did not say יוצרות, or perhaps they began at ונתיקין; he was up there the entire day.)

In the דרשה he reiterates his פסק: there is a מצוה on every יהודי to come to ארץ ישראל. Living there raises one to a far higher מדרגה.

"כל הדר בחוץ לארץ כמי שאין לו אלוקה"

The גמרא at the close of כתובות[7]:

כל הדר בחוץ לארץ — כמי שאין לו אלוקה.
Anyone who lives outside ארץ ישראל — it is as if he has no אלוקים.

Those of us in Lawrence — these are literally חז"ל's words — it is as if we have no אלוקים. The רמב"ן and his תלמיד the רשב"א explain at length: not חס ושלום that we do not believe — we serve the same הקדוש ברוך הוא as the יהודים in ארץ ישראל. The mystical structure: every land and every nation has a שר in heaven; the שפע a nation receives flows through that שר, that מלאך. יהודים in ארץ ישראל receive directly from הקדוש ברוך הוא. In חוץ לארץ we serve Him; the שפע arrives through different conduits. Less direct connection.

Leaving Family for the מצוה

In the דרשה the רמב"ן writes about himself: "Because I am so adamant in my view that there is a מצוה to live in ארץ ישראל, this is what brought me here. I was a wanderer through countries until I arrived. I left my home; I left my homestead in Spain." The גמרא in עירובין[22]: sometimes תלמידי חכמים become "like ravens" to their children — placing תורה ומצות above their own children's daily needs. The רמב"ן writes openly that he left his wife and children in Spain. Why? "Because I would like to sit in the bosom of my mother — I want to cling to הקדוש ברוך הוא, to the שכינה הקדושה that is so much more present in ארץ ישראל."

Some historians suggest the רמב"ן's motive was not the Pope and the trial. From the דרשה, his stated motive is straightforward: it was the מצוה. מצות עשה מדאורייתא — every Jew, as soon as possible, packs his bags, books a ticket, and finds a place in ארץ ישראל.

VIII. The רמב"ם's Position — Resolutions

The מגילת אסתר

One אחרון from the era between ראשונים and אחרונים — מגילת אסתר[15] on ספר המצוות, by an Italian scholar named Moshe d'Leone (fifteenth century) — proposed: yes, the רמב"ם truly held there is no מצוה of ישוב ארץ ישראל today; the מצוה applied only when there was a בית המקדש. After the חורבן, no מצוה.

Why It Cannot Be

The רמב"ם's own halachic body of work refutes מגילת אסתר. He himself פוסק:

  • A husband may compel his wife and a wife may compel her husband to make עלייה[8] — operative בזמן הזה.
  • The הלכה in הלכות שבת[9] regarding שלשת ימים before שבת: one normally cannot start a journey three days before שבת if it might lead to חילול שבת, but for עלייה לארץ ישראל one may set out even on ערב שבת.
  • הלכות מזוזה[10]: in חוץ לארץ one has thirty days; in ארץ ישראל one must put up the מזוזה immediately. Why? Because חז"ל wanted יהודים settled there with peace of mind — and if you leave, the מזוזה already there draws the next יהודי to take the apartment.

All of these are בזמן הזה הלכות. רב עובדיה יוסף זצ"ל in שו"ת יביע אומר[16] uses sharp language: "All the אחרונים rejected the מגילת אסתר בחיוב בשתי ידיים — with both hands." The רמב"ם agrees there is a מצוה. The question is only why he did not count it.

Why the רמב"ם Did Not Count It

The standard answer: the רמב"ם does not separately enumerate מצות that are all-encompassing. Where one מצוה contains many specific מצות, he counts the specifics rather than the umbrella. ישוב ארץ ישראל includes the entire סדר זרעים: שמיטה, יובל, תרומות ומעשרות, the מצות התלויות בארץ. The רמב"ם counts those; he does not separately count the umbrella under which they sit. This is not omission of the מצוה — it is a structural feature of his enumeration.

The רמב"ם Himself Made עלייה

Decisive proof. The ספר חרדים, by רבי אלעזר אזכרי (a חכם of צפת in the early 1600s; his יארצייט just this past week), brings a letter of the רמב"ם[11]: "I arrived in ארץ ישראל by boat on ג' סיון. I landed in עכו." He then writes to his children: "This day is a יום טוב for our family. Every ג' סיון, make a party, make a משתה, rejoice. Do not go to work. It is a personal יום טוב for our family for generations."

If the רמב"ם arrived in ארץ ישראל and established that day as a permanent family יום טוב — clearly he held ישוב ארץ ישראל is a מצוה.

IX. The Other Side — רבינו חיים הכהן

Intellectual honesty requires acknowledging the ראשונים who diverged.

תוספות at the close of כתובות[12], on the סוגיא of compelling a spouse to make עלייה, quotes רבינו חיים הכהן (a great תלמיד of רבינו תם, twelfth century): "Today there is no מצוה to live in ארץ ישראל." Two reasons:

  1. סכנה — twelfth-century travel was perilous, the journey took months, and life in ארץ ישראל for שומרי תורה ומצות was difficult. (This argument has no force today; the only place where there is real ongoing danger is עוטף עזה — and some would say davka there is where one should go.)
  2. Concern for מצות התלויות בארץ — an unaccustomed Jew might stumble in תרומות ומעשרות, שמיטה, יובל.

The argument struck many as strange — the entire purpose of life is to fulfill as many מצות as possible! Why deny יהודים the opportunity to fulfill more? "רצה הקדוש ברוך הוא לזכות את ישראל, לפיכך הרבה להם תורה ומצוות"?

The Forgery Argument

The מהרי"ט, the מבי"ט, the רשב"ש, and the רשב"ץ[13] — major פוסקים from the fifteenth and sixteenth centuries — all argue that רבינו חיים הכהן could not have said this. They have a מסורה that the words in תוספות are a תלמיד טועה's interpolation — a forgery. The base of the שיטה is shaky.

The סאטמאר רב זצ"ל's ספר ויואל משה[23], dedicated entirely to this סוגיא, leans heavily on this רבינו חיים הכהן. The reliance is precarious given the פוסקים' rejection.

X. The תרומת הדשן

The תרומת הדשן[14] received a letter from ארץ ישראל asking: "Am I fulfilling מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל?" His answer: "Yes, one hundred percent."

The questioner pushed back: "Then why are you not coming with your entire ישיבה?" The תרומת הדשן's answer is historically revealing. He writes that אשכנזי יהודים who made עלייה in the thirteenth century suffered persecution — not only from the Arabs and the ישמעאלים, but also from the ספרדים. The Sephardic קהילה in ירושלים had been settled long; the new אשכנזים showing up disrupted things; tensions ran high. The תרומת הדשן writes about it as "כידוע": as is known.

This is סכנה in a different register — but the conclusion is the same: he does not require himself or his תלמידים to make עלייה under those conditions. Today the conditions have changed entirely.

XI. Three שיטות in Brief

  1. רמב"ן — מצוה דאורייתא, every day, until our time. The רמב"ן writes that this מצוה should make a יהודי weep when he is not in ארץ ישראל.
  2. רמב"ם — agrees there is a מצוה (per the consensus of אחרונים rejecting מגילת אסתר); did not separately enumerate it because it umbrellas the מצות התלויות בארץ.
  3. רבינו חיים הכהן (תוספות in כתובות) — no מצוה today, due to סכנה and חשש כשלון. Likely a תלמיד טועה interpolation per major פוסקים.

XII. The ספרי on Leaving ארץ ישראל

The רמב"ן brings from the ספרי[21]: a story of four or five תנאים from the close of בית שני — רבי יהודה בן בתירא, נסיא בן חרש, רבי חנניה, רבי נתן — who traveled and reached a place called "פלטיא", on the border of ארץ ישראל. As they crossed the border, leaving the land, they remembered: we are leaving ארץ ישראל. They raised their eyes, wept until their cheeks were full of tears, tore their garments, and recited the פסוק about leaving the land.

And then they said the line that has echoed through history:

ישיבת ארץ ישראל שקולה כנגד כל המצוות.
Dwelling in ארץ ישראל is equivalent to all the מצות.

Very few מצות receive such a description.

The Rebbe shares — the painful resonance of these words for him this past week. He went to ארץ ישראל to bury his father, כרפס משכבו. "It was so brief — fourteen hours total. I had not been in ארץ ישראל for fifteen years. Fifteen years. And here הקדוש ברוך הוא arranged that I needed to enter for fourteen hours and then leave. I came to the cemetery, buried him, sat שבעה for two hours at my brother's apartment, and was on the way back to the airport. My heart was torn to pieces leaving."

Those of you who go and come, go and come — do not take it for granted. Chaim Eyer, who lives there — do not take it for granted.

The גמרא on Four אמות

The גמרא in כתובות: "כל המהלך ארבע אמות בארץ ישראל הרי הוא בן עולם הבא". The Rebbe's father, when they would walk through בני ברק as children and have to take a detour, used to say: "Don't worry — four אמות in ארץ ישראל, another four אמות in ארץ ישראל."

The רמב"ן brings another גמרא at the close of כתובות: even a שפחה — a maidservant — has a portion in עולם הבא if she lives in ארץ ישראל. The רמב"ן: this is not a שפחה who is שומרת תורה ומצוות — that anyone gets עולם הבא from. This שפחה knows nothing; all she does is live in ארץ ישראל. חז"ל cannot speak this way of the land and you tell me it is not a מצוה?

XIII. Rav Avraham Yitzchak HaKohen Kook זצ"ל

The Rebbe, with characteristic candor: I tell you a third time — despite Brisk and Satmar, I have read more of רב קוק's writings than perhaps you have. He had one of the most amazing hearts of the past century — beating with love for ארץ ישראל and love for every יהודי.

רב קוק was radical in his love for ארץ ישראל; he even held that secular יהודים building ארץ ישראל were performing the מצוה. The חרדי world today distances itself from his השקפה, and many גדולים across the spectrum did not agree with him on every point. But one must read how he writes.

The Letter to the רידב"ז

The רידב"ז — Rabbi Yaakov David Wilovsky, author of the רידב"ז — wrote to רב קוק a sharp letter, in connection with the היתר מכירה debate: "I hear you have become a Zionist." ("Zionist," in some communities, is a slur.)

רב קוק[19], who before his עלייה had been a major Lithuanian גאון from Volozhin and was on track to become an unambiguous גדול הדור in any setting, wrote back. The Rebbe transcribed the response:

"You mock me that in my old age I have become a Zionist. No problem. Accuse me of being a Zionist — if Zionist means I follow the גמרא. The גמרא says that a שפחה living in ארץ ישראל has more connection to הקדוש ברוך הוא than a Jew in חוץ לארץ. The גמרא says four אמות in ארץ ישראל bring you to עולם הבא. The ירושלמי[20] in שבת and כלאיים says twice: 'כל הדר בארץ ישראל ואוכל חולין בטהרה כאילו קיים את כל התורה כולה.' If that is what Zionist means, I will wear it as a badge of honor."

In the same letter, רב קוק writes about the secular יהודים building ארץ ישראל: do not worry; this is the beginning; they will return. Their grandchildren — many of them — are returning.

The Rebbe: We know what the State of Israel did to many of the early עולים — putting them in camps, removing כיפות and ציציות, working hard to make them secular. Many were successful at it. But the תשובה movement among the grandchildren — Migdal HaEmek under Rav Grossman and the many ישיבות קירוב across ארץ ישראל — is succeeding amazingly. Slowly the יהודים who love the land, who love הקדוש ברוך הוא, who love their fellow יהודי, are returning. רב קוק's vision is unfolding.

XIV. רב משה פיינשטיין זצ"ל — מצוה קיומית

For יהודים in America — רב משה's יהודים — where did רב משה פיינשטיין[17] stand on this fundamental question?

The Basic Position

רב משה held — one hundred percent — that we פסקען like the רמב"ן: there is a מצוה. The many הלכות in אורח חיים built on ישוב ארץ ישראל presuppose it.

מצוה קיומית vs. מצוה חיובית

רב משה distinguishes the structure. Compare to ציצית: you are commanded to wear ציצית, but the מצוה only triggers if you wear a בגד with four corners. If you wear a non-four-cornered בגד, no חיוב. (Today, חז"ל have made it our חיוב to wear a four-cornered טלית קטן, but the underlying structure is conditional.)

רב משה's reading: ישוב ארץ ישראל works the same way. There is no absolute חיוב to go to ארץ ישראל. If you are there, you are מקיים a מצוה. If you are not, you are not in transgression. מצוה קיומית rather than מצוה חיובית. If you can go and have the means, you should go; you will be מקיים a מצוה. But there is no חיוב obligating עלייה over the alternatives of building a life elsewhere.

XV. The ציץ אליעזר — Everything Changed in 1948

The ציץ אליעזר[18] — Rav Eliezer Yehuda Waldenberg זצ"ל, a great פוסק who served on the רבנות in ארץ ישראל for decades — disagrees with רב משה. One hundred percent it is a מצוה חיובית on every יהודי.

Two Dimensions

The ציץ אליעזר identifies two distinct מצוות:

  • Individual — a מצוה on every יהודי פרטי.
  • Collective — a מצוה on כלל ישראל through כח הרבים; the strength of כלל ישראל in ארץ ישראל derives from numbers.

The Changed Reality

The ציץ אליעזר writes: do not tell me the מצוה does not apply today, or that there is סכנה, or any other concern the אחרונים raised. After 1948, everything changed. The State of Israel is a state that welcomes Jewish settlement; נפש בנפש and other programs subsidize עלייה; the dangers of the past — Arab persecution of new עולים, journey hazards, governmental restrictions — are gone. There is no פטור remaining. Every יחיד has a חיוב to make עלייה if he can.

XVI. The Open Question of the Recent גדולים

The Rebbe poses the unavoidable question: why did so many גדולים who could have made עלייה not do so? Forget the אחרונים of earlier generations — why not Rav Moshe Feinstein? Why not Rav Soloveitchik (we know there was discussion of his becoming Chief Rabbi of Tel Aviv; it didn't materialize)?

According to the רמב"ן it is a מצוה דאורייתא; according to the רמב"ם, at minimum a מצוה. Why not? The Rebbe leaves the question open. Each had reasons — some of which connect back to the bigger ideological dynamics of חרדי life in America. The question stands.

XVII. Alternatives for One Who Cannot Go

The פוסקים describe several other ways to participate in מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל:

Buying Property

If you cannot physically move, buy a place in ארץ ישראל. It does not have to be the nicest apartment. You are מקיים the מצוה. The Rebbe quotes his own רבי: "Invest in something — משיח is coming; where are you going to go?" We invest in Florida and the Midwest — invest in ארץ ישראל. Even using it three times a year for יום טוב or summer is real ישוב.

Supporting יהודים in ארץ ישראל

Financial and material support of יהודים in ארץ ישראל is itself ישוב ארץ ישראל.

XVIII. Closing

For today, the essential takeaway: if you have an opportunity to go, go. If you have an opportunity to buy land, do so. And if you sit here and love the יהודים of ארץ ישראל and ארץ ישראל itself, and you want to be משתתף in their צער — financial support is real participation.

May we be זוכה that הקדוש ברוך הוא bring the גאולה שלמה:

ופדויי ה' ישובון, ובאו לציון ברינה.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. במדבר ל"ג:נ"ג — "והורשתם את הארץ וישבתם בה, כי לכם נתתי את הארץ לרשת אתה". — The principal פסוק on מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל in פרשת מסעי: a doubled ציווי — to dispossess the seven nations ("והורשתם") and to dwell in the land ("וישבתם בה") — anchored by the divine grant: "For I have given the land to you to inherit it." The רמב"ן's position rests substantially on this פסוק; רש"י's reading detaches the ציווי register and treats it as a הבטחה. The פסוק recurs in ספר דברים and in ראשונים's halachic discussions across the question. ↩
  2. רש"י on במדבר ל"ג:נ"ג — "והורשתם את הארץ — והורשתם אותה מיושביה, ואז וישבתם בה". — רש"י's reading takes the פסוק as a sequenced הבטחה: dispossess the inhabitants, then you will be able to dwell. The רמב"ן understands רש"י as concluding from this that the פסוק is descriptive (a divine assurance for the generation entering the land) rather than prescriptive (a standing חיוב on every יהודי). Whether this is רש"י's actual position on מצות ישוב in toto, or only his reading of this particular פסוק, is itself debated by the אחרונים. ↩
  3. רמב"ן on במדבר ל"ג:נ"ג (and parallel discussions on פרשת אחרי מות in ויקרא י"ח) — מצוה דאורייתא in two components: כיבוש and ישיבה. — רבינו משה בן נחמן (the רמב"ן, c. 1194–1270, Girona and ארץ ישראל) develops his position on מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל across his פירוש על התורה: the פסוק in מסעי is a clear ציווי, with two distinct components — כיבוש (the conquest dimension, applicable in conditions where the land is held by hostile nations) and ישיבה (the dwelling dimension, applicable always). The מצוה is נוהגת בזמן הזה — operative across the גלות and beyond. The רמב"ן sharpens the disagreement with רש"י by name: "שגגה גדולה היא בעיני". ↩
  4. השגות הרמב"ן על ספר המצוות לרמב"ם, מצוה ד' שכחה — restoring מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל to the count of תרי"ג. — In his השגות at the close of the רמב"ם's ספר המצוות, the רמב"ן lists the מצות the רמב"ם failed to include and that he restores. מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל appears in this list as one of the gravest omissions. The רמב"ן writes that the מצוה is חמורה in his estimation, a חיוב on every יהודי in every generation, and that the פסוקים demanding עלייה are too numerous to ignore. ↩
  5. ספר הויכוח (the רמב"ן's account of the disputation at Barcelona, 20–24 July 1263, before King James I of Aragon against the apostate Pablo Christiani; the רמב"ן was joined in attendance by Raymond of Penyafort, head of the Dominican Order). The רמב"ן's record runs to several thousand words and presents the four-day debate as a sustained victory; the surviving Latin Christian record reaches the opposite verdict. Both records survive. The publication of the רמב"ן's account a few years after the disputation triggered the Christian reaction that culminated in Pope Clement IV's order for the רמב"ן's prosecution and his subsequent flight to ארץ ישראל in 1267.
    ↩
  6. דרשת ראש השנה של הרמב"ן — delivered at עכו in 1269; surviving in his collected דרשות. — The רמב"ן's דרשת ראש השנה in עכו — running to seventy or eighty pages of printed text — is preserved in his collected דרשות. The דרשה is autobiographical in places: the רמב"ן describes his commitment to מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל as the motive for leaving Spain, the צער of separation from his wife and children, and the מעלה of life in the land — "לחסות בצל אמי", sheltering in the bosom of his mother (ארץ ישראל). The דרשה stands among the most personal תורה texts the ראשונים bequeathed. ↩
  7. משנה כתובות פי"ג מי"א and גמרא כתובות ק"י ע"ב–קי"א ע"א — compelling spouse for עלייה; "כל הדר בחוץ לארץ כמי שאין לו אלוקה"; "כל המהלך ארבע אמות בארץ ישראל"; the שפחה in עולם הבא. — The closing פרק of כתובות contains the densest concentration of חז"ל's teachings on מעלת ארץ ישראל. The משנה rules that either spouse may compel the other to make עלייה (with גט available as alternative). The גמרא develops the famous statements: living outside ארץ ישראל is "as if one has no אלוקים" (because שפע reaches the חוץ לארץ through intermediate שרים, not directly); four אמות in ארץ ישראל grant עולם הבא; even an unschooled שפחה living in the land has a portion in עולם הבא. These סוגיות together are the textual ground of the רמב"ן's position and of רב קוק's response to the רידב"ז. ↩
  8. רמב"ם, משנה תורה, הלכות אישות פי"ג / הלכות מלכים פ"ה הלכה י"ב — compelling spouse for עלייה; איסור יציאה. — The רמב"ם codifies the פסק of the משנה in כתובות: a husband may compel his wife to make עלייה, and a wife may compel her husband, with גט as the alternative. He further rules on the איסור to leave ארץ ישראל for חוץ לארץ except in narrow circumstances (severe famine, marriage, תורה learning unavailable in ארץ ישראל). The פסקים are operative בזמן הזה and contradict the מגילת אסתר's reading that the רמב"ם held the מצוה applies only when there is a בית המקדש. ↩
  9. רמב"ם, הלכות שבת פ"ל; שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן רמ"ח — שלשת ימים before שבת; exception for עלייה לארץ ישראל. — The standard דין: one does not begin a sea or long-distance journey within three days of שבת if it would create a likelihood of חילול שבת. The רמב"ם codifies (and שולחן ערוך follows) an exception: for עלייה לארץ ישראל, one may begin the journey even on ערב שבת. The exception is intelligible only on the premise that עלייה itself constitutes a מצוה grave enough to override the standard restriction; it is a halachic argument against the מגילת אסתר. ↩
  10. רמב"ם, הלכות מזוזה פ"ה הלכה י"א — חיוב מזוזה immediate in ארץ ישראל; thirty-day delay in חוץ לארץ. — The רמב"ם codifies a structural חילוק: in חוץ לארץ, a renter has thirty days before becoming חייב in מזוזה; in ארץ ישראל, the חיוב is immediate. חז"ל's reasoning: ישוב ארץ ישראל is itself a מצוה; the immediate חיוב מזוזה in ארץ ישראל incentivizes settlement and ensures that even if the original tenant departs, the מזוזה remains as a draw for the next יהודי to take the apartment. The פסק is structurally inseparable from מצות ישוב. ↩
  11. ספר חרדים (רבי אלעזר אזכרי) — bringing the רמב"ם's letter on his עלייה; ג' סיון as a family יום טוב. — רבי אלעזר אזכרי (1533–1600, צפת; member of the kabbalistic circle of צפת alongside the אריז"ל and רבי יוסף קארו; author of ספר חרדים and the פיוט "ידיד נפש") preserves a letter of the רמב"ם from his arrival in ארץ ישראל on ג' סיון: the רמב"ם establishes the day as a permanent יום טוב for his family across generations — a משתה, abstention from מלאכה, festive demeanor. The letter is the strongest single piece of evidence that the רמב"ם personally regarded ישוב ארץ ישראל as a מצוה חיובית, decisively against the מגילת אסתר's reading. ↩
  12. תוספות כתובות ק"י ע"ב, ד"ה הוא אומר לעלות — citing רבינו חיים הכהן: no מצוה today. — תוספות at the close of כתובות brings רבינו חיים הכהן (a תלמיד of רבינו תם, twelfth century, France): "בזמן הזה אינה מצוה לדור בארץ ישראל" — there is no current מצוה to live in ארץ ישראל. The two reasons: סכנת הדרכים (the journey) and חשש כשלון במצות התלויות בארץ (potential failure in תרומות ומעשרות, שמיטה, יובל). This single source is the textual base for every later פוסק who wished to relax the חיוב of עלייה; major פוסקים rejected the source as a תלמיד טועה interpolation. ↩
  13. מהרי"ט, מבי"ט, רשב"ש, רשב"ץ — fifteenth–sixteenth century פוסקים rejecting תוספות's citation as a תלמיד טועה interpolation. — A cluster of major פוסקים: מהרי"ט (רבי יוסף בן משה טראני, 1568–1639, צפת and Constantinople); מבי"ט (רבי משה בן יוסף טראני, 1500–1580, צפת; father of the מהרי"ט); רשב"ש (רבי שלמה בן שמעון דוראן, c. 1400–1467, North Africa); רשב"ץ (רבי שמעון בן צמח דוראן, 1361–1444, Algiers; father of the רשב"ש) — all argue, on internal grounds and external מסורה, that the תוספות's citation of רבינו חיים הכהן is a תלמיד טועה's interpolation that does not represent רבינו חיים הכהן's actual position. The cluster is decisive in the eyes of most later פוסקים; רב עובדיה יוסף in יביע אומר follows them. ↩
  14. תרומת הדשן (רבי ישראל איסרליין) — fifteenth-century סכנה in ארץ ישראל; אשכנזים and ספרדים. — רבי ישראל איסרליין (the תרומת הדשן, c. 1390–1460, Austria) responds to a question from ארץ ישראל: yes, the מצוה of ישוב ארץ ישראל applies; but he himself does not bring his ישיבה over because of the סכנה faced by אשכנזים in fifteenth-century ארץ ישראל — both from the surrounding ישמעאלים and from intra-Jewish friction with the long-established ספרדי קהילה in ירושלים. The תשובה is historically valuable as a snapshot of fifteenth-century conditions; the conditions it describes do not obtain today. ↩
  15. מגילת אסתר על ספר המצוות לרמב"ם (רבי יצחק די ליאון, fifteenth century, Italy) — defending the רמב"ם's omission as principled. — רבי יצחק די ליאון (Isaac de Leon, fifteenth century, Italy/Spain) wrote the מגילת אסתר as a defense of the רמב"ם's ספר המצוות against the רמב"ן's השגות. On ישוב ארץ ישראל, the מגילת אסתר argues that the רמב"ם deliberately excluded the מצוה because in his view it applied only when the בית המקדש stood; after the חורבן the מצוה lapsed. רב עובדיה יוסף in יביע אומר rejects the מגילת אסתר "בחיוב בשתי ידיים", citing the רמב"ם's own פסקים in הלכות אישות, הלכות שבת, and הלכות מזוזה as decisive evidence that the רמב"ם held the מצוה applies בזמן הזה. ↩
  16. שו"ת יביע אומר (רב עובדיה יוסף זצ"ל) — rejection of מגילת אסתר; פסק with the רמב"ן. — רב עובדיה יוסף זצ"ל (1920–2013), in שו"ת יביע אומר, treats מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל at length and rules with the רמב"ן: the מצוה is a full חיוב דאורייתא in our generation. He demonstrates that the רמב"ם's own פסקים require this conclusion and rejects the מגילת אסתר's narrowing in characteristically sharp language: "all the אחרונים rejected him בחיוב בשתי ידיים". The תשובה appears in the חלקים on יורה דעה; precise חלק and סימן not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  17. שו"ת אגרות משה (רב משה פיינשטיין זצ"ל) — מצוה קיומית; the ציצית structural analogy. — רב משה פיינשטיין זצ"ל (1895–1986), in שו"ת אגרות משה on אבן העזר and אורח חיים, develops the structural distinction between מצוה חיובית (an absolute affirmative חיוב) and מצוה קיומית (a מצוה one fulfills if and when the conditions arise but is not under standing obligation to seek out). His analogy: ציצית at the דאורייתא level — one is not חייב to wear a four-cornered בגד, but if one does, the חיוב of attaching ציציות triggers. רב משה's reading: ישוב ארץ ישראל works the same way — if one is in ארץ ישראל, one is מקיים a מצוה; one is not under absolute חיוב to relocate. (The position is contested; the ציץ אליעזר and רב עובדיה יוסף hold it is מצוה חיובית proper.) ↩
  18. שו"ת ציץ אליעזר (Rav Eliezer Yehuda Waldenberg זצ"ל) — מצוה חיובית; כח הרבים; the changed reality after 1948. — רב אליעזר יהודה ולדנברג זצ"ל (1915–2006, Jerusalem; member of the בית דין הגדול of the Israeli רבנות; author of the multi-volume שו"ת ציץ אליעזר) develops the position that מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל is a full מצוה חיובית on every יחיד, with an additional collective dimension on כלל ישראל (the strength of יישוב deriving from כח הרבים). After the establishment of the State of Israel in 1948 and the dramatic improvement in conditions for new עולים, the ציץ אליעזר writes that the historical פטורים of earlier generations — danger of the journey, persecution by surrounding nations, restrictive Ottoman governance — no longer obtain. The full חיוב stands. ↩
  19. אגרות הראי"ה (רב אברהם יצחק הכהן קוק זצ"ל) — letter to the רידב"ז; vision of secular יהודים' eventual return. — רב אברהם יצחק הכהן קוק זצ"ל (1865–1935, born in Latvia; תלמיד of רב נפתלי צבי יהודה ברלין at Volozhin; first Ashkenazi Chief Rabbi of ארץ ישראל from 1921), in his collected letters (אגרות הראי"ה) — particularly the correspondence around the היתר מכירה debate of שמיטה 5670 (1909–10) with the רידב"ז (רבי יעקב דוד וילובסקי, 1845–1913, author of the פירוש רידב"ז on the ירושלמי; settled in Tzfas in 1903). רב קוק's reply to the רידב"ז's charge of being a "Zionist" stands as one of the great expressions of his theology of ארץ ישראל: he embraces the label as a halachic position grounded in the גמרא's and the ירושלמי's teachings on the מעלת of life in the land. ↩
  20. ירושלמי שבת פ"א ה"ג and ירושלמי כלאיים פ"ט ה"ד — "כל הדר בארץ ישראל ואוכל חולין בטהרה כאילו קיים את כל התורה כולה". — The ירושלמי twice repeats the striking דרשה: anyone who lives in ארץ ישראל and eats חולין בטהרה (ordinary food in a state of ritual purity, as the חברים of the משנה period did) is regarded as having fulfilled the entire תורה. The teaching is invoked by רב קוק in his letter to the רידב"ז as one of the חז"ל sources legitimating the most maximalist halachic-theological reading of life in the land. ↩
  21. ספרי, פרשת ראה — the תנאים at פלטיא; "ישיבת ארץ ישראל שקולה כנגד כל המצוות". — The ספרי on פרשת ראה records the מעשה of four תנאים — רבי יהודה בן בתירא, רבי מתיא בן חרש, רבי חנניה בן אחי רבי יהושע, and רבי יונתן — who reached פלטיא on the border of ארץ ישראל. As they crossed the border outward (in the standard reading of the text), they wept, tore their garments, and recited "וירשתם אותה וישבתם בה", declaring: "ישיבת ארץ ישראל שקולה כנגד כל המצוות שבתורה" — dwelling in ארץ ישראל is equivalent to all the מצות in the תורה. The רמב"ן brings the ספרי as a central proof for his position; the line stands among the most-cited חז"ל texts on מעלת ארץ ישראל. ↩
  22. גמרא עירובין כ"ב ע"א — תלמידי חכמים who become "like ravens" to their children for the sake of תורה. — The סוגיא in עירובין develops the demanding image of תלמידי חכמים who, in the pursuit of תורה, become "אכזרים על בניהם כעורב" — like ravens, who do not properly care for their young. The image is not endorsed approvingly; it captures a מציאות that חז"ל register honestly. The רמב"ן brings the סוגיא in his דרשת ראש השנה at עכו as the framework for understanding his own decision to leave wife and children in Spain in pursuit of מצות ישוב ארץ ישראל — a personal מסירות נפש the דרשה records candidly. ↩
  23. ספר ויואל משה (רבי יואל טייטלבוים, סאטמאר רב זצ"ל) — building the case against contemporary ישוב on רבינו חיים הכהן. — רבי יואל טייטלבוים (the Satmar Rebbe, 1887–1979, Hungary, then Williamsburg), in ספר ויואל משה (published 1959–1961), develops the סאטמאר halachic-theological position against the State of Israel and against the חיוב of contemporary עלייה. The ספר is in three parts: "מאמר שלוש שבועות", "מאמר ישוב ארץ ישראל", and "מאמר לשון הקודש". The position on ישוב rests substantially on תוספות's citation of רבינו חיים הכהן; the Rebbe of this שיעור notes the precariousness of the foundation given the cluster of פוסקים rejecting that citation as a תלמיד טועה interpolation. ↩
  24. Personal seal of the רמב"ן, recovered in archaeological excavations in ארץ ישראל in 1972; bears the inscription "רבינו משה בן נחמן"; on permanent display at the Israel Museum in ירושלים. The seal is among the most significant material artifacts of the era of the ראשונים recovered to date and substantiates the רמב"ן's residence in ארץ ישראל from 1267 onward.
