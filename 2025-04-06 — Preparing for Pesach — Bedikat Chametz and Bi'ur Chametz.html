Preparing for פסח — בדיקת חמץ and ביעור חמץ

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 6 April 2025 (ח' ניסן תשפ"ה)  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

A pause from the running track of הלכות תפילה, six days before פסח. The unique איסור of "בל יראה ובל ימצא"; the רמב"ם's reading that "תשביתו" is fulfilled by ביטול alone; the rabbinic enactment of בדיקה and ביעור through the תוספות' and ר"ן's competing reasons; the מנחת חינוך's sharp question on whether תשביתו is קום ועשה or שב ואל תעשה with three concrete נפקא מינות; the חתם סופר and the רדב"ז on חמץ as the יצר הרע we must find in order to defeat; and a brief historical note on מכירת חמץ as a product of the eighteenth-century European Jewish economy.

I. הכנה Before the מצוה

ברוך השם, מזל טוב once again — and may all our occasions be joyous. Today I am departing from our weekly study of הלכות תפילה. My רבותיי taught me that to authentically experience a יום טוב one cannot merely fall into it. בדיקת חמץ, ביעור חמץ, the סדר with all its מצוות — these need הכנה. The חסידים teach: preparation before a מצוה is itself essential; appreciating, valuing, treasuring the מצוה begins with understanding what we are about to do — both spiritually and concretely.

So today we will set תפילה aside and turn to the הלכות and the עבודת ה' of בדיקת חמץ and ביעור חמץ.

To open with a מילתא דבדיחותא told לשם שמים: רבי איצלה חריף[13] of Slonim — a גאון עולם, and famous more for his sharp בון מויט than for his תורה. (Curiously, almost no תורה is quoted in his name; everyone knows the witticisms.) On ערב פסח his רבנית asked: "Have you finished with all the חמץ?" He answered: "I burned everything — but there is one piece of חמץ I have not been able to get rid of for thirty-five years." She, equally sharp, answered: "That's what you're worried about? My father sold that חמץ to a גוי גמור thirty-five years ago!" (חז"ל's לשון: when we say "גוי גמור," we always mean a Jew. A non-Jew is never called a גוי גמור.) Let us not speak to our wives this way.

II. The Unique איסור of חמץ

חמץ is unlike any other איסור in the תורה. Three layers of prohibition:

  1. איסור אכילה — eating חמץ on פסח; חיוב כרת.
  2. איסור הנאה — even non-eating uses such as feeding it to one's animal.
  3. בל יראה ובל ימצא — a Jew may not own חמץ on פסח, even if he never eats it. The תורה commands the elimination of possession, not merely consumption.

This third element has no parallel in the rest of the תורה. בשר וחלב is forbidden to eat — but the תורה does not forbid owning בשר וחלב. חלב (forbidden fats), דם, and other איסורי אכילה can sit in your house. חמץ on פסח alone the תורה demands you remove from your domain.

The תוספות's Reason

The תוספות at the opening of מסכת פסחים[1] explain why. חמץ is what we eat every day of the year. בשר וחלב is forbidden always; a יהודי is conditioned to never eat it. חמץ is the inverse: a daily food becomes forbidden for one week. The תורה anticipates the natural mistake — you wake up tired after the סדר, you walk into the kitchen, you reach. The תורה removes the temptation by removing the חמץ entirely. בל יראה is a סייג against accidental אכילת חמץ with its חיוב כרת.

III. ביטול Alone, מדאורייתא

The רמב"ם[4] writes — and it is the פשט of the גמרא in the opening of פסחים[3] — that מדאורייתא, the מצוה of "תשביתו" is fulfilled through ביטול alone.

If you only had the תורה — without the רבנן who layer everything — your ערב פסח would be very simple. No בדיקה; no שריפה (מדאורייתא there is no obligation to burn the חמץ). Just sit at the right moment, focus your mind, and declare:

כל חמירא וחמיעא דאיכא ברשותי, דלא חזיתיה ודלא בערתיה, ליבטל ולהוי הפקר כעפרא דארעא.
All חמץ in my possession is nullified, rendered ownerless, like dust of the earth.

Once you have honestly made that ביטול, the חמץ is no longer yours. It can sit in your cabinets all פסח; you have not transgressed בל יראה ובל ימצא, because there is no חמץ of yours to be seen or found. Like the dust on your floor: it lies in your house, but it is not yours; anyone may take it.

Imagine how easy ערב פסח would be: the family stays seated; everyone says "כל חמירא" with כוונה; שלום על ישראל, we are done. A neighbor on פסח needs a sandwich? Help yourself; it is not mine.

IV. The תקנת חכמים — בדיקה and ביעור

The first משנה in פסחים[2]: "לילה ארבעה עשר בודקין את החמץ לאור הנר" — on the night of the 14th of ניסן we check by candlelight. And we burn the חמץ on the morning of the 14th. Active. Why did חז"ל add to the תורה's requirement?

The תוספות

The תוספות: ביטול is purely a מצב נפשי — a mental act. The words alone are not the ביטול; the כוונה is. Not everyone can authentically reach that mental state. Many people simply mouth the words without any inner act of release. (Hence the משנה ברורה[7]'s ruling: if you do not understand the Aramaic of "כל חמירא", translate it for your wife and children — open the ArtScroll, read in English. The ביטול requires understanding.)

Without authentic ביטול, the חמץ remains the person's; בל יראה ובל ימצא is being violated. חז"ל's תקנה: don't rely on the inner state alone. Make it physical. Check, find, remove. מעשה works even where מחשבה failed.

The ר"ן

The ר"ן[5] gives a different reason. He concedes that ביטול works מדאורייתא for בל יראה. The problem he addresses is downstream: you wake up פסח morning, see a beautiful bottle of schnapps or a געשמאקע חמץ roll, and your hand reaches out. Your earlier ביטול only protects against בל יראה; it does not protect against the איסור אכילה. חז"ל intervened: physically remove the חמץ from the home, and the temptation goes with it.

V. The מנחת חינוך's Question

The ספר מנחת חינוך[6] — written by רבי יוסף באבאד, רב of Tarnopol in western Ukraine (1801–1874; remarkably, his birth and death fell on the same day of the year — a special סימן) — is one of the great ספרים of analytical למדנות. He raises a fundamental question on תשביתו.

Two Categories of מצוה

  1. מצות קום ועשה — fulfilled by active performance: תפילין, שופר, סוכה, מצה.
  2. מצות שב ואל תעשה — fulfilled by inaction. The מצות עשה of "וביום השביעי תשבות": every moment one refrains from מלאכה, one is fulfilling the מצוה of resting. Hands tied behind one's back, sitting still on שבת — the מצוה is being performed.

Which Is תשביתו?

Did the תורה command an active destruction — get up, find חמץ, burn it, perform ביטול, do something? Or did the תורה simply require not having חמץ — a שב ואל תעשה in which the absence is itself the fulfillment?

VI. Three נפקא מינות

The מנחת חינוך brings three concrete cases where the answer matters.

1. The Person Who Has No חמץ

Someone who is gluten-free; חמץ has not entered his house in seven months. ערב פסח arrives. Does he need to buy חמץ in order to burn it?

  • If תשביתו is קום ועשה — he must perform an act; he should buy חמץ in order to discharge the מצוה.
  • If תשביתו is שב ואל תעשה — he is already יוצא; he has no חמץ; that is precisely what the תורה wanted.

The מנחת חינוך raises the first option seriously, even if it sounds strange. Maybe the תורה wanted you to buy חמץ just to burn it.

2. Eating חמץ as ביעור

You find a piece of חמץ on פסח. You must remove it. Could you eat it? It would, after all, no longer be in your possession.

If this happens on שבת afternoon (when burning is forbidden anyway), and the איסור אכילה is operative — and you eat — were you מקיים תשביתו through the eating?

The objection: this is מצוה הבאה בעבירה — many פוסקים hold that a מצוה performed through an עבירה is invalid. Eating חמץ is the עבירה; you cannot dress it up as a מצוה.

The מנחת חינוך says: this depends on our question.

  • If תשביתו is קום ועשה — the act of removal is the מצוה. Here, the act of removal is the eating itself. מצוה הבאה בעבירה; invalid.
  • If תשביתו is שב ואל תעשה — only the state of not having חמץ is the מצוה. The eating was a הכשר, a means to that state. Not מצוה הבאה בעבירה; the מצוה may be valid (though one is still חייב כרת for the אכילת חמץ).

3. Stealing Another's ביעור

You walk to your ביעור with a bag of חמץ. Someone grabs the bag and throws it into your fire before you can. He has stolen your מצוה.

The גמרא's general rule (e.g., for כיסוי הדם, where one who covers another's blood owes the slaughterer ten זוזים)[11]: one who steals a מצוה pays.

  • If תשביתו is קום ועשה — he stole your מצוה; he owes ten זוזים.
  • If תשביתו is שב ואל תעשה — he helped you. You should say "יישר כח". No payment.

VII. חמץ as the יצר הרע

The חתם סופר's Question on מצה

The חתם סופר in his פירוש on the הגדה[8] raises a counter-intuitive observation. חז"ל teach: מצה may only be made from a grain that could become חמץ. Rice flour, corn flour, ground potato — none of these may be used for מצה (no matter how baked). Only the five מיני דגן, which can ferment into חמץ, qualify.

Counter-intuitive: the most dangerous ingredient on פסח is מצה itself. It is flour and water — exactly the formula for חמץ. We rush to bake it within eighteen minutes; my father, on ערב פסח, was מקפיד on קפולות[10] — those folds in מצה where the dough may have doubled over and not baked through. We would sit and check pounds of מצות, breaking off the folds and discarding them before the זמן ביעור.

Why did the תורה tell us to take precisely the most dangerous ingredient and make our מצוה from it? Why not use rice — which the גמרא tells us cannot become חמץ (it merely undergoes סרחון, decay, not fermentation) — and have a worry-free פסח?

The רדב"ז — חמץ Is the יצר הרע

The רדב"ז[9] writes — not as חסידות, not as קבלה, but as the simple פשט in the איסור — that חמץ represents the יצר הרע. The תורה's extreme stringency on חמץ — even a משהו forbidden, even owning it forbidden — is precisely because the חמץ we hunt is, simultaneously, the יצר הרע we hunt.

The חתם סופר's Answer

The עבודה of אכילת מצה is the journey from potential חמץ to מצה. Take the same flour that could ferment into the יצר הרע and arrest it within eighteen minutes — transform it into קדושה. צדיקים who have transmuted their יצר הרע into עבודת ה' are not the audience for this מצוה. The מצוה is for the rest of us — those still struggling, those whose flour can still ferment. Rice flour does not have potential; it cannot teach the עבודה.

This applies equally to תשביתו. The מצוה is not absence but finding and burning. פסח is the season for going into the corners of one's heart and rooting out the יצר הרע — searching it out, breaking it, defeating it.

This may sit behind the מנחת חינוך's very question. If תשביתו were merely the absence of חמץ, the gluten-free Jew would be יוצא automatically. But if תשביתו is the עבודה of confrontation — finding the יצר הרע in order to burn it — then the gluten-free Jew is missing the עבודה of פסח. He needs to confront חמץ, not just to lack it.

VIII. The Search Within

The other side of the same teaching: many of us walk into פסח thinking we are fine. "I am fine compared to others. I learn the דף; I daven three times a day; I give צדקה; I put on תפילין. I am fine."

The פסוק: there is no such thing as a person who is wholly צדיק. The תורה's instruction at the פרשה: go check for חמץ. Go to the hidden recesses of your heart, find the חמץ you are carrying, and burn it. Sitting on ערב פסח declaring "I have no חמץ" is not the עבודה. The עבודה is the מעשה of בדיקה — of looking honestly, by candlelight, into the corners.

IX. יחץ

This explains a striking detail of the סדר. Throughout the תורה, our מצוות require objects to be whole. A שופר with a hole is invalid; an אתרוג with a blemish is invalid; the box of תפילין must be perfectly square. Wholeness is the rule.

And then on the סדר we perform יחץ: we deliberately break the middle מצה in half.

The teaching: פסח is the night to acknowledge that we are not whole. However righteous we believe we are, there is a piece missing. We come to הקדוש ברוך הוא through יחץ not whole but vulnerable — "we are only half; help us become whole." The עבודה of פסח begins with that admission.

X. A Note on מכירת חמץ

Someone asked about חמץ גמור — the practice of selling actual חמץ (not just חמץ-touched utensils) to a non-Jew over פסח.

The Rebbe's family מנהג: I do not sell חמץ גמור, because my father did not, and his father did not. If my grandfather came for פסח and learned there was חמץ גמור in the house, he would have walked out before the סדר.

The historical reality of מכירת חמץ[12] is fascinating, and one day we will dedicate a full שיעור to it. In earlier generations there was no such practice. You had חמץ; you got rid of it; that was that. מכירת חמץ developed in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries in Europe in response to a specific economic situation:

The most popular respectable פרנסה for the European יהודי in that era was running an inn or a tavern — the קרעטשמער Jews, with the local governor's license, operating distilleries with barrels of whisky and grain-based spirits. To require these יהודים to destroy their entire stock for פסח would have been economic ruin. The פוסקים of those generations developed the formal mechanism of מכירת חמץ to allow these יהודים to continue.

Some פוסקים hold that מכירת חמץ for actual חמץ גמור was developed only for cases like the schnapps of the tavern owner — significant economic loss; serious enjoyment over the year — not for the marginal cookies. Why sell a few dollars' worth of cookies to keep them post-פסח? Better to throw them away. Others hold that מכירת חמץ applies fully to all חמץ גמור. A live מחלוקת for another שיעור.

This is what I love most: history shaping הלכה. What looks like a self-evident מנהג today is in fact a 300-year-old answer to a specific economic question.

XI. Closing

May we be זוכה to fulfill בדיקת חמץ and ביעור חמץ with full heart. ביטול is real; have כוונה when you say "כל חמירא וחמיעא"; understand the words.

And — beneath the הלכה — let us search for the חמץ in the corners of our own hearts, find the יצר הרע we have been hiding, and burn it. May we merit משיח צדקנו במהרה בימינו, אמן.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. תוספות פסחים ב' ע"א (ד"ה אור) — uniqueness of בל יראה; חמץ as a year-round food; the rationale for the rabbinic בדיקה. — The opening תוספות in מסכת פסחים develops the answer to the structural question: why does the תורה uniquely add איסור הנאה and בל יראה ובל ימצא to the איסור אכילה of חמץ, when no other איסור אכילה in the תורה carries these layers? The answer: חמץ is what we eat the rest of the year, and is therefore uniquely tempting — the prohibition operates as a סייג against accidental אכילה, with its חיוב כרת. The same logic grounds the תוספות's reason for the rabbinic תקנת בדיקה: ביטול alone, being a mental act, is unreliable; חז"ל required physical removal as backup. ↩
  2. משנה פסחים א':א' — "אור לארבעה עשר בודקין את החמץ לאור הנר." — The opening משנה of מסכת פסחים: on the night of the 14th of ניסן we check for חמץ by candlelight. The משנה codifies the rabbinic תקנה of בדיקת חמץ; the גמרא's opening discussion explores the timing ("אור" — does it mean morning or evening?) and the parameters of the search. ↩
  3. גמרא פסחים ד' ע"ב — ביטול as the קיום of "תשביתו" מדאורייתא; בדיקה as תקנת חכמים. — The סוגיא establishes that מדאורייתא the קיום of "תשביתו שאור מבתיכם" (שמות י"ב:ט"ו) is achieved through ביטול alone; the חמץ is rendered הפקר, ceases to be the person's, and the איסור בל יראה ובל ימצא does not apply. The rabbinic layer of בדיקה and physical ביעור was added on top of this דאורייתא base for the reasons developed by תוספות and the ר"ן. ↩
  4. רמב"ם הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ב' הלכה ב' — ביטול as the מצוה דאורייתא. — The רמב"ם codifies the פשט of the סוגיא: "ומה היא השבתה זו האמורה בתורה? היא שיבטל בלבו ויחשוב אותו כעפר". The דאורייתא requirement is purely a mental-declarative act: rendering the חמץ like dust in one's heart. The active rabbinic בדיקה and שריפה are תקנת חכמים built on top of this base. The רמב"ם's formulation shapes all subsequent פסקי הלכה: the ביטול is the foundation; בדיקה the supporting structure. ↩
  5. חידושי הר"ן פסחים ב' ע"א — בדיקה as גזירה שמא יאכל. — רבינו נסים גירונדי (the ר"ן, c. 1320–1376, Barcelona) offers a different rationale for the תקנת בדיקה than the תוספות's. He concedes that ביטול works מדאורייתא for בל יראה ובל ימצא; the תקנה targets a downstream concern — the איסור אכילה. Even after ביטול, חמץ physically present in the home tempts the eater on פסח; the תקנה is to remove the חמץ physically so that the eater is not led into איסור אכילה with its חיוב כרת. ↩
  6. ספר מנחת חינוך (רבי יוסף באבאד, Tarnopol, 1801–1874), מצוה ט' — קום ועשה vs. שב ואל תעשה in "תשביתו"; three נפקא מינות. — רבי יוסף באבאד, רב of Tarnopol in eastern Galicia (today Ternopil, western Ukraine), composed the ספר מנחת חינוך as an analytical commentary on the ספר החינוך — exploring the parameters of each מצוה through חקירות and נפקא מינות. The work became one of the foundational ספרי למדנות of nineteenth-century למדן-style. On "תשביתו" he raises the חקירה whether the מצוה is קום ועשה (active destruction) or שב ואל תעשה (the state of not having חמץ), with three concrete נפקא מינות: the gluten-free Jew, eating found חמץ on שבת, and stealing another's ביעור. ↩
  7. משנה ברורה סימן תל"ד סעיף ב' — translating "כל חמירא" into a language the מבטל understands. — The משנה ברורה rules that the ביטול requires understanding: one who does not understand the Aramaic of "כל חמירא וחמיעא" must translate it into a language he understands (Yiddish, English, Hebrew) before reciting. The פסק follows from the תוספות's premise that ביטול is essentially a mental-declarative act of release; meaningless words cannot effect it. This is also the basis for instructing one's wife and children to recite the נוסח in a language they understand on ערב פסח. ↩
  8. חתם סופר, פירוש על ההגדה / דרשות חתם סופר on פסח — מצה made from grains capable of becoming חמץ; the עבודה from חמץ to מצה. — רבי משה סופר (the חתם סופר, 1762–1839, Pressburg/Bratislava), in his commentary on the הגדה and his דרשות on פסח, develops the חידוש: מצה may only be made from the five מיני דגן — precisely those grains that can become חמץ. The עבודה of אכילת מצה is therefore the עבודה of taking the very ingredient that could ferment into the יצר הרע and arresting it — within eighteen minutes — into קדושה. The מצוה addresses the still-struggling יהודי, not the perfected צדיק; rice flour, which cannot ferment, lacks the פוטנציאל needed for the עבודה. ↩
  9. שו"ת הרדב"ז on חמץ as the יצר הרע; the פשט behind the stringency of the איסור. — רבי דוד בן זמרא (the רדב"ז, 1479–1573, Egypt and Tzfas), in his שו"ת, develops the reading that חמץ's extreme חומרא in the תורה — the only איסור אכילה with איסור הנאה and בל יראה; forbidden even במשהו; חיוב כרת for אכילה — is grounded in חמץ as a representation of the יצר הרע. The רדב"ז presents this not as חסידות or קבלה but as the פשט of the חומרא: the תורה's war against חמץ is, simultaneously, our annual עבודה of confronting and defeating the יצר הרע. ↩
  10. משנה ברורה on סימן תס"א — קפולות in מצה; סרחון of rice (פסחים ל"ה ע"א). — The משנה ברורה records the חומרא of קפולות: doubled-over folds in baked מצה where the dough may not have baked through, leaving uncooked dough between the layers — at risk of fermentation. Many בעלי בתים inspect their מצות on ערב פסח and break off קפולות before the זמן ביעור. The גמרא in פסחים ל"ה ע"א establishes that rice (אורז) does not undergo חימוץ but only סרחון (decay) — explaining why rice flour cannot be used for מצה and grounding the חתם סופר's rhetorical question about why we don't simply use rice. ↩
  11. גמרא חולין פ"ז ע"א — ten זוזים for one who steals a מצוה (כיסוי הדם). — The סוגיא in חולין records the דין that one who covers another's blood after שחיטה, depriving the שוחט of the מצוה of כיסוי הדם, owes the שוחט ten gold זוזים as a קנס for the stolen מצוה. The סוגיא establishes the general principle (cited later by פוסקים in many contexts) that one who deprives another of a מצוה הבאה לידו is liable for קנס in this fixed amount; the principle grounds the מנחת חינוך's third נפקא מינה — whether someone who throws another's חמץ into the fire owes the ten זוזים, depending on whether "תשביתו" is קום ועשה. ↩

Historical development of מכירת חמץ in seventeenth- and eighteenth-century European פוסקים: the formal mechanism for selling חמץ גמור to a non-Jew over פסח emerged in response to the economic structure of the קרעטשמער (innkeeper / tavern-keeper) Jewish economy in Poland, Galicia, and Bohemia, where Jewish lessees of noble distilleries and taverns held large stocks of grain spirits whose destruction would mean financial ruin. The mechanism was developed by פוסקים across this period (the בית מאיר, the חק יעקב, the פני יהושע, and others) and codified in the שולחן ערוך הרב and later פוסקים. There remains a live מחלוקת among contemporary פוסקים whether the היתר applies fully to all חמץ גמור or only to cases of significant economic loss; many בעלי נפש follow the older מנהג of disposing of חמץ גמור entirely.
