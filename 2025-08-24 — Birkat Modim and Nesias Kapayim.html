ברכת מודים and נשיאת כפים

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 24 August 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

The continuation of ברכת מודים with the חפץ חיים's standing pause; the מדרש that all קרבנות and תפילות will be בטל in the future except תודה; מודים דרבנן as the gathered formulations of רב פפא's "למימרינהו לתרווייהו"; the unforgettable story of רב אלישיב at age 102 learning to say "thank you" in English; the silent מודים that should not be silent. Then the long puzzle of נשיאת כפים in חוץ לארץ — why we do not דוכן daily, the failed attempts to restore it (the גר"א in jail; רב חיים מוולוז'ין and the fire that consumed half of Volozhin; the בעל התניא; רב נתן הכהן אדלר in Frankfurt as the only success); חזקת כהונה in גלות; and the regional differences across the גליל.

Sponsored לעילוי נשמת הרב זצ"ל, ישועה בן רחמים.

I. The Primacy of מודים

The גמרא teaches a striking principle. חז"ל understood the human reality: a יהודי cannot sustain כוונה through every ברכה of שמונה עשרה. It is too difficult.

The ירושלמי[2]: one of the אמוראים declared, "I am fortunate that my body bows down מאליו at מודים and at מגן אברהם — because if not, I would not even know to do that." The body knows; the mind drifts.

The standard rule in the גמרא: the one ברכה requiring absolute כוונה is מגן אברהם. There is a different גרסה equating מודים with מגן אברהם. At minimum, one must have כוונה at one of the two — and ideally at both.

The חפץ חיים

Once someone had the חוצפה to ask the חפץ חיים: "רבי, why do you stand so long during שמונה עשרה? Just say the words with כוונה and finish."

The חפץ חיים: "You're mistaken. I daven שמונה עשרה very quickly. At מודים I stop and pause. I cannot get enough of this emotion — of expressing thanks and gratitude to הקדוש ברוך הוא for every moment of my life. My שמונה עשרה is short; the מודים is long."

II. What כוונה in מודים

The ברכה says we thank הקדוש ברוך הוא for everything He does בקר וערב וצהרים. The מפרשים: our נשמה, which we entrust to הקדוש ברוך הוא each night with "בידך אפקיד רוחי" at קריאת שמע על המטה, returns intact each morning. We are alive.

The תורת חיים[3] quotes קדמונים: a person should thank הקדוש ברוך הוא for every single breath. דוד המלך closes תהלים: "כל הנשמה תהלל י-ה" — every breath should praise. If we functioned properly we would thank for every inhale and exhale. Who can? We cannot. So in מודים we say "על נשמותינו הפקודות לך" with the כוונה: we thank You for every breath. To meditate on this and have proper כוונה takes time. That is why the חפץ חיים stood long here.

III. The Bowing — Equal to אבות

Another proof of מודים's status: we bow. We bow at אבות and at מודים — the four bows of שמונה עשרה. Bowing is the body's expression of complete השתעבדות: "We are Yours." Just as at אבות, so at מודים — at the פתיחה and the חתימה, identical posture.

IV. The Eternal תודה

A striking מדרש[4]: all קרבנות will be בטל לעתיד לבוא — except קרבן תודה. (The straightforward פשט of the רמב"ם: קרבנות will return when משיח comes. But the מדרש envisions a still later moment when all but תודה become בטל.) The שולחן ערוך brings the מדרש להלכה — unusual for the שולחן ערוך to bring a מדרש; it underlines the standing of תודה.

The Four Who Must Bring קרבן תודה

The תורה: four categories. The סימן: חיים (from "חיים יודוך"):

  • ח — חולה שנתרפא.
  • י — ים: a sea traveler.
  • י — released from prison or great difficulty.
  • ם — מדבר: a desert traveler.

One who would bring קרבן תודה today, in the absence of the בית המקדש, says ברכת הגומל instead. When we say מזמור לתודה daily, we partially fulfill our תודה obligation. We have no idea how many times we should have brought קרבן תודה this past year — how many times our lives were saved without our knowing.

The same principle applies to תפילה. Since תפילה stands כנגד הקרבנות, the מדרש teaches: just as all קרבנות become בטל except תודה, so all תפילות will become בטל except one — מודים אנחנו לך. מודים is eternal.

V. מודים דרבנן

Origin

The גמרא in סוטה מ'[1]: when the שליח ציבור said מודים אנחנו לך, the ציבור bowed with him and said their own version of thanksgiving. There were several different גרסאות among the אמוראים. רב פפא — characteristically — settled it at the end of the סוגיא with "למימרינהו לתרווייהו": say all of them.

One אמורא: "ברוך א-ל ההודאות". Another: "אתה הוא ה' אלקינו...". Per רב פפא, we incorporate them all. We call this מודים דרבנן.

Why "דרבנן"?

It is a fair question — everything in תפילה is from the רבנן. The answer: this ברכה is called מודים דרבנן because it is a combination of all the לשונות the various אמוראים proposed. We collect them; hence "דרבנן".

Why Say It At All?

A deeper question: why does the ציבור say its own מודים? In other places we are יוצא through the שליח ציבור. But חז"ל taught that you cannot thank הקדוש ברוך הוא through a שליח. תודה demands the personal voice.

Rav Elyashiv at 102

רב אלישיב זצ"ל[19] — the פוסק הדור in ירושלים, the גדול הדור, who never left ירושלים; never left his four אמות, learning יומם ולילה for nearly a hundred years — at the age of 102 underwent open-heart surgery. They flew the best surgeon from America to ארץ ישראל rather than fly Rav Elyashiv abroad.

After the surgery, when he woke, he turned to his attendant and asked, "How do you say 'יישר כוח'? How do you say 'תודה רבה' in English?" He did not know the words "thank you." They taught him.

He called the doctor in. "Thank you very much." Those around him said: "רבי, you could have told us; we would have thanked him in English for you. You could have said it in לשון הקודש and we would have translated."

Rav Elyashiv answered: "That is exactly why we say מודים דרבנן! You do not send someone else to thank. When you have to thank, even if it is in a different language, learn that language yourself. Don't send a שליח ציבור to thank on your behalf."

The ציבור joins, bows, thanks הקדוש ברוך הוא personally. Thanking through a שליח is not a real thank-you.

"על שאנו מודים לך"

The closing line. From childhood the Rebbe understood it as: we thank הקדוש ברוך הוא that we have the ability to thank Him — for the gift of gratitude itself. The ר"ן[5] in a תשובה reads it differently: "על שאנו מודים לך" means in addition to what we thanked You for in the silent תפילת לחש, we now add these לשונות.

VI. A Common Halachic Mistake

Many שליחי ציבור say מודים silently during חזרת הש"ץ — quietly while the קהל says מודים דרבנן, thinking, "Why waste my voice? Nobody can hear me anyway." They raise their voices only at the conclusion.

This is wrong.

The שליח ציבור must say every word of חזרת הש"ץ aloud — even מודים. Out loud, even though the ציבור will not hear it because they are saying their own מודים דרבנן. That was the תקנה. We do not technically need the חזרה today — everyone davens individually — but once it was incorporated, we cannot deviate.

Some פוסקים require ten people in the vicinity of the שליח ציבור who hear every word. Practically difficult — "שלשה קולות לא ישתמעו", three voices cannot be heard simultaneously — but that is the פסק.

It is a מצוה to be מתקן. If you want to conduct חזרת הש"ץ as it is supposed to be conducted, the שליח ציבור must say every word aloud — including מודים.

VII. נשיאת כפים — A Long Puzzle

A real puzzle. Why is it that we in חוץ לארץ do not דוכן daily?

In ארץ ישראל they do every day. Among ספרדים in חוץ לארץ, every day. Even in an אשכנזי shul in ארץ ישראל with an entirely אשכנזי מנין, they דוכן daily. Only the אשכנזים in חוץ לארץ reserve the practice for יום טוב. No פוסק has a fully satisfying explanation.

The מצוה

ברכת כהנים is one of the תרי"ג מצוות[6]. The רמב"ם, the חינוך, the ראשונים: the כהנים must bless כלל ישראל daily. When a כהן blesses, he fulfills three מצוות עשה, because the פסוק uses three expressions: "כה תברכו, אמור להם, ושמו את שמי על בני ישראל ואני אברכם". The חינוך: "בכל מקום, בכל זמן, בכל יום".

How is it that we in חוץ לארץ stopped fulfilling this?

Not at מנחה

One exception even in ארץ ישראל: no דוכן at מנחה. (At נעילה on יום הכפורים there is variation; some shuls do, some don't.)

The רמב"ם's explanation[7]: מנחה follows the day's main meal. In those generations the principal meal was in the afternoon, and three drinks were standard at meals: milk, water, and wine. Wine was much more common at meals than today; חז"ל were wary of שכרות at מנחה. נשיאת כפים has the status of עבודה — like the עבודה in the בית המקדש — and a כהן may not perform עבודה while intoxicated. Hence: not at מנחה. שחרית is fine — who drinks wine before שחרית?

נשיאת כפים as עבודה

The תורה: "וידבר ה' אל משה ואל אהרן... ויברך את העם". The משנה[8] records נשיאת כפים in the בית המקדש daily — the בעלי מעמדות stood in the עזרה, the כהנים blessed them after הודאה. The blessing is עבודה; that is why the שכרות rule applies; it is also why this is taken so seriously.

VIII. The Old מנהג in חוץ לארץ

So why don't we דוכן in חוץ לארץ? An ancient מנהג. The פוסקים: as far back as the מהר"ם מרוטנבורג — about seven hundred years ago in Europe — they did not דוכן; only a few times a year, only on יום טוב, only at מוסף. The פוסקים and historians struggle to explain why.

The רמ"א's Reason

The רמ"א[9] brings the famous טעם: in גלות, יהודים in חוץ לארץ were so consumed with the difficulty of פרנסה that they could not have proper כוונה. ברכת כהנים requires אהבה ושמחה — not just no שכרות but real love and joy. With no שמחה in גלות, the practice fell away. The בית יוסף[10] already records this dynamic.

On יום טוב: there is שמחה, no work, the day is free; the כהנים can דוכן.

What About שבת?

The רמ"א: most יהודים on שבת are mentally occupied with the week's business. (The הלכה permits thinking about business on שבת — only speaking business is forbidden under "דבר דבר"; if you cannot fully restrain yourself and you think about deals, that is permitted. "נשבת גמר" is the basis.) So even on שבת the proper מנוחת הנפש is not assumed. Only יום טוב, where everyone is in a different zone.

Difficulties With This Reason

This is a difficult טעם. Are we to say יהודים in ארץ ישראל today have an easier time with פרנסה? Worried about the next bill, the deal closing, the stocks. Many such questions. The רמ"א's reason has the feel of an after-the-fact justification for a מנהג whose origin is genuinely obscure. But that is what we have.

IX. The Failed Attempts to Restore

Several גדולים tried to overturn the מנהג and reinstate daily דוכן in חוץ לארץ. The pattern is striking.

The גר"א

The גר"א in Vilna decided enough is enough. He gathered his תלמידים: "Tomorrow we begin daily דוכן. We are changing the ancient מנהג."

The next day they put the גר"א in jail. The dispute between חסידים and מתנגדים was raging; an informer reported him to the non-Jewish authorities. The גר"א read it as a sign from heaven. When he was released, he retracted on the spot: "Forget about it. No daily דוכן."

Reb Chaim of Volozhin

The גר"א's great תלמיד, Reb Chaim of Volozhin, did not learn the lesson. The נצי"ב (whose יארצייט fell on שבת or Friday in this season) — Reb Chaim's grandson — records hearing from his father, רבי יצחק מוולוז'ין, son of Reb Chaim:

After the גר"א's passing, Reb Chaim in Volozhin (a city in Belarus; the ישיבה still stands as a museum) decided to restore daily נשיאת כפים. He told his תלמידים: "Tomorrow we begin a new מנהג in the בית מדרש. We start דוכן daily."

That night, half the city of Volozhin burned down. Wooden buildings; one house caught fire, then the next, then the next. Reb Chaim said: "I wanted to break an ancient מנהג. I do not know why הקדוש ברוך הוא did this; apparently the שפע in חוץ לארץ flows differently than in ארץ ישראל. We should not do it." He retracted.

The ערוך השלחן[11], recording these stories, says: "It is as if a בת קול went out from heaven: Don't do this."

The בעל התניא

Some say the בעל התניא also tried. The Lubavitcher Rebbe gave a שיחה[12] on this. שולחן ערוך הרב does not record an attempt; the Rebbe's שיחה says: "The אלטע זיידע, the רבי the זקן בעל התניא, also wanted to restore it" — and something happened, there was confusion, and he retracted.

Rav Nosson HaKohen Adler — The Only One Who Succeeded

One יהודי succeeded.

We are traveling in two weeks to the חתם סופר in Pressburg. How did the חתם סופר get to Pressburg? He was born in Frankfurt. His רבי: רבי נתן הכהן אדלר. רב נתן אדלר was a גאון, צדיק, and מקובל who did unusual things in Frankfurt — a שטעט יקי city, and he was a מקובל. He changed the נוסח from נוסח אשכנז; in Frankfurt this was unheard of, and the רבנים threw him out of the city. The חתם סופר left with him as a young child and never returned to Frankfurt. He went to Pressburg and became the גדול הדור there. (The Hirsch Museum in Frankfurt has a great deal on this.)

רב נתן אדלר was a כהן. In his בית מדרש, he instituted daily נשיאת כפים. He was the only one who succeeded — until they expelled him from the city. He always said ברכת כהנים. Fascinating. No one else succeeded.

X. חזקת כהונה — Lost in גלות

Some פוסקים attribute the מנהג to a halachic concern: כהנים in חוץ לארץ have lost their חזקת כהונה. The שאלת מרגליות[13] has an entire תשובה on it.

I hope no כהן here will be offended. The line is not about your father — your father was a כהן; his father was a כהן; you are a כהן. You know and believe it. The question is structural across דורות of גלות.

The Story of the כהן from Pinsk

A famous מעשה[20]: a man emigrated from Pinsk in Belarus to America in the early 1900s — the Goldene Medina. He arrived at a new קהילה looking for a כהן. In the new world, he decided to pass himself off as a כהן; back in the שטעטל he had been a simple person sitting at the back of the shul, but here he could be a כהן. He bought a new suit, polished his shoes, walked into a shul where no one knew him.

They needed a כהן for the first עליה. "איך בין הכהן!" He received the עליה, felt important. After davening he went to greet the רב. The רב looked at him: "Shmerel! I know you. You are from Pinsk. I'm from Pinsk too. שמערל, I don't understand. Your father was not a כהן — I knew him. Your grandfather was not a כהן. How are you suddenly a כהן?"

Shmerel: "Rabbi, this is a new country. If you can be a רב, I can be a כהן."

The story is humorous and the underlying point is real. Rav Frank and Rabbi Margoliot[14] hold that כהנים across גלות have lost their חזקת כהונה. Many families have a יחוס בריף at home — a documented family letter; not all do. There was a family in Lakewood, into the mid-2000s, performing דוכן and פדיון הבן; on a family trip to קברי אבות abroad they discovered, looking at the מצבות of their elders, that they were not כהנים at all. Imagine — all the פדיון הבן ceremonies they had performed needed to be redone with another כהן.

Why ארץ ישראל Is Different

Why do the ספרדים in ארץ ישראל דוכן? Historical fact: the כהנים there never left the land continuously since the חורבן — and therefore never lost their חזקה. They never became scattered through גלות the way European כהנים did.

DNA and כהונה

Today there are DNA tests. There are contemporary תשובות on this. There is an island in Tunisia called Djerba populated entirely by כהנים — עיר הכהנים; DNA testing apparently confirms common descent from זרע אהרן הכהן. Whether to rely on DNA להלכה is its own question.

The first major halachic case on DNA[15]: after September 11, 2001. A special בית דין was set up for the עגונות; many יהודים died, remains were processed for years, and the question was whether DNA matches sufficed for היתר. The פוסקים: Rav Shmuel Fuerst of Chicago, Rav Avrohom Felder (ראש ישיבה in South Fallsburg), Rav Eliyahu Levine of Lakewood (the מומחה on חכמת ההנדסה and contemporary technical questions). They authored a four-hundred-page unpublished תשובה; their conclusion was not to rely on DNA testing. By extension: not relied on for כהונה either.

The Documented Families

The Baal Shem Tov[18]: there are three families with established יחוס as כהנים — Rapoport, Katz, and one other (some say Margolios; not all Margolios are כהנים). The ברכה of the Baal Shem Tov on these families is taken as substantiation.

XI. Regional Differences in ארץ ישראל — The גליל

One more layer. Even in ארץ ישראל, not everywhere do אשכנזים דוכן daily. ספרדים do everywhere. But אשכנזים in the גליל — Haifa, Tiberias, Tzfat — only דוכן on שבת and יום טוב, not during the week. Walk into an אשכנזי shul in Tzfat, in Tiberias, in Haifa, all the way down to Hadera — they don't דוכן during the week. (Miron דוכן daily because so many people from ירושלים come there; the מנהג from ירושלים rules.)

The Historical Explanation

The גליל was thinly populated for stretches of history. Tzfat was a major city in the time of the בית יוסף and the אריז"ל; then a famous earthquake in the 1700s emptied much of Tzfat — survivors went to ירושלים. Who came to resettle Tzfat? European יהודים: the תלמידים of the Baal Shem Tov — Reb Mendel of Vitebsk among the first — and the תלמידי הגר"א. They brought their חוץ לארץ מנהג — no daily דוכן — and chose not to reinstitute it. The compromise: שבת and יום טוב they דוכן, weekdays they don't.

So even today in ארץ ישראל, large קהילות in the גליל do not דוכן during the week. The תלמידי הגר"א tried to דוכן daily like their רבי; the חסידים chose not to. To this day some רבנים regard the חוץ לארץ מנהג in the גליל as a מנהג טעות and try to reinstitute. It hasn't taken.

Kfar Hasidim — One Exception

One place — the ישיבה at Kfar Hasidim, today called Rechasim — does דוכן daily. It was instituted there by Rav Noach Shimonowitz[16] (his חידושי רב נח is a major ספר). The northern רבנים still debate the underlying מחלוקת.

רב קוק's Stand

There are letters from רב קוק[17] on this. He once traveled from ירושלים to Haifa. He himself דוכןed daily; he was a כהן. They told him in Haifa: "רב, here we don't do that." רב קוק stood up: "I don't care, I am דוכןing." He blessed the קהילה; nothing happened. But he דוכןed only when he was there; once he left, no one continued the practice.

XII. Closing

From מודים, where the חפץ חיים stood pausing because he could not get enough of thanking הקדוש ברוך הוא, and where רב אלישיב at 102 learned to say "thank you" in English in his own voice — through to נשיאת כפים, where attempt after attempt to restore the מצוה in חוץ לארץ was met with prison cells and city fires — the underlying theme is the same: תפילה moves through structures we did not invent and cannot dismantle, and through מצוות we did not impose and cannot easily restore. We work within the structure as it has come down to us, with the חפץ חיים's pauses and Rav Elyashiv's English "thank you" as our models. בעזרת השם, may משיח bring us to a world where כהנים bless כלל ישראל daily again, and where "thank You" is the only תפילה that remains.

The statement grounds the standard פסק that one must have כוונה at minimum at מגן אברהם (the consensus position); a parallel גרסה requires כוונה at מודים as well. ↩
  3. תורת חיים on thanking for every breath; תהלים ק"נ:ו' — "כל הנשמה תהלל י-ה". — The תורת חיים (one of the early פירושים on the סידור) develops the כוונה for "על נשמותינו הפקודות לך" in מודים: a יהודי should ideally thank הקדוש ברוך הוא for every breath, in line with דוד המלך's closing of תהלים — "כל הנשמה תהלל י-ה הללוי-ה" (תהלים ק"נ:ו'), which חז"ל read as praise for every breath, not only with every breath. The structural impossibility of conscious thanks for every inhale and exhale grounds the gathered formulation of מודים as the daily fulfillment of the structural חיוב. ↩
  4. מדרש ויקרא רבה ט':ז' on קרבן תודה — never בטל לעתיד לבוא; brought to הלכה in שולחן ערוך; the four categories of "חיים". — The מדרש in ויקרא רבה records the חידוש: "לעתיד לבוא, כל הקרבנות בטלים, וקרבן תודה אינו בטל" — in the future, all קרבנות will be annulled except the קרבן תודה. The מדרש extends the חידוש to תפילה: all תפילות will be annulled except מודים. The שולחן ערוך brings the מדרש להלכה — uncharacteristic for the שולחן ערוך, which generally limits itself to halachic sources — testifying to the structural standing of תודה. The four categories required to bring קרבן תודה per the תורה (in תהלים ק"ז) are remembered by the acronym "חיים": חולה, ים (sea), יסורים (released from prison/affliction), מדבר (desert). ↩
  5. שו"ת הר"ן on "על שאנו מודים לך" — alternate reading. — רבינו נסים גירונדי (the ר"ן, c. 1320–1376, Barcelona), in his שו"ת, addresses the closing line of מודים דרבנן: "על שאנו מודים לך". The standard reading: we thank You for the gift of being able to thank You. The ר"ן's alternative reading: in addition to all the thanksgivings we offered in the silent תפילת לחש, we now add these לשונות in מודים דרבנן. The two readings are not mutually exclusive; both are preserved in the מסורת. ↩
  6. רמב"ם, ספר המצוות מצות עשה כ"ו; חינוך מצוה שע"ח; ראשונים — ברכת כהנים as one of תרי"ג מצוות; three מצוות עשה in the פסוק; "בכל מקום, בכל זמן, בכל יום". — The רמב"ם in ספר המצוות counts ברכת כהנים as מצוה כ"ו of the מצוות עשה. The ספר החינוך on פרשת נשא (מצוה שע"ח) develops the פרטים: a כהן who blesses fulfills three מצוות עשה, since the פסוק uses three expressions: "כה תברכו את בני ישראל אמור להם... ושמו את שמי על בני ישראל ואני אברכם" (במדבר ו':כ"ג-כ"ז). The חינוך's formulation "בכל מקום, בכל זמן, בכל יום" sets the daily expectation; the חוץ לארץ מנהג deviates from this expectation in a way that has resisted multiple attempted corrections. ↩
  7. רמב"ם, הלכות תפילה ונשיאת כפים פט"ו — מנחה and שכרות; נשיאת כפים as עבודה. — The רמב"ם in הלכות תפילה ונשיאת כפים develops the פרטי הדינים of ברכת כהנים at length. פט"ו addresses the איסור for a כהן who has drunk wine to ascend the דוכן, paralleling the איסור for a כהן intoxicated to perform עבודה in the בית המקדש. The structural identification of נשיאת כפים with עבודה grounds the מנהג not to דוכן at מנחה when the day's main meal (with wine) typically preceded the תפילה. ↩
  8. משנה תמיד פ"ה מ"א; משנה תמיד פ"ז — נשיאת כפים in the בית המקדש daily. — The משנה in מסכת תמיד records the daily order of עבודה in the בית המקדש: after the תמיד, the כהנים ascended the דוכן and recited ברכת כהנים over the בעלי מעמדות standing in the עזרה. The משנה at the close of the מסכת describes the לויים' שירה following. The structural placement of ברכת כהנים within the סדר העבודה is the textual ground for treating נשיאת כפים as עבודה proper, with the corresponding איסורי שכרות and the requirement of קדושה. ↩
  9. רמ"א, אורח חיים סימן קכ"ח סעיף מ"ד — the פרנסה reason for חוץ לארץ; דבר דבר on שבת. — The רמ"א in סימן קכ"ח, סעיף מ"ד, addresses the חוץ לארץ מנהג directly: "המנהג בארצות אלו שאין נושאין כפים אלא בימים טובים". He cites the explanation that יהודים in חוץ לארץ are too consumed with פרנסה on weekdays and even on שבת (the איסור of דבר דבר in ישעיהו נ"ח:י"ג applies to speaking business but not to thinking about it; שבת remains mentally occupied with weekday concerns) to have the requisite אהבה ושמחה for ברכת כהנים. Only יום טוב, with its festive abstention from work and its festive שמחה, supplies the proper conditions. The רמ"א's טעם is widely cited but recognized as a post hoc rationalization of an older מנהג whose true origin is obscure. ↩
  10. בית יוסף, אורח חיים סימן קכ"ח — praising the בני ארץ ישראל and Egypt for daily דוכן. — The בית יוסף in סימן קכ"ח records the contrast: בני ארץ ישראל, Egypt, and the broader ספרדי world maintain daily דוכן in line with the simple פשט of the תורה and the פסק of the רמב"ם and the ראשונים. The בית יוסף praises this practice and laments the European חוץ לארץ deviation; the רמ"א defends the European מנהג with the פרנסה-and-שמחה reasoning. The two positions sit side-by-side in סימן קכ"ח as the מקור for the ספרדי-אשכנזי divergence on this הלכה. ↩
  11. ערוך השלחן אורח חיים סימן קכ"ח — recording the stories of the גר"א and Reb Chaim of Volozhin; "כאילו בת קול יצאה". — The ערוך השלחן, in his treatment of סימן קכ"ח, records the famous attempts by the גר"א in Vilna and Reb Chaim of Volozhin to restore daily נשיאת כפים in חוץ לארץ, and the providential interventions that frustrated each attempt — the גר"א's imprisonment by the non-Jewish authorities (precipitated by an informer in the חסידים-מתנגדים conflict), and the fire that consumed half of Volozhin the night Reb Chaim announced the change. The ערוך השלחן's formulation: "It is as if a בת קול went out from heaven: do not change this מנהג." The ערוך השלחן reads the providential pattern as confirmation of the deep authority of the ancient חוץ לארץ מנהג, even where its טעם is obscure. ↩

  13. שאלת מרגליות (Rabbi Reuven Margoliot) — extended תשובה on loss of חזקת כהונה in גלות. — Rabbi Reuven Margoliot (1889–1971, Lvov and later Jerusalem; Israeli scholar and תלמיד חכם; author of שערי זוהר and many other works) develops in his שאלת מרגליות a sustained תשובה on the question of חזקת כהונה across דורות of גלות. His position: the long pattern of dispersal, persecution, name-changes, conversions in and out, and informal community-formation across European קהילות has eroded the firm halachic חזקה that an individual claiming כהונה is in fact a כהן from זרע אהרן. The שיטה grounds the practical reluctance to דוכן daily and to apply other דיני כהונה with full strictness in contemporary חוץ לארץ conditions. The שיטה is contested; many פוסקים maintain חזקת כהונה for those whose families have transmitted the identity continuously. ↩
  14. Rav Tzvi Pesach Frank זצ"ל (רב צבי פסח פרנק, 1873–1960, אב"ד of ירושלים; author of הר צבי) and Rabbi Reuven Margoliot (see entry on שאלת מרגליות) — the principal twentieth-century פוסקים to develop in detail the שיטה that כהנים in חוץ לארץ have lost their חזקת כהונה. Their position grounds the practical קולא on certain דיני כהונה and connects to the broader question of why נשיאת כפים daily remained a חוץ לארץ outlier. The contrary שיטה — that contemporary כהנים retain valid חזקה — is held by most פוסקים in practice; the question is unresolved. The Lakewood family case the Rebbe describes — where a family discovered through visiting ancestral קברים that they were not actually כהנים after years of performing דוכן and פדיון הבן — illustrates the practical concern.

  17. אגרות הראי"ה — רב אברהם יצחק הכהן קוק זצ"ל on daily נשיאת כפים; the Haifa episode. — רב קוק זצ"ל (1865–1935, first Ashkenazi Chief Rabbi of ארץ ישראל; a כהן), in his collected letters (אגרות הראי"ה) and through transmitted מעשים, addresses the גליל question. The episode the Rebbe brings: on a visit from ירושלים to Haifa, רב קוק insisted on דוכןing daily as he did in ירושלים, despite the local מנהג against it. The episode illustrates the מתח between the two halachic sensibilities and the practical respect גדולים have shown to deeply rooted regional מנהגים even where they personally differ. ↩

  19. Rav Yosef Shalom Elyashiv זצ"ל (1910–2012; פוסק הדור of his generation in ירושלים; spent virtually his entire adult life within his four אמות learning יומם ולילה) underwent open-heart surgery at age 102 in 2012, performed by an American cardiac surgeon flown to Israel for the procedure. The story grounds the Rebbe's structural reading of מודים דרבנן: תודה must be in one's own voice, not through a שליח.

The "כהן from Pinsk" anecdote — circulating in early-twentieth-century American Jewish humor and memoir literature as a paradigmatic illustration of the loosening of חזקת כהונה in immigrant America. A man from a Belarusian שטעטל, known to his original קהילה as a non-כהן, presents himself as a כהן in his new American קהילה; when challenged by a רב who knew him from the old country, he answers, "If you can be a רב here, I can be a כהן." The story functions as both punchline and structural commentary: the same dislocation that allowed unqualified men to assume rabbinical positions in early twentieth-century America also opened the door to false claims of כהונה. The Rebbe brings it as a vivid illustration of the underlying halachic concern that motivates the שאלת מרגליות's שיטה.
