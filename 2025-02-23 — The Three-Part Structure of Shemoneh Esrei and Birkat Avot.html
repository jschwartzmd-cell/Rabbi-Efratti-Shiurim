The Three-Part Structure of שמונה עשרה and ברכת אבות

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 23 February 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026  ·  Mareh Mekomos upgraded 5 May 2026

Why does שמונה עשרה open with three ברכות of שבח, then twelve of בקשה, then three of הודאה? Two answers — the מבי"ט's and the תרומת הדשן's. The strange figure of נחום המדי whose תורה was almost universally rejected. The opening of ברכת אבות: why we say אלקי אברהם, אלקי יצחק, אלקי יעקב separately rather than together — including the גמרא in בבא מציעא on אליהו הנביא, רבי חייא and his sons, and the פולסא דנורא.

I. The Routine of תפילה

The גמרא in ברכות tells us something extraordinary: even הקדוש ברוך הוא Himself, so to speak, davens. תפילה is a רום מעלה in the עולם. Yet because we daven three times a day, the act becomes routine and we are מזלזל in it. The Rebbe's note: someone once davened so quickly the Rebbe could not keep up, and afterwards justified it as a סגולה for הקדוש ברוך הוא to accept the תפילה ברצון, citing "במהרה". That is not the פשט. Don't use it as an excuse.

II. The Three-Part Structure of שמונה עשרה

The גמרא in ברכות ל"ד ע"א[1] teaches a foundational כלל: a person may not request personal בקשות in the first three ברכות nor in the last three ברכות. The architecture follows naturally:

  1. First three — ברכות השבח: אבות, גבורות, קדושת השם. Praise of הקדוש ברוך הוא; no personal בקשה.
  2. Middle twelve — בקשות: דעת, תשובה, סליחה, גאולה, רפואה, פרנסה, and the rest. Here the יהודי asks for everything he needs.
  3. Last three — הודאה: עבודה, מודים, שלום. Taking leave of the רבונו של עולם after the audience.

משה רבינו as the Model

The גמרא grounds this in משה רבינו at the opening of פרשת ואתחנן.[2] משה wished to enter ארץ ישראל. He did not begin by asking. He opened with praise:

ה' אלקים, אתה החילות להראות את עבדך את גדלך ואת ידך החזקה, אשר מי א-ל בשמים ובארץ אשר יעשה כמעשיך וכגבורותיך.

Then he requested entry to ארץ ישראל: "אעברה נא." First שבח, then בקשה. משל למה הדבר דומה — רבי חנינא's formulation in the גמרא: a servant approaching his master.

III. Is This Not חנופה?

This raises a sharp question. If you need a favor — a hundred dollars or a million — you do not open by saying, "You are amazing, you are a great בעל צדקה, you have such fine מידות!" and then ask for the money. That is חנופה. So how can we do precisely this with הקדוש ברוך הוא?

Two תירוצים, complementary rather than competing.

The מבי"ט — בית אלקים

The מבי"ט — רבי משה בן יוסף טראני — addresses this in שער התפילה of ספר בית אלקים.[3]

The מבי"ט was one of the great גאוני צפת in the golden age — contemporary with the אלשיך הקדוש, the בית יוסף, the רמ"ק, and the בעל החרדים. Born in Salonika, he came to ארץ ישראל at eighteen, learned by the מהריט"ץ (the בית יוסף's רבי), studied בחברותא with the בית יוסף himself, and received סמיכה at twenty-five. He served as דיין in the בית דין of the בית יוסף for fifty-four years — and his מצבה in צפת records exactly that: נ"ד years, the גימטריא of דיין itself.

The מבי"ט's answer: there is a fundamental difference between flattering a human being and praising הקדוש ברוך הוא. Human flattery is for the recipient's benefit — and that is חנופה. הקדוש ברוך הוא needs nothing; He is שלם בכל מיני שלמות ולא חסר שום דבר.

So who needs the praise? You do. Before asking הקדוש ברוך הוא for פרנסה, רפואה, שידוכים, you need to internalize that He alone can grant any of it. The first three ברכות work on you: they install in you the recognition that He is מחיה מתים, that He is הקדוש, that everything depends on Him. Only after that recognition is the בקשה properly framed.

The תרומת הדשן — Bypassing the מקטרגים

The תרומת הדשן, in his הקדמה, gives a kabbalistic answer.[4]

The תרומת הדשן here is רבי יהודה לייב הלר's elder brother — author of the קצות החושן's companion volume of the same name.[5] (The two brothers, both גאוני עולם in the 1700s.) The well-known story: when these ספרים were brought to the גר"א for הסכמה, he initially refused — "This is פלפול; you are wasting paper." The תלמידים left the ספר with him and went home. He stayed up the entire night, learning page by page, and concluded: "This is the future of כלל ישראל; this תורה belongs in every בית מדרש." By morning, he sent for them and gave the most beautiful הסכמה. The Rebbe's דרך: read the הקדמות of every ספר — that is where the חיבה, the era, and the personality of the מחבר live. Sometimes all I know is the הקדמה.

The תרומת הדשן writes: do you really think you can stand before הקדוש ברוך הוא and ask for whatever you wish? What about the לשון הרע spoken on the way to shul? The עברה from last night for which no תשובה was done? Each עברה generates a מקטרג and a משטין — spiritual prosecutors who block the תפילה from ascending. This is not metaphor; the זוהר הקדוש[6] describes it directly. We daven, but the תפילה never lifts off.

The Baal Shem Tov once entered a בית מדרש — said to be in Apt — and remarked: "This בית מדרש is full of תפילה!" The קהילה swelled with pride. The Baal Shem Tov clarified: "I mean the תפילה never left. It is full of תפילה because it is full of talking during davening, full of עברות; the תפילות have no wings, so they remain in the room."

The mechanism of the first three ברכות, on this reading, is to bypass the מקטרגים. Praise of הקדוש ברוך הוא opens a pathway the prosecutors cannot block. Once that door is open, the personal בקשות can ascend behind it.

IV. נחום המדי — A Rare Rejected תנא

One of the foundational sources for this very סדר — first praise, then request — comes from a strange figure in ש"ס: נחום המדי, a תנא from מודי (a מלוכה near the border of Turkey and Iraq).

Almost everything נחום המדי said was rejected.[7] The גמרא uses an unprecedented לשון: "תשתכח דברי מעיה" — let his words be forgotten. Once בר מניומי (also from מודי) approached אביי with a complaint: "It is not fair! No one from מודי gets credit; נחום המדי is a great תנא and you reject everything he says." אביי answered that there was one הלכה they did פסקען like him; בר מניומי retorted: "That's only because others say it too."

This reflects an old tension between חכמי ארץ ישראל and חכמי חוץ לארץ. It is also the kind of treatment otherwise reserved, in ש"ס, for רבי אליעזר בן הורקנוס after the תנור של עכנאי.[8]

נחום איש גמזו?

Some ספרים identify נחום המדי with נחום איש גמזו — the רבי of רבי עקיבא, who said about everything that happened to him "גם זו לטובה". רבי חננאל and others in תענית[9] note that there is also a city in ארץ ישראל called גמזו (correctly pronounced Gimzo; today there is a מושב גמזו between לוד and מודיעין) — so the title may be both: "the man from גמזו" and "גם זו לטובה". The Rebbe: if the identification holds, picture נחום המדי hearing the חכמים reject everything he says and replying "גם זו לטובה". We are glad he had that מדה.

The One הלכה We פסקען Like Him

The one הלכה the גמרא retains from נחום המדי is precisely the structural rule we have been discussing: first שבח, then בקשה. There was a מחלוקת תנאים; some held the opposite. The הלכה follows נחום המדי — partly because the חכמים agreed with him on this point, partly (the גמרא's framing) because of משה רבינו's precedent in ואתחנן.

V. Personal בקשות — Where They Belong

If one wants to add personal בקשות beyond the נוסח of the אנשי כנסת הגדולה, where do they go?

  • Inside שמע קולנו — the general ברכה of acceptance, where סידורים note one may insert personal needs. Even if one already said סלח לנו, one may add a specific בקשת מחילה here.
  • After שמונה עשרה, before stepping back — the גמרא permits one to stand for as long as one wishes after the formal ברכות, davening for whatever one needs. Even before the three steps back, the תפילה is already complete; personal בקשות at length are appropriate.

The Rebbe's standing puzzle: there is no explicit ברכה in שמונה עשרה for יידישע קינדלעך — the most important בקשה a father or grandfather has. Some read "לדור ודור" as covering it, but it is not explicit. There are long פירושים on this; the practical answer is to add it in שמע קולנו.

VI. The קושיא of "זכרנו לחיים"

רב האי גאון (born 938, lived to age 100; son of רב שרירא גאון; the most prominent גאון of his era) raised the obvious question: in עשרת ימי תשובה we add "זכרנו לחיים" right inside the first three ברכות. Is this not a personal בקשה?

His answer: in his ישיבה at פומבדיתא some did not say it; להלכה, however, we do say it because it is a צורך for the ציבור rather than a private request. The טור in סימן תרי"ג records the discussion in full.[10]

The חסידים add a פשט: it is also חיים — but which חיים? חיים for His sake, not personal pleasure. On this reading the בקשה is itself a form of שבח: we want life as a vehicle for עבודת ה'.

VII. ברכת אבות — The Missing מלכות

חז"ל teach: "כל ברכה שאין בה מלכות אינה ברכה" — every ברכה must contain "מלך העולם". Yet the most consequential ברכה in שמונה עשרה, ברכת אבות, has no "מלך העולם". Where is the מלכות?

תוספות in ברכות מ' ע"א

תוספות[11] answer: when we say "ברוך אתה ה' אלקינו ואלקי אבותינו, אלקי אברהם, אלקי יצחק, ואלקי יעקב," the very mention of אברהם אבינו functions as the מלכות. אברהם אבינו was the one who revealed the מלכות of הקדוש ברוך הוא to the world. This is unique to ברכת אבות; in no other ברכה may one substitute for "מלך".

An Alternate תירוץ — דוד המלך

Other ראשונים read the מלכות later in the ברכה: at "מגן דוד" (in נוסח) or in the surrounding לשון. דוד המלך, after all, was the מלך; the גמרא records that דוד wished to be mentioned in ברכת אבות, and that wish is honored.

VIII. כוונה — The כלי and the נשמה

Every word of שמונה עשרה is a gem. But the זוהר הקדוש teaches that the words are a גוף — a כלי — and the כוונה is the נשמה. A magnificent crystal vase without flowers is empty; a beautiful בכר without wine is hollow. The words composed by the אנשי כנסת הגדולה ask to be filled with understanding of what we are saying.

The יערות דבש on חזנות

The יערות דבש (רבי יונתן אייבשיץ),[12] in his collection of דרשות from Prague, raises a sharp question. The גמרא asks why הלל is not recited on ראש השנה and answers that even the מלאכי השרת do not say שירה on the ימים נוראים — ספרי חיים וספרי מתים פתוחים לפני ה'. Yet the great synagogues of Prague featured elaborate choirs and חזנים precisely on the ימים נוראים. How? If מלאכים refrain from שירה, do we sing more elaborately than they?

The יערות דבש critiques חזנים who perform without כוונה and heart — the empty vessel — but concludes: when חזנות elevates the כוונה of the קהל, it is good. Today even by the great רבנים, choirs are accepted on this condition. The point is not to come away saying "the choir was beautiful today," but to come away having actually davened.

IX. אלקי אברהם, אלקי יצחק, ואלקי יעקב — Why Three Times?

Why repeat "אלקי" before each אב? Why not "אלקי אברהם יצחק ויעקב"?

"זה א-לי ואנוהו, אלקי אבי וארוממנהו"

The פסוק in שמות ט"ו:ב[13] sets the framework. There are two ingredients in a Jew's relationship with הקדוש ברוך הוא:

  1. "אלקי אבי" — the מסורה of faith from father and grandfather, traceable to אברהם, יצחק, and יעקב.
  2. "זה א-לי" — but first and foremost, each person must forge his own personal connection: this is my אלוקים. Not because father believed; because I, here, do.

So we say אלקי אברהם, אלקי יצחק, אלקי יעקב — separately. Each אב built his own personal connection to הקדוש ברוך הוא, distinct from the others'. אברהם's חסד, יצחק's גבורה, יעקב's תפארת — three different relationships, three separate "אלקי"s. Standing in שמונה עשרה, we are saying: I, too, am building my own relationship with הקדוש ברוך הוא — He is mine, in my way.

X. The גמרא in בבא מציעא — A Deeper Reason

There is a deeper reason we say the three names separately, found in בבא מציעא פ"ה ע"ב[14] — one of the most striking passages in ש"ס.

The מעשה with אליהו הנביא

אליהו הנביא used to come daily to learn with רבינו הקדוש. One day he came late. רבי asked why.

"I was busy waking up the אבות הקדושים."

"Why do you need to wake them?"

"They need to daven; I must wake them."

"Wake all three at once," רבי suggested. "Let them daven, put them back to sleep, and come learn with me."

"No," אליהו answered. "I wake אברהם, wash his hands, he davens, I put him back to sleep. Then יצחק. Then יעקב."

"Why?"

The Power of Their Combined תפילה

The גמרא's answer: if the three אבות הקדושים davened together, simultaneously, in one מנין, their combined תפילה would be so powerful that משיח would arrive before his time. The world would be shaken to its foundations; the natural order would break.

רבי חייא and His Sons

רבינו הקדוש was astounded. "Is there anyone alive today whose תפילה together would have such power?"

אליהו answered: "רבי חייא and his sons."

רבי summoned רבי חייא, his תלמיד, and instructed him: "Daven together with your sons." רבי חייא: "I daven with them every day — I at שחרית, they at seven, in the same shul." רבי: "No — together, simultaneously, in the same מנין."

They obeyed. The גמרא records what happened:

  • At "משיב הרוח" — the wind began to fell trees.
  • At "מחיה מתים" — the graves began to shake; the dead were preparing to rise.

The מלאכים in שמים intervened: this cannot be — משיח will arrive premature. They asked: who revealed this secret? רבי: אליהו הנביא told me.

The גמרא: אליהו הנביא received sixty lashes from a פולסא דנורא — a sword of fire — for revealing the secret. From that day on, רבי חייא and his sons never davened in the same מנין again.

The Rebbe's wry application: if your child ever says "טאטי, I don't want to daven in the same מנין as you," consider it a קומפלימענט. Daven before him, in an earlier מנין.

This is the deeper reason we say "אלקי אברהם", then "אלקי יצחק", then "אלקי יעקב" — separately. Naming them together would invoke their combined כוח תפילה, and the world is not yet ready.

XI. "הפני מזרח מאירין עד חברון"

The משנה in יומא[15] describes how the בית המקדש determined when to begin the קרבן תמיד של שחר: the watchman would call out, "הפני מזרח מאירין עד חברון?" — does the eastern light reach as far as חברון? If yes, the עבודה began.

The גמרא asks: why specifically חברון? Why not just "is the east bright"? The answer: because the daily עבודה opens with זכירת אבות. The אבות הקדושים are buried in חברון; one mention of the place activates their זכות, and the עבודה ascends ברצון.

שמונה עשרה stands כנגד the קרבן תמיד. So שמונה עשרה too begins with the אבות — exactly as the עבודה began with חברון. The סאדיגורער רבי[16] taught: to be מפעיל לטובה in שמים, one need not say a צדיק's entire name — just "בערדיטשעב" alone reminds הקדוש ברוך הוא of the זכות of the רב of Berditchev. So too the single word "חברון"; so too the opening "אלקי אברהם".

XII. Synthesis

The opening of שמונה עשרה is the gateway. The first three ברכות are not flattery — they are the תיקון of the דעת of the מתפלל (מבי"ט) and the bypass past the מקטרגים (תרומת הדשן). They are anchored in משה רבינו's precedent in ואתחנן and codified through the lone accepted פסק of נחום המדי.

Within them, ברכת אבות opens by invoking each אב separately: to model that each יהודי forge his own "זה א-לי" while standing on the inheritance of "אלקי אבי"; and to keep the world standing, since the combined כוח of אברהם, יצחק, ויעקב in one breath — like רבי חייא and his sons — would tear open the natural order before its time. We open as the עבודה in the בית המקדש opened: with חברון, with the אבות, with the זכות that lifts everything that follows. בעזרת השם, next week we continue into the body of the ברכה.

  11. Tosfos, Berachos 40a, s.v. אמר רב. — On the principle כל ברכה שאין בה מלכות אינה ברכה and the absence of explicit מלך העולם in ברכת אבות. Tosfos' answer: the mention of אברהם אבינו functions as the מלכות, since he was the one who revealed the מלכות שמים to the world. Unique to ברכת אבות; not a general substitute for מלך. ↩

Yearos Devash (R' Yonasan Eybeschütz) — דרשות from Prague (general reference; the דרשה on חזנות on the ימים נוראים). — The sharp קושיא on elaborate חזנות on the ימים נוראים: even the מלאכי השרת do not say שירה with ספרי חיים וספרי מתים פתוחים. The resolution: when חזנות elevates the כוונה of the קהל, it serves תפילה; when it is performance, it is empty vessel. ↩
  13. שמות ט"ו:ב — זה א-לי ואנוהו אלקי אבי וארוממנהו. — The dual structure of אמונה: אלקי אבי (the inherited מסורה) and זה א-לי (the personally forged connection). Underwrites the separate אלקי אברהם, אלקי יצחק, אלקי יעקב: each אב built his own personal connection, distinct from the others'. ↩
  14. Bava Metzia 85b. — אליהו הנביא waking the אבות separately rather than together; the explanation that combined תפילה would bring משיח before his time; רבי instructing רבי חייא and his sons to daven together; the wind felling trees at משיב הרוח; the graves shaking at מחיה מתים; אליהו הנביא's sixty lashes from a פולסא דנורא. The deep reason for the separate אלקי for each אב: the world cannot yet bear their combined כוח תפילה. ↩
  15. Mishnah Yoma 3:1; cf. Gemara ad loc. on הפני מזרח מאירין עד חברון. — The משנה on the watchman's call to begin the תמיד של שחר: specifically חברון (where the אבות are buried) rather than a generic "is the east bright?" The עבודה opens with זכירת אבות; שמונה עשרה mirrors that opening because it stands כנגד the תמיד. ↩
  16. Source-type note (Chassidic): Sadigura Rebbe (Ruzhin dynasty) — the teaching that single-word invocation of a צדיק's name (בערדיטשעב) suffices to activate that צדיק's זכות in שמים. Preserved as oral Chassidic tradition; the principle parallels the single-word חברון in the תמיד opening and אלקי אברהם at the head of שמונה עשרה.
