תפילת נחם and מודים

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded 27 July 2025 (one week before תשעה באב)  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

A two-part שיעור. תפילת נחם as the once-a-year ברכה with a unique חתימה — sourced only in the ירושלמי, with רב מונא's structural rule (past in מודים, future in רצה); the רי"ף's placement in ברכת ירושלים; the question of why we say it only at מנחה; the תאריכי החורבן across the seventh through tenth of אב with רבי יוחנן's שיטה. Then ברכת מודים — bowing all the way down, the גמרא in בבא קמא on the spine and the snake, the meaning of מודה as acknowledge, and a profound reading that the missing ברכה for children is hidden in "לדור ודור".

לעילוי נשמת רבינו ר' נחמן מנדל בן ר' נחמן מנדל הלוי זצ"ל.

I. ברכות Said Only Once a Year

An opening riddle. Which ברכות do we say only once a year? שהחיינו on ליל יום הכפורים; "על אכילת מצה"; "על ביעור חמץ"; "על מקרא מגילה" (per certain opinions); "טבילת יום הכפורים" per רב סעדיה גאון. There is one more — the חתימה of תפילת נחם at מנחה תשעה באב: "ברוך אתה ה' מנחם ציון ובונה ירושלים".

The דוב Licking Honey

The famous מעשה: רבי יונתן אייבשיץ[16] was wagered by a nobleman that no one could teach a bear to daven. רבי יונתן אייבשיץ placed honey on every page of a סידור; the bear learned to open the סידור and turn the pages, just as many יהודים mumble through תפילה with no כוונה.

The נמשל: when we daven שמונה עשרה daily, we can fall into the bear's posture — words turning over without engagement. On מנחה תשעה באב, the סידור opens and there is a ברכה we have not said all year — נחם. Suddenly we wake up. We start thinking about what we are saying. The whole year is implicitly contained in that moment of attention.

II. The Source of תפילת נחם

Only in the ירושלמי

תפילת נחם does not appear in the בבלי. Only in the ירושלמי[1]. רב סעדיה גאון[2] writes: even though in בבל they did not recite it, since the ירושלמי records it and בני ארץ ישראל say it, "there is no loss in saying it." Not framed as obligatory; framed as available. The שולחן ערוך rules that we say it.

The Principle of מעין המאורע

The ירושלמי establishes a structural rule: on every special day, we add מעין המאורע to שמונה עשרה — content tied to the day's events. חנוכה and פורים: על הניסים. תשעה באב: נחם.

רב מונא's Rule

רב מונא, an אמורא who served as ראש ישיבה in ציפורי, was asked by his תלמיד רב אבדימי דמן ציפורי in which ברכה of שמונה עשרה we insert נחם. The expected answer would be ולירושלים עירך. רב מונא instead gave a structural rule:

Anything speaking about the past — add in מודים.
Anything speaking about the future — add in רצה.

This explains:

  • על הניסים (חנוכה, פורים) speaks of past ניסים — מודים.
  • יעלה ויבוא speaks of future remembrance and משיח — רצה.

Where Does נחם Belong?

נחם mainly speaks of the future — we ask הקדוש ברוך הוא to console us, rebuild the בית המקדש, restore ירושלים. By רב מונא's rule it should go in רצה. But it also speaks of the past חורבן. A מחלוקת ראשונים: past or future? רצה or מודים?

Our פסק — לירושלים עירך

We follow neither side of the ירושלמי's rule. The רי"ף[4]: we place נחם in ברכת ולירושלים עירך, on a different basis — the הלכה in עבודה זרה ח'[3] that one may add מעין ענין הברכה in any ברכה. נחם speaks about ירושלים, so it belongs in ברכת ירושלים.

III. If You Forgot נחם

An entire סימן in שולחן ערוך is dedicated to this ברכה — סימן תקנ"ז, הלכות תשעה באב[6].

If You Forgot in ירושלים

By the ירושלמי's rule (future → רצה) one would return and say it in רצה. But the טור and the ט"ז[5]: say it in שמע קולנו — anything can be added there.

Critical detail: when said in שמע קולנו, do not recite the special חתימה "ברוך אתה ה' מנחם ציון ובונה ירושלים". Conclude as usual: "ברוך אתה ה' שומע תפילה". You forfeit the once-a-year חתימה.

If You Forgot Entirely

If you finished שמונה עשרה and forgot נחם completely — you do not repeat. The כלל: an addition to שמונה עשרה for the day's מאורע, where the day does not have a קרבן מוסף — forgetting does not require repetition.

  • יעלה ויבוא on ראש חודש: there is a מוסף — repeat.
  • נחם on תשעה באב: there is no מוסף — do not repeat.

IV. Do Not Alter the נוסח

The ברכה describes ירושלים as destroyed and degraded. A certain רב in ארץ ישראל proposed changing the נוסח: "What does 'ירושלים חרבה' mean? Today ירושלים is built and magnificent! There is no בית המקדש, but the city itself is not destroyed." He suggested rephrasing toward praise: "ברוך ה' — we see the great change, just give us the בית המקדש."

חס ושלום. It is forbidden to deviate from the מסורת. ירושלים is still destroyed, still degraded, still desolate. Without מלכות בית דוד, without the בית המקדש, without קרבנות, without the כהנים at their stations בשירה ובזמרה — it is destroyed. We are a nation that wakes daily anticipating חזון הנחמה, and until that day, despite ברוך השם the יישוב in ארץ ישראל, the תורה and מצוות being kept there in great quantity, ירושלים remains in the language of חורבן. חס ושלום to alter the נוסח.

"באש שרפתו ובאש אתה עתיד לבנותו"

The ברכה closes: "כי אתה ה' באש שרפתו, ובאש אתה עתיד לבנותו". הקדוש ברוך הוא burned the בית המקדש — through the גוים, with fire. הקדוש ברוך הוא will rebuild it with fire — the בית המקדש השלישי descends from שמים in fire, as the ראשונים and אחרונים say.

An אחרונים debate: there is a מצות עשה of "בנין בית הבחירה". If the בית המקדש השלישי descends from שמים, how do we discharge the מצוה? Some say we will do the גימורים — the gates, the finishing work, the השלמה.

V. Why Only at מנחה?

A real question. We say על הניסים at every תפילה on חנוכה and פורים. We say יעלה ויבוא at שחרית, מנחה, and מעריב. Why only נחם at מנחה?

The רא"ש

The רא"ש writes that he never understood the מנהג. It is illogical. He said it should be recited at every תפילה of תשעה באב.

The מחבר and the רמ"א

The מחבר in שולחן ערוך rules that one says it at every תפילה; many ספרדים follow. The רמ"א rules: only at מנחה.

The רמ"א's Reason

The רמ"א, drawing on the בית יוסף and the ראשונים: the burning of the בית המקדש began only at the close of the day of תשעה באב — late afternoon, into the night. Therefore נחם belongs only at the close of the day. Earlier in the day there is no נחמה yet — the בית המקדש is, so to speak, still burning.

VI. The Dates of the חורבן

An interesting fact: "תשעה באב" is not explicit in תנ"ך as the date of the חורבן.

Two פסוקים in תנ"ך

  • ירמיהו[7]: נבוכדנצר burned the בית המקדש, the king's palace, and ירושלים on the tenth of אב.
  • ספר מלכים: a similar but not identical פסוק records the seventh of אב.

Neither says the ninth. We fast on the ninth. What is going on?

The קראים

The קראים — who reject חז"ל and follow only the text of תנ"ך — fasted on both. Seventh and tenth, as the פסוקים say. We follow חז"ל; חז"ל resolved the contradiction.

The Process — תענית כ"ט

חז"ל in תענית כ"ט[8]: there was a process. נבוזראדן שר הטבחים (ימ"ש) entered the בית המקדש on the seventh of אב, as מלכים records. He came with all the גוים; they desecrated the בית המקדש: "אוכלין ושותין ומקרקרין" — the Hebrew word for partying. On the seventh, eighth, and ninth days they had not yet set fire. Only on the ninth, after midday — toward sunset — they ignited the fire. It continued to burn into the tenth.

This explains the parallel הלכה we keep: no meat or wine from Sunday night through midday Monday — like the way we treat the day after תשעה באב. Some opinions held one should fast on the tenth as well; we no longer do.

One הלכה: after midday on תשעה באב, one may sit on a chair. The Rebbe shares a family joke: when his younger brother was a small child, he came home from גן or first grade convinced that after midday תשעה באב was over — not just sitting on chairs, but eating and drinking. He put on shoes and started a meal. To this day the family teases him.

The basis of the קולא after midday: there is a מסורה that משיח was born on תשעה באב. The מדרש: בן דוד משיח was born — whatever that means precisely. Since משיח was born around midday, half the day already carries נחמה.

VII. רבי יוחנן's שיטה — Why Not the Tenth?

A striking גמרא: רבי יוחנן[9] said: "Had I lived in the time of the חורבן, I would not have established the ninth as the fast day. I would have established the tenth." Because the primary burning was on the tenth.

חז"ל: we do not agree with רבי יוחנן. We mark the day of the "תחילת הפורענות" — the beginning of the punishment, when the fire was set. That is the ninth.

A צירוף for the למדנים — בבא קמא כ"ב—כ"ג

A famous מחלוקת in בבא קמא[10]: the תורה says "כי תצא אש" — when fire goes out and damages, you pay. Why? משום ממונו (because it is your property, like your animal damaging) or משום חיציו (because it is your arrow — your action)?

רבי יוחנן לשיטתו: משום חיציו. The fire is like an arrow you shot; the damage at the destination is your action. If so, when נבוזראדן ignited the fire on the ninth, he kept shooting arrows; the primary damage on the tenth is also part of his action. רבי יוחנן's שיטה is internally consistent — the severity of the arrows landed on the tenth, so the fast should mark the tenth. We hold תחילת הפורענות is the primary moment; the ninth.

VIII. The נוסח of נחם Itself

When recited with the חתימה, the ברכה is enough to bring tears.

ירושלים כאלמנה, כאשה עקרה שלא ילדה — and the גוים destroyed it; "על כן ציון במר תבכה".

(Shlomo Simchi[15] has a beautiful ניגון on these words.) "לפי ליבי על חללי" — let my heart break over the חללים — because הקדוש ברוך הוא ignited it; "ובו אש אתה עתיד לבנותה" — He will rebuild with fire.

Even though all this is destruction, the first word of the ברכה is נחם — now is the time for נחמה. The time for משיח to be born. Even as the בית המקדש burns, we already have נחמה because we believe the נחמה will come.

IX. ברכת מודים

Returning to our regular סדר. We have reached מודים אנחנו לך.

The חיוב of Bowing

At מודים, the הלכה requires bowing — at the פתיחה ("מודים") and at the חתימה ("ברוך אתה ה'"). The four required bows in שמונה עשרה: opening and closing of ברכת אבות; opening and closing of מודים.

The גמרא in בבא קמא

A frightening גמרא[11]: one who does not bow at מודים — after seven years his spine turns into a snake. What does this mean?

תוספות[12] takes it up. There is a small bone in the spine — the עצם הלוז — that, per חז"ל, never decomposes. (We devoted an entire שיעור to it; doctors debate which bone exactly.) From this bone, הקדוש ברוך הוא reconstructs the body for תחיית המתים. תוספות proposes — and ultimately rejects as too harsh — that one who does not bow at מודים sees that bone turn into a snake; if it does, no תחיית המתים. Too harsh; תוספות concludes that there will be some lesser punishment, with snake-like characteristics.

Why a Snake?

Because the נחש was the original כפוי טובה. הקדוש ברוך הוא created the נחש as the most clever creature; the נחש betrayed Him by leading אדם and חוה to the עץ הדעת. We are still trying to repair that חטא; חז"ל say every soul was present at it. The original כפוי טובה was the נחש. One who cannot bring himself to bow at מודים — who cannot acknowledge the טובה being given to him at every breath — turns into the נחש.

The Halachic Posture

The הלכה[14]: bow all the way down — "עד שיתפקקו כל חוליות שבשדרה". Stand up at "ה'" (in "שם ה' אלקינו") and likewise at חתימה — "ברוך אתה" bowing, "ה'" standing erect. The bow is the body's expression of total acknowledgment: "כל עצמותי תאמרנה"[13]. Not just the mouth thanking, but the body itself in the act of acknowledgment.

למעשה — those who have back problems must be careful. The פוסקים address it. The Rebbe himself sometimes has back trouble; bending too far hurts afterward. Use judgment; the הלכה is לכתחילה; we are not asking anyone to injure himself. But if you can do it, do it the right way every day.

X. The Meaning of "מודים"

Simply: "thanks." In משנה and גמרא, however, "מודה" means "acknowledges, agrees, admits." "מודים חכמים לרבי" — the חכמים agree.

The two senses fold together. The essence of תודה is acknowledgment — the recognition that הקדוש ברוך הוא gives us life every moment, protects us every moment. That recognition obligates the thanks. So "מודים" simultaneously: we acknowledge that You are God and that You sustain us — and we therefore thank You.

XI. "לדור ודור" — The Hidden תפילה for Children

The Missing ברכה

The Rebbe noted previously that שמונה עשרה contains no explicit ברכה for children. The most important thing in our lives, the continuity of our family, the נחת of grandchildren in shul — and there is no formal ברכה? We add it in שמע קולנו in our own words. But why no formal ברכה?

One אחרון: the ברכה for children is hidden in "לדור ודור" here in מודים.

The Rebbe: if you are בחורים, daven now for your future children. Why wait? You can daven now for a good שידוך and for good children at the same moment. Why not?

Personal: right before the Rebbe came back to America after three years in ארץ ישראל — knowing it was over, the freezer in Lakewood awaited, the שידוכים process, all the rest — he asked himself how to say goodbye to ארץ ישראל. He went to מירון. He spent the whole night by רבי שמעון בר יוחאי. He started davening for an easy שידוך — the freezer is unpleasant; he ended up going through it, so apparently the כוונה wasn't enough. But that night he asked himself, "Why stop here? Who knows the next time I'll be in מירון?" He started davening for good children. He ended up davening more for good children than for the שידוך itself.

You are allowed to daven for good children before you are married. Every יהודי should daven that his children, his grandchildren, his great-grandchildren be good יהודים.

The Three-Year-Old's ברכות

The Rebbe was preparing a פרקי אבות שיעור upstairs. The other children were away in camp. Only one was home — the youngest, a three-year-old just past his אפשערניש, beginning to learn ברכות. He played with magna-tiles on the floor and started saying — not eating, just saying — "ברוך אתה ה' אלקינו מלך העולם..." running through "שהכל", "מזונות", "האדמה", going through every ברכה without eating anything.

It hurt to hear שם השם in vain — but the Rebbe decided not to shush him. Let him practice. It is חינוך. When a child practices, it is not ברכה לבטלה; it is the ברכה in its highest form. There is no greater קיום for a ברכה than the practice of a child learning to say it.

The Rebbe sat upstairs hearing him, and the words of "לדור ודור נודה לך" rose in him: we are זוכה that our children — small children, in this generation, exposed to a world filled with everything in the palm of their hand — still come to shul, still say a ברכה, still recognize הקדוש ברוך הוא. We should dance.

Have כוונה at "לדור ודור": even though there is no explicit ברכה for children, the תפילה is held in those words. דורות continuing; נחת from them; the chain unbroken.

XII. Closing

From תפילת נחם at מנחה תשעה באב, where the year's accumulated mourning rises in a single חתימה; through מודים, where the body bends to acknowledge what the mind already knows; into "לדור ודור", where every יהודי davens for the children he has and the children he has not yet had — the entire arc of תפילה is the same arc: חורבן answered by נחמה; כפוי טובה answered by הכרת הטוב; broken generation answered by faithful generation. May we be זוכה to all of it. בעזרת השם, may we hear בשורות טובות very soon.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. תלמוד ירושלמי ברכות פ"ד ה"ג — source of תפילת נחם; רב מונא's rule of past in מודים and future in רצה. — The ירושלמי in ברכות records both the תקנה of saying נחם on תשעה באב (absent in the בבלי) and the structural rule transmitted by רב מונא to רב אבדימי דמן ציפורי: any מעין המאורע insertion that addresses past events belongs in ברכת מודים; one that addresses future events belongs in ברכת רצה. The rule is the structural ground for the placement of על הניסים (past — מודים) and יעלה ויבוא (future — רצה); נחם straddles past and future and is the principal exception, ultimately placed by the רי"ף in ברכת ירושלים on a different basis. ↩
  2. סידור רב סעדיה גאון — saying נחם despite the בבלי's silence. — רב סעדיה גאון (882–942, head of the ישיבה of סורא), in his סידור, addresses the absence of נחם from the בבלי: even though it does not appear in the Babylonian מסורה, the ירושלמי records it and the בני ארץ ישראל recite it; the practice has spread; "אין הפסד באמירתו". The פסק ultimately enters the שולחן ערוך as a binding מנהג. ↩
  3. גמרא עבודה זרה ז' ע"ב — ח' ע"א — adding מעין ענין הברכה in any ברכה. — The סוגיא in עבודה זרה establishes the principle that one may add personal בקשות within any ברכה of שמונה עשרה when the addition is מעין ענין הברכה — substantively within its theme. The principle is the structural ground for the רי"ף's placement of נחם in ברכת ולירושלים עירך: although נחם does not perfectly fit the ירושלמי's past/future rule, its content is מעין הברכה of ירושלים and therefore belongs there. ↩
  4. רי"ף, תענית — placing נחם in ברכת ולירושלים עירך. — רבינו יצחק אלפסי (the רי"ף, 1013–1103, North Africa and Spain), in his halachic compilation on תענית, codifies the placement of נחם in ברכת ירושלים. The רי"ף's פסק overrides the ירושלמי's past/future structural rule on the basis of the סוגיא in עבודה זרה permitting מעין ענין הברכה placement. The פסק is followed by the טור, the שולחן ערוך, and standard סידורים. ↩
  5. טור and ט"ז on אורח חיים סימן תקנ"ז — saying נחם in שמע קולנו if forgotten in ירושלים; not reciting the unique חתימה. — The טור in סימן תקנ"ז rules that one who forgot נחם in ברכת ירושלים says it in שמע קולנו rather than returning to ירושלים. The ט"ז there sharpens: when said in שמע קולנו, the special חתימה "מנחם ציון ובונה ירושלים" is not recited — one closes with the standard "שומע תפילה". The unique once-a-year חתימה is forfeited when נחם is recited outside its proper place. ↩
  6. שולחן ערוך אורח חיים סימן תקנ"ז — הלכות תפילת נחם; רמ"א on saying נחם only at מנחה. — The שולחן ערוך in סימן תקנ"ז codifies the laws of תפילת נחם: placement, חתימה, what to do when forgotten. The מחבר rules that נחם is said at all three תפילות of תשעה באב, in line with ספרדי מנהג. The רמ"א rules: אשכנז מנהג is to say נחם only at מנחה, on the basis that the בית המקדש began burning only late in the day. משנה ברורה there develops the practical applications, including the דין that one who forgot does not repeat (no מוסף on תשעה באב). ↩
  7. ירמיהו נ"ב:י"ב-י"ג — tenth of אב; מלכים ב' כ"ה:ח'-ט' — seventh of אב. — Two פסוקים in תנ"ך record the חורבן's date with apparent contradiction. ירמיהו (in his closing פרק, the historical narrative paralleling מלכים ב') gives the tenth of אב; מלכים ב' gives the seventh. Neither gives the ninth, which is our fast date. חז"ל resolve the apparent contradiction in תענית כ"ט: the seventh marks נבוזראדן's entry into the בית המקדש; the ninth marks the setting of the fire (late in the day); the tenth marks the height of the burning. The קראים, who reject חז"ל's harmonization, fasted on both the seventh and the tenth. ↩
  8. גמרא תענית כ"ט ע"א — the process from the seventh through the tenth. — The סוגיא in תענית reconstructs the unfolding of the חורבן across four days: on the seventh of אב, נבוזראדן entered the בית המקדש; on the seventh, eighth, and ninth, the גוים desecrated the precincts ("אוכלין ושותין ומקרקרין") without yet setting fire; on the ninth toward sunset they kindled the fire; on the tenth the fire burned at its peak. The סוגיא grounds both the placement of the fast on the ninth ("תחילת הפורענות") and the parallel מנהג of restraint extending into the tenth (no meat or wine until midday). ↩
  9. גמרא תענית כ"ט ע"א — רבי יוחנן's שיטה that the fast should have been the tenth. — In the same סוגיא, רבי יוחנן states: "אילמלי הייתי באותו הדור, לא קבעתיו אלא בעשירי, מפני שרובו של היכל בו נשרף" — had I been in that generation, I would have established the fast on the tenth, since the majority of the היכל's burning occurred then. חז"ל reject this view: the fast marks תחילת הפורענות (the beginning of the punishment) — the moment the fire was set — not the moment of greatest damage. The שיטה aligns with רבי יוחנן's broader halachic framework, particularly his שיטה in בבא קמא on "משום חיציו" — that fire is one's arrow, with the consequences at the destination part of the original act. ↩
  10. גמרא בבא קמא כ"ב ע"א — כ"ג ע"א — "כי תצא אש"; משום ממונו versus משום חיציו; רבי יוחנן לשיטתו. — The famous סוגיא in פרק שני of בבא קמא on אבות נזיקין: רבי יוחנן and ריש לקיש dispute the underlying basis of the חיוב for damages caused by fire. ריש לקיש: משום ממונו — fire is treated like one's animal that wandered out and damaged. רבי יוחנן: משום חיציו — fire is treated like one's arrow that one shot, with the damage at impact part of the original action. The למדנים link רבי יוחנן's שיטה לשיטתו: just as fire is one's continuing arrow, so the burning of the בית המקדש across the ninth into the tenth is one continuous act whose severity peaks on the tenth — hence רבי יוחנן's שיטה on the date of the fast. ↩
  11. גמרא בבא קמא ט"ז ע"א — bowing at מודים; the warning of the spine and the snake. — The סוגיא in בבא קמא records the chilling דרשה: "מי שאינו כורע במודים, שדרתו נעשית נחש לאחר שבע שנים" — one who does not bow at מודים, his spine becomes a snake after seven years. The סוגיא establishes both the חיוב of bowing at מודים (one of the four required bows in שמונה עשרה) and the deep significance attached to the act: bowing is the body's expression of הכרת הטוב, the structural antithesis of the original כפירה of the נחש. ↩
  12. תוספות בבא קמא ט"ז ע"א — עצם הלוז and תחיית המתים; rejecting the harshest reading. — תוספות on the דרשה takes up the question of what it means concretely for the spine to "become a snake". חז"ל teach that one bone in the spine — the עצם הלוז — does not decompose and serves as the seed of תחיית המתים. תוספות proposes that the דרשה means this bone itself becomes a snake, foreclosing תחיית המתים for the offender — but rejects this reading as too harsh. He concludes that the דרשה indicates a lesser punishment with snake-like characteristics, preserving the possibility of תחיית המתים while marking the seriousness of the עברה. ↩
  13. תהלים ל"ה:י' — "כל עצמותי תאמרנה ה' מי כמוך". — דוד המלך's פסוק: "All my bones shall declare, ה', who is like You?" The פסוק grounds the halachic requirement that bowing at מודים involves the entire body — not merely a verbal acknowledgment. The bow until "שיתפקקו כל חוליות שבשדרה" — until the vertebrae loosen — is the body's structural participation in the תפילה's הכרת הטוב. ↩
  14. משנה ברורה אורח חיים סימן קי"ג — bowing all the way at מודים; allowances for those with back conditions. — The משנה ברורה on סימן קי"ג codifies the laws of bowing in שמונה עשרה: the four required bows, the depth required ("עד שיתפקקו כל חוליות שבשדרה"), the moments to stand erect ("ה'" in "שם ה' אלקינו"), the prohibition against bowing in ברכות where חז"ל did not institute it. משנה ברורה also addresses individuals with back conditions: the חיוב is לכתחילה; one is not required to injure oneself; a partial bow that signals הכנעה is acceptable when full bowing is medically inadvisable. ↩
