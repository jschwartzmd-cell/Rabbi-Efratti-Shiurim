תחנון, נפילת אפים, and the Origins of והוא רחום

From the קיצור של״ה, the Mediterranean Exiles, and the תפילה of חזקיהו המלך
Recorded י״א חשון תשפ״ו · 10 November 2025 · Reworked from the original שיעור
לעילוי נשמת הרב חיים זצ״ל ביום היארצייט שלו
ולעילוי נשמת כל נשמות עם ישראל

Last week we began discussing תחנון. This week we go deeper. We need to be clear about its different components — וידוי and שלש עשרה מידות, נפילת אפים itself, and the long Monday-and-Thursday addition of והוא רחום. Each piece has a separate origin story, and the origin stories are unusually rich.

I. Structure — וידוי, שלש עשרה מידות, then נפילת אפים

וידוי and the Thirteen Attributes After שמונה עשרה

As we explained, the וידוי and the שלש עשרה מידות we recite before נפילת אפים constitute a separate section from נפילת אפים itself. In נוסח ספרד we first say the שלש עשרה מידות, though we still begin with the וידוי. The זוהר הקדוש[1] teaches that immediately after שמונה עשרה — to prevent the מקטרגים from blocking our תפילה from ascending to כסא הכבוד — we recite וידוי. Then we say קל ארך אפים, an ancient פיוט that has always served as the introduction to the שלש עשרה מידות in סליחות and on יום כיפור. Following that, the שלש עשרה מידות של רחמים themselves.

It is crucial to grasp: this whole institution of וידוי and שלש עשרה מידות has no source in גמרא or in basic הלכה. It exists because the זוהר הקדוש instructs it, and the האריז״ל was extremely meticulous about it. He insisted that one say וידוי and the שלש עשרה מידות immediately after שמונה עשרה, and that the immediacy is essential.

II. The Source of the שלש עשרה מידות

The גמרא in ראש השנה

The שלש עשרה מידות are well known from סליחות. The famous גמרא in ראש השנה דף י״ז[2] states: after מעשה העגל, הקדוש ברוך הוא told משה רבינו, in order that I may forgive כלל ישראל, I will teach you what to say. הקדוש ברוך הוא wrapped Himself in a טלית. The גמרא says it explicitly, and adds: were it not for the fact that the גמרא states this, we would not be permitted to say so — that הקדוש ברוך הוא, כביכול, wrapped Himself in a טלית and showed משה: "This is what you should say."

The מהרש״א — A White אור

The מהרש״א[3] asks the obvious question: who wore a טלית? What does it mean that הקדוש ברוך הוא wraps Himself in a טלית? He explains based on the פסוק we recite before donning our own טלית in the morning — עוטה אור כשלמה. הקדוש ברוך הוא wraps Himself in light as in a garment. The מהרש״א says הקדוש ברוך הוא did not literally wear a physical טלית; rather there was a kind of אור, an aura, surrounding the שכינה — and within that אור He recited the שלש עשרה מידות.

The מהרש״א adds something interesting: this אור is white. I confess I do not know what "white spiritual אור" means — but I suspect this may be the source of our being so meticulous about white טליתות, careful that they are not soiled or dirty. הקדוש ברוך הוא's טלית, כביכול, is white; perhaps that is the source.

III. נפילת אפים — What Is It, Really?

Originally a רשות

Now to נפילת אפים itself. Last week we mentioned that many חסידים are not particular about נפילת אפים — they do not say תחנון whenever there is a יארצייט — and we discussed the dispute extensively. We must understand that the entire institution of נפילת אפים is not, in its origin, an obligation. It was originally only רשות.

Look through the entire ש״ס. You will find no source obligating נפילת אפים. The רמב״ש in his תשובות[4] writes outright that it is only רשות. This is the source for why many people — not only חסידים — cancel תחנון when they have a sufficient excuse. (Just how good the excuse must be is itself a wide-ranging dispute.)

Rav Heschel notes a useful parallel: תפילת ערבית was also originally רשות. Yet כלל ישראל accepted it upon themselves as חיוב. The שלחן ערוך writes that ultimately כלל ישראל accepted נפילת אפים as a חיוב too — and that is why one is obligated to say it on appropriate days. (Clearly not on ראש חודש, and not on the various other days specified in שלחן ערוך — and if you are חסיד, then never, by your מנהג.)

IV. A רמז in the Torah — "ואת מעכה"

I forgot to mention this last week, and it ties into what we read at the end of the עקדה. How does פרשת וירא conclude? When we read the עקדה on ראש השנה we add the final section. What is its connection to ראש השנה? Why not place it on the fourth day of the week? Why include it in the קריאה of ראש השנה?

The בעל שם טוב[5] teaches: every name in the תורה contains hints. The words ואת מעכה, says the בעל שם טוב, are ראשי תיבות:

וידוי אחר תפילה מגיע עד כסא הכבוד.

Worth remembering. There are hints in the תורה, as the זוהר already taught, that when you recite וידוי after שמונה עשרה, you plead before הקדוש ברוך הוא — and then you nullify yourself, you fall and say "I am unworthy of anything," you cry חטאתי, עויתי, פשעתי — and then הקדוש ברוך הוא accepts the תפילה.

V. The History of the Words of נפילת אפים

The Old נוסח

The original נפילת אפים had no uniform נוסח. Today we recite רחום וחנון חטאתי לפניך and then ה' אל באפך תוכיחני — קפיטל ו' in תהלים. But in those days, things were different. The לבוש[6] mentions a different קפיטל from תהלים altogether.

The זוהר הקדוש in fact instructs that one should recite during נפילת אפים קפיטל כ״ה in תהלים[7], where דוד surrenders himself to הקדוש ברוך הוא: לדוד אליך ה' נפשי אשא — "I am going to surrender my נפש to You."

The Danger

The זוהר warns: you may say this only with the proper כוונה. Without correct כוונה it is dangerous — dangerous that, חס ושלום, הקדוש ברוך הוא will take a person before his time, without אריכות ימים. Consequently many people, seeing this in the זוהר, said: "I will not even open this קפיטל. I will not begin." Despite its being the original source of נפילת אפים, the danger is too great. To say אליך ה' נפשי אשא without כוונה is to tell הקדוש ברוך הוא, "Take my נפש." It is supposed to be מסירת נפש; but מסירת נפש without כוונה turns into an open door.

What the ספרדים and the אשכנזים Did

The ספרדים do say קפיטל כ״ה — but without performing נפילת אפים at all. They recite the קפיטל and call it לדוד; they do not fall, because to combine the falling with that קפיטל without כוונה is regarded as too dangerous.

Among the אשכנזים, over the years, the printed סידור substituted קפיטל ו' — ה' אל באפך תוכיחני — a kind of וידוי, beseeching הקדוש ברוך הוא; you all know what קפיטל ו' says. The shift from קפיטל כ״ה to קפיטל ו' was driven precisely by this concern.

VI. Why No תחנון at מנחה?

An interesting question — why does כלל ישראל essentially not say תחנון at מנחה? Last week we mentioned the סמ״ע's sharp criticism of those who skip תחנון for every יארצייט. But at מנחה, indeed, the מנהג developed not to say it. Why?

Because it is dangerous to say תחנון at night. If you recite these things at night, the כחות הטומאה are aroused. (The ראשונים use the term מקצת בנטיעת for someone who does what he should not do at the wrong time.) Many people daven מנחה late, and by the time they finish שמונה עשרה it is already after שקיעה — at which point it is forbidden to say תחנון. Therefore the קהל simply canceled תחנון at מנחה in general; even those who daven מנחה in the middle of the day, far from שקיעה, refrain. The danger of נפילת אפים without proper כוונה is real. נפילת אפים done with proper כוונה is געוואלדיג. Done without it, dangerous.

VII. ויאמר דוד אל גד — A Strange פסוק

Has Anyone Wondered?

Has anyone among you, while saying תחנון in the morning, ever been startled by the פסוק ויאמר דוד אל גד? You say it. What is this פסוק doing here? I understand נפלה נא ביד ה' — falling. But by the way, that phrase says something different from what we are doing: נפלה נא ביד ה' means "let us fall into the hand of ה'", not "fall before ה'." We are falling before Him, not into His hand. So what is this פסוק doing in our תחנון?

The Source — שמואל ב'

The פסוק comes from the very last chapters of ספר שמואל, in שמואל ב' פרק כ״ד[8]. דוד המלך sent יואב, his general, to count כלל ישראל from north to south — a census. We do not count Jews. (I will not enter into why; it is a sugya unto itself, and a serious one. We do not count Jews.) דוד erred by counting them, and גד — the נביא in דוד's days, גד החוזה — came to him and said, "You have done a terrible thing. ה' is offering three options. Choose your punishment."

The three options:

  1. Seven years of רעב in ארץ ישראל.
  2. Three months of an enemy in pursuit, with terrible troubles, פוגרומען or whatever it might be.
  3. Three days of דבר — a plague.

דוד answered (this is our פסוק): each of the punishments is very difficult — but I prefer to fall into the hand of ה', not into the hand of man. If I choose רעב, I will be in the hand of man — the wealthy will corner the market and squeeze. If I choose three months of an enemy, that too is in the hand of man. I prefer to be punished by ה'. Let there be a דבר in כלל ישראל. And indeed, seventy thousand Jews died in those three days. We think Corona was terrible? Three days, seventy thousand. That is what the פסוק says.

What Is It Doing in תחנון?

But what is this פסוק doing in תחנון? Who placed it in our סידורים?

This is precisely where it becomes important to research the source of every תפילה. There is a serious possibility that this passage is a Sabbatean addition. I do not want to alarm you; do not stop saying it if you say it. But the trail leads, perhaps, somewhere we should be aware of.

VIII. The קיצור של״ה — A Hidden גמטריא

Who יחיאל מיכל עפשטיין Was

There was a yid named יחיאל מיכל עפשטיין. (Not the famous מיכל עפשטיין of the ערוך השלחן — there are many מיכל עפשטיינס in Jewish history.) This particular יחיאל מיכל עפשטיין lived in the early seventeenth century. He wrote a ספר called קיצור של״ה[9].

You all know the של״ה — שני לוחות הברית by רבי ישעיה הלוי הורוויץ, one of the most important ספרים on the Jewish bookshelf. Coming fifty to seventy years after the שלחן ערוך, this yid wrote an abridged version. The של״ה הקדוש is beautiful but very long; an abbreviated version was a useful thing to have.

Open the קיצור של״ה carefully, however, and you find many things the original של״ה does not even mention. That anomaly did not escape the eye of one of Jewish history's most relentless defenders of orthodoxy.

רבי יעקב עמדן and תורת הקנאות

רבי יעקב עמדן[10] — the great warrior against the surviving Sabbateans, the followers of שבתי צבי in the seventeenth century — wrote an entire ספר called תורת הקנאות documenting שבתי צבי and his תלמידים, demonstrating how their influence persisted in כלל ישראל decades after his apostasy. רבי יעקב עמדן had eyes like a hawk. Whenever any new ספר was published he immediately examined the הקדמה and the הסכמות. If he detected even a faint whiff of שבתאות he responded with great קנאות, writing sharply and fearlessly.

The Discovery

When the קיצור של״ה was published, רבי יעקב עמדן obtained a copy. He opened it and immediately issued a קול קורא: no one is permitted to bring this ספר into his home. The מחבר is a Sabbatean — a כופר who believes himself bound to the false משיח. His proof? In the הקדמה, the author makes reference to שבתי צבי.

Open the הקדמה of קיצור של״ה and you will find no explicit mention of שבתי צבי anywhere. So what was רבי יעקב עמדן seeing?

The Hidden גמטריא

The answer requires extreme attention — you have to be a יעקב עמדן yourself to catch it. In the brief הקדמה, ר' ישראל מיכל עפשטיין concludes:

"And I daven, and I write down for myself to remember, and I am מתפלל that ה' should help כלל ישראל to be זוכה to משיח. By learning this ספר may they merit to see משיח האמיתי, and may they merit ימות המשיח."

On the surface — perfectly fine. But there were two סימנים, two apostrophes, placed over those four words: משיח האמיתי and ימות המשיח. Whenever you see such an apostrophe, it is a סימן for the serious reader to analyze the word — to calculate its גמטריא, or to look at the ראשי תיבות.

The numerical value of משיח האמיתי ימות המשיח is exactly 814. That is precisely the גמטריא of שבתי צבי[11]. רבי יעקב עמדן caught it instantly: "I am convinced — this is Sabbatean. Get this ספר out of my home. No one should buy it."

The Impact on Our תפילה — ויהי נועם

The פסוק ויהי נועם ה' אלקינו עלינו that is recited before תחנון — specifically because it contains the words כי פלא פעל ה' — originates from that very ספר, the קיצור של״ה. Therefore the הגאון מווילנא and the משנה ברורה[12] write that one should not recite it. Many פוסקים concur. This is why in some Ashkenazic סידורים the passage appears in small print or in parentheses — not everyone says it.

I myself used to say it as a child, because I would say everything printed in the סידור. Later I left it off. The וועלט still says it, and many קהילות maintain the מנהג. The fact that the source may be Sabbatean does not necessarily invalidate the words themselves. The הגאון מווילנא offered an additional reason for omitting it: it speaks of פורענות — of plagues and calamities — and we prefer not to invoke such matters before תחנון. The סדור בני יששכר, a famous ספר from an Italian מקובל, also omits it. The practice varies. In most printed סידורים it appears, and if you wish to recite it, there is a מקור — though that מקור is questionable.

IX. נתן נטע הנובר and ספר שערי ציון

There was a yid by the name of נתן נטע הנובר[13]. He is best known for one major work — ספר יוון מצולה. He lived through the תח״ת — the gezeiros of Bogdan Chmielnicki of 1648–1649, when the Cossacks swept through Poland and killed between twenty and fifty thousand Jews. Many fled, and he was among them. He was a חשוב רב from western Ukraine. As an Ashkenazic yid he made his way to Italy, to ויניציה, where he met a מקובל who was a תלמיד of רב חיים ויטאל — the chief תלמיד of the האריז״ל. The מקובל took such a liking to him that נתן נטע became his תלמיד and himself a מקובל.

ספר יוון מצולה is the most important historical source on the events of תח״ת. (The ש״ך, who was also there, wrote his own — מגילת איפה.) But נתן נטע wrote another ספר as well — שערי ציון[14]. In it he records that many of the תפילות he transmits were מקובל from his רב, ר' חיים הכהן from חלב in Syria. נתן נטע writes that he is recording these תפילות so that כלל ישראל should say them.

Remarkably, every single סידור today contains these תפילות, though we do not know exactly how they spread so universally. Which תפילות? The תפילות we say when we open the ארון on שבת, on יום טוב, on ראש השנה, and on יום כיפור — all from שערי ציון. Everyone says them with reverence. People pay substantial sums for the זכות of opening the ארון. Yet none of these תפילות existed in כלל ישראל before the תלמיד of the תלמיד of the האריז״ל decided to teach them. He recorded them in his ספר; somehow they spread like wildfire. Every סידור now includes them — including the תפילה you say before entering the סוכה, the תפילה before taking the לולב, and many others. Some come instead from the קיצור של״ה, which preceded שערי ציון slightly — which is why a few people specifically do not say a few of these תפילות.

X. והוא רחום — The תפילה of שני וחמישי

Why Monday and Thursday at All?

On Monday and Thursday, in shul, we say והוא רחום יכפר עון followed by a long תפילה running all the way to קריאת שמע. Why on these two days? What is so special about שני וחמישי? When we fast, we fast on Monday and Thursday. We read the תורה on Monday and Thursday. The גמרא in בבא קמא דף פ״ב ע״ב[15] says it was a תקנת עזרא to read the תורה on these two days.

The ראשונים give two opposite reasons — and they truly oppose each other.

The First שיטה — Days of רחמים

Some ראשונים hold that Monday and Thursday are days of רחמים. משה רבינו ascended הר סיני to receive the second לוחות on a Thursday, and he descended forty days later on a Monday — that day was יום כיפור. Therefore Monday and Thursday became days of רחמים forever. If they are days of רחמים, you ask for more — you add תפילות in your שחרית. You stay an extra five minutes for והוא רחום.

The Second שיטה — Days of דין

Other ראשונים say פונקט פערקערט — exactly the opposite. Monday and Thursday are days of דין, because one of the תקנות עזרא was that every city must have a בית דין sitting on Monday and Thursday. Anyone who learns כתובות knows this from the very first משנה[16]. When there is דין below, there is דין above — so the זוהר teaches. The גמרא says you may not do הקזת דם — bloodletting — on Monday and Thursday, because they are days of דין. When there is דין here, there is דין there. And precisely because there is דין above, we have to ask for more רחמים — and that is why we add the extra תחנונים.

A curious מחלוקת — either days of רחמים or days of דין. But where did this specific תפילה come from?

XI. The Story Behind והוא רחום

An Ancient תפילה from the Times of the תנאים

This story has several versions and is brought in many ראשונים — the כל בו, the רוקח[17], and others. This is one of the most ancient תפילות in our סידור, and most people say it twice a week without knowing where it came from.

The Three Ships

After Titus destroyed the בית המקדש השני, many yidden were exiled. According to one account, Titus sent three ships out into the Mediterranean without sailors, without navigation — only sails. לכל רוח — wherever the wind would take them. Three full ships of men and women adrift in the Mediterranean. One ended up in פורטוגל, another in איטליה, another in אפריקה. (The versions disagree on exact locations.)

The King's Test

The ship that ended up — let us say in פורטוגל — disembarked, and the king of פורטוגל greeted them. "Where are you coming from?" "We are Jews." "What are Jews?" "We are descendants of אברהם אבינו." Hundreds of yidden, men and women, strangers from a foreign land. "What are you doing here?" "We were exiled when Titus destroyed the בית המקדש."

These were תנאים — yidden from two thousand years ago.

The king said, "You are children of אברהם אבינו? I know a little of the Bible. We just read about אברהם אבינו thrown into אור כשדים and emerging unscathed. Let us see if you can pull that off. If I throw you into a fire and you come out like אברהם אבינו, like your forefather, then you are indeed his descendants. You will be welcome in my land — homes, real estate, vineyards, fields, farms — whatever you want. But if not, I will send you straight back on the ship."

Three Days of Tefillah and Taanis

The three biggest, most חשוב תנאים on that ship gathered. "What are we going to do? We are not אברהם אבינו. This does not happen every day." They sat and davened and davened. They fasted three days. During those three days the three חכמים composed והוא רחום:

  1. One of them wrote from והוא רחום until אין כמוך לחנון ורחום.
  2. The second wrote from אין כמוך until ה' אלוקי ישראל שומע תרועת עמך.
  3. The third wrote the third section to the end.

They davened it over and over and over.

The Dream of the ירושלמי

On the third day the king demanded one of them be thrown into the fire. They turned to one another: "What did each of you dream the past three nights?" The first two had nothing of significance to report. The third one — the כל בו writes — was a great ירושלמי, but he was not really a תלמיד חכם in the strict sense; he did not know תנ״ך extensively. (Someone who does not know תנ״ך is not called a תלמיד חכם.) "What did you dream?" "I dreamt a פסוק." "What פסוק?" "I cannot remember the פסוק. All I remember is that there were two times the word כי and three times the word לא."

Today, with Google or ChatGPT, you would find it in seconds. But the תלמידי חכמים among them said immediately: "I have it!" Right on the spot. The פסוק is in ישעיהו פרק מ״ג[18]:

כי תעבור במים אתך אני ובנהרות לא ישטפוך, כי תלך במו אש לא תכוה ולהבה לא תבער בך.

Two times כי, three times לא. (This is also the famous niggun — the words of that beautiful melody.) They said to him: "You are the one we are choosing to throw into the fire, because you had the dream. As ישעיהו promises: you will be cast into fire and you will not burn." He answered: "All right, no problem."

The נס

They brought him before the king. They threw him into the fire. The fire split. He walked in. He walked out. Unharmed. The king declared, "Wow! You are indeed children of אברהם אבינו!"

The תקנה

The king accepted them. He gave them a place to live, money, real estate. They settled there, and Yiddishkeit flourished — whether פורטוגל or אפריקה, we are not entirely certain. After the מעשה, they sent letters to all the קהילות in כלל ישראל: "We did this. We were מתקן this תפילה והוא רחום, we said it over and over, it helped us; the king accepted us and the man was saved. Therefore we ask all of כלל ישראל to say it." All the קהילות accepted upon themselves to say it. That is why we say והוא רחום twice every week, asking הקדוש ברוך הוא to save us from גלות and have רחמים. It is a beautiful, very moving, crystal-clear תפילה. It is so important that one may not be מפסיק during it. The whole thing comes from before the ראשונים, from the תנאים themselves. We even have the names of the three תנאים, though there are different versions.

XII. The Insertion of רבי עזריאל

As you look through והוא רחום you will notice one piece that is not from those three תנאים. It is the line ה' אלוקי ישראל שומע תרועת עמך ורחם על עדת עמך — that beautiful refrain at the end of each stanza.

Who wrote it? רבי עזריאל — and you can see his name in it. Look at the words עוזרנו אלוקי ישענו על דבר כבוד שמך. עוזרנו spells ע-ז-ר-נ-ו; the יוד of ישענו completes it: עזריאל. There was a yid by that name, one of the ראשונים. He composed this addition, and one tradition holds that this is how it became part of והוא רחום.

XIII. The תפילה of חזקיהו המלך

There is, however, another שיטה for where the body of the תפילה comes from. We know the story of סנחריב. The ראשונים say that in the days of סנחריב מלך אשור, when he laid siege to ירושלים, חזקיהו המלך woke up in the middle of the night, went to the בית המקדש, and davened for two hours straight[19]. He davened and davened. The entire army — one hundred and eighty-five thousand soldiers — fell.

The question everyone wants to know: what did חזקיהו say that could fell one hundred and eighty-five thousand אשורים?

The answer is in the ראשי תיבות. One stanza spells out חזקיהו:

חשוב ציון · זכור ה' · קום ה' · י־ה־ו...

חזקיהו המלך said this תפילה. When we say it every Monday and Thursday we are commemorating his תפילה — and asking הקדוש ברוך הוא that all the enemies of כלל ישראל should fall as the army of סנחריב fell.

סיכום

Three braided strands run through this שיעור. The וידוי and שלש עשרה מידות before נפילת אפים — קבלי, instituted by the האריז״ל, our defense against the מקטרגים. נפילת אפים itself — originally רשות, taken on by כלל ישראל as חיוב, accompanied by genuine danger when said without כוונה, and accompanied by tremendous power when said with it. And the long Monday-and-Thursday addition of והוא רחום — composed in three pieces by three תנאים adrift on a ship in the Mediterranean, completed by רבי עזריאל's signature, and tied by another tradition all the way back to the תפילה of חזקיהו that broke the army of סנחריב. Every word in our סידור has a story. הלוואי we should say each of them with the כוונה it deserves.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. זוהר חלק ג', פרשת בלק רכ"ב ע"א; שער הכוונות, דרושי נפילת אפים. — The זוהר instructs that immediately after שמונה עשרה the קהל should recite וידוי followed by the י"ג מידות as a defense against the מקטרגים seeking to block the תפילה from ascending. The אריז"ל, in שער הכוונות, codifies this for daily תפילה — the source of universal נוסח ספרד practice and partial נוסח אשכנז practice. ↩
  2. ראש השנה י"ז ע"ב — origin of the שלש עשרה מידות. — "אמר רבי יוחנן: אלמלא מקרא כתוב אי אפשר לאמרו — מלמד שנתעטף הקדוש ברוך הוא כשליח צבור והראה לו למשה סדר תפילה, אמר לו: כל זמן שישראל חוטאין יעשו לפני כסדר הזה ואני מוחל להם". The גמרא after חטא העגל derives the standard text of the י"ג מידות (שמות ל"ד:ו'-ז') and its central role in תפילת הציבור in סליחות, תחנון, and יום הכפורים. ↩
  3. חידושי אגדות מהרש"א, ראש השנה י"ז ע"ב. — The מהרש"א (ר' שמואל אליעזר אידלס, 1555–1631) on the גמרא's description of הקב"ה, כביכול, wrapping Himself in a טלית: this is not literal but a kind of אור surrounding the שכינה, in keeping with עוטה אור כשלמה (תהלים ק"ד:ב'). The שיעור reads the מהרש"א's description of "white" אור as a possible source for the מנהג to wear white טליתות; verify by reading the מהרש"א directly. ↩
  4. שו"ת הרשב"ש, ר' שלמה בן שמעון בן צמח דוראן (Algiers, 1400–1467); cf. שו"ת תשב"ץ.
    Note: The שיעור references "רמב"ש" — likely the רשב"ש, son of the תשב"ץ. The principle that נפילת אפים is a רשות rather than a חיוב is widely held in the ראשונים (e.g., רמב"ם הלכות תפילה פ"ה ה"י"ג, רא"ש ברכות). Verify exact סימן in the printed שו"ת רשב"ש or תשב"ץ.
    ↩
  5. בעל שם טוב על התורה, פרשת וירא — ראשי תיבות of ואת מעכה. — The בעל שם טוב (ר' ישראל בן אליעזר, c. 1700–1760), in his בעל שם טוב על התורה (compiled posthumously) on פרשת וירא: the names at the close of the פרשה in בראשית כ"ב:כ"ד — "רְאוּמָה ... אֶת טֶבַח ... וְאֶת מַעֲכָה" — encode רמזים. The reading of ואת מעכה as ראשי תיבות of וידוי אחר תפילה מגיע עד כסא הכבוד is widely cited in חסידי literature; verify exact citation in בעל שם טוב על התורה. ↩
  6. לבוש on או"ח קל"א. — ר' מרדכי יפה (1530–1612), author of the לבוש, codifies the various early customs around the text of נפילת אפים. He references the older נוסח (linked to תהלים פרק כ"ה) as well as the פרק ו' alternative. The לבוש is a transitional code between the טור/בית יוסף and the later משנה ברורה; see לבוש החור on או"ח קל"א. ↩
  7. תהלים פרק כ"ה — לדוד אליך ה' נפשי אשא. — The original קפיטל the זוהר directs for נפילת אפים: an אקרוסטיכון (alphabetic acrostic) קפיטל in which דוד says "אליך ה' נפשי אשא" — "to You, ה', I lift my soul." The זוהר's warning regarding the danger of saying it without proper כוונה (the סכנת אריכות ימים) led Sephardic קהילות to recite the קפיטל seated, without literal נפילה, and Ashkenazic קהילות to substitute תהלים פרק ו' (ה' אל באפך תוכיחני) over time. ↩
  8. שמואל ב' פרק כ"ד; דברי הימים א' פרק כ"א. — דוד המלך's חטא of counting כלל ישראל through יואב; גד החוזה presents the three options of punishment; דוד's answer: "נפלה נא ביד ה' כי רבים רחמיו וביד אדם אל אפלה" (שמואל ב' כ"ד:י"ד). The פסוק recited in תחנון ("ויאמר דוד אל גד...") is taken from this פרק. The שיעור's note that the placement of this פסוק in the daily תחנון may be of late origin — possibly tied to Sabbatean-era liturgical insertions — should be cross-checked against the לבוש, מגן אברהם, and the משנה ברורה on או"ח קל"א. ↩
  9. קיצור של"ה, ר' יחיאל מיכל אפשטיין (Frankfurt, late 17th century). — Abridgment of שני לוחות הברית by ר' ישעיה הלוי הורוויץ. First printed Frankfurt-am-Main 1683. The author (ר' יחיאל מיכל אפשטיין; not to be confused with the 19th-century ערוך השלחן) was a popular halachic and ethical compiler. ר' יעקב עמדן later identified Sabbatean material in the הקדמה via the גמטריא 814 (see fn 11) and issued a strong חרם against the work. Despite the controversy several of its תפילות — including ויהי נועם and other liturgical insertions — became widely incorporated into Ashkenazic סדורים. ↩
  10. תורת הקנאות, ר' יעקב עמדן (1697–1776). — ר' יעקב ישראל בן צבי אשכנזי עמדן ("יעב"ץ"), son of the חכם צבי, was the leading anti-Sabbatean polemicist of the 18th century. תורת הקנאות (Altona, 1752) is his catalog of שבתי צבי's teachings and the persistence of crypto-Sabbatean doctrine in חיבורים appearing in his lifetime, including his identification of Sabbatean coding in the קיצור של"ה. He also wrote בית יהונתן הסופר as part of his pursuit of ר' יהונתן אייבשיץ on Sabbatean grounds — one of the great controversies of his era. ↩
  11. Gematria: משיח האמיתי ימות המשיח = שבתי צבי = 814. — The numerical value of שבתי צבי calculates as ש(300) + ב(2) + ת(400) + י(10) + צ(90) + ב(2) + י(10) = 814. ר' יעקב עמדן's identification of this גמטריא match in the הקדמה of the קיצור של"ה is the basis for his charge of Sabbatean coding. Cf. תורת הקנאות ad loc. ↩
  12. ביאור הגר"א; משנה ברורה, או"ח קל"א on ויהי נועם. — The גר"א (in his מעשה רב and ביאור הגר"א) and the משנה ברורה note that some קהילות omit ויהי נועם before תחנון. Two reasons given: (1) the source קיצור של"ה is suspect; (2) the פסוק contains language of פורענות, inappropriate before תחנון. The סדור בני יששכר also omits it. Most printed Ashkenazic סידורים retain it, often in small print or parentheses to flag the מנהג variation. ↩
  13. יוון מצולה, ר' נתן נטע הנובר (d. 1683). — ר' נתן נטע הנובר of Zaslaw, Volhynia, survivor and chronicler of the תח"ת massacres (1648–49) under Bohdan Khmelnytsky's Cossack revolt. יוון מצולה (Venice, 1653) is the central historical source on the גזירות תח"ת, in which an estimated 20,000–100,000 Jews were murdered. Hanover later studied קבלה under ר' חיים הכהן of Aleppo (a תלמיד of ר' חיים ויטאל) in Italy. He was eventually murdered by Turkish soldiers near Ungvár in 1683. ↩
  14. שערי ציון, ר' נתן נטע הנובר (Prague, 1662). — הנובר's second major work — a kabbalistic סדור of supplementary תפילות drawn from the אריז"ל via ר' חיים הכהן. From שערי ציון entered תפילות universally recited today, including the בריך שמיה formula recited when opening the ארון (originally from the זוהר, but mediated through שערי ציון), the יהי רצון before entering the סוכה, and the יהי רצון on ארבעת המינים. שערי ציון went through dozens of editions in the 17th–18th centuries; many of its תפילות rapidly entered standard סדורים. ↩
  15. בבא קמא פ"ב ע"א — תקנת עזרא for Monday and Thursday קריאת התורה. — The גמרא records the ten תקנות of עזרא הסופר, among them: "שיהו קורין במנחה בשבת... ותקנו שיהו קורין בשני ובחמישי". The reasoning given (פ"ב ע"א): so that no three days should pass without תורה for the עם — based on שמות ט"ו:כ"ב, "וילכו שלושת ימים במדבר ולא מצאו מים", with תורה the parallel of מים. The תקנה structures the entire weekly liturgical rhythm including the additional והוא רחום and longer תחנון on these days. (Note: שיעור cited ב"ק פ"ב ע"ב; the principal passage is ע"א.) ↩
  16. משנה כתובות פ"א מ"א; בבא קמא פ"ב ע"א — Monday and Thursday as days of בית דין. — The opening משנה of כתובות: "בתולה נשאת ליום הרביעי, ואלמנה ליום החמישי, שפעמים בשבת בתי דינין יושבין בעיירות — ביום השני וביום החמישי". בית דין sits in every city on Monday and Thursday — also a תקנת עזרא. The זוהר connects this to דין in שמים on those days, and to the איסור of הקזת דם on Monday and Thursday (שבת קכ"ט ע"ב). The שיעור develops this as the second שיטה for why we add תחנונים on these days — to invoke רחמים in the face of intensified דין. ↩
  17. כל בו (anonymous, 13th century); רוקח (ר' אלעזר מגרמייזא, c. 1176–1238). — Both ראשונים bring versions of the סיפור behind והוא רחום: three תנאים exiled by Titus aboard ships set adrift on the Mediterranean, the king's challenge of fire, three days of תפילה and תענית during which the three composed the פיוט in three sections, the חלום of the פסוק in ישעיהו פרק מ"ג, and the נס through which the chosen תנא emerged from the fire unharmed. Verify exact citation in printed editions of ספר הרוקח and כל בו (siman re: תפילת שני וחמישי); the סיפור appears in fuller form in the רוקח. ↩
  18. ישעיהו מ"ג:ב' — "כי תעבור במים אתך אני... כי תלך במו אש לא תכוה". — The פסוק: "כי תעבור במים אתך אני, ובנהרות לא ישטפוך; כי תלך במו אש לא תכוה ולהבה לא תבער בך". ה''s promise to כלל ישראל through ישעיהו that He will accompany them through fire and water — two times כי, three times לא. The פסוק is the basis of the famous "כי תעבור במים" niggun and central to the והוא רחום origin סיפור. ↩
  19. מלכים ב' פרק י"ט; ישעיהו פרק ל"ז; סנהדרין צ"ה ע"ב — חזקיהו's תפילה and the fall of 185,000. — The siege of ירושלים by סנחריב; חזקיהו's תפילה; the angel of ה' who struck 185,000 of the Assyrian army in a single night. חז"ל in סנהדרין צ"ה develop the magnitude and method of the נס. The שיעור's tradition that the תפילה of חזקיהו is encoded in the ראשי תיבות of one stanza of והוא רחום (ח-ז-ק-י-ה-ו) is part of an alternative מסורה on the origin of the פיוט, alongside the three-תנאים tradition. ↩
