The Customs of Mourning During ספירת העומר

From רבי עקיבא's תלמידים to the Crusades, and the Halacha That Followed
Recorded 12 April 2026 · Reworked from the original שיעור
Sponsored by the Shulman family לזכר their Zayde

This morning we discuss the מנהגים of ספירת העומר. We have already learned the הלכות of ספירה itself — the actual count. Today we explore not just the הלכה but the background of the הלכה. What is the source for all the customs of אבלות we observe between פסח and שבועות? The answer takes us through a גמרא in יבמות, the silence of the early ראשונים, a single גאון in סורא, the Crusades of 1096, two competing readings of one word in the גמרא, and a thicket of contemporary שיטות on music, haircuts, and a cappella.

I. The גמרא in יבמות — The Tragedy of רבי עקיבא's תלמידים

The Story

The classic source is the גמרא in מסכת יבמות דף ס״ב ע״ב[1]. This is one of the most famous גמרות in ש״ס. It tells the story of how תורה שבעל פה almost died — and was rebuilt — through רבי עקיבא.

In the days of רבי עקיבא, he had a massive ישיבה: twenty-four thousand תלמידים. The גמרא says they were לא נהגו כבוד זה בזה — they did not show proper כבוד to one another. There are a thousand and one explanations of what this means. We are speaking of קדושה עליונה, of גדולי תנאים. What does it mean that they lacked proper conduct? It is hard to fathom.

But there was some real failing in בין אדם לחברו. We know that you can be the biggest תלמיד חכם in the world — if you do not have proper בין אדם לחברו, if you do not know how to relate to other people, something is fundamentally wrong. The תורה is then גארנישט ווערט. Worthless. The תורה is supposed to teach you how to interact with other people. Without good מידות, tragedy strikes.

The מגפה

And tragedy struck. The גמרא even gives the geographic span — from גבת to אנטיפרס — a swath of ארץ ישראל from north to south where all the תלמידים of רבי עקיבא lived and died. They did not die randomly over years. They died in the span of a few weeks — מפסח עד עצרת, between פסח and שבועות. Twenty-four thousand תלמידים, in those weeks.

The גמרא says the tragedy was so deep that היה העולם שמם — the world was desolate. תורה would have been forgotten. All the תנאים were dying.

The Nature of the Death

The גמרא says specifically what they died from: מיתת אסכרה. Doctors and academics have written extensively about this; some identify it with diphtheria — a contagious viral illness that closes the upper airways. Diphtheria today is uncommon because of vaccinations; since 1950 in America there have been only fifty-seven cases. In their day there was no vaccine, and twenty-four thousand died.

The Severity of אסכרה

The גמרא in ברכות דף ח'[2] says there are nine hundred and three types of death. Why? Because the פסוק in משלי[3] says למות תוצאות — and the gematria of תוצאות is nine hundred and three.

The most difficult death of all, says the גמרא, is אסכרה — the very death that רבי עקיבא's תלמידים died. The גמרא's image: it is like trying to remove a small thorn from a ball of cotton or wool. You cannot pull the thorn cleanly; many strands of wool come along with it. That is what the נשמה going out feels like — pain, static, tension, fibers dragging.

By contrast, the easiest death is מיתת נשיקה. The גמרא's image: it is like pulling a hair out of a glass of milk. Smooth, clean, nothing left behind. Whoever is זוכה to מיתת נשיקה is on a high מדרגה. Twenty-four thousand תלמידים of רבי עקיבא died the opposite way — choking, asphyxiated.

רבי עקיבא Rebuilds תורה שבעל פה

After they all died the גמרא says רבי עקיבא had no one left to teach. ארץ ישראל was emptied of תלמידי חכמים. Five תלמידי חכמים remained in the south of ארץ ישראל: רבי מאיר, רבי יהודה, רבי יוסי, רבי שמעון בר יוחאי, and רבי אלעזר בן שמוע[4]. רבי עקיבא went down to them and gathered them up. He did not give up. רבי עקיבא is the yid who never gives up — as we discussed at the הגדה of פסח. He went down south and taught those five. From those five, we have all of יהדות today — every page of תורה שבעל פה. That is why we associate תורה שבעל פה with רבי עקיבא — he is its father. If he had given up, חס ושלום, there would be no תורה today.

II. The Striking Silence of the Early Sources

The גמרא Itself Says Nothing About מנהגים

Here is the first surprise. The גמרא makes zero mention of any halachic consequence of this tragic episode. The גמרא tells the story — that is all. It does not say that since they died one cannot get married, take haircuts, listen to music, or observe מנהגי אבלות. The גמרא says nothing of the sort.

The Silence of the Major ראשונים

Most of our main ראשונים — the foundation stones of הלכה — say nothing. The רמב״ם — nothing. The רי״ף — nothing. The רא״ש — nothing[5]. None of them mention מנהגי ספירה.

The Yemenite Tradition

By the way — Yemenite yidden, as we have discussed many times learning תפילה, source all their הלכות from the רמב״ם. Their adherence to the רמב״ם is so total that even when the שלחן ערוך does not pasken like the רמב״ם, they do. (Hence many halachic differences between תימני yidden and the rest of the diaspora.)

Yemenite yidden — many of them, until today — treat ספירה like a regular weekday because the רמב״ם does not mention it. There is a strong Yemenite community led by Rabbi Yitzchak Ratzabi (I believe he was נפטר recently — a very חשוב yid). Some Yemenites who came to ארץ ישראל adopted our מנהגים, including not getting married during ספירה. But essentially, the original Yemenite practice was to follow the רמב״ם as written. I assume the רמב״ם himself permitted his community in מצרים to get married during ספירה, to listen to music, and to take haircuts. There was nothing in their sources to forbid it.

III. The First Source — רב נטרונאי גאון

The First to Mention It

The first authority to mention the מנהג of not getting married during ספירה is רב נטרונאי גאון[6] — a גאון from about 1,150 years ago, in the 850s, the ninth century. His father was רב הילאי גאון. רב נטרונאי led a great ישיבה in southern Iraq in סורא. He is the first to record this מנהג. Drawing on the גמרא in יבמות, he writes: how can our nation, in such a tragic time between פסח and שבועות — when all these תלמידים died — get married? Therefore we do not get married during ספירה.

What the Tradition Looked Like

Here is the fascinating point: רב נטרונאי גאון believed there was already some tradition to refrain from marriage at this time of year because of אבלות. But the רי״ף, the רמב״ם, the רא״ש, and many of the other גאונים never write it. There was apparently a tradition floating among certain קהילות, and רב נטרונאי codified it into his הלכות. Once a גאון puts something into writing, the chain begins. We — yidden who love to take on חומרות — adopted it. From there it eventually entered the שלחן ערוך.

IV. A Second Reason — The First Crusade, 1096

Pope Urban II

And there is a second reason — a thousand years after רבי עקיבא's תלמידים. The ט״ז[7] brings it; it is in the שלחן ערוך. In 1096 came the First Crusade. Pope Urban II declared that the Christians had to redeem the Church of the Holy Sepulcher — where they claim ישו is buried in the Christian Quarter of the Old City of ירושלים. He said the Muslims, the "infidels," had to be driven out and the location had to be taken back. He gathered soldiers and Christians and they began riding across אשכנז.

The Destruction of שו״ם

They started in Germany, in the valley of the Rhine — we visited those places about two years ago. They went to the three main קהילות of כלל ישראל in אשכנז at the time. This was 1096 — a thousand years to the day, as it were, from רבי עקיבא's תלמידים. Between פסח and שבועות, they slaughtered yidden. Some sources say twelve thousand killed; some say more. They wiped out the three קהילות we know in our literature as שו״ם[8]:

  • ש — שפירא (Speyer)
  • ו — וורמיזא (Worms)
  • מ — מגנצא (Mainz)

We toured those cities thoroughly. We saw and heard much from Yehuda Geber. שו״ם was the nucleus of אשכנז in those days.

The Scale of the Tragedy

Twelve thousand may sound small to us — we live with the memory of six million. But in those days twelve thousand was virtually everything. Twelve thousand yidden killed. The Jewish chroniclers of the era — and yidden in those communities were writing it down — record the horrific massacres in detail.

Convert or Die — and the Anonymous of Mainz

The Christians arrived saying: convert to Christianity or we kill you. The stories — and there is a major מחלוקת ראשונים about them — record that in many cities, before the Christians could force the conversion, the yidden killed themselves. Slaughtered their own children.

Their reasoning: they were applying, in a way, the logic of בן סורר ומורה — the גמרא's idea that one kills a rebellious twelve-year-old now, before he becomes a thief and a murderer. They figured their children would be raised by Christians and would commit עבודה זרה, מחללי שבת, עריות; better to die now innocent than to live a lifetime in apostasy.

One of the chroniclers — known in academic literature as the Anonymous of Mainz[9], an עד ראיה — describes it. There is one story you cannot read without weeping. He describes an אשה צדקת named Rachel with four children. The youngest was Aaron. When she heard the Crusaders had reached Mainz, she took her three older children and killed them — she did not want them to fall into Christian hands. The youngest, Aaron, watched his brothers murdered by his own mother. He ran away crying, "Mama, do not touch me!" and hid under the bed. The witness saw the mother — having performed what she believed was the right thing — pull him out by his leg from under the bed and say, "Aaron, you are not going to escape my sword." She killed him, and then she killed herself.

The Halachic Debate

Many ראשונים held that what these yidden did was, in strict הלכה, רציחה — שפיכות דמים — actual murder, and we regret it. You are not allowed to speak about these yidden critically; they are heilig. You are not allowed to be מוציא לעז on ראשונים. They did what they had to do. Whether it was strictly halachically permitted to kill oneself and one's children rather than fall into Christian hands — to this day there is a real debate among the אחרונים. But that is what happened. That is part of our history. These are our forefathers — most of us trace to אשכנז, and these are our ancestors.

The Survivors

How are we still here? Survivors fled to France — that is where רש״י had his ישיבה. This is during רש״י's lifetime. רש״י's ישיבה grew, and his קהילה grew, because so many fled north from the Rhine valley to escape the Crusaders. France itself was struck — Crusaders killed there too — but the chain of תורה did not break.

The Timing — and the ט״ז's Conclusion

The whole episode took place between פסח and שבועות in 1096. Says the ט״ז: not only did twenty-four thousand תלמידים of רבי עקיבא die between פסח and שבועות — we now have a second chapter in history, in our own ancestors' lifetimes, when more yidden died in the same window. That is why these days are ימי אבל. That is why we do not get married.

V. אב הרחמים — A תפילה Born in Worms

We have precise historical records. We know that on ראש חודש סיון the Crusaders reached Worms. The תפילה of אב הרחמים[10] we recite every שבת was composed specifically to commemorate those killings. It is an extraordinarily emotional תפילה composed in that era. This is why during ספירת העומר we say אב הרחמים even on ראש חודש, when it is otherwise omitted. In ספירה we recite it specifically to commemorate the קדושי שו״ם of the Crusades — alongside the תלמידי רבי עקיבא before them.

A Hungarian Connection My grandparents and all those who came from Hungary know well that these are the days when in a Hungarian shul, hundreds of yidden say קדיש simultaneously for their aunts, uncles, and grandparents. The Hungarian community understands that these days are extraordinarily holy and meaningful — they include the period leading up to יום השואה. Historians estimate that in the late eleventh century the devastation in France and western Germany was immense; not comparable in scale to the six million, but in proportion to the Jewish population of that era it was catastrophic. אשכנז in 1096 was those three קהילות. We descend from them. These events remain real to us.

VI. The Kabbalistic Reason — ימי דין

רבינו ירוחם on קרבן העומר

There is also a reason given in the ראשונים rooted in קבלה for why these days carry אבלות — indeed, why they are ימי דין. Why did הקדוש ברוך הוא decree that specifically this period between פסח and שבועות should host both the deaths of רבי עקיבא's תלמידים and the Crusades?

רבינו ירוחם[11], one of the great ראשונים of France whose ספר is constantly quoted by the טור and בית יוסף, gives a fascinating insight. The קרבן העומר comes from שעורים (barley). The word שעורה is phonetically related to סערה — "storm." Since the קרבן העומר is from barley, and this קרבן initiates the count to שבועות, there is a play on words: שעורים is like סערה. The term סער represents a storm — Hashem's stormy attitude during this very window between פסח and שבועות. Therefore, since these are generally ימי דין, one does not get married during this period.

The Connection to Other ימי דין

The connection to ימי דין is in the משנה in ראש השנה[12]: ארבעה ראשי שנים הם, ובפסח נידונין על התבואה. There is an aspect of יום הדין at פסח. Between פסח and שבועות there is a general extended period of judgment.

The רמב״ן and the Apparent Contradiction

And yet — the רמב״ן in פרשת אמור[13] states that the days between פסח and שבועות are like one extended חול המועד, possessing a קדושה similar to חול המועד. Two readings, almost in tension. Spiritually elevated like חול המועד; halachically ימי דין; and historically — the period in which our people were repeatedly struck. Jewish history transformed the רמב״ן's idealized reading. We maintain the מנהג not to marry during this period, at least according to those who follow רבינו ירוחם.

VII. What the ראשונים Actually Forbid

Two reasons — a thousand years apart — for why we do not get married. Worth repeating: the only thing the ראשונים mention is marriage. נישואין. That is it. Nothing about haircuts. Nothing about not listening to music. Nothing about dancing. Nothing about not buying new clothing. None of these later איסורים exist in the writings of the ראשונים we have today.

VIII. Two Versions of the גמרא — When Does the Mourning End?

The Question

If רבי עקיבא's תלמידים died between פסח and שבועות, then on the simple reading the entire forty-nine-day period should carry the prohibition. But that is not what we do. The אחרונים narrowed it. Most of us today do not observe מנהגי אבלות for the full period — we stop at ל״ג בעומר.

What Is the Source?

This has nothing intrinsically to do with רבי שמעון בר יוחאי or the way ל״ג בעומר is celebrated in ארץ ישראל. (While I understand making fires has become popular, I always tell my children not to dance around the fire — the תוספתא[14] says dancing around fire is מדרכי האמורי; we do not perform actions of pagan practice.)

The Two Variants in יבמות ס״ב

The source is a textual variant in the same גמרא in יבמות we cited at the start. Our standard text reads מפסח ועד עצרת — from פסח until שבועות. But there is a different נוסחא that adds one word, and changes everything. The variant reads:

מפסח עד פרוס העצרת.

What does פרוס mean? The word comes from מפרסת פרסה — we just saw it in פרשת שמיני. It means "half" or "slice." So עד פרוס העצרת means "until half-before שבועות." And the ראשונים cite a גמרא in בכורות דף נ״ז[15] that whenever חז״ל say פרוס, they mean fifteen days before. Fifteen days before שבועות is exactly ל״ג בעומר.

The Practical Conclusion

According to this version, the תלמידים of רבי עקיבא stopped dying on ל״ג בעומר. If so, אבלות ends on ל״ג בעומר. This is why the ערוך[16] adopts this version — otherwise we would be in mourning all the way to שבועות.

A Second Variant — The 33 Days of Death

There is yet another variation in the ראשונים. According to it, the תלמידים did not stop dying on ל״ג בעומר — they continued dying until שבועות. However, on the days between פסח and שבועות when we do not say תחנון — they did not die. Imagine: הקדוש ברוך הוא was taking twenty-four thousand תלמידים, but on the יומים טובים of that period, no deaths.

Count the days when we do not say תחנון: seven days of פסח, ראש חודש אייר, ראש חודש סיון (counting from פסח onward, not including ראש חודש ניסן), and the שבתות between פסח and שבועות. The total is exactly thirty-three. So according to this שיטה, the תלמידים died for thirty-three days spread out; they died, then on שבת nobody died, then מחר another thousand died, etc. But there are exactly thirty-three days of actual death. We then take those thirty-three and treat them as a consecutive block of אבלות — though in the historical reality the deaths went all the way to שבועות.

The Practical Difference

The practical difference between the two variants is minimal. The מגן אברהם brings a much more stringent שיטה which we will not enter here.

IX. שלחן ערוך and רמ״א — Two ספירות

When Mourning Ends

Per the ראשונים' second variant, the mourning ends fifteen days before שבועות — that is, ל״ג בעומר. The major מחלוקת between the שלחן ערוך and the רמ״א[17]:

  • According to the ספרדים: haircuts are permitted only on ל״ד בעומר in the morning.
  • According to אשכנזים following the רמ״א: already on ל״ג בעומר one may take a haircut.

Especially in ארץ ישראל, where they celebrate the הילולא of רבי שמעון בר יוחאי, they are certainly more lenient.

The רמ״א's Crucial Ruling — Choose Your Thirty-Three

Here is the critical ruling. You may choose which thirty-three days you observe. The רמ״א explicitly permits this. You must observe אבלות for thirty-three days; which thirty-three is up to you.

Two options:

  1. הספירה הראשונה: begin immediately after פסח and continue until ל״ג בעומר.
  2. הספירה השנייה: begin from ראש חודש אייר and continue all the way to שבועות.

What does the עולם do? For me, the first ספירה is always easier — by ל״ג בעומר you are free for חתונות. Many yidden want to be available for weddings before שבועות — there can be ten or more חתונות in those weeks. Some yidden specifically want to be able to take haircuts after the end of ניסן — for them this week (the days leading up to ראש חודש אייר) is the opportunity, and they keep the second ספירה. According to the רמ״א either is one hundred percent acceptable.

Switching From Year to Year

And the רמ״א is famous for a further leniency: you may switch your ספירה from year to year. He rules that there is no problem because both מנהגים stem from the same story. Mourn for thirty-three days; whichever thirty-three works for you this year — fine.

But the רמ״א flags one concern. If nobody made a חתונה until after ל״ג בעומר, no problem. Once it became normal for some yidden to have weddings during the first ספירה and others during the second, you are now likely to be invited to a חתונה during what should be your own ספירה of mourning. What do we do?

X. Attending חתונות — The Guest's Position

The רמ״א's essential ruling: if my own מנהג is the first ספירה or the second ספירה, and a friend invites me to a חתונה during my mourning period, what do I do? It is not a problem at all. The restriction of תרתי דסתרי (holding two contradictory positions) applies to the חתן and כלה — they cannot take both. For the rest of us as guests, it is perfectly fine. You may attend a חתונה in this week and you may attend after ל״ג בעומר, regardless of which ספירה you personally observe. These are all מנהגים in the שלחן ערוך. Do not be מזלזל in them, but when there is reason to be lenient, we are lenient — and the רמ״א says exactly that.

The חתם סופר on רב משולם איגרא's קול קורא

The חתם סופר[18] writes a תשובה describing his predecessor in פרעסבורג, רב משולם איגרא — one of the גאוני הדור whose ספרים are very difficult to learn. רב משולם was a ראש תלמוד תורה and later רב in פרעסבורג. When he was נפטר, the חתם סופר was elected and brought his ישיבה.

The חתם סופר describes that רב משולם איגרא once issued a קול קורא — signs posted throughout פרעסבורג — declaring: yidden are playing תרתי דסתרי. They attend חתונות in the first ספירה and they attend חתונות in the second ספירה. תרתי דסתרי — you cannot do this. You must make up your mind.

The חתם סופר explains that the principle is sound: a yid cannot dance at two weddings simultaneously. But מעיקר הדין there is no problem with attending. Keep whichever ספירה you choose; it is acceptable. Perhaps in his particular year there was a problem in פרעסבורג because the entire ספירה was being eroded — yidden being too lenient. But fundamentally, according to the דין, there is no issue.

XI. The רמב״ן's Reading — These Days Are Holy

As the רמב״ן said, these days are like חול המועד — the holiest period of the year, a time of elevation and קדושה. Even the שלחן ערוך states: while you may not get married, you may become engaged. The שלחן ערוך adds something remarkable: if a yid is קופץ — that is the lashon — a yid who "jumps" and decides "I am going to get married right now" — we do not protest. It is not the end of the world. Nobody today actually does that, but the הלכה is clear: this is a מנהג, not an איסור.

A Note on Officiating

One of the אחרונים — I believe the מגן אברהם or a similar source — writes that according to רב משולם איגרא, there is a particular issue for a רב. If you are a רב and you receive an invitation to officiate at a חתונה this week, and another invitation arrives for after ל״ג בעומר, that creates a difficulty. The resolution: it is on the חתן and כלה to choose which is their ספירה. Anyone invited to be משמח חתן וכלה may dance, sing, and join in whatever brings joy.

XII. Music During ספירה

A Common Misconception

A common misconception: that listening to music carries the same stringency as refraining from marriage. It does not. The איסור of music during ספירה has generated extensive discussion, especially in our era of recording technology. Live versus recorded; vocal versus instrumental.

The ביצ'ער קודנער's Lenient Position

The ביצ'ער קודנער[19] offered a unique היתר that has not gained widespread Orthodox acceptance. He held that the prohibition of קול אשה in the גמרא applies only to live singing. Therefore, on his view, listening to a woman's voice on a recording — radio, records, tapes, CDs, digital — would be permitted. Following the same reasoning, if non-live music is not technically כלי זמר and is not שיר in the formal sense, it would also not be problematic during ספירה. For a yid who follows this שיטה, recorded music — including chazzanus — would be permitted. Most פוסקים do not accept this. The mainstream practice prohibits even recorded music during ספירה, which led to the popular custom of listening only to a cappella.

The A Cappella Controversy

But there is even a debate over a cappella. During ספירה, virtually every store — particularly meat stores before שבת — plays a cappella in the background. Is it really מותר? The common understanding is plain: a cappella is people singing — not music. The fact that it is recorded and now playing from an MP3 player does not change its essential nature.

The Stringent View — רב משה and רב שמואל וואזנער

רב משה פיינשטיין[20] presented a חידוש that surprises many. Both he and the שבט הלוי (רב שמואל וואזנער)[21] — and a number of contemporary פוסקים — take issue with a cappella. Their reasoning: once a machine records people singing, the machine itself becomes a כלי זמר. We tend to think of a כלי זמר as a trumpet, a violin, a guitar, a drum. According to this שיטה, the tape recorder — the digital player — is itself a כלי זמר. Therefore, regardless of whether the sound is instrumental music or human voices, the device is the instrument. They are מחמיר and require complete abstention from such recordings during ספירה.

Some suggest the מנחת יצחק supports this stringent position because the sound is electrical. The matter requires more analysis; there is debate whether the מנחת יצחק's שיטה really lands here. The counterargument: not every sound-producing device is a כלי זמר in the traditional sense. We have established categories of what an instrument is; the question is whether electronic reproduction fundamentally changes the nature of human singing.

רב שמואל קמנצקי — Letter and Spirit

Through Rabbi Kleinman of Flatbush, who publishes the קובץ הלכות of רב שמואל קמנצקי[22], we have a clearly more lenient position. רב שמואל's view: it is yidden singing; there is no fundamental problem with listening. But even he adds an important principle. There is the letter of the law, and there is the spirit of the law. Even if technically permitted, the spirit of ספירה is אבלות. So even a yid who follows the lenient שיטה should minimize even technically permissible music. Listen — but moderately.

The Historical היתר for Mental Health

A significant precedent comes from the שו״ת משנה שכיר[23] (the שמחה יערוף, רב in Semiyah in pre-war Hungary). In a famous תשובה he ruled that if a yid is emotionally down and needs music for genuine mental health — to lift his spirits — and the music is not the dance-inducing kind, it is permitted. The key distinction: the איסור is primarily about music that would lead to dancing. If music is needed for real mental wellness, there is room for leniency.

Modern application: exercising on a treadmill during ספירה or during the שלשת ימי הגבלה (or, much more relevant, during בין המצרים). May one listen to music to exercise? It depends on whether this is genuine need versus simple entertainment. The preferable option is a cappella. But if a yid genuinely needs music for his wellbeing, there is room — based on the שמחה יערוף's precedent.

XIII. The Question of Singing Itself

The שבט הלוי's Stringency

The שבט הלוי (רב שמואל וואזנער) took an even more stringent position: a yid should refrain from singing entirely during ספירה. This חומרא has not gained widespread acceptance, since many question its basis. The accepted prohibitions are playing music, dancing at חתונות, and taking haircuts. Who said you cannot sing at all?

The לקט יושר

There is a historical source. The לקט יושר[24], written by a תלמיד of the תרומת הדשן (a major source of הלכה from the 1300s, which records what his רבי actually did), writes that his רבי, the תרומת הדשן, did not sing on מוצאי שבת during ספירה. Communities sing אליהו הנביא after הבדלה — the תרומת הדשן refrained during ספירת העומר. This source provides at least some support for the שבט הלוי's position that even singing brings שמחה.

שבת in the Middle

Important — שבת is not a time of אבלות. Nevertheless the לקט יושר's testimony suggests that even the singing traditionally associated with מוצאי שבת should be curtailed during ספירה. The beautiful custom of singing after הבדלה — including the holy פיוטים of הבדלה and קידוש לבנה — has largely fallen out in many communities. This is unfortunate. These are sacred songs that should be preserved.

XIV. Haircuts and Shaving

The Meaning of "להשתפר"

Refraining from haircuts during ספירה is well-established. But its parameters — particularly around shaving — invite debate. According to some authorities, including Rabbi Kalish, the איסור specifically concerns להשתפר — haircuts — not necessarily shaving. The distinction matters practically.

Historical Development

An honest examination of the early פוסקים shows that many do not mention shaving at all. The whole no-shaving practice is a מנהג that developed over time, especially after the periods of tragedy. כלל ישראל has accepted the practice to a certain extent, but its parameters were not universally defined in earlier generations.

Contemporary Leniencies

Some פוסקים, including reportedly רב משה פיינשטיין, permit shaving for those who shave regularly throughout the year. The logic: maintaining your normal grooming standard, particularly for professional reasons, does not constitute the kind of beautification that is prohibited during the אבלות.

רב יוסף דוב סולוביצ'יק was notably lenient regarding shaving, even during בין המצרים. His position rested on careful analysis of the טור and other ראשונים. He distinguished among the different types of mourning practices and found room for leniency.

לכבוד שבת — there is a widely accepted practice permitting haircuts and shaving in honor of שבת during certain periods of mourning. שבת overrides certain mourning restrictions.

XV. Buying New Things — A Common Misconception

The Three Weeks vs. ספירת העומר

There is a significant misconception that one may not purchase new clothing during ספירה. This is a misapplication of the rule of בין המצרים.

During שלשת ימי הגבלה — the Three Weeks before תשעה באב — there is a custom not to purchase new clothing or new fruits. The reason: one cannot recite שהחיינו during a period of national mourning. People mistakenly extend that to ספירת העומר. The comparison is unfounded.

Two Reasons

First, the nature of the אבלות is different. The אבלות of בין המצרים has a basis in the גמרא itself. The אבלות of ספירה does not appear in the גמרא; it does not appear in the early ראשונים. While we have sources that codified later practices into law, the יסוד of the two periods is fundamentally different.

Second — and decisive — the entire premise of not buying new items during the Three Weeks rests on not being able to make שהחיינו. When was the last time anyone made שהחיינו on a new suit? In previous generations, perhaps a hundred years ago, buying a suit was a major event — once every two years, a real שמחה, a שהחיינו moment. Today nobody makes שהחיינו on a new suit. We own multiple suits. The acquisition of clothing is routine, not exceptional. Without a שהחיינו situation in the first place, the underlying premise of the restriction does not apply to ספירה.

The Status of the Custom

One of the אחרונים addresses this directly. Some yidden mistakenly say buying new clothes during ספירה is forbidden. He dismisses it as שיחת נשים — folk practice without halachic foundation. (The term in halachic literature refers to unfounded customs and superstitions — what we might call a "bubbe-meise." There is even a תשובה of one of the ראשונים that suggests if you hear שיחת נשים from actual נשים צדקניות — like our own pious grandmothers — one should not be מזלזל in their tradition. Nevertheless, in this case, the restriction lacks halachic foundation; it is folk practice.)

The practical ruling: buying new clothing during ספירת העומר is not problematic. Do not create unnecessary stringencies based on a misapplication of בין המצרים.

XVI. The מנהגים of ארץ ישראל and the חסידים

Today in ארץ ישראל, once ל״ג בעומר arrives, the חסידים and yidden who follow the האריז״ל and קבלה are more stringent. They hold that the days of ספירה are ימי דין and refrain from haircuts. I believe in Skverer חסידות they take haircuts only on ערב יום טוב.

XVII. After ל״ג בעומר for Those Keeping the Second ספירה

For yidden who observe the second ספירה (from ראש חודש אייר until שבועות), shaving during the week of ל״ג בעומר is no problem at all.

סיכום

The אבלות of ספירה is real and meaningful, expressing our mourning for the תלמידי רבי עקיבא and the קדושי שו״ם. The primary restrictions — refraining from marriage, from haircuts (with various leniencies), and from music — are well-established. But we must be careful not to pile on stringencies that lack basis in הלכה or authoritative מנהג. The רמ״א leaves room for choice — first ספירה or second ספירה, switching from year to year, attending other yidden's weddings even during your own mourning. Each yid should consult his own פוסק for specific applications, particularly where there are legitimate differences among the גדולי הוראה. The ראשונים did not give us a rigid system; they gave us a way to honor a complex history without breaking a normal Jewish life.

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. יבמות ס"ב ע"ב. — The classical סוגיא: "שנים עשר אלף זוגים תלמידים היו לו לרבי עקיבא, וכולן מתו בפרק אחד מפני שלא נהגו כבוד זה לזה, והיה העולם שמם, עד שבא רבי עקיבא אצל רבותינו שבדרום ושנאה להם — רבי מאיר ורבי יהודה ורבי יוסי ורבי שמעון ורבי אלעזר בן שמוע, והם הם העמידו תורה אותה שעה". The גמרא identifies the cause of death as אסכרה, gives the geographic span (מגבת ועד אנטיפרס), and records the period as מפסח עד עצרת (with the variant עד פרוס העצרת in the alternate גירסה). ↩
  2. ברכות ח' ע"א. — "תניא היה רבי מאיר אומר: תשע מאות ושלשה מיני מיתה נבראו בעולם, שנאמר 'למות תוצאות' — תוצאות בגימטריא הכי הוו". The גמרא develops the categorization: the harshest is אסכרה (likened to a thorn pulled from wool); the easiest is מיתת נשיקה (likened to a hair pulled from milk). The death of ר' עקיבא's תלמידים by אסכרה is treated as the most extreme calamity. ↩
  3. תהלים ס"ח:כ"א — "לאלוקים ה' למות תוצאות". — Note: the שיעור attributes the פסוק to משלי; the principal מקור is תהלים ס"ח:כ"א. The gematria of תוצאות: ת(400) + ו(6) + צ(90) + א(1) + ו(6) + ת(400) = 903. The גמרא derives the 903 types of death from this פסוק. ↩
  4. The five surviving תלמידים: ר' מאיר, ר' יהודה, ר' יוסי, ר' שמעון בר יוחאי, ר' אלעזר בן שמוע. — These five became the dominant figures of the second-generation תנאים after ר' עקיבא and shaped virtually all of תורה שבעל פה. ר' מאיר is the editor of the foundational משנה, transmitted via רבי (רבי יהודה הנשיא); ר' שמעון בר יוחאי is the source of the זוהר tradition. The גמרא emphasizes their role in the survival of תורה after the catastrophe. ↩
  5. רמב"ם; רי"ף; רא"ש — none mentions מנהגי אבלות in ספירה. — A striking phenomenon. The three foundational halachic codifiers of the medieval period are silent on the entire framework of ספירה mourning that later became universal. The רמב"ם in הלכות תפילה ונשיאת כפים and הלכות תמידין ומוספין discusses the מצוה of counting but not the mourning. The רי"ף on יבמות ס"ב records the story of ר' עקיבא's תלמידים without halachic implication. The רא"ש follows. This silence supports the שיעור's framing: the מנהגי ספירה are post-תלמוד and post-רמב"ם developments. ↩
  6. תשובות רב נטרונאי גאון (Sura, c. 853–858). — רב נטרונאי בן הילאי גאון, mentioned earlier in this series (see file 49 fn 16; file 51 fn 12). His תשובה is the earliest known halachic source for the מנהג of refraining from נישואין during ספירת העומר. The תשובה is preserved in the manuscript collections of his תשובות; verify exact citation against ed. Brody (Yad HaRav Herzog, 1994). ↩
  7. ט"ז, או"ח תצ"ג. — The ט"ז (ר' דוד הלוי סגל, 1586–1667) on the שלחן ערוך's codification of ספירה mourning. He brings the גזירות תתנ"ו (1096 First Crusade) as an additional layer of historical mourning that reinforces the existing מנהג derived from ר' עקיבא's תלמידים. The ט"ז's framing — two layered tragedies in the same window — is widely cited. ↩
  8. קהילות שו"ם — Speyer, Worms, Mainz; First Crusade massacres of ה'תתנ"ו (May–June 1096). — The three קהילות of the Rhine Valley, foundational to medieval Ashkenazic Jewry. The First Crusade mobs led by Count Emicho of Flonheim and others massacred Jewish communities along the Rhine in spring 1096, beginning at Speyer (May 3) and continuing through Worms (May 18–25) and Mainz (May 27). Estimated victims range from 5,000 to 10,000+. The Hebrew chronicles — קרוניקת שלמה בר שמשון, קרוניקת אליעזר בר נתן, the Anonymous of Mainz — preserve eyewitness detail. The קינות for תשעה באב include several that commemorate קדושי שו"ם. Modern collection: A. M. Habermann, Sefer Gezerot Ashkenaz veTzarfat (Jerusalem 1945). ↩
  9. Anonymous of Mainz (קרוניקת אנונימוס ממגנצא) — Hebrew chronicle of the 1096 massacre. — One of the three primary Hebrew chronicles of the First Crusade massacres, preserved anonymously and named for its origin in Mainz. Documents the systematic Jewish defense and the choice of kiddush Hashem (martyrdom-by-suicide and parental killing) over forced conversion. The story of Rachel and her four children, including Aaron pulled from beneath the bed, is the most often-cited episode from the Mainz chronicle and remains halachically debated to the present day. Verify against the printed Hebrew text in Habermann. ↩
  10. תפילת אב הרחמים — composed for the קדושי שו"ם. — The תפילה recited in שחרית של שבת after קריאת התורה: "אב הרחמים שוכן מרומים, ברחמיו העצומים, הוא יפקוד ברחמים החסידים והישרים והתמימים...". Composed in Ashkenaz in the wake of the 1096 massacres to memorialize קדושי שו"ם. The פוסקים codify that אב הרחמים is recited on שבתות of ספירת העומר (even when otherwise omitted, e.g., on a שבת that is also ר"ח) precisely because the קדושי שו"ם were murdered in this window. Verify in משנה ברורה on the סדר תפילות שבת. ↩
  11. תולדות אדם וחוה, רבינו ירוחם בר משולם (Provence, c. 1290–1350). — A foundational ראשון in halachic codification, quoted by the טור, בית יוסף, and many later פוסקים. His structural reasoning that the period from פסח to שבועות is itself ימי דין — anchored in the wordplay שעורה ↔ סערה and the משנה in ר"ה on "בפסח נידונין על התבואה" — provides a kabbalistic-halachic framework for the entire period that goes beyond the historical ר' עקיבא tragedy. ↩
  12. משנה ראש השנה פ"א מ"ב. — "בארבעה פרקים העולם נידון: בפסח על התבואה, בעצרת על פירות האילן, בראש השנה כל באי עולם עוברין לפניו כבני מרון, ובחג נידונין על המים". Four times a year the world is judged. The שיעור reads פסח's judgment — על התבואה — as extending into the period that culminates with עצרת, accounting for the דין-character of the entire ספירה stretch. ↩
  13. רמב"ן על ויקרא כ"ג:ל"ו; רמב"ן על ויקרא כ"ג:ט"ו-ט"ז. — The רמב"ן on פרשת אמור develops his framework that פסח and שבועות are essentially one חג with ספירה as the connective חול המועד: "כל מה שבין פסח לעצרת הוא כחול המועד". The רמב"ן's reading is celebrated as a חידוש in spiritual conception of the ספירה period. The שיעור juxtaposes this elevated reading against the historical reality and against רבינו ירוחם's judgment-of-the-grain framing. ↩
  14. תוספתא שבת פ"ז; cf. משנה ע"ז פ"א מ"ז; סנהדרין נ"ב ע"ב. — The תוספתא on דרכי האמורי — pagan practices that חז"ל forbade as derivative of idolatrous worship. The שיעור invokes the principle to caution against literal dancing around bonfires on ל"ג בעומר in styles that mimic ancient pagan fire-rituals. The mainstream מנהג of lighting מדורות on ל"ג בעומר is rooted in קבלי sources connected to רשב"י's הילולא; the mode of celebration matters. ↩
  15. בכורות נ"ז ע"ב — פרוס as fifteen days before. — The גמרא's definition: "כל היכא דתני 'פרוס' — חמיסר יומי" — wherever the term פרוס appears in חז"ל, it denotes fifteen days prior. Therefore פרוס העצרת = fifteen days before שבועות = ל"ג בעומר precisely. The textual variant "מפסח עד פרוס העצרת" in יבמות ס"ב ע"ב thus generates the ל"ג בעומר halachic framework. ↩
  16. ערוך, ערך פרס, ר' נתן בעל הערוך (Rome, c. 1035–1106). — The ערוך, the foundational lexicon of תלמודי Hebrew and Aramaic by ר' נתן בן יחיאל of Rome. He cites the variant text "מפסח עד פרוס העצרת" for יבמות ס"ב ע"ב and derives from it the ל"ג בעומר terminus of ספירה mourning. The ערוך's citation is the principal source for this variant in subsequent halachic literature. (Note: this ערוך is the lexicon, not the ערוך השלחן of ר' יחיאל מיכל עפשטיין.) ↩
  17. שלחן ערוך, או"ח תצ"ג; רמ"א ad loc. — The principal סימן on מנהגי ספירת העומר. The מחבר codifies the prohibition of נישואין and תספורת through ל"ד בעומר morning (Sephardic מנהג). The רמ"א in his glosses permits the Ashkenazic מנהג of taking haircuts already on ל"ג בעומר, and codifies the choice between the two ספירות (first half / second half of the period) with permission to switch annually and to attend others' weddings during one's own mourning period. Foundational source for all subsequent Ashkenazic practice. ↩
  18. שו"ת חתם סופר, או"ח קמ"ב. — The חתם סופר's תשובה describing the קול קורא of his predecessor ר' משולם איגרא (1742–1801) in Pressburg. ר' משולם איגרא, one of the great פוסקים of his generation, was concerned that yidden were attending weddings throughout both halves of ספירה — effectively keeping no אבלות. His public proclamation aimed to enforce a single consistent ספירה. The חתם סופר defends the underlying דין (no inherent תרתי דסתרי for the guest) while explaining the contextual concern that animated ר' משולם's ruling. ↩

  24. לקט יושר, ר' יוסף בר משה (תלמיד התרומת הדשן, 15th century). — לקט יושר, written by ר' יוסף בן משה (a תלמיד of the תרומת הדשן, ר' ישראל איסרליין), records the actual halachic and ritual practice of his רב on a day-to-day basis — one of the most valuable Ashkenazic מנהגים documents of the late ראשונים period. His testimony that the תרומת הדשן refrained from singing on מוצאי שבת during ספירה is one of the few early Ashkenazic sources for the broader איסור of any שמחה-related singing during the period. ↩
