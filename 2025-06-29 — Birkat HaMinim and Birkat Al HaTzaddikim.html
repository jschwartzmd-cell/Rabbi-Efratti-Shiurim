ברכת המינים and ברכת על הצדיקים

Shiur of Rabbi Ephrati — Sunday Morning Series on תפילה
Recorded כ"ג סיון תשפ"ה · 29 June 2025  ·  Reworked 3 May 2026

Continuing ברכת המינים: the רמב"ם's framing of רבן גמליאל ביבנה; the meaning of "מינים" as an acronym for "מאמינים בישו הנוצרי"; the ערוך's פירוש with the troubling addition of Benjamin Mussafia (later exposed as a Sabbatean); the long history of Christian censorship — from Nicholas Donin and the Paris Talmud disputation (1242) to the substitution of "עכו"ם" for "מינים"; the original moment at קריעת ים סוף; the precise לשונות of the ברכה through a מעשה from the פריערדיקער רבי. Then ברכת על הצדיקים with its six categories — צדיקים, חסידים, זקני שארית עמך, פליטת בית סופריהם, גרי הצדק, and "ועלינו"; the אריז"ל's extraordinary כוונה on "ושים חלקנו עמהם"; and Rav Yisrael Salanter on Reb Naftali Amsterdam.

I. The רמב"ם's Framing

Picking up from last week. The רמב"ם in הלכות תפילה פ"ב ה"א[1] writes that ברכת המינים was instituted "בימי רבן גמליאל". There is a small מחלוקת on which רבן גמליאל — רבן גמליאל הזקן from בית שני times, or רבן גמליאל the grandson, son of רבן שמעון בן גמליאל. Most likely the grandson, since he was the one in יבנה; רבן גמליאל הזקן was still in the time of the בית המקדש.

The ראשונים do not call this "ברכת המינים". They call it "קללת המינים". It is the only ברכה in שמונה עשרה that consists entirely of קללות — three times a day we curse, and most of us do not even fully know what the words mean.

II. The מינים in Real Time

As we explained last week, the מינים are ישו and his תלמידים. The ראשונים: in their day they invested enormous effort to convince כלל ישראל of ישו's messianic claim and the doctrine of the trinity.

Reb Faivy raised an important קושיא: the trinity doctrine arrived much later than ישו's lifetime. Constantine and the early Church Councils developed it. Looking into this — Reb Faivy is correct: trinitarian theology emerged gradually over the first few Christian centuries, not in one moment. The earliest seeds appear in the first and second centuries; the formal doctrine matured later. This does not affect the תקנה — even without the trinity, ישו's messianic claim and the early מינים's missionary effort were enough.

The רמב"ם stresses: the מינים were part of our own community. Not two separate groups. Christian-Jews — Judeo-Christians — fully integrated into the קהילה, davening in our בתי כנסת. They distorted פסוקים in תנ"ך with reinterpretations. They were so successful that the early Christian movement was, almost entirely, a Jewish movement. Paul (some identify him as שאול) was the figure who carried the message to the Romans, who until then were pagan; the more intellectually inclined Romans found Christianity more compelling than the older paganism. Christianity went global from there.

The תקנה followed: when a מין stood as שליח ציבור and reached "ולמלשינים", he skipped it (he could not curse himself); the קהילה identified him; he was removed.

III. The Original Moment — קריעת ים סוף

The מדרש gives every ברכה in שמונה עשרה a moment when the מלאכי השרת first uttered it. For "ולמלשינים": at קריעת ים סוף, when the Egyptians drowned and כלל ישראל stood at the shore — before "אז ישיר משה", before the שירה — the מלאכים opened: "ברוך אתה ה' שובר אויבים ומכניע זדים".

The ברכה existed in some sense already in the מסורה; רבן גמליאל reactivated it for the new context. שמואל הקטן stood and formalized it[2] for שמונה עשרה.

IV. The Censored נוסחאות and Nicholas Donin

This ברכה is so heavily censored that — uniquely in שמונה עשרה — there is no agreed-upon נוסח. The ראשונים show variation upon variation: "ולמלשינים", "ולמינים", "מלכות הרשעה", "כל הרשעים", "רשעה", "מלכות זדון". The variation is itself the historical record of the ברכה's pressure history.

רש"י's פירוש

רש"י in many places in the בבלי glosses "מינים" as "תלמידי ישו הנוצרי" — students of ישו the Christian. These רש"י's were rediscovered only in recent centuries because they had been censored out of the printed editions for hundreds of years.

Nicholas Donin and the Paris Disputation (1242)

The first יהודי to censor the תלמוד was Nicholas Donin[17], a תלמיד of רבינו יחיאל מפריז[18] — one of the great בעלי התוספות who ran an enormous ישיבה in France in the thirteenth century (and later moved to עכו in ארץ ישראל). Donin was apparently a brilliant בחור who began drifting toward אפיקורסות; רבינו יחיאל מפריז placed him under חרם.

Two years later Donin converted to Christianity, attached himself to the local משטר and the Church, ultimately to the Pope, and informed: "In their תלמוד the Jews mock ישו and his תלמידים." The Paris Disputation followed, and in 1242 the תלמוד was burned.

It was Donin who introduced the substitution that runs through our printed editions: every place the גמרא said "מינים", change to "עכו"ם" (an acronym for עובדי כוכבים ומזלות). The Church accepted the substitution because Donin was a תלמיד חכם and knew where to look. Sometimes they overshot: the word "מין" appears already in בראשית — "למינהו" — meaning kind, species. So when חז"ל wrote "כל מיני קטניות", the censor's substitution rendered it "כל עכו"ם קטניות" — a comic absurdity.

V. The Meaning of "מינים"

The Acronym

A widespread tradition (the source of which is unclear, but worth knowing): "מינים" is itself an ראשי תיבות — "מאמינים בישו הנוצרי". Believers in ישו the Christian. Some ancient נוסחאות read "ולמינים אל תהי תקווה".

The ערוך and Benjamin Mussafia

The ספר הערוך by רבינו נתן מרומי[15] — a תלמיד of רבינו חננאל and רבינו גרשום, the first to author a Talmudic dictionary; quoted constantly by רש"י and תוספות — gives the standard פירוש.

A later edition of the ערוך was prepared by Benjamin Mussafia — sixteenth-century Italian-trained scholar, lived most of his life in Holland, an extraordinary linguist and תלמיד חכם. (To call him "רב" is technically an exaggeration.) It was only later discovered that Mussafia was a Sabbatean. How? Gershom Scholem[16], who researched the Sabbatai Zvi episode and printed the contemporary documents, published the original letter from רבנים who accepted Sabbatai Zvi as משיח. Mussafia was a signatory.

Mussafia's editorial additions to the ערוך include sharp comments — "אביי ורבא had no idea what the משנה's language meant; they did not know Greek or Latin or old Italian, so the גמרא says things unconnected to the משנה's actual words"; on this and similar grounds, many distanced themselves from Mussafia.

On the entry "מין", however, Mussafia adds something striking. The ערוך gives the standard פירוש; Mussafia comments: in those days the חבורת ישו had separated themselves from us — the actual believers — and called themselves "מאמינים": "We are the true believers; we understand תנ"ך properly." חז"ל in יבנה, in response, named them "מינים" — they removed the א' from "מאמינים". Sharp. Mussafia also offers a parallel reading: "מינים" because there are so many kinds of כופרים; ultimately, all the same — תלמידי ישו.

VI. The מעשה in עבודה זרה כ"ז ע"ב

The גמרא in עבודה זרה כ"ז ע"ב[3] records a מעשה from צפורי: a young man who claimed to be a תלמיד of ישו performed apparent healings in his name.

The Rebbe shares: when this מעשה was first mentioned in our shul, I did not immediately recognize it as גמרא — I knew the story but had forgotten its location in ש"ס. This reminds us of the תורה of our רבותינו, particularly Rav Kalish זצ"ל. You could not catch him on a גמרא in מסכת עבודה זרה — he sat in בית מדרש in his suit and tie, fully בקי in this difficult מסכת. Even a hundred years on, it is a מצוה to thank הקדוש ברוך הוא that we merited a יהודי like that — one whom you could not catch on סנהדרין ק"א, ק"ט, or any other סוגיא.

VII. The לשונות of the ברכה — A מעשה

The ברכה's לשון: "תֵעָקֵר, תִשָּׁבֵר, תְּכַלֶּה, תַשְׁפִּילֵם, תַכְנִיעֵם, תְּמַגֵּר" — six different verbs of destruction.

A מעשה from the פריערדיקער רבי of Lubavitch

The Frierdiker Rebbe[20] — רבי יוסף יצחק שניאורסון — had a חסיד whose son, sixteen or seventeen years old, was a good boy who had begun to drift, falling in with בחורים who were not so פרום. The father came to the Rebbe in distress.

The Rebbe — a צדיק, a prince among men, with a manner of speaking with people that was simply remarkable — called the בחור in. He opened the סידור to ברכת המינים and went through the לשונות:

"You know what תֵעָקֵר means?" "Yes — to uproot." "תִשָּׁבֵר?" "To break." "תְּכַלֶּה?" "To finish off, to be rid of them." "תַשְׁפִּילֵם?" "To humiliate." "תַכְנִיעֵם?" "To subdue." "And תְּמַגֵּר?"

The בחור didn't know. The Rebbe answered: "I don't know precisely either, but I know it appears in this list — alongside the others. Each of these is a different mode of destruction we daven down on enemies of כלל ישראל." The משל: "You are a good בחור. But if you attach to those friends, the same לשונות will land on them and on the friend nearby. אוי לרשע אוי לשכנו — רש"י on this week's פרשה."

For the record: תְּמַגֵּר means to fragment, to crumble.

Each לשון is a different mode of השמדה; each is the תפילה we daven down on the enemies of כלל ישראל. The כוונה in our day — the Rebbe shares his own — should reach toward the Ayatollah, toward Mamdani, toward whichever current רשע a person can name. The ברכה's scope expanded across centuries from תלמידי ישו alone to all רשעים of all generations.

The Distinction Between אויבים and זדים

As we discussed last week:

  • אויבים — those with malicious intent against כלל ישראל who have not yet acted on the intent.
  • זדים — those who are actively attempting to harm. Today, anyone in עזה actively seeking to kill a יהודי falls in this category.

VIII. שמואל הקטן — Father of כלל ישראל

שמואל הקטן was נפטר without children. כלל ישראל — every one of us — are his children. Three times a day, every יהודי recites the ברכה he composed. We are the inheritors of the תנא who chose to leave us nothing of his own except the quoted פסוק[4]: "בנפול אויבך אל תשמח".

IX. ברכת על הצדיקים — The Connection to the Previous ברכה

The גמרא in מגילה[5] on the סדר: a פסוק in תהלים[7] — "וכל קרני רשעים אגדע, תרוממנה קרני צדיק" — when the קרני רשעים (the power and pride of the רשעים) is cut off, then the קרני צדיק rise. The סדר in שמונה עשרה mirrors that פסוק[6]: first ברכת המינים — cutting down the רשעים; then על הצדיקים — raising the צדיקים. The ברכה begins with a ו' — direct continuation.

And — this is critical — the תפילה here is not only for לעתיד לבוא. We have the right today, even before משיח, to ask that the רשעים and מינים be uprooted now.

X. The Six Categories

ברכת על הצדיקים contains six distinct categories:

  1. צדיקים
  2. חסידים
  3. זקני שארית עמך בית ישראל
  4. פליטת בית סופריהם
  5. גרי הצדק
  6. "ועלינו"

1–2. צדיקים and חסידים

Some hold צדיקים are the classical צדיקים and חסידים are בעלי תשובה. Each יהודי davens for both. There is a difficulty with this reading: the גמרא in ברכות ל"ד[8] says "במקום שבעלי תשובה עומדים, צדיקים גמורים אינם יכולים לעמוד" — the בעלי תשובה stand at a higher level. If חסידים are בעלי תשובה, they should be listed first. The question stands.

3. זקני שארית עמך בית ישראל

The Rebbe shares his own כוונה: when the Rebbe says these words, he davens for the זקני הדור. He used to daven for מרן הגאון רבי משה פיינשטיין זצ"ל; today, for הגאון רבי אהרן שכטר שליט"א.

"זקן" in the technical sense — the גמרא in קידושין ל"ב ע"ב[9]: "זקן זה שקנה חכמה". Not just elderly — a תלמיד חכם. We need the עמודים; we need the שאריות. If you have a רבי, daven for him in this clause — that he live, be healthy, continue his השפעה.

A מעשה — רבי יצחק לחריף and the Yesod HaAvodah

רבי יצחק לחריף (רבי יצחק שפירא) was the רב of Slonim — the "חריף" after his name reflecting his sharpness. Slonim had a major חסידות presence; the Yesod HaAvodah[25] himself, one of the Slonimer Rebbes, lived there. רבי יצחק, a מתנגד, had his disputes with the חסידים.

He once met the Yesod HaAvodah after the latter had become Rebbe and asked, sharp as ever: "Yesterday you were a simple בעל הבית sitting in the שטיבל. What happened — you changed overnight?"

The Yesod HaAvodah answered: "We have a קבלה from the Baal Shem Tov. The Baal Shem Tov teaches that just as תרומה — when you take a single grain of wheat from a pile and declare 'this is תרומה', what changed in the wheat? Nothing. It is the same wheat as the rest of the pile. But the moment you set it apart and designate it תרומה, anyone non-כהן who eats it is חייב מיתה בידי שמים. The status of the wheat changes.

"The Baal Shem Tov taught that whenever ten יהודים gather and elevate one of them, designate him as רבי — even if he is just an ordinary grain — once he is set apart as תרומה, his prior status becomes חולין. That is what happened to me," he told רבי יצחק. "They made me רבי. I am a simple person, modest — I'll grant you that. But because of that תרומה elevation I have become רבי."

רבי יצחק לחריף answered immediately: "The משנה at the opening of תרומות says 'חומר בתרומה' — you are חמור [a donkey], and you are מחמיר!"

Both stories — the חריפות and the חסידות — leave us, despite the verbal jousting, with the אמת at the core: we believe in real רביים.

4. פליטת בית סופריהם

Who are the סופרים? The גמרא in קידושין ל'[10] says חז"ל, אמוראים and תנאים, were called סופרים because they would count (סופרים from לספור) the letters of the תורה. The קידושין סוגיא on counting the פסוקים, letters, and words of the תורה represents a fascinating תלמוד genre.

But חז"ל also called the מלמדי תינוקות — the רביים in חדר and elementary school who teach young children — סופרים. The פוסקים highlight that this clause should hold the רביים of our children specifically in mind.

רב שמשון רפאל הירש on "פליטת"

רב שמשון רפאל הירש[19] in his ספר התפילה explains the word "פליטה" — survivors, remnants. There are very few of them; most ordinary בעלי בתים do not want the position. The pay is poor. A sixth-grade רבי in Lakewood — what does he earn? They struggle. Some commute to Brooklyn or to the Five Towns to make ends meet.

The Rebbe shares about his younger brother. He had a choice: their grandfather had a business and offered to bring him in. He also had the option to become a sixth-grade רבי. He took the רבי position. Teaching young children is not a career; it is a calling. You are loading boxes into your car at the start of the year. The people doing this with sincerity are מוסרי נפש.

The Rebbe spoke recently with a יהודי in his mid-seventies who learned in a particular Jerusalem חדר as a child. (Years later there was a full court case opened over the beatings the children had received.) Of twenty-seven or twenty-eight boys in his class, only three are פרום today. The cause was the רבי — an אלטער איד, a Holocaust survivor full of bitterness and anger, who hit the boys. The יהודי told the Rebbe: "I asked myself why I'm פרום today and most of them are not. ברוך השם — the רבי had רחמנות on me. I was an orphan; my father had died. So me, he didn't hit. That is why I am פרום today."

In that generation, the פליטת בית סופריהם were people in difficult circumstances doing what they could. Today there are רביים — young people who chose a path in life to teach the next generation תורה. As כלל ישראל, our חיוב per חז"ל is to thank them, bless them, and daven for them — that they continue and succeed.

If you are a grandfather with seven, eight, ten, twenty grandchildren in חדרים across the world — pause and think of their מלמדים. The מלמדים should be good, calm; they should take their medication or grab another cup of coffee before they enter the classroom; they should not hit. Daven for it in this clause every morning.

5. גרי הצדק

The Rebbe shares a teaching he saw long ago in a דרשה from a Prague בית כנסת: this clause is an opportunity to fulfill a מצוה from the תרי"ג that most people never get to fulfill. The פסוק[11]: "ואהבתם את הגר". When was the last time you had explicit כוונה to fulfill the מצוה of loving the גר?

If you have כוונה on "ועל גרי הצדק", you fulfill the מצוה. Three times a day. "ואהבת לרעך כמוך" tied directly to the most marginalized member of כלל ישראל.

The Rebbe shares the story of an חייל in צה"ל who was mid-conversion when October 7 happened. He ran to the בית דין of the רבנות: "Finish my גירות! Speed up my conversion!" They asked why. "Because I am about to deploy to עזה, to the battlefield. I want to die as a יהודי." Such גוים exist today — wanting to become יהודים. They deserve the ברכה.

6. "ועלינו"

Rav Natan asks: how did we end up in this list? We have davened for צדיקים, חסידים, זקני שארית, סופרים, בעלי תשובה, גרים — the great categories of כלל ישראל. And then: "ועלינו" — and on us?

The answer: even though we are not in those clubs, we have an הזדמנות to daven that we be granted entry. "ושים חלקנו עמהם" — make our portion be with them. Three powerful words to dwell on.

Rav Yisrael Salanter to Reb Naftali Amsterdam

Rav Naftali Amsterdam — a great תלמיד of Rav Yisrael Salanter[21] — once said to his רבי: "I have neither the זכות of the רמ"א, nor the לימוד of the גר"א. Who am I? What am I?"

Rav Yisrael Salanter's famous answer: "The אויבערשטער does not want you to be a גאון like the מקובל. He does not want you to be a צדיק like the Baal Shem Tov. He does not want you to be a רב like the חתם סופר. He wants you, Naftali — to be the גאון Naftali can be, the צדיק Naftali can be."

That is the meaning of "ושים חלקנו עמהם". Not that we are about to become a Baal Shem Tov or a Lubavitcher Rebbe. That we maximize our own potential. When we do, we enter the club of צדיקים and חסידים, and we have the right to daven this תפילה daily.

XI. The אריז"ל's פירוש on "חלקנו"

An extraordinary, deep פירוש from the אריז"ל[22] in שער הכוונות, brought by רב חיים ויטאל. The Rebbe shares it as לידיעה, not necessarily as the כוונה we will adopt going forward.

The Mechanism

When a יהודי performs מצוות and later transgresses, do the מצוות stand or fall? The גמרא in סוטה[12] takes this up. The אריז"ל's reading: a עברה does not extinguish earlier מצוות. Rather — and this is the חידוש — the spiritual כוחות generated by those מצוות (the מלאכים created through them) are transferred to צדיקי הדור.

(The חפץ חיים[23] writes the same about לשון הרע: when you speak לשון הרע on someone, your מצוות transfer to him. Children in חדר say playfully "Don't speak לשון הרע on me — now I have all your מצוות!" People take it as a children's joke; the חפץ חיים writes it as תורה.)

Two Types of צדיקים

When the מצוות arrive at the צדיקי הדור, the צדיק faces a choice:

  • Some צדיקים claim them: "Wonderful — all these אורות, all these מלאכים, are now mine."
  • Other צדיקים refuse: "I will do my own מצוות; I do not need yours." They hold the מלאכים and the אורות in trust. When the יהודי who originally generated them does תשובה, those צדיקים return them.

The כוונה

The אריז"ל's teacher instructed him on the כוונה for "חלקנו": "חלקנו" refers to the מצוות we performed and which were temporarily transferred away because of later עברות. We daven: "ושים חלקנו עמהם" — let our portion be with the צדיקים of the second kind, those who will return what is ours when we do תשובה. Let the צדיק holding our מצוות in trust be the kind who gives them back, not the kind who keeps them.

XII. בטחון — Real and Pretended

Underneath the entire ברכה: we are seeking to be among the צדיקים of real בטחון. רבינו אברהם בן הרמב"ם in המספיק לעובדי ה'[24]: everyone uses the words "אמונה" and "בטחון" all the time. Easy to pronounce. True אמונה and בטחון are held by very few — יחידי סגולה.

When we ascend to שמים we will be asked: "צפית לישועה?" — did you wait for משיח? Did you actually have בטחון? The בושה of having spoken these words a thousand times and not actually having held them is the embarrassment we daven not to face. "ושים חלקנו עמהם": place our portion with those whose בטחון was real.

XIII. The Original Moment — יעקב and יוסף

The מדרש[13]: this ברכה was first uttered when יעקב אבינו was נפטר. Before יעקב went down to מצרים, הקדוש ברוך הוא told him[14]: "ויוסף ישית ידו על עיניך" — יוסף will outlive you and place his hands on your eyes (closing them in death, by מנהג). At the close of פרשת ויחי, יוסף stood beside יעקב's deathbed, placed his hands on his father's eyes, kissed him and wept. The הבטחה from years before had been fulfilled exactly.

At that moment, the מלאכים proclaimed: every הבטחה הקדוש ברוך הוא makes to צדיקים is fulfilled. We daven for the same — that we be among those for whom הקדוש ברוך הוא's הבטחות hold true.

XIV. Closing

בעזרת השם, next week — "ובנה ירושלים עיר הקודש במהרה בימינו".

מראה מקומות  ·  Mareh Mekomos

  1. רמב"ם, הלכות תפילה פ"ב ה"א — בימי רבן גמליאל; the תקנה of ברכת המינים. — The רמב"ם in the second פרק of הלכות תפילה records the historical context: "In the days of רבן גמליאל, when the מינים were many in ישראל and were a צרה to ישראל... רבן גמליאל and his בית דין stood and instituted another ברכה in which there is a בקשה from הקדוש ברוך הוא to destroy the מינים." The רמב"ם's framing — מינים as members of כלל ישראל who had drifted into Christian-Jewish syncretism — establishes the foundational picture for the תקנה. ↩
  2. גמרא ברכות כ"ח ע"ב — שמואל הקטן standing and composing ברכת המינים. — The סוגיא in ברכות records the moment: רבן גמליאל asked his תלמידים who could compose the new ברכה; שמואל הקטן stood and composed it. The following year he forgot the wording and labored for hours to recover it; the חכמים permitted him to continue. The סוגיא grounds the unique status of the nineteenth ברכה — composed not by the אנשי כנסת הגדולה but by a single תנא in response to the historical crisis of the מינים. ↩
  3. גמרא עבודה זרה כ"ז ע"ב — the תלמיד ישו in צפורי. — The סוגיא in עבודה זרה records a מעשה from צפורי in the early תנא period: a young man claiming to be a תלמיד of ישו performed apparent healings in his name, drawing יהודים toward Christian beliefs. The סוגיא rules sharply on the prohibition of accepting healing from such figures even in סכנת נפשות. The גמרא is one of the rare uncensored survivals of explicit reference to ישו and his תלמידים in our printed editions. ↩
  4. משלי כ"ד:י"ז — "בנפול אויבך אל תשמח, ובכשלו אל יגל לבך". — שלמה המלך's פסוק, the only משנה contribution of שמואל הקטן in פרקי אבות (פרק ד'). The placement is paradigmatic: the author of ברכת המינים — the most explicitly anti-enemy ברכה in שמונה עשרה — chose as his single contribution to the משנה a warning against rejoicing in the enemy's downfall. The תורה of the תנא and the תורה of the ברכה stand in productive tension. ↩
  5. גמרא מגילה י"ז ע"ב — י"ח ע"א — order of the ברכות; the סדר from תהלים. — The סוגיא on the order of ברכות שמונה עשרה in מגילה derives the structural סדר from פסוקים in תנ"ך, including the placement of על הצדיקים immediately following ברכת המינים. The סוגיא ties this sequence to the פסוק in תהלים ע"ה:י"א: when the קרני רשעים are cut off, the קרני צדיק rise. ↩
  6. גמרא מגילה י"ח ע"א — full development of the סדר. — Continuation of the סוגיא on the structural ordering of שמונה עשרה's ברכות: the גמרא extends the derivation through additional פסוקים establishing the placement of each ברכה. The סדר is not a pragmatic arrangement but a textually grounded structural progression keyed to תנ"ך. ↩
  7. תהלים ע"ה:י"א — "וכל קרני רשעים אגדע, תרוממנה קרני צדיק". — אסף's מזמור in תהלים ע"ה: "And I will cut off all the horns of the רשעים; the horns of the צדיק will be raised up." The פסוק establishes the structural pairing: the destruction of the רשעים' power and the elevation of the צדיקים' power are two sides of one event, not two separate moments. חז"ל's ordering of שמונה עשרה embeds the פסוק's structure: ברכת המינים followed immediately by על הצדיקים. ↩
  8. גמרא ברכות ל"ד ע"ב — "במקום שבעלי תשובה עומדים, אין צדיקים גמורים יכולים לעמוד". — The גמרא's חידוש: in the place where בעלי תשובה stand, even צדיקים גמורים cannot stand. The בעל תשובה who confronted his own חטא and overcame it through תשובה reaches a higher מדרגה than the צדיק who never confronted such a נסיון. The סוגיא raises a difficulty for the standard פירוש of על הצדיקים: if חסידים = בעלי תשובה, the order should reverse — חסידים first, then צדיקים; the ברכה's order is the reverse. ↩
  9. גמרא קידושין ל"ב ע"ב — "זקן זה שקנה חכמה". — The סוגיא in קידושין on the מצוה of מפני שיבה תקום distinguishes between mere chronological "זקן" (the elderly) and the technical דרשה reading: "זקן" as "זה שקנה חכמה" — one who has acquired תורה. The חיוב of standing extends to a young תלמיד חכם as well as to an elderly תלמיד חכם; the elderly without תורה still receive כבוד on the פשט level. The סוגיא grounds the proper כוונה on "זקני שארית עמך" in על הצדיקים: davening for the תלמידי חכמים of the generation, not merely the chronologically aged. ↩
  10. גמרא קידושין ל' ע"א — חז"ל as סופרים who counted the letters of the תורה. — The סוגיא in קידושין records that חז"ל are called סופרים because they would count (לספור) the פסוקים, words, and letters of the תורה — establishing the precision of the textual transmission. The סוגיא reports the count of letters in the תורה, words, and the placement of the middle letter and middle פסוק. Independently, the term סופרים applies to מלמדי תינוקות — elementary school רביים teaching the תורה to children. Both senses converge in the פוסקים's reading of "פליטת בית סופריהם": daven for the תלמידי חכמים who carry on the chain of transmission, including the מלמדים who train the youngest. ↩
  11. דברים י':י"ט — "ואהבתם את הגר, כי גרים הייתם בארץ מצרים". — משה רבינו's פסוק in פרשת עקב: "And you shall love the גר, for you were גרים in מצרים." The פסוק is one of three places the תורה commands אהבת הגר (the others in ויקרא י"ט and elsewhere); it is counted by the רמב"ם as מצוות עשה. The פסוק is the textual ground for the practical teaching that "ועל גרי הצדק" in על הצדיקים can be the daily fulfillment of מצוות אהבת הגר for those who do not encounter גרים in person. ↩
  12. גמרא סוטה כ"א ע"א — interaction of מצוות and עברות; תורה as a force that protects. — The סוגיא in סוטה develops חז"ל's reading of how מצוות and עברות interact: תורה protects, מצוה shields, but the protection has limits. The אריז"ל, drawing on the structural framework of this סוגיא and on the זוהר's parallel discussions, develops the kabbalistic חידוש: a עברה does not annihilate prior מצוות but transfers their spiritual products to צדיקים until the בעל מצוה does תשובה. The mechanism allows the מצוות to remain in the יהודי's ledger while their immediate effects pass elsewhere. ↩
  13. מדרש on "ושים חלקנו" — יעקב אבינו and יוסף; the fulfillment of הקדוש ברוך הוא's הבטחה. — The מדרש tradition tying each ברכה in שמונה עשרה to a moment in תנ"ך places the original "ברוך משען ומבטח לצדיקים" at יעקב אבינו's deathbed: יוסף places his hands on his father's eyes, fulfilling the הבטחה from בראשית מ"ו:ד. The מלאכים, witnessing the precise fulfillment of a הבטחה made years earlier, opened with the ברכה: הקדוש ברוך הוא is the support and security of the צדיקים; the הבטחות made to them are kept exactly. We daven daily that we be among the צדיקים for whom His הבטחות stand. ↩
  14. בראשית מ"ו:ד — "אנכי ארד עמך מצרימה ואנכי אעלך גם עלה, ויוסף ישית ידו על עיניך". — הקדוש ברוך הוא's הבטחה to יעקב אבינו at the night-vision in באר שבע on the eve of his descent to מצרים: "I will go down with you to מצרים; I will surely bring you up; and יוסף will place his hand on your eyes." The third clause — that יוסף will outlive יעקב and close his eyes (the standard מנהג of a son closing the eyes of his deceased father) — is fulfilled at the close of פרשת ויחי (בראשית נ':א'): "ויפל יוסף על פני אביו, ויבך עליו וישק לו". The פסוק grounds the מדרש's placement of the original "ברוך משען ומבטח לצדיקים" at this moment of fulfilled promise. ↩
  15. ספר הערוך (רבינו נתן מרומי) — entry on "מין"; later edition with additions by Benjamin Mussafia. — רבינו נתן בן יחיאל מרומי (the בעל הערוך, c. 1035–1106, Rome; תלמיד of רבינו חננאל, רבינו גרשום מאור הגולה's grandsons, and others) authored the ספר הערוך, the first major Talmudic-aramaic dictionary. The ערוך is cited extensively by רש"י (who was a contemporary), תוספות, and the later ראשונים. Benjamin Mussafia (c. 1606–1675, born in Spain, raised in Hamburg, lived most of his life in Amsterdam; physician and linguist; signatory of the rabbinic letter accepting Sabbatai Zvi as משיח — see פנים אחרות on the next entry) prepared a later edition of the ערוך with extensive philological additions. His comment on the entry "מין": חז"ל in יבנה coined the term by removing the א' from "מאמינים", the self-designation of the early Christian-Jews. ↩
  16. Gershom Scholem (1897–1982; founding chair of Jewish Mysticism at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem; foundational scholar of קבלה and the Sabbatean movement) — published in his foundational study Sabbatai Sevi: The Mystical Messiah (1973; original Hebrew 1957) and in earlier monographs the documentary record of the seventeenth-century Sabbatean movement, including the rabbinic letter from Hamburg accepting Sabbatai Zvi as משיח in 1665–1666. Benjamin Mussafia was among the signatories. The discovery exposed the previously unsuspected Sabbatean affiliation of a חכם whose linguistic additions to the ערוך remain widely consulted; the affiliation explains certain of his sharp comments against the אמוראים — characteristic of Sabbatean polemic.

  17. Nicholas Donin of La Rochelle (active first half of the thirteenth century; born יהודי, תלמיד of רבינו יחיאל מפריז) — placed under חרם by רבינו יחיאל for heretical views; converted to Christianity around 1225 and joined the Franciscan order; in 1238 presented Pope Gregory IX with a list of thirty-five accusations against the תלמוד, including charges of blasphemy against ישו. The Paris Disputation of 1240 — the public trial of the תלמוד in Paris before King Louis IX with רבינו יחיאל, רבי משה מקוצי, רבי יהודה בן דוד, and רבי שמואל בן שלמה מפלייז defending — followed. The verdict came in 1242: twenty-four cartloads of תלמוד manuscripts burned in Paris. Donin's substitution scheme — replacing "מינים" with "עכו"ם", "גוים", or "כותים" in printed editions — became the template for subsequent Christian censorship of the תלמוד, which persisted in printed editions until twentieth-century scholarly recoveries.

  18. רבינו יחיאל מפריז — one of the בעלי התוספות; defender at the Paris Disputation; later עליה to עכו. — רבינו יחיאל בן יוסף מפריז (c. 1200–1265) was one of the great בעלי התוספות of the thirteenth century, ראש ישיבה of the ישיבה of Paris, with hundreds of תלמידים. He served as the principal Jewish defender at the Paris Disputation of 1240 against Nicholas Donin (his former תלמיד). Following the burning of the תלמוד, he made עלייה with his תלמידים to ארץ ישראל in 1257 and established a ישיבה in עכו. He is buried in ארץ ישראל; his חרם on Donin years before the conversion is recorded in subsequent תוספות sources as an early sign of Donin's drift. ↩
  19. רב שמשון רפאל הירש, ספר התפילה — "פליטת בית סופריהם" as the small surviving remnant. — רב שמשון רפאל הירש (1808–1888, founding רב of קהילת ישורון in Frankfurt; foundational figure of Torah im Derech Eretz), in his commentary on the סידור (The Hirsch Siddur; Hebrew: ספר התפילות), explains the word "פליטה" in "פליטת בית סופריהם": a small surviving remnant. The מלמדי תינוקות in every generation are few, drawn from those willing to accept the difficult conditions of the work — modest pay, demanding hours, emotional and physical labor. The תפילה daven that this פליטה survive and continue. ↩

Rav Yisrael Salanter (רבי ישראל ליפקין מסלנט זצ"ל, 1810–1883, Lithuania; founder of the מוסר movement) — and his תלמיד Rav Naftali Amsterdam (1832–1916, ראש ישיבה in Kovno; one of Rav Yisrael's three principal תלמידים, alongside Rav Itzele of Peterburg and Rav Simcha Zissel Ziv of Kelm). The exchange — Reb Naftali asking how he can amount to anything when he lacks the זכות of the רמ"א and the לימוד of the גר"א, Rav Yisrael answering that הקדוש ברוך הוא wants him to be the צדיק Naftali can be — circulates as a paradigmatic מוסר story across the generations. The point: "ושים חלקנו עמהם" is not a request to become someone else but a request to maximize the יחוד of one's own נשמה.

  22. אריז"ל in שער הכוונות, transmitted by רב חיים ויטאל — "חלקנו" and the transfer of מצוות to צדיקי הדור. — רבי יצחק לוריא (the אריז"ל, 1534–1572, Tzfas), as transmitted in שער הכוונות and פרי עץ חיים by רבי חיים ויטאל, develops the kabbalistic mechanism of מצוות transfer: when a יהודי performs a מצוה and later transgresses, the אורות and מלאכים generated by the מצוה are not annihilated but redirected to צדיקי הדור. The צדיק may either claim them as his own or hold them in trust until the original בעל מצוה does תשובה. The כוונה for "ושים חלקנו עמהם": that the צדיקים currently holding our transferred מצוות be of the second kind — those who will return them upon our תשובה. The חידוש reframes the ברכה from a request for spiritual proximity to צדיקים into a request about the disposition of one's own dispersed זכויות. ↩
  23. חפץ חיים, הלכות לשון הרע / שמירת הלשון — speaking לשון הרע transfers מצוות to the victim. — רבי ישראל מאיר הכהן מראדין (the חפץ חיים, 1838–1933), in חפץ חיים (1873) on the laws of לשון הרע and in the parallel שמירת הלשון, develops the kabbalistic-halachic principle that speaking לשון הרע transfers the speaker's זכויות to the victim — a teaching that parallels the אריז"ל's mechanism of מצוות transfer through עברה. The teaching grounds the popular children's-חדר phrase: "Don't speak לשון הרע on me — now I have all your מצוות!" What sounds like a joke is, in the חפץ חיים's framing, kabbalistically literal: the speaker's spiritual חשבון is debited and the listener's is credited. ↩
  24. רבינו אברהם בן הרמב"ם, המספיק לעובדי ה' — true אמונה and בטחון as the rare possession of יחידי סגולה. — רבינו אברהם בן הרמב"ם (1186–1237, Egypt; son of the רמב"ם and his successor as head of the Jewish community), in המספיק לעובדי ה' (an extensive Judeo-Arabic spiritual-philosophical work; only fragments survive, primarily on בטחון and תפילה), develops a sharp חידוש: while everyone uses the words אמונה and בטחון casually, true אמונה and בטחון in the technical sense — actually living in unwavering reliance on הקדוש ברוך הוא across the practical צרות of life — are held by very few יחידי סגולה in any generation. The כוונה in "ושים חלקנו עמהם" on the level of בטחון: that we be granted real בטחון, the rare kind, and not the cheap surface בטחון of casual usage. ↩

The Yesod HaAvodah of Slonim (רבי אברהם וויינברג זצ"ל, 1804–1883, founder of חסידות סלאנים; תלמיד of the Kobriner Rebbe and the Lechovitcher Rebbe; author of יסוד העבודה) and רבי יצחק חריף (רבי יצחק שפירא; nineteenth-century רב of Slonim, known by the appellation "חריף") — the verbal exchange on the תרומה analogy circulates in חסידות oral מסורה as a paradigmatic example of the encounter between חסידות and the מתנגד world.
