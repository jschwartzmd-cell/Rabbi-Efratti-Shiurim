Today's שיעור begins with a personal note — a story of השגחה פרטית and a יארצייט I almost forgot — and then turns to קריאת התורה: the surprising history of who said בריך שמיה and who did not, the בני אשכנז's caution toward קבלה, and a series of הלכות and מנהגים for honoring the ספר תורה as it travels from ארון to בימה and back.

I. A Personal Word — השגחה פרטית and a Yahrtzeit

Two יארצייטן

I am sponsoring today's breakfast and שיעור for two reasons. My father was נפטר nine months ago; his יארצייט will be right after פסח. My grandfather — my father's father — has his יארצייט in כסלו. I was very close to my grandfather. I consider myself his תלמיד. I have spoken to the עולם about him several times.

This year is the first יארצייט of my grandfather without my father here to remind me. Every year without fail, my father would call me: "Light a candle for סבא. You were his תלמיד. Learn a שטיקעל from his ספר." I spent years learning with him — ages eighteen to twenty-one, during my time in ארץ ישראל. Almost every שבת I would visit him in פתח תקוה and we would learn. He wrote extensively — ספרים called ועמודים לעמודים and תלמוד לתלמידים — works on how to teach גמרא in the תיכון system in Israel, where students learn קודש and חול together. He approached learning with a researcher's methodology, an academic lens. The works did not become popular in the ישיבה world, but they were profound.

The Story

This year, for the first time, I completely forgot the יארצייט. Until one hour before שקיעה.

A man I had never met — sent by Rabbi Meyer Travis from our adopted קהילה in ארץ ישראל — arrived at my door to deliver a new ספר. Rabbi Travis had published a controversial work dealing with contemporary הלכה issues, including very sensitive areas like שאלות in transgender; the ספר had received הסכמות from a number of brave רבנים, including one from our own רב זצ״ל.

As this stranger was leaving my house, he asked: "You are Avishai Efrati's son?" Yes. "Isn't today your grandfather's יארצייט?" The man happened to be the gabbai at a ותיקין minyan on שבת morning where my father had davened — a minyan I did not even know existed. He remembered my grandfather's יארצייט from the gabbai's lists. הקדוש ברוך הוא sent the reminder through a complete stranger, with a ספר from ארץ ישראל, one hour before שקיעה. There was just enough time to light a candle and learn לעילוי נשמתו. That is why I am sponsoring today's שיעור.

Between Two Worlds My grandfather was a great תלמיד חכם. He learned by the Satmar רבי and was one of the best בחורים in סאטמאר ישיבה. When he came to ארץ ישראל he became close to the חזון איש and was a חברותא of his. When he moved to ארץ ישראל he changed his name from Weiss to Ephrati — what was called "עיברות השם," Hebraicizing the name. He changed his השקפה and became a professor of תלמוד at Bar-Ilan, yet always held himself as a תלמיד חכם. My father took a different path entirely — he went to פוניבז' and became a פארברענטער yid, a fervent ben Torah, with a different approach. I once attended יום העצמאות services with my grandfather in his shul in פתח תקוה — a different world from the one my father chose, though both were worlds of תורה and יראת שמים.

II. בריך שמיה — Why Frankfurt Doesn't Say It

An Embarrassing Question

Last week we began discussing בריך שמיה, the passage we recite when opening the ארון קודש. After last week's שיעור someone informed me — I was embarrassed by my ignorance — that in פראנקפורט, they did not say בריך שמיה at all. This deserves explanation.

When Do We Say It?

As we discussed last week, some נוסחאות only say בריך שמיה on שבת, יום טוב, and ראש חודש. Why? Because בריך שמיה says בריך כתרך — blessed be Your כתר. We invoke the קדושה של כתר only when we say כתר יתנו לך — and that is specifically on שבת, יום טוב, and ראש חודש.

בריך שמיה Is Relatively New

בריך שמיה is a piece quoted from the זוהר[1]. As we have discussed many times, much of our סדר התפילה comes from the האריז״ל — even those of us who do not consider ourselves מקובלים. The זוהר was first printed in 1557 in קושטא. Before that it was known only to יחידי סגולה. After printing — and especially through the האריז״ל's influence — בריך שמיה spread rapidly into Ashkenaz and Sephardi siddurim. Within a few decades of the זוהר's printing, בריך שמיה appeared in nearly every סידור. By the mid-1600s it was standard practice. Except in פראנקפורט.

III. The Frankfurt Position — דברי קהלת

The יקעס and Their מנהגים

There is a ספר called דברי קהלת[2], a collection of מנהגי פרענקפורט. פרענקפורט was the bastion of יקקישע מנהגים — the central קהילה from which German Jewish customs emanated. In the late 1700s and early 1800s a yid named רב שלמה זלמן גייגער served as a דיין there for many years. The Geigers were an established Yekkish family. רב שלמה זלמן compiled the מנהגי פרענקפורט in his ספר because he believed it was crucial for future generations to preserve the authentic מנהגים.

The Clear Ruling

In דברי קהלת רב גייגער writes plainly: אין אומרים בריך שמיה בפרענקפורט. Why? Because it comes from the זוהר, and in פרענקפורט they stayed far away from זוהר.

The Two Brothers

The Geiger family represents one of the most painful dichotomies in German Jewish history. רב שלמה זלמן had a brother — אברהם גייגער, also known as Dr. Abraham Geiger. Dr. Abraham Geiger was a brilliant תלמיד חכם who became the intellectual father of the Reform movement. He attended the University of Bonn, where he was actually a חברותא of רב שמשון רפאל הירש. רב הירש tried desperately to pull his brilliant friend back from the left; ultimately they clashed bitterly. Dr. Geiger engaged in biblical criticism, removed ירושלים from the נוסח התפילה, and famously declared, "We do not need ירושלים — Germany is our ירושלים." Berlin became their ירושלים. As the משך חכמה[3] writes at the end of פרשת בחקתי, the Reform movement claimed Berlin as their ירושלים. His brother, רב שלמה זלמן, remained traditional and faithful to the authentic מנהגים of פרענקפורט.

IV. רב שמשון רפאל הירש and the Future of German Jewry

The Vision

There was significant מחלוקת between רב שמשון רפאל הירש and other German רבנים — רב יעקב עטלינגער (the ערוך לנר) and רב שלמה זלמן בעמברגער (the ווירצבורגער רב) — over how to handle the future. רב הירש saw that the Reform movement was tearing the soul out of German Judaism — introducing organs into shul, changing הלכה, promoting assimilation. His vision was to breathe life and ruach back into Yiddishkeit through his philosophy of תורה עם דרך ארץ. He introduced practices like הקפות on שמחת תורה with dancing — something that had simply not been done in Germany before. Traditional German רבנים opposed these innovations: they preferred their old, established מנהגים, and were suspicious of anything new, even with the best of intentions.

Secession from Reform

When German civil law first permitted formal secession from the unified קהילה, רב הירש decided the time had come. He issued a קול קורא that every family must secede and could not remain together with the Reform. He founded קהל עדת ישורון — KAJ. רב בעמברגער and others opposed the move. The Reform were then part and parcel of the community — a spectrum from right to left, "one happy family." They argued: "Why do this? It will be a חורבן לדורות!"

רב הירש was not immediately successful. Only eighty families followed him at first. We speak of him as the leader of German Jewry — but the reality is that the truly frum, ultra-Orthodox German yidden who upheld his movement numbered eighty families. Yet his legacy as הירש's champion, hero, and גדול endures, and his influence on כלל ישראל far beyond German Jewry is incalculable. Anyone who reads his writings knows: the most beautiful ספר on פרשת השבוע is the Hirsch Chumash. The five-volume English edition now available is extraordinary.

V. The יקעס and קבלה — Historical Context

The Real Question

The question is not why פרענקפורט did not say בריך שמיה. The real question is: why did we start saying it? For anyone seriously studying Jewish history, it is essential to understand the historic attitude toward קבלה — חכמת הנסתר, the secrets of the תורה — among בני אשכנז.

We — those of us whose ancestors came from Europe — historically frowned upon learning קבלה, studying קבלה, and living life according to קבלה. Even though today the האריז״ל's teachings are embedded in our סידור, this was not always the case.

The Shadow of שבתי צבי

Why the resistance? Because of שבתי צבי in the 1600s. He rose up and declared that the true secret of Yiddishkeit was not in תלמוד and פוסקים but in קבלה and זוהר. He led one of the most catastrophic false-messianic movements in Jewish history, causing devastation across the Jewish world. The worst transgression of all, חז״ל teach us, is when one takes תורה and twists it for personal advantage. The holy is turned into the profane. שבתי צבי did exactly that — and ultimately apostatized to Islam.

The חרם in Amsterdam

The trauma of שבתי צבי created a profound mistrust of קבלה among Ashkenaz, especially among the יקעס. They associated קבלה with danger, with deviation from the solid ground of גמרא and הלכה. The opposition reached such heights that in אמסטרדם a חרם was issued: anyone who possessed a copy of the של״ה הקדוש — a ספר we quote regularly today — was required to surrender it to the קהילה within three days for גניזה. The decree appears in the פנקס הקהילה, the communal record. That was the intensity of the fear.

The חתם סופר's Position

The חתם סופר[4] writes in a תשובה — a stunning statement — that he does not approve of people learning קבלה, especially זוהר. Despite our constant citing of the זוהר today, the חתם סופר testified that he never heard a single word from his three great רבנים — רבי נתן אדלר, רבי זלמן, and others — about זוהר הקדוש. Perhaps they learned it בייחידות, but they never taught it publicly. Open the תשובות חתם סופר and you will scarcely find a citation of the זוהר. He understood the danger.

The מהרש״א

Even before שבתי צבי, the מהרש״א[5] (the cousin of the רמ״א, whose קבר we visited in Lublin) writes: I do not want to hear from זוהר. We follow תלמוד and פוסקים. Once you venture into זוהר, who knows where you will end up?

The נודע ביהודה Against לשם יחוד

The נודע ביהודה[6] — רבי יחזקאל לנדא, chief rabbi of פראג in the eighteenth century — writes a pointed תשובה: "I do not understand. Every day you say before performing a מצוה, לשם יחוד קודשא בריך הוא ושכינתיה. Before תפילין, before טלית, before לולב, before everything. Do you know what you are saying? The whole formula comes from זוהר, from קבלה. You do not understand it — do not say it." In two תשובות he forbids saying לשם יחוד. His תלמידים, including רבי צבי הירש, waged war against those who recited לשם יחוד, precisely because it originates from קבלה.

The Principle — הלכה Over קבלה

Much of this — though not all — relates to שבתי צבי, who ruined it for everyone. He learned זוהר and קבלה and changed הלכה based on them, which is absolutely forbidden. The רמ״א[7] writes in דרכי משה: whenever there is a conflict between קבלה and הלכה, always follow הלכה, never קבלה.

VI. The Transformation — The בעל שם טוב and Our Generation

What Changed?

Why does everyone learn זוהר today? The answer lies with the בעל שם טוב, who spread the light of חסידות. חסידות is entirely based on זוהר. The בעל שם טוב taught that in our generation, for משיח to come, we need זוהר and the teachings of קבלה.

The חפץ חיים himself told בחורים in his ישיבה in ראדין: even if you do not understand what you are saying, read it. Much of it is מדרשים and חז״ל. The parts you understand are good; the parts you do not understand are still a זכות to recite. Something genuinely shifted in our generation. Today we accept זוהר — even with the חתם סופר's reservations on certain components — as the work of רבי שמעון בר יוחאי. (Some elements may have been added by later חכמים from the twelfth or thirteenth centuries, but every word of זוהר is קדוש to us today.) The זוהר was canonized — perhaps by the האריז״ל.

פרענקפורט's Steadfast Stand

פרענקפורט, in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, held steadfastly to its caution toward זוהר because of the dangers it had seen. The יקעס do not say בריך שמיה even today. I am certain רב הירש agreed with this — he eventually became the רב of פרענקפורט and led פרענקפורט Jewry triumphantly.

VII. The Two Difficult Phrases in בריך שמיה

"בר אלהים" — Son of God

Another reason they did not say it: in בריך שמיה you say לא על אינש רחיצנא ולא על בר אלהים סמיכנא — I rely not on man nor on a "son of God." Who is "בר אלהים"?

The רבנים of the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries who opposed בריך שמיה argued: we cannot say this in shul. In that era every תפילה, every ברכה, every word uttered in shul was scrutinized by the authorities — especially the Christian authorities. "This is what we are going to say every time we take out a ספר תורה — that we do not rely on Jesus? They claim Jesus is the son of God. We do not want to utter the words. We do not want to inflame the Catholics against the Jewish people. Let us refrain entirely."

The ירושלמי on בר אלהים

And there is something deeper. The ירושלמי in מסכת שבת[8] recounts that when נבוכדנצר threw חנניה, מישאל, and עזריה into the fire, and הקדוש ברוך הוא made a נס for them, the פסוק in דניאל פרק ג describes how נבוכדנצר saw the three of them alive — the fire did not touch them — plus a fourth in the fire who appeared כבר אלהים. A מלאך — perhaps גבריאל — who saved them from the fire looked like a בר אלהים. The ירושלמי teaches: when נבוכדנצר uttered those words — "son of God" — a מלאך came and struck him on the mouth and said: take it back! You are not allowed to speak about מלאכים like that. Do not call them בר אלהים.

The lesson: we never refer to anyone as בר אלהים. In תורה we do say אלהים as חול — meaning דיינים, anyone with halachic authority. But at the end of בראשית, when the פסוק says בני אלהים, the תרגום renders it דיינא — judges, not אלהים. We are forbidden to call them אלהא; that is off limits. So they opposed saying this phrase even though we are obviously talking about מלאכים — not Jesus. We do not call מלאכים בר אלהים. It is dangerous. Another reason they did not say it.

"אנא עבדא דקדשא בריך הוא"

Last week — without realizing the full context — I mentioned that one needs serious חוצפה to declare אנא עבדא דקדשא בריך הוא: I am a servant of הקדוש ברוך הוא. Can you really claim that? I quoted my רב in פלאטבוש who taught that one should say I want to become a servant. However, this week I discovered that the phrase is in fact a פסוק in תהלים — we are quoting דוד המלך. That changes the picture entirely; we say it because דוד himself said it.

The יעב״ץ on Authorship of the זוהר

Regarding the מחלוקת whether the זוהר was authored by רבי שמעון בר יוחאי — this is dangerous territory. The יעב״ץ (רבי יעקב עמדן) wrote an entire ספר called מטפחת ספרים[9] arguing that the זוהר was not authored by רשב״י. These are dangerous claims to repeat publicly. If the section is a piece from רשב״י, and רשב״י himself elsewhere says you may not say בר אלהים, that is a real קושיא — and the יעב״ץ uses it as a proof that this part of the זוהר was not written by רשב״י. Regardless, we say it.

VIII. The חפץ חיים's Personal Practice

Regarding אנא עבדא דקדשא בריך הוא, I discovered something remarkable. In משנה ברורה[10] the חפץ חיים writes two or three times that we say בריך שמיה — that is our מנהג. (It was relatively new in his day, though already established.)

His grandson, Hillel Zaks — the זאקס family is well known; one zaks is a son-in-law of the חפץ חיים, and מענדל זאקס was a ראש ישיבה in YU — testified that his grandfather, the חפץ חיים, did not personally say בריך שמיה, even though he wrote in his ספר that one should. This is not the only place where the חפץ חיים did not adhere to his own משנה ברורה. For us, everything is משנה ברורה; for him, on a few items, a higher private standard. Why? It is explained that the חפץ חיים did not want to utter the words אנא עבדא דקדשא בריך הוא. That is for whom we daven. We say it. The בעל שם טוב taught that when you say בריך שמיה with כוונה it is the most beautiful thing in the world and can open the gates of רחמים. We say it.

IX. גדלו ה' איתי — Six Words for Six Steps

The אבודרהם's Count

After taking the ספר תורה out of the ארון we say גדלו ה' אתי ונרוממה שמו יחדיו — exalt השם with me, and together let us elevate His name. This is a פסוק from תהלים. But why specifically this פסוק?

The אבודרהם[11] counted every word of תפילה. He noticed that this פסוק contains exactly six words. He brings, in the name of מחזור ויטרי and other ראשונים, the reason — and it is fascinating.

The Story of דוד המלך and the ארון

When דוד המלך brought the ארון הברית from בית עובד אדום הגתי to עיר דוד, the journey is famously recounted in שמואל ב'[12]. The ארון had been captured by the פלשתים and ended up by עובד אדום. The גמרא at the end of מסכת ברכות says he was blessed extraordinarily — sextuplets, his daughters had sextuplets — within a year his family had multiplied tremendously, and everyone recognized it was the merit of the ארון.

Eventually דוד decided it was time to bring the ארון to עיר דוד. As they brought it, דוד המלך was dancing — jumping, with far more intensity than we dance on שמחת תורה. מיכל בת שאול looked out the window and said to דוד, "Is this how a מלך dances? This is how a פּראָסטאַק — a peasant — dances!" Because of that mockery she had no children. One must never mock someone who humbles himself in honor of the תורה. The greatest כבוד is to be משפיל oneself, to dance before it, to express אהבה for the ארון and the לוחות inside.

The נביא tells us that the procession was very slow. Every six steps they put down the ארון and brought קרבנות. (The גמרא discusses different שיטות regarding what was brought.) Imagine the scene: דוד dancing, and every six steps a stop for קרבנות.

Says the אבודרהם: when we take the ספר תורה out of the ארון and walk it to the בימה, we are reenacting that very march. The six words of גדלו ה' אתי ונרוממה שמו יחדיו mirror those six steps, celebrating הקדוש ברוך הוא at every interval.

X. הלכות of Greeting and Escorting the ספר תורה

Walking with the תורה

What is the הלכה about coming up to the ארון to greet the ספר תורה? It is not a true הלכה למעשה but a beautiful מנהג. What is הלכה למעשה is that if the ספר תורה passes by your seat, you should escort it. The שלחן ערוך says one walks the ספר תורה to the בימה. The האריז״ל used to walk the ספר תורה all the way from his seat to the בימה — beautiful, though not always possible in a crowded shul.

Looking at the Letters During הגבהה

Another practice of the האריז״ל that ספרדים maintain to this day: they perform הגבהה before the קריאה, while we do it after. The שלחן ערוך follows מסכת סופרים[13] — a מאמר חז״ל that when you pick up the ספר תורה you do הגבהה right before the קריאה.

The האריז״ל would come to the בימה, walk the ספר תורה there, stop, and during the הגבהה look carefully at the ספר to see if he could read a word or letter. He said this brings אור גדול על נשמתך — a great light onto your נשמה. That is the language of עץ חיים.

There is also a מנהג among the מקובלים: if you find a word whose first letter is the first letter of your name, that is your connection with one of the שישים רבוא — the six hundred thousand. (It is not literally true that the תורה contains six hundred thousand letters, but the concept stands: there is an ענין of שישים רבוא, and each נשמה has a connection with the תורה.) The מנהג continues today — you will see people running to the ספר תורה after הגבהה to look. It is a סגולה for שמירת עינים, for pure eyes, and brings unbelievable light into the נשמה.

XI. Why אשכנזים Do הגבהה After the קריאה

We perform הגבהה afterward. Why, when the שלחן ערוך and the מקור in חז״ל say to do it before? The מגן אברהם[14] explains. What if there is a mistake in the ספר תורה? If you do הגבהה before, and a פסול is later discovered, you cannot honestly say וזאת התורה אשר שם משה לפני בני ישראל. So we wait. Only after the entire קריאה of the day has been completed without trouble — ברוך השם, smooth, no problem — do we lift it and declare with full conviction: this is exactly the תורה, word for word, letter for letter, that הקדוש ברוך הוא gave to משה רבינו. There is a known מעשה of a פסול discovered after וזאת התורה had been said. Better to wait.

XII. Uncommon כיבודים

There is a כיבוד I have never seen sold in any shul, and it would be wonderful to implement here. The גואל who performs גלילה — rolling and binding the ספר תורה — receives the מעיל and the גארטל from someone standing by the בימה. Consider: the very act of picking up the מעיל from the בימה and handing it to the מגלל is itself a כיבוד מופלג, a distinguished honor. I have never seen any shul sell it, but even if it is not sold, one should give it as a כיבוד. The same אור זרוע[15] that teaches us the כיבוד of opening the ארון and removing the ספר תורה teaches that handing the מעיל is equally a כיבוד.

XIII. The מנהג of פתיחה for an Expectant Father

There is a מנהג to give the כיבוד of פתיחה — opening the ארון — to a yid whose wife is expecting. Where does this come from? It is relatively new. The חיד״א in מחזה כהונה תמרי תקניין[16] brings it explicitly. He writes that it is a סגולה: just as the expectant father opens the קודש הקדשים, so too should הקדוש ברוך הוא open the רחם, and may everything proceed smoothly.

The חיד״א adds: if a woman is מקשה ללדת — having a difficult labor — what should one do? Someone once came to our גדול and asked, "What do I say? What do I do for my wife?" He answered, "Say יצחק." Why יצחק? Because יצחק brings forth children easily. Our מנהג is to give פתיחה. רב חיים פלאג'י[17], one of the great ספרדי גדולים, writes an important הלכה: make sure that whoever opens the ארון for this יולדת also closes it. Whoever receives the פתיחה should also do the סגירה — because, חס ושלום, you do not want the רחם to remain open.

XIV. The Sanctity of Silence at הוצאת ספר תורה

I must address something — and I am giving מוסר to myself first, חס ושלום. Sometimes we get carried away in those minutes between opening the ספר תורה and everyone saying גדלו ה' איתי. People are walking the ספר תורה, the gabbai is figuring out עליות, and there is שמועסן until the ספר is opened and the gabbai begins calling.

It is not supposed to be like that. The חיד״א gives sharp מוסר against talking while the ספר is being carried, placed on the בימה, and opened. It is an exceedingly auspicious moment. In strict הלכה I cannot point you to a שלחן ערוך that explicitly forbids speech during these seconds, but the חיד״א says: be careful, do not talk. Say the words. Say לך ה' הגדולה. Say רחמנא ירחם עמא מסכנא — a beautiful תפילה composed by רבי יוחנן בן זכאי for עמא דמסכנא. The חיד״א says that בריך שמיה — discussed last week — should ideally be said before we take out the ספר. Some קהילות had the מנהג to say it while walking the ספר; we say it before. Either way: do not talk.

There is even a שאלה among the אחרונים: may one stop פסוקי דזמרה to say בריך שמיה and the other פסוקים? The מהרש״ג — רבי שמעון גרינפלד, the שם אריה[18], one of the great גדולים in Hungary — has an entire תשובה. In the end he holds you should not stop פסוקי דזמרה — but כדי בכך, such is the importance of these פסוקים for greeting the ספר תורה, that he genuinely entertains it.

XV. The Meaning of ברכו

When the gabbai announces ברכו, what is he really doing? The ראשונים[19] — all of them, the מפרשי תפילה — explain that ברכו is the gabbai's call for silence. Rabosai, hush — we are about to have ברכות, ברכת התורה from the עולה. Be silent. Answer אמן. That is the message every week. Just as משה רבינו, before he sang the שירה of אז ישיר, called out האזינו — "listen with me" — the gabbai is calling us to the same listening. Next week בעזרת השם we will discuss ברכו and ברכת התורה in depth.

XVI. A Possible New מנהג in our Shul — Splitting the Honors

Let me float a suggestion — though this is the gabbai's domain. Last night I discovered something in one of the ראשונים. The אור זרוע (רבי יצחק מוינא, chief rabbi of Vienna, one of the בעלי התוספות), brought down also in the מרדכי[20], describes an incident in shul where a fight erupted. The gabbai had sold the כיבוד of פתיחת הארון and removing the ספר תורה to a yid; the שליח ציבור protested: "What do you mean? This is mine! I am the חזן, I daven שחרית, I take the ספר תורה out of the ארון and carry it to the בימה. Why are you giving it to someone else?"

The מרדכי rules that each individual action by the תורה can be split and assigned to different people. One person opens the ארון; another removes the ספר; the שליח ציבור carries it to the בימה. That is, in fact, the מנהג we already practice — exactly that מרדכי is its source.

סיכום

Have a wonderful week. May we all be זוכה to honor the ספר תורה with the כבוד and יראת שמים it deserves — silent at the open ארון, attentive at the בימה, careful with every כיבוד, and ever conscious of how we carry, look at, and speak about הקדוש ברוך הוא's תורה.

Pre-printing, the זוהר circulated in manuscript among יחידי סגולה; the בריך שמיה passage came into mainstream סדורים through the אריז"ל's influence (16th century Tzfat) and especially via שערי ציון of ר' נתן נטע הנובר (Prague 1662). ↩
  2. דברי קהלת, ר' שלמה זלמן גייגער (Frankfurt-am-Main, 1862). — ר' שלמה זלמן גייגער (1792–1878) was a דיין in Frankfurt and the leading codifier of מנהגי פראנקפורט in the post-emancipation era. דברי קהלת (Frankfurt 1862) records the city's distinctive מנהגים of תפילה and קהילה life. He was the older brother of Abraham Geiger (1810–1874), the architect of the German Reform movement; the contrast between the brothers is a central episode in 19th-century German Jewish history. ↩
  3. משך חכמה, ר' מאיר שמחה הכהן מדווינסק (1843–1926), end of פרשת בחקתי. — The famous concluding פרשה of משך חכמה on ויקרא כ"ו: a stark prophetic warning that those Jews who declare "Berlin is our Jerusalem" will be uprooted; that כלל ישראל's claim to history runs through ירושלים, not through any diaspora capital. Written before the Shoah; widely cited as a prescient text on the cost of the Reform repudiation of ירושלים. The שיעור ties ר' מאיר שמחה's warning to Abraham Geiger's removal of ירושלים from the Reform liturgy. ↩
  4. שו"ת חתם סופר on study of קבלה.
    Note: The חתם סופר (ר' משה סופר, Pressburg, 1762–1839) addresses the question of public study of זוהר and קבלה in several תשובות across או"ח and יו"ד; he was famously cautious about חכמת הנסתר, although he did teach it in private to advanced תלמידים. The שיעור's broad characterization that he never heard זוהר from his three רבנים (ר' נתן אדלר, ר' פנחס הורוויץ the הפלאה, etc.) is from his own צוואה and biographical sources; verify exact סימן/biographical citation.
    ↩
  5. חידושי אגדות מהרש"א on הלכה over קבלה.
    Note: The מהרש"א (ר' שמואל אליעזר אידלס, 1555–1631) was a relative of the רמ"א's circle (their shared lineage runs through the Heller family). His general posture of caution toward קבלה-derived practices is reflected across his חידושי אגדות; the שיעור's specific quotation should be cross-checked against primary מקור. Lublin (his קבר) was a major center of pre-war תלמיד חכם tradition.
    ↩
  6. שו"ת נודע ביהודה, יו"ד מהדורה תניינא סימן צ"ג; או"ח קמ"ז. — ר' יחזקאל לנדא (Prague, 1713–1793) addresses the recital of לשם יחוד קודשא בריך הוא ושכינתיה before מצוות: he opposes the practice on the grounds that those who say it do not understand its kabbalistic content, and that קבלה-derived formulae cannot be casually integrated into mainstream הלכה. His תלמידים (ר' צבי הירש לנדא and others) carried the polemic forward. Verify exact סימן; the principal תשובה is יו"ד מהדורה תניינא צ"ג. ↩
  7. דרכי משה, רמ"א; cf. משנה ברורה כ"ה ס"ק מ"ב. — The רמ"א in דרכי משה (and codified in his glosses on the שלחן ערוך) establishes the principle: where there is a conflict between קבלה/זוהר and the הלכה as paskened in the תלמוד and פוסקים, the הלכה prevails. Where there is no conflict, קבלה may inform practice. This principle structures most subsequent Ashkenazic engagement with קבלה-derived מנהגים. ↩
  8. דניאל פרק ג'; ירושלמי שבת פ"ו ה"ט. — דניאל פרק ג': the story of חנניה, מישאל, ועזריה thrown into the furnace by נבוכדנצר, and נבוכדנצר's observation of a fourth figure "דָּמֵה לְבַר אֱלָהִין" (ג':כ"ה) — "resembling a son of God" (interpreted by חז"ל as the angel גבריאל). The ירושלמי in שבת records that an angel struck נבוכדנצר on the mouth and forced him to retract the words. The שיעור invokes this to explain the discomfort of saying בר אלהים in בריך שמיה. Verify exact location in ירושלמי שבת ch. 6; the בר אלהים source is also in פסחים קי"ח ע"א. ↩
  9. מטפחת ספרים, יעב"ץ (ר' יעקב עמדן, Altona, 1768). — The יעב"ץ's critical study of the זוהר, in which he argues — based on internal textual analysis — that significant portions of the זוהר are post-Tannaitic accretions and could not have been authored entirely by רשב"י. The work is highly controversial: most subsequent פוסקים and מקובלים reject the יעב"ץ's conclusions and treat the זוהר as fully authoritative. The יעב"ץ uses passages such as the בר אלהים language in בריך שמיה as evidence for his thesis. Cited cautiously by the שיעור. ↩
  10. משנה ברורה, או"ח קל"ד; חפץ חיים's personal practice. — The משנה ברורה codifies the universal recital of בריך שמיה. The שיעור's biographical detail — that the חפץ חיים personally did not say אנא עבדא from a sense of ענוה — is reported by his תלמידים and grandson R' Hillel Zaks. (R' Mendel Zaks, the חפץ חיים's son-in-law, was a ראש ישיבה in YU's RIETS for many years.) Verify the biographical citation in printed Chofetz Chaim biographies (e.g., The Chafetz Chaim: His Life and Works). ↩
  11. אבודרהם, on סדר הוצאת ספר תורה; cf. מחזור ויטרי. — ר' דוד אבודרהם (Seville, mid-14th century) counts the six words of גדלו ה' אתי ונרוממה שמו יחדיו (תהלים ל"ד:ד') as a parallel to the six steps after which דוד המלך stopped to bring קרבנות on the way of the ארון. The מחזור ויטרי records the same parallel as part of the institutional reasoning behind the פסוק's recital at הוצאת ספר תורה. ↩
  12. שמואל ב' פרק ו'; ברכות ס"ג ע"ב – ס"ד ע"א. — דוד המלך bringing the ארון from the house of עובד אדום הגתי to עיר דוד: the פסוק "ויהי כי צעדו נשאי ארון ה' ששה צעדים, ויזבח שור ומריא" (שמואל ב' ו':י"ג); the dancing of דוד; the rebuke of מיכל בת שאול; her childlessness as consequence (שמואל ב' ו':כ"ג). The גמרא at the end of ברכות develops the blessings of עובד אדום's house — sextuplets every birth — as the merit of hosting the ארון. ↩
  13. מסכת סופרים פרק י"ד; עץ חיים. — מסכת סופרים codifies the order of הוצאת ספר תורה, including the placement of הגבהה before the קריאה. The עץ חיים of ר' חיים ויטאל records the אריז"ל's כוונות for הגבהה: gazing at the letters draws an אור גדול onto the נשמה, and finding a letter corresponding to one's name reveals one's connection to one of the שישים רבוא letters of תורה (a kabbalistic concept rather than a literal letter-count). ↩
  14. מגן אברהם, או"ח קל"ד. — The מגן אברהם explains the Ashkenazi מנהג to perform הגבהה after the קריאה: should a פסול be discovered during the קריאה, one cannot honestly say "וזאת התורה אשר שם משה לפני בני ישראל". Better to wait until the קריאה has concluded without incident before declaring this. ספרדים follow the אריז"ל and lift before the קריאה. ↩
  15. אור זרוע, ר' יצחק מוינא (1180–1250). — ר' יצחק בן משה מוינא, one of the great בעלי התוספות; his אור זרוע is a vast halachic compendium that became the basis for many practices later codified in the מרדכי and the שלחן ערוך. He treats the various כיבודים at הוצאת ספר תורה as distinct halachic items — including the under-recognized כיבוד of handing the מעיל and גארטל to the מגלל. ↩
  16. מחזיק ברכה / מורה באצבע, חיד"א, on פתיחת הארון as a סגולה for הריון and birth.
    Note: The שיעור attributes the source to "מחזה כהונה תמרי תקניין" — verify the exact title; the חיד"א's discussions of סגולות for הריון appear primarily in מחזיק ברכה, מורה באצבע, and ברכי יוסף. The סגולה: the expectant father opens the ארון just as הקב"ה, פותח את הרחם, opens the womb. Widely practiced in Ashkenazic and Sephardic קהילות.
    ↩
  17. כף החיים (ר' יעקב חיים סופר); ר' חיים פלאג'י, כף החיים / רוח חיים.
    Note: ר' חיים פלאג'י (Izmir, 1788–1869), one of the leading Sephardic פוסקים of the 19th century, codifies the דין that the same individual who opens the ארון for an expectant father (or for a יולדת מקשה) should also close it — to ensure the סגולה structure is symmetrically completed. Verify exact סעיף in his halachic writings (likely רוח חיים).
    ↩
  18. שו"ת מהרש"ג (שם אריה), ר' שמעון גרינפלד.
    Note: The שיעור references "the מהרש"ג, ר' שמעון גרינפלד, the שם אריה." The שם אריה is in fact the work of ר' אריה ליב באלחובר; the שו"ת מהרש"ג is by ר' שמעון גרינפלד (Hungary, 1860–1930), known as the Sumeger Rav. Verify which work is intended; the תשובה on whether to interrupt פסוקי דזמרה for בריך שמיה belongs to one of the two.

  19. אבודרהם; סדור הרוקח; מחזור ויטרי on ברכו. — The ראשונים on the meaning of ברכו: a call to the ציבור to silence and to attend, paralleling משה רבינו's "האזינו השמים ואדברה" (דברים ל"ב:א'). The עולה who is about to recite the ברכת התורה can only do so in the presence of an attentive ציבור. ↩
  20. אור זרוע on splitting כיבודי הוצאה והכנסה; cited in מרדכי. — The אור זרוע's account of a dispute in shul over whether the כיבוד of פתיחת הארון belongs to the שליח ציבור or could be sold to another. The מרדכי codifies the principle that each act — opening the ארון, removing the ספר, carrying it to the בימה, replacing it, and closing — is a discrete כיבוד that may be assigned to different individuals. This is the basis of the standard contemporary practice of selling each item separately. ↩
