שמונה עשרה של שבת and ויכולו

Why the Shabbos עמידה is shortened — and what it means to be a שותף with the קב״ה in מעשה בראשית
Recorded 7 June 2026 · כ״א סיון תשפ״ו · Reworked from the original (abbreviated) שיעור
Dedication Today's שיעור and breakfast are sponsored by Reb Chezky Bedian and his rebbetzin, and by Reb Larry and his rebbetzin, לעילוי נשמת חנה בת אליהו אריה עזרא — marking the twenty-fifth יארצייט of מאליא. May her נשמה have an עלייה in גן עדן; every ברכה made and every word of תורה learned today should be a true זכות for her blessed soul.

Today's שיעור was abbreviated. The Miami rabbanim were running a breakfast for רפואה ותקווה at Rabbi Usher Stern's home — supporting mental-health initiatives in ארץ ישראל — and the שיעור also had to begin with מקבל פנים for Rabbi Shapiro, an einikel of Reb Chaim of Volozhin and Rosh Kollel of the וואלאזשינער Kollel in ארץ ישראל, who carries the Brisk-Volozhin legacy forward. Within those constraints, Rabbi Ephrati opened the next sugya in our journey through תפילה: שמונה עשרה של שבת. The עמידה of שבת is significantly shortened. Why? The שיעור walked through both the simple בבלי answer (מפני כבוד שבת — don't be מטריח the tzibbur) and the deeper ירושלמי answer (אין מקום לעצבות בשבת — personal בקשות import sadness and lack into a day where neither belongs), then opened the practical question of whether silent thought-בקשות are allowed (the Chasam Sofer's chiddush on שבת היא מלזעוק), and finally landed on the central new piece: ויכולו. Why three times? Why standing? What are the two angels doing with their hands on your head? And why does the שיעור close with the כלבו — and a striking 17th-century tradition that it may have been written by a woman?

I. Why Is שבת Shemoneh Esrei Shortened? — Two Core Reasons

A) The Bavli — מפני כבוד שבת

The simplest answer comes from the גמרא בברכות[1]: מפני כבוד השבת. חז״ל did not want to be מטריח the tzibbur with the full eighteen-and-a-half ברכות on Shabbos. Note the asymmetry: the rest of the davening on שבת is actually longer than the weekday — there is פסוקי דזמרה expansion, the קריאת התורה, מוסף, the long קדושות. Only the עמידה — the essential, central act of תפילה — gets shortened. The contraction is targeted, not general. The principle is כבוד שבת: at the structural heart of the davening, the kehillah gets relief.

B) The Yerushalmi — No Place for Sadness on שבת

The ירושלמי[2] gives a deeper reason — and the שיעור treated it as the conceptually load-bearing one. Asking personal צרכים on שבת is itself a slight to שבת. Why? Because the moment a Yid says רפאנו ה' ונרפא with kavanah, what is he thinking about? He is thinking about the חולה he has been davening for since Tuesday — perhaps the woman whose levaya he is attending later this afternoon. The thought carries sadness. The thought carries לחץ. And שבת קודש, says חז״ל, is כאילו מלאכתך עשויה — as though all your work were already done. Sadness, anxiety, פרנסה pressure, the heaviness of the cholim list — none of it has a מקום on שבת.

That is the formal halachic ground on which the middle thirteen weekday ברכות are removed. We keep the first three (שבח), the last three (הודאה), and we replace the entire middle with a single ברכה — קדושת היום — built around the קדושה of Shabbos itself rather than any list of human deficits.

II. The Structure — Three, One, Three = Seven

The architecture is 3 + 1 + 3: three opening ברכות of שבח, one middle ברכה of קדושת היום (the נוסח of which shifts across the four Shabbos tefillot — אתה קדשת on Friday night, ישמח משה on Shabbos morning, אתה אחד at מנחה, תכנת שבת at מוסף), and three closing ברכות of הודאה. Seven in total. The number is not accidental.

A) The Seven קוֹל ה' in תהלים כ״ט

Chazal connect the seven ברכות of the Shabbos עמידה to the seven occurrences of קוֹל ה' in תהלים כ״ט[3] — the mizmor of הָבוּ לה' בְּנֵי אֵלִים that we say in קבלת שבת:

קוֹל ה' עַל הַמָּיִם · קוֹל ה' בַּכֹּחַ · קוֹל ה' בֶּהָדָר · קוֹל ה' שֹׁבֵר אֲרָזִים · קוֹל ה' חֹצֵב לַהֲבוֹת אֵשׁ · קוֹל ה' יָחִיל מִדְבָּר · קוֹל ה' יְחוֹלֵל אַיָּלוֹת

Seven utterances of the Divine voice; seven ברכות of the Shabbos עמידה. The mizmor is not a generic praise pasted into Kabbalas Shabbos — it is, on this reading, the textual lattice from which the Shabbos עמידה takes its shape.

B) The Seven Names of הקדוש ברוך הוא in מזמור שיר ליום השבת

A parallel chazal: תהלים צ״ב[4] — מזמור שיר ליום השבת — contains seven invocations of הקדוש ברוך הוא's Name. Same number, different pasuk. The seven ברכות mention הקדוש ברוך הוא's Name seven times; the שבת mizmor mentions Him seven times. The structure is doubled and the lattice deepens.

III. פנים חדשות — The Shabbos Self

Underneath the structural answers sits a hashkafic one. שבת bestows נשמה יתירה and פנים חדשות. The expression פנים חדשות באו לכאן belongs technically to the שבע ברכות of a חתן וכלה — but the שיעור pressed the question: why does the chasan-and-kallah unit have a phrase about new faces arriving? Because every Shabbos is itself a פנים חדשות. Every Yid, on Shabbos, is a fundamentally different person from who he was on Wednesday — re-souled, re-faced, re-given.

And this is the inner reason for the removal of the middle ברכות. The entire weekday middle is built around lack — דעת I do not yet have, רפואה I am missing, פרנסה I am short of, גאולה not yet arrived. The Shabbos consciousness is the opposite: I lack nothing. The wholeness bestowed by קדושת היום fills the space the bakashos otherwise occupy. To ask for what you lack on Shabbos is to deny what Shabbos has given you.

IV. May One Be מבקש in מחשבה? — Rav Avish's Question

Rav Avish in the kehillah raised a sharp question. If דיבור-בקשות are halachically out of place on Shabbos, what about מחשבה? השם יודע מחשבות אדם — Hashem knows your thoughts. Can a person think a בקשה during the Shabbos עמידה for a חולה he is carrying?

A) הרהור מותר — The Standard Frame

The simplest answer is well-known: הרהור מותר. The שולחן ערוך codifies that thoughts of weekday matters are halachically permitted on Shabbos even where speech of them would not be. The pasuk in ישעיה נ״ח[5] — אִם תָּשִׁיב מִשַּׁבָּת רַגְלֶךָ ... וְכִבַּדְתּוֹ מֵעֲשׂוֹת דְּרָכֶיךָ מִמְּצוֹא חֶפְצְךָ וְדַבֵּר דָּבָר — is read by the Gemara through דבר דבר: your speech on שבת should not be like your speech of חול. Speech is restricted; thought is permitted. The zemiros line is the most popular reflex of this principle: חֶפְצֵיכֶם אֲסוּרִים וְגַם לַחֲשׁוֹב חֶשְׁבּוֹנוֹת ... הִרְהוּר מֻתָּר לְשַׁדֵּךְ הַבָּנוֹת.

B) The Chasam Sofer's Chiddush — שבת היא מלזעוק

But the שיעור brought a striking חתם סופר[6] that extends the principle into a specific structural reading of the Shabbos עמידה. The customary line when one wishes another a refuah on Shabbos is שבת היא מלזעוק ורפואה קרובה לבוא — "Shabbos is a day on which we hold back from crying out, and a complete healing is near to come." The עולם reads this as a gentle deflection: don't ask for refuah today, it will come without your asking.

The Chasam Sofer reads it as a halachic mechanism. On a weekday, if you have a חולה לא עלינו to daven for, you wait until רפאנו — the eighth ברכה — and you insert a יהי רצון aloud. On Shabbos, where the middle thirteen ברכות are missing, you have no רפאנו to wait for. But the second ברכה of שמונה עשרה contains the words סוֹמֵךְ נוֹפְלִים וְרוֹפֵא חוֹלִים — Hashem is named explicitly as the healer of the sick. The Chasam Sofer's chiddush: at רופא חולים in the second ברכה, you can think — silently, in מחשבה — of the חולה. You cannot speak; הפסק would be a problem in the middle of a ברכה. But the מחשבה is permitted. Therefore שבת היא מלזעוק ורפואה קרובה לבוא: on Shabbos one does not cry out — but refuah is close, because the silent בקשה comes earlier in the עמידה (at the second ברכה) than it would on a weekday (at the eighth).

A Belzer רמז The שיעור preserved a beautiful רמז that the Belzer Rebbe used to say. The pasuk מֹשֶׁה יְדַבֵּר וְהָאֱלֹקִים יַעֲנֶנּוּ בְקוֹל reads as ר״ת: משה = שבת היא מלזעוק והאלוקים יעננו בקול, and קול = רפואה קרובה לבוא. The familiar phrase is encoded in the pasuk's first letters. The רמז is widely repeated; the Chasam Sofer's פשט on the same phrase is the load-bearing piece for the question of silent בקשות.

The Chasam Sofer's reading still requires care. Even though הרהור מותר is the baseline, and even though the עמידה's second ברכה contains the right language, the broader Yerushalmi point — that שבת is meant to be a day on which we live the wholeness, not the lack — has independent weight. The שיעור left this as צריך עיון: a real halachic anchor, but to be applied with sensitivity to the inner מנוחה of Shabbos.

V. מי שבירך לחולים on Shabbos — Communal Reality

The same tension surfaces in the communal practice of saying מי שבירך לחולים after the קריאת התורה. Why is this not itself a violation of "no בקשות on Shabbos"? Because the framing is communal-priestly rather than personal-petitionary: the שליח ציבור (or the gabbai) leads the bakashah on behalf of the kehillah, with the kehillah unified in collective focus. The energy of the moment is not my lack but our care.

Rabbi Ephrati shared a pointed personal observation. A woman from the עזרת נשים had come to him three weeks earlier and said: I come to shul once a week. The מי שבירך is the meaningful moment for me — I carry a whole list of חולים and that is the time I want to focus together with the kehillah, name by name. And people talk through it. Rabbi Ephrati asked Yossi Lichtenstein (the gabbai of this kehillah) to begin every מי שבירך with an explicit announcement — רבותי, please be silent — and the discipline has held since. The frame: when מי שבירך is conducted with כוונה and quiet, it does not contradict the Shabbos prohibition on personal בקשות; it expresses the communal version of what the personal version is structurally not allowed to do.

VI. ברכת המזון on Shabbos — Why the Parnasah Language Remains

A related anomaly: every benching on Shabbos contains the line רְעֵנוּ זוּנֵנוּ פַּרְנְסֵנוּ וְכַלְכְּלֵנוּ — explicit petitionary language about פרנסה. If personal בקשות are removed from the עמידה, why are they retained in ברכת המזון? The ראשונים discuss this within the distinct liturgical architecture of ברכת המזון, which differs structurally from the עמידה — the מקור is a separate תקנה, the placement is different, the conceptual frame is different. The שיעור flagged the discussion for future treatment but did not finish it today.

VII. A Practical Digression — Scooters and Bikes on Shabbos

The שיעור pivoted into an extended digression — partly driven by audience questions and partly by the relevance of the underlying pasuk to the broader question of what we may or may not do on Shabbos. The שיעור acknowledged it was off-topic, but the underlying pesukim are the same ones that ground the Yerushalmi's logic above.

A) The Pasuk — שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא הִלּוּכְךָ בְּשַׁבָּת כְּהִלּוּכְךָ בְּחֹל

The Gemara reads the pasuk in ישעיה נ״ח through three parallel phrases: שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא דִּבּוּרְךָ בְּשַׁבָּת כְּדִבּוּרְךָ בְּחֹל (speech), שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא הִלּוּכְךָ בְּשַׁבָּת כְּהִלּוּכְךָ בְּחֹל (movement / mode of transport), and שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא חֶפְצְךָ בְּשַׁבָּת כְּחֶפְצְךָ בְּחֹל (objects and concerns).[7] Walking on Shabbos should not look like walking on a weekday. The original הילוך applied to long strides, business posture, and similar non-Shabbos gait; the principle was extended by the ראשונים and אחרונים to questions about modes of transport.

B) The Lawrence Scooter Question

Rabbi Ephrati was asked about an emerging practice in Lawrence: people riding non-motorized kick-scooters to shul on Shabbos. The questioner pressed: if no one uses the scooter during the week — only on Shabbos — then it is not הילוכך בחול, and the underlying objection collapses. Rabbi Ephrati's response: cute. (He quoted Rabbi Kalish ז״ל's stock response to such שטיקלעך of pilpul.) The argument does not stand because the structural category of הילוך is not about this person's weekday habits but about the broader cultural mode of transport — and scooters belong to the weekday mode. The שיעור's psak in chutz-laaretz where shul is reachable on foot: אסור.

C) The באר הלכה Leniency — Distance and מצוה

The שיעור flagged a באר הלכה[8] that allows a limited היתר for someone who would otherwise be unable to reach shul — too far in heat and humidity, no alternative shul in walking distance. The leniency is narrow and context-specific, applies to צורך מצוה in cases of significant difficulty, and requires local הוראה. The Miami climate (90+ degrees and humid) plus the absence of intermediate shuls is precisely the scenario the באר הלכה might address.

D) Bikes — A Separate Problem

Bikes carry a separate halachic concern: the realistic possibility of mid-ride repair — chain slipping, tire issues — implicates תיקון / מתקן. A simple kick-scooter rarely faces this; bikes do. The two cases should not be merged. The שיעור's instinct on bikes: still אסור in the standard case, and the questions surrounding bikes are conceptually distinct from the scooter discussion above.

An Italian Rishon and a Gondola The שיעור preserved a small historical aside that bears on the general structural question. One of the great ראשונים — Rabbi Ephrati associated this tentatively with the אור זרוע, but flagged that he needed to verify the source — lived in Venice. In Venice the standard mode of transport is the gondola, propelled by a non-Jewish gondolier. The Rishon used to take a gondola on Shabbos: he was not doing the work; the goy was; he was simply riding. The leniency was based on the fact that there was no realistic alternative mode of transport in Venice and the Yid himself was performing no מלאכה. The נודע ביהודה discusses the analogous question of riding a train on Shabbos through similar lines of reasoning. The case is not directly applicable to the scooter discussion — the scooter requires the rider's own propulsion — but it shows how the ראשונים navigated transport questions through a combination of הילוך analysis, the מלאכה analysis, and the availability of alternative modes.

VIII. ויכולו — Why Three Times?

We return now to the central new piece of the שיעור. After the three opening ברכות of the Friday-night עמידה, we open the קדושת היום ברכה with אַתָּה קִדַּשְׁתָּ אֶת יוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי לִשְׁמֶךָ, declare תַּכְלִית מַעֲשֵׂה שָׁמַיִם וָאָרֶץ, and then continue: וְכֵן כָּתוּב בְּתוֹרָתֶךָ — and recite ויכולו.

We say ויכולו three times on Friday night, all within approximately one hour of each other. First in לחש inside the עמידה. Second aloud with the tzibbur after the עמידה. Third at קידוש in the home. Why three times — and what is ויכולו doing in the first place?

A) Becoming a שותף With הקדוש ברוך הוא

The גמרא בשבת[9] makes a striking declaration: every Friday night, anyone who recites ויכולו, מַעֲלֶה עָלָיו הַכָּתוּב כְּאִלּוּ נַעֲשָׂה שֻׁתָּף לְהַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא בְּמַעֲשֵׂה בְרֵאשִׁית — it is as though he became a partner with הקדוש ברוך הוא in the creation of the world. The praise is unusually high. Why does saying ויכולו make a person a partner in creation?

The בן איש חי[10] says a פנים-changing ווארט. הקדוש ברוך הוא created שבת — a יום המנוחה, a יום הקדושה. We also create שבת. There is a halachah of תוספת שבת: we extend Shabbos beyond its strict halachic boundary, adding from חול to קודש. We accept Shabbos a little early (with all the gradations between Plag-mincha early-Shabbos shuls and the standard candle-lighting time); we extend Shabbos a little late at the end. Those added moments — they are not yet Shabbos by the natural calendar. We make them Shabbos by our act of acceptance. In that sense we partner with הקדוש ברוך הוא in the creation of Shabbos: He made the original; we extend it. When we declare ויכולו we testify to the original creation; the testifying itself is the partnership.

B) Three Recitations — Rabbeinu Yerucham and the Tur

רבינו ירוחם[11] explains the threefold recitation through the principle of חזקה: saying something three times establishes the matter firmly. Three declarations of ויכולו strengthen the underlying אמונה — אמונה in הקדוש ברוך הוא's creation of the world, אמונה in His שביתה on the seventh day. The triple repetition is not redundancy; it is structural reinforcement of the foundational belief.

The טור[12] adds an unexpected technical reason for the second recitation specifically — the one we do aloud with the tzibbur after the עמידה. When שבת coincides with יום טוב, ויכולו may be omitted from the עמידה (to preserve the integrity of the יום טוב נוסח without inserting a whole new שבת section into a different ברכה). Reciting ויכולו publicly after the עמידה ensures that the עדות (see below) is maintained even in that case. Once that became the practice for Yom-Tov-she-chal-b'Shabbos, it extended to every Friday night for consistency. The שיעור flagged this as a strange-but-real reason: a once-or-twice-a-year edge case shaping the practice for every Shabbos of the year.

IX. ויכולו as הגדת עדות

The deeper frame: ויכולו is testimony. The pesukim of בראשית that we recite — וַיְכֻלּוּ הַשָּׁמַיִם וְהָאָרֶץ וְכָל צְבָאָם ... וַיְבָרֶךְ אֱלֹקִים אֶת יוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי וַיְקַדֵּשׁ אֹתוֹ[13] — are not just praise. They are formal עדות that הקדוש ברוך הוא created the world and rested on the seventh day. Every Friday night, every Yid in every shul, becomes an עד at a metaphysical בית דין.

A) Standing — וְעָמְדוּ שְׁנֵי הָאֲנָשִׁים אֲשֶׁר לָהֶם הָרִיב

Because ויכולו is עדות, the halachah requires standing for the public recitation. The pasuk in דברים י״ט[14] — וְעָמְדוּ שְׁנֵי הָאֲנָשִׁים אֲשֶׁר לָהֶם הָרִיב לִפְנֵי ה' — establishes that עדות must be given standing. The same principle carries over to ויכולו: when you testify, you stand.

B) Mishnah Berurah vs. Chazon Ish — Joint or Sequential?

The משנה ברורה[15] codifies that ויכולו should be said together with at least one other person — עַל פִּי שְׁנַיִם עֵדִים יָקוּם דָּבָר. Many people, if they miss ויכולו with the tzibbur, will hunt for another individual to say it with so as to fulfill the two-witnesses requirement together.

The חזון איש[16] pushed back sharply. In a real בית דין, two witnesses do not testify simultaneously; the Mishna in סנהדרין[17] establishes that עדים are interrogated sequentially — the first עד is heard and questioned, then the second עד is heard and questioned. If ויכולו is really עדות, the Chazon Ish argued, the simultaneous joint recitation is structurally wrong. The proper analogue would be one person saying it, and then the next, not both at once. The Chazon Ish therefore did not insist on the joint recitation.

כלל ישראל nonetheless did not adopt the Chazon Ish's מנהג here. The Mishna Berurah's practice — finding another person and reciting ויכולו together aloud — prevailed. Most shuls today follow the Mishna Berurah. The Chazon Ish's critique stands on its own conceptual ground but did not become normative.

X. The Two Angels and the כפרה of Withholding עדות

Now to the most striking piece of the sugya. The גמרא בשבת[18] teaches that every Yid is accompanied at all times by two מלאכים. We do not see them; they are there. On Friday night, when a Yid says ויכולו, the two מלאכים place their hands on his head and proclaim וְסָר עֲוֹנֶךָ וְחַטָּאתְךָ תְּכֻפָּר[19] — your sin is removed and your transgression is forgiven.

Which sin specifically? Every chassidish vort offers a different answer; the שיעור brought the answer of the כלבו[20] and the wider line of ראשונים. The halachah of עדות includes a duty to testify: if you know עדות, you are מחויב to come forward. ויקרא ה' פסוק א'[21] — וְשָׁמְעָה קוֹל אָלָה וְהוּא עֵד אוֹ רָאָה אוֹ יָדָע אִם לוֹא יַגִּיד וְנָשָׂא עֲוֹנוֹ — codifies that withholding known testimony is itself a sin. (Rabbi Ephrati noted the popular parallel: the MTA on the New York subway prints "if you see something, say something" on every train — secular halachah catching up to ויקרא.)

Every Yid, the entire week, has been holding an unstated עדות in his back pocket: he knows הקדוש ברוך הוא created the world. He has been carrying that knowledge silently. When he stands up on Friday night and declares ויכולו, he discharges the עדות — he says aloud what he has known. The withholding-עדות sin is rectified at that moment. The two מלאכים place their hands on his head and proclaim your sin is gone.

A) Why the Head? — מחשבה and הרהור תשובה

Rabbi Ephrati pressed the question that the כלבו presses: why specifically the head? The answer reaches deep. A רשע is פסול לעדות — disqualified from giving testimony. To give valid עדות, you must first be מהרהר בתשובה. The head represents מחשבה — and that is where teshuvah, on the deepest level, lives.

The גמרא בקידושין[22] drives this home. If a known רשע approaches a woman with הֲרֵי אַתְּ מְקֻדֶּשֶׁת לִי עַל מְנָת שֶׁאֲנִי צַדִּיק גָּמוּר, the Gemara holds we are חושש for the קידושין — we treat them as halachically valid. Why? Because in one second, the man may have had a הרהור תשובה; in one second, a person can move from רשע to צדיק גמור. The transformation is real, instantaneous, and halachically operative. The Mishnah accepts that the קידושין might genuinely have taken effect.

Apply this to ויכולו. Every Friday night, the two מלאכים approach. Before they can certify the עדות, the Yid must be מהרהר בתשובה — must have a moment of inner תשובה that re-qualifies him to give the testimony. The hands on the head are the symbolic certification: your head — your מחשבה — is the place where teshuvah lives, and we confirm that the teshuvah has happened. Once the מלאכים have certified the מחשבה, the עדות is valid, and the structural sin of withholding it is removed.

A historical mנהג — מלקות before being עד at a חופה Rabbi Ephrati flagged a remarkable מנהג preserved in the earlier ראשונים' literature — found in a ספר reprinted from a גניזה discovery — that the עדים called up to witness a חופה were given מלקות before serving as עדים, specifically to be מעורר them to תשובה so that the עדות would be qualified. The practice has been long discontinued — Rabbi Ephrati noted the spectacle it would create at any modern חופה — but the conceptual logic is the same as the מלאכים on Friday night: before serving as עד, do teshuvah. The practice survives in spirit in the requirement that ויכולו's testifier be מהרהר בתשובה in his mind.

XI. The כלבו — A Mystery Author

The שיעור closed with an extended biographical-historical note on the source we have been leaning on for the ויכולו discussion — the ספר כלבו[23]. The name itself is striking. כלבו reads literally as כל בו — everything is in it. In modern Israeli Hebrew the same phrase names the all-purpose hardware store. The author named his book "everything is in it": a confident, almost brazen claim, and an apt one. The כלבו is a comprehensive treatment of the structure of תפילה, ברכות, and the daily/weekly/yearly liturgical cycle. The בית יוסף cites it by the hundreds.

And yet: no one knows who wrote it. The author did not put his name on the work, and the ראשונים and אחרונים have debated the identity for centuries. Several candidates are offered; none has consensus. (The identity of the author is not a technical detail — for the בית יוסף and others to weigh the כלבו's psak among other ראשונים, the identification matters, and the indeterminacy is one of the genuine open questions in the corpus of הלכה.)

A) The 17th-Century Turkish Tradition

Rabbi Ephrati brought a striking aחronos record. A Rav in 17th-century Turkey wrote that he had heard a tradition that the כלבו was authored by a woman — which would make it the only sefer in the corpus of the ראשונים attributed to female authorship. Having stated the tradition, however, the Turkish Rav rejected it: I went through the entire ספר and concluded it is impossible. The level of חכמה is a חכמת איש, not a חכמת אשה.

Rabbi Ephrati did not let that conclusion stand uncontested. He pressed back — חס ושלום, but the historical record is more complex than the 17th-century Turkish Rav allowed.

B) Women Scholars in the Historical Record

The Maharshal's great-grandmother. The מהרש״ל[24] records in a תשובה that his great-grandmother gave שיעור to בחורים in a ישיבה — from behind a curtain. The same source records that she wore תפילין. (The שיעור flagged the תפילין piece as a separate sugya it would not enter today.)

The Chavos Yair's grandmother. The חוות יאיר[25] writes in his הקדמה that his grandmother gave שיעור to men. The combined Maharshal-and-Chavos-Yair record from two different family lines and two different centuries is not isolated lore; it is documented within the תשובות literature.

The Chida and the Pnei Yehoshua's rebbetzin. The חיד״א[26] records visiting the פני יהושע in Frankfurt and sitting at his Shabbos table. The פני יהושע said a דבר תורה. The rebbetzin piped up — and argued against him. The פני יהושע acknowledged: the rebbetzin is גערעכט. (Rabbi Ephrati allowed: it may have been said partly for שלום בית; he would not pretend otherwise.) But the Chida writes that he was מתפעל — astonished — that the rebbetzin could engage at that level. The episode is in his writings.

The cumulative record is that learned women teaching from behind מחיצות, learned rebbetzins arguing with their husbands at the Shabbos table, and learned women in family lines of major poskim are documented in the תשובות literature of the ראשונים and אחרונים. The 17th-century Turkish Rav's "a woman could not have written such a book" is, in the שיעור's framing, perhaps too quick. Whether the כלבו was actually authored by a woman remains unresolved; the broader historical record suggests it is not impossible in the way the Turkish Rav assumed.

XII. Closing — Why the שיעור Was Short

The שיעור closed with an apology for the abridged form. Rabbi Ephrati had to leave for the רפואה ותקווה breakfast at Rabbi Usher Stern's home, and the time had also been spent on the proper מקבל פנים for Rabbi Shapiro. The intent had been to develop the structural sugya more carefully — particularly the question of why ברכת המזון's פרנסה language is permitted on Shabbos and the connection of ויכולו's עדות to the broader sugya of testimony. Those threads will be continued אם ירצה השם next week.

The arc of the שיעור in three movements: (i) Why the Shabbos עמידה is shortened — the בבלי's מפני כבוד שבת and the deeper ירושלמי of "no place for sadness on Shabbos," with the structural 3+1+3 = 7 reflecting the seven קוֹל ה' in תהלים כ״ט and the seven Names of הקב״ה in מזמור שיר ליום השבת. Underneath: the hashkafah of פנים חדשות — the Shabbos Yid is not defined by lack. The Chasam Sofer's chiddush that one may make silent בקשה in מחשבה at סומך נופלים ורופא חולים in the second ברכה. (ii) A practical digression on scooters and bikes — the pasuk in ישעיה נ״ח on הילוך, the באר הלכה's narrow leniency for distance and מצוה, the Italian Rishon's gondola, and the bike's distinct mechanical problem. (iii) ויכולו: why three times (רבינו ירוחם's חזקה and the טור's יום-טוב-she-chal-b'Shabbos reason), the עדות frame (Mishna Berurah's joint recital vs. Chazon Ish's sequential model), and the two מלאכים placing their hands on the head — the head representing מחשבה for the הרהור תשובה that re-qualifies the Yid to give valid עדות. Closing with the כלבו's mystery author and the long historical record of learned women teaching, arguing, and (in some traditions) writing within the corpus of the ראשונים.

מראה מקומות

  1. בבלי ברכות כ״א ע״א — מפני כבוד שבת. — The Gemara's straightforward reason for the contraction of the Shabbos עמידה: not to be מטריח the tzibbur on the day of rest. The structural asymmetry — only the עמידה is shortened, while פסוקי דזמרה, the קריאה, and מוסף are expanded — is what makes the explanation conceptually load-bearing rather than generic. The exact daf was given in the שיעור as ברכות כ״א (the standard discussion appears in ברכות כ״ט as well; verify the specific reference before citing). ↩
  2. ירושלמי שבת / ירושלמי ברכות — no personal בקשות on Shabbos. — The Yerushalmi articulates the deeper, conceptually load-bearing reason: personal בקשות on Shabbos import sadness and consciousness of lack into a day whose essence is כאילו מלאכתך עשויה. The principle is the conceptual ground for the removal of the middle thirteen ברכות in favor of a single ברכת קדושת היום. Exact reference (Yerushalmi Shabbos vs. Yerushalmi Berachos vs. Yerushalmi Taanis) not specified in the שיעור; the framework is widely cited in the ראשונים. ↩
    The Yerushalmi reference was given in the שיעור in general terms. The locus classicus is sometimes cited as Yerushalmi Shabbos פרק ט״ו or Yerushalmi Berachos פרק ד'; verify the specific citation in the Yerushalmi before relying on a precise wording.
  3. תהלים פרק כ״ט — seven קוֹל ה' paralleling the seven Shabbos ברכות. — The mizmor of הָבוּ לה' בְּנֵי אֵלִים recited in Kabbalas Shabbos contains seven occurrences of the phrase קוֹל ה'. חז״ל connect the count to the seven ברכות of the Shabbos עמידה: the seven Divine utterances structurally parallel the seven liturgical declarations. The connection elevates the mizmor from generic praise to structural lattice for the Shabbos עמידה. ↩
  4. תהלים פרק צ״ב — seven Divine Names in מזמור שיר ליום השבת. — The pasuk-count of הקדוש ברוך הוא's Name in מזמור שיר ליום השבת totals seven; the count parallels the seven Names invoked across the seven ברכות of the Shabbos עמידה. The שיעור brought this as a parallel structural reading alongside the seven קוֹל ה'. ↩
  5. ישעיה פרק נ״ח פסוקים י״ג–י״ד; שבת קי״ג ע״א–ע״ב — דבר דבר and הרהור מותר. — The pasuk: אִם תָּשִׁיב מִשַּׁבָּת רַגְלֶךָ עֲשׂוֹת חֲפָצֶיךָ בְּיוֹם קָדְשִׁי וְקָרָאתָ לַשַּׁבָּת עֹנֶג ... וְכִבַּדְתּוֹ מֵעֲשׂוֹת דְּרָכֶיךָ מִמְּצוֹא חֶפְצְךָ וְדַבֵּר דָּבָר. The Gemara in שבת reads דבר דבר through the principle שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא דִּבּוּרְךָ בְּשַׁבָּת כְּדִבּוּרְךָ בְּחֹל: speech of weekday matters is restricted, but the underlying thought is permitted (הרהור מותר). The popular reflex in the zemiros: חֶפְצֵיכֶם אֲסוּרִים וְגַם לַחֲשׁוֹב חֶשְׁבּוֹנוֹת ... הִרְהוּר מֻתָּר לְשַׁדֵּךְ הַבָּנוֹת. ↩
  6. שו״ת חתם סופר — chiddush on שבת היא מלזעוק ורפואה קרובה לבוא. — Rabbi Moshe Sofer (1762–1839, Pressburg) reads the customary "Shabbos line" for wishing refuah as a structural mechanism rather than a deflection. On a weekday the silent בקשה for a חולה waits until the eighth ברכה (רפאנו); on Shabbos, where the middle thirteen ברכות are absent, the words סוֹמֵךְ נוֹפְלִים וְרוֹפֵא חוֹלִים in the second ברכה provide an earlier moment at which silent מחשבה-בקשה for the חולה can attach. The phrase שבת היא מלזעוק ורפואה קרובה לבוא on this reading is literal: one does not cry out (no דיבור-בקשה), but refuah is closer (the מחשבה-בקשה comes earlier in the עמידה). Exact siman in the Chasam Sofer not specified in the שיעור. ↩
    The Chasam Sofer reference was widely cited but the specific siman in the Chasam Sofer's שו״ת was not given in the שיעור. Verify in the standard editions of שו״ת חתם סופר before citing as halachah; the framework is well-attested in the Chasam Sofer's broader writings on Shabbos tefillah.
  7. שבת קי״ג ע״א–ע״ב — הילוך, דיבור, and חפץ on Shabbos. — The Gemara reads ישעיה נ״ח's וְכִבַּדְתּוֹ מֵעֲשׂוֹת דְּרָכֶיךָ מִמְּצוֹא חֶפְצְךָ וְדַבֵּר דָּבָר as three parallel principles: weekday-style movement (הילוך), weekday-style concerns (חפץ), and weekday-style speech (דיבור) are all restricted. The original applications of הילוך in the Gemara involve long strides and business posture; the ראשונים and אחרונים extended the principle to modes of transport. The scooter discussion sits inside this conceptual frame. ↩
  8. ביאור הלכה — narrow leniency for those who would otherwise miss תפילה בציבור. — The Beur Halacha (ר' ישראל מאיר הכהן מראדין, the Chofetz Chaim, 1838–1933) addresses circumstances where significant difficulty (distance, weather, infirmity) would prevent a person from reaching shul. A limited היתר exists for צורך מצוה in these specific cases. The leniency is narrow and requires local הוראה; Rabbi Ephrati flagged it as the closest written source for any defense of the Lawrence scooter practice, but explicitly did not adopt it as his own psak. Exact siman not specified in the שיעור. ↩
    The Beur Halacha citation was given in general terms in the שיעור. The standard discussions of הליכה to shul under difficulty appear across the Mishnah Berurah's treatment of הלכות שבת (סימנים ש״א–ש״ז on עובדין דחול and related principles); verify the specific siman in the Beur Halacha before relying on the precise scope of the leniency.
  9. שבת קי״ט ע״ב — saying ויכולו makes one a שותף להקב״ה במעשה בראשית. — The famous Gemara that grounds the entire ויכולו discussion. The phrasing כָּל הָאוֹמֵר וַיְכֻלּוּ בְּעֶרֶב שַׁבָּת מַעֲלֶה עָלָיו הַכָּתוּב כְּאִלּוּ נַעֲשָׂה שֻׁתָּף לְהַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא בְּמַעֲשֵׂה בְרֵאשִׁית is one of the strongest single-action praises in all of חז״ל. The reference was given in the שיעור as שבת קי״ז; the standard locus is שבת קי״ט ע״ב — verify against your edition. ↩
  10. בן איש חי / בן יהוידע — תוספת שבת and the partnership in creation. — Rabbi Yosef Chaim of Baghdad (1834–1909) explains the שותף language through the halachah of תוספת שבת. הקדוש ברוך הוא created the original Shabbos; we extend it by accepting Shabbos a little early and extending it a little late, sanctifying liminal moments through our own acceptance. That extension is the human contribution to the creation of Shabbos as a continuing reality, and the partnership the Gemara names is the structural reflection of this combined act. The Ben Yehoyada and the Ben Ish Chai discuss the principle in multiple places; exact citation not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  11. רבינו ירוחם — three recitations as חזקה. — Rabbeinu Yerucham ben Meshullam (c. 1290–1350, Provence) is one of the major halachic codifiers of the period between the Rosh and the Tur. His ספר תולדות אדם וחוה and ספר מישרים are widely cited by the בית יוסף. On the threefold recitation of ויכולו, Rabbeinu Yerucham reads the structure through the principle that three repetitions establish חזקה — strengthening the underlying אמונה in הקדוש ברוך הוא's creation and שביתה. Exact location in Rabbeinu Yerucham's writings not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  12. טור אורח חיים סימן רס״ח — the Yom-Tov-she-chal-b'Shabbos reason for the second recital. — ר' יעקב בן אשר (the Tur, c. 1269–1343) explains the second, public recitation of ויכולו (after the עמידה, recited aloud with the tzibbur) through the edge case of יום טוב falling on Shabbos. In that case ויכולו may be omitted from the עמידה to preserve the integrity of the יום טוב נוסח (which substitutes אתה בחרתנו and the יום טוב additions for the Shabbos נוסח); reciting ויכולו publicly after the עמידה ensures the עדות is maintained in that case. Once the public recitation was instituted for the Yom-Tov-she-chal-b'Shabbos case, it extended for consistency to every Friday night of the year. The reason is striking because the practice's primary case occurs perhaps once or twice a year, yet shapes every Shabbos. ↩
  13. בראשית ב' פסוקים א'–ג' — the pesukim of ויכולו. — וַיְכֻלּוּ הַשָּׁמַיִם וְהָאָרֶץ וְכָל צְבָאָם. וַיְכַל אֱלֹקִים בַּיּוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי מְלַאכְתּוֹ אֲשֶׁר עָשָׂה וַיִּשְׁבֹּת בַּיּוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי מִכָּל מְלַאכְתּוֹ אֲשֶׁר עָשָׂה. וַיְבָרֶךְ אֱלֹקִים אֶת יוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי וַיְקַדֵּשׁ אֹתוֹ כִּי בוֹ שָׁבַת מִכָּל מְלַאכְתּוֹ אֲשֶׁר בָּרָא אֱלֹקִים לַעֲשׂוֹת. The three pesukim are the formal עדות recited at all three recitations of ויכולו. The closing word לַעֲשׂוֹת — "to do" — is the textual anchor of the Ben Ish Chai's reading that we are partners in completing creation through תוספת שבת. ↩
  14. דברים פרק י״ט פסוק י״ז — וְעָמְדוּ שְׁנֵי הָאֲנָשִׁים. — The pasuk that establishes that עדות is given standing: וְעָמְדוּ שְׁנֵי הָאֲנָשִׁים אֲשֶׁר לָהֶם הָרִיב לִפְנֵי ה' לִפְנֵי הַכֹּהֲנִים וְהַשֹּׁפְטִים אֲשֶׁר יִהְיוּ בַּיָּמִים הָהֵם. The standing-while-testifying derivation applies to ויכולו's status as הגדת עדות and grounds the halachah of standing for the public recitation. The companion pasuk עַל פִּי שְׁנַיִם עֵדִים יָקוּם דָּבָר (דברים י״ט:ט״ו) grounds the requirement to recite ויכולו with at least one other person. ↩
  15. משנה ברורה סימן רס״ח — joint recitation of ויכולו. — The Mishna Berurah codifies the practice of reciting ויכולו together with at least one other person, grounding the halachah in the pasuk עַל פִּי שְׁנַיִם עֵדִים יָקוּם דָּבָר. The practical reflex: those who miss the public recitation will find another individual after davening and say ויכולו with him aloud, to fulfill the two-witnesses requirement together. The Mishna Berurah's psak is the normative practice in most contemporary Ashkenazic kehillot. ↩
  16. חזון איש — pushback against simultaneous עדות. — ר' אברהם ישעיהו קרליץ (the Chazon Ish, 1878–1953) argued that if ויכולו is genuinely הגדת עדות, the simultaneous joint recital is structurally inappropriate — actual עדים in a בית דין are interrogated sequentially, not simultaneously, per the Mishna in סנהדרין. The Chazon Ish therefore did not insist on the joint recitation. The position carries logical force but did not become normative; כלל ישראל follows the Mishna Berurah's joint-recital practice. ↩
  17. סנהדרין ג' ע״א; סנהדרין ל' ע״ב — sequential interrogation of עדים. — The Mishna and Gemara in סנהדרין establish that עדים are heard and questioned one at a time. The procedure (חקירות ודרישות) is sequential: the first עד delivers his testimony and is interrogated; the second עד is brought separately and his testimony tested against the first. The Chazon Ish leverages this structural fact against the joint recitation of ויכולו: if the model is genuine עדות, the model is sequential, not simultaneous. ↩
  18. שבת קי״ט ע״ב — the two מלאכים place their hands on the head. — The Gemara following the famous "שותף" passage describes the two מלאכים who accompany every Yid placing their hands on his head when he recites ויכולו and proclaiming וְסָר עֲוֹנֶךָ וְחַטָּאתְךָ תְּכֻפָּר. The image is one of the most concrete descriptions of angelic action in the entire Talmud and is the conceptual ground for the כלבו's reading of ויכולו as the discharge of withheld עדות. ↩
  19. ישעיה פרק ו' פסוק ז' — וְסָר עֲוֹנֶךָ וְחַטָּאתְךָ תְּכֻפָּר. — The pasuk in ישעיהו's vision of the heichal: the seraph touches Yeshayahu's lips with the burning coal and announces that his sin is removed and his transgression forgiven. The Gemara in שבת borrows the language for the מלאכים' announcement on Friday night: the discharge of withheld עדות is, in the Gemara's frame, structurally parallel to the cleansing the seraph performs in the prophetic vision. ↩
  20. כלבו — the מלאכים' announcement as removal of the sin of withholding עדות. — The ספר כלבו offers the reading the שיעור adopted: the מלאכים' proclamation refers specifically to the halachah of אם לא יגיד ונשא עונו — the sin of failing to come forward with known testimony. Every Yid carries the silent עדות that הקדוש ברוך הוא created the world; declaring ויכולו discharges the עדות and rectifies the structural liability. The כלבו's reading anchors the whole sugya and is the source for the further question of why the מלאכים place their hands specifically on the head (the answer connects to הרהור תשובה — see fn22). ↩
  21. ויקרא פרק ה' פסוק א' — אִם לוֹא יַגִּיד וְנָשָׂא עֲוֹנוֹ. — The pasuk that establishes the halachic duty of testimony: וְנֶפֶשׁ כִּי תֶחֱטָא וְשָׁמְעָה קוֹל אָלָה וְהוּא עֵד אוֹ רָאָה אוֹ יָדָע אִם לוֹא יַגִּיד וְנָשָׂא עֲוֹנוֹ. One who has standing as an עד and fails to come forward bears the structural sin. The הלכה is codified in רמב״ם הלכות עדות and in the שולחן ערוך (חושן משפט סימן כ״ח); the structural principle is what the כלבו leverages to explain the מלאכים' announcement on Friday night. ↩
  22. קידושין מ״ט ע״ב — על מנת שאני צדיק גמור and instantaneous teshuvah. — The Gemara teaches that if a known רשע offers קידושין to a woman with the conditional עַל מְנָת שֶׁאֲנִי צַדִּיק גָּמוּר, we are חושש for the קידושין — we treat them as halachically valid. The reason: in the time it took to speak the words, the man may have undergone an internal הרהור תשובה that transformed him from רשע to צדיק. The conceptual point — that teshuvah is instantaneous and operates through מחשבה — is the foundation for the כלבו's reading of why the מלאכים' hands rest specifically on the head: the head represents מחשבה, the locus where teshuvah lives, and the מלאכים certify the התעוררות תשובה that re-qualifies the Yid to give valid עדות. ↩
  23. ספר כלבו — the mystery-author Rishon on תפילה and ברכות. — A major early-14th-century halachic-liturgical work covering ברכות, הלכות תפילה, the יום-tov cycle, and structural questions of the siddur. The name כלבו reads as כל בו — "everything is in it." The work was widely cited by the בית יוסף and the broader Sephardic halachic tradition; its author did not put his name on the work, and the identification has been debated for centuries. The mainstream candidates include ר' אהרן הכהן מלוניל and several others; no consensus has emerged. The 17th-century Turkish tradition reported in the שיעור — that the author may have been a woman — was rejected by the Rav who recorded it but is preserved as a real position in the historical record. ↩
    The specific 17th-century Turkish source for the female-authorship tradition was not named in the שיעור. The position is well-attested in the historical literature on the כלבו's authorship debate; verify against published treatments of the כלבו's transmission history before citing the Turkish Rav's words verbatim.
  24. שו״ת מהרש״ל — his great-grandmother giving שיעור from behind a curtain. — ר' שלמה לוריא (the Maharshal, 1510–1573, of Lublin) records in a תשובה that his great-grandmother gave שיעור to בחורים in a ישיבה from behind a curtain (מחיצה) and that she wore תפילין. The exact siman in the Maharshal's שו״ת was not specified in the שיעור. The record is one of the standard historical anchors for the existence of learned women teaching within the framework of Ashkenaz in the 15th–16th centuries. Whether the practice was widespread or exceptional is contested in the modern scholarly literature; the Maharshal's own report establishes that it existed and was, at least within his family, respected. ↩
    The Maharshal citation was given without a specific siman in the שיעור. The standard reference is to שו״ת מהרש״ל סימן כ"ט (or surrounding teshuvos on related questions of women's halachic practice); verify before citing the exact wording.
  25. שו״ת חוות יאיר, הקדמה — his grandmother giving שיעור to men. — ר' יאיר חיים בכרך (the Chavos Yair, 1639–1702, Worms) records in the introduction to his שו״ת חוות יאיר that his grandmother — בעלה כפרק שירה Bella, daughter of ר' אברהם of Lemberg — gave שיעור to men. The record stands alongside the Maharshal's report from a different family line and a different century as one of the standard documented cases of learned women teaching within Ashkenaz. The Chavos Yair's report is part of a larger reflection in the הקדמה on family scholarship and women's חכמה. The reference was given in the שיעור in general terms. ↩
  26. חיד״א, in his historical writings — visiting the פני יהושע in Frankfurt. — ר' חיים יוסף דוד אזולאי (the Chida, 1724–1806) records in his autobiographical/historical writings (likely מעגל טוב or one of his shorter biographical works) visiting the פני יהושע (ר' יעקב יהושע פאלק, 1680–1756) in Frankfurt. At the שבת table the פני יהושע said a דבר תורה; the rebbetzin spoke up and argued against him. The פני יהושע acknowledged that the rebbetzin is correct. The Chida writes that he was מתפעל at the rebbetzin's ability to engage at that level. The episode is preserved in his historical record. Exact citation in the Chida's writings not specified in the שיעור. ↩
