הַמַּפִּיל, הריני מוחל, and the Maharam's Prison Seder

The arc: the pointed opening מַשָׁל of the illiterate man in גן עדן and the illiterate gatekeeper at the גיהנום gate; the גמרא in ברכות ס' ע"ב and רבינו חננאל's girsa-addition of ויאמר; the three functions of הַמַּפִּיל (הודאה על השינה, תקוה להשכמה, בקשת שמירה מחלומות רעים); the central machloikes of the Chayei Adam (הַמַּפִּיל at night is ברכת שבח on מנהגו של עולם, not ברכת הנהנין — hence no hefsek concern) against the Mishnah Berurah (the singular לשון of the ברכה makes it ברכת הנהנין — hefsek is a real problem); the Chayei Adam's biographical anchor as a merchant-baal-halacha; Rav Asher Weiss's structural pushback that sleep is not a הנאה that enters the גוף like food, drink, or smell (per תוספות ברכות מ"ג ע"ב); the Magen Avraham on sequence — do you say קריאת שמע before or after הַמַּפִּיל?; the מקובלים's practice of not saying הַמַּפִּיל after חצות; the heart of the nightly סדר — הריני מוחל וסולח — with the Zohar's story of ר' אבא and the double נס; the comparison to Yosef HaTzadik's forgiveness of his brothers; the Pele Yoetz's warning that the offender's forgiveness-formula does not absolve the offender's duty to seek מחילה; the Noda B'Yehuda's question in the צל"ח on why we ask ושים חלקנו בתורתך right before sleep and the Arizal's answer of the ישיבה של מעלה; the Maharam of Rothenburg's own nightly סדר preserved by תלמידים from his cell in Ensisheim (First פרשה of קריאת שמע, המלאך הגואל, לישועתך קיויתי, יושב בסתר, השכיבנו, ברכת כהנים); the שישים גיבורים as the sixty letters of ברכת כהנים; ברכת השכיבנו without חתימת ברכה per the Tur / הרא"ש; and מימיני מיכאל with the ארגמן acronym as the ring of מלאכים around the sleeping Yid.

I. Opening — The Mashal of the Two Illiterates

Rabbi Ephrati opened with a pointed מַשָׁל that framed the entire שיעור. It carries humor and bite; it is also structurally serious.

A man decided to live a life of עבירות and thought he had found the perfect legal workaround. "If I never learn Torah — never even learn to read — no one can have a טענה on me. If I cannot read the שולחן ערוך, I cannot be held responsible for what it says." He avoided reading his whole life. Avoided the sefarim. Avoided being held responsible.

After 120 he arrived in שמים. They showed him a list of עבירות. He protested his defense: "I can't read!" Fine — they sent him to גן עדן. But what is גן עדן? חז״ל teach that נשמות there learn תורה. He found himself surrounded by ספרים, שטענדערס, ישיבות, רבנים. Delightful at first. Then restless.

He asked the מלאך in charge: "Let me visit my friends in גיהנום for a few hours." The מלאך warned him: "Leaving גיהנום is not simple." He insisted. The מלאך gave him a שטר — a writ — authorizing a brief visit. He went.

A few hours later, the heat and anguish were more than he could bear. He hurried back to the gate of גן עדן. At the entrance stood the שומר — the שטן at the door. The man presented his שטר. The gatekeeper looked at it, looked back at him, and said: "Sorry. I can't read."

Why the מַשָׁל Opens the שיעור The point Rabbi Ephrati drove home: a Yid's נשמה is bound to תורה. Avoidance is not freedom — it is self-betrayal. The soul's natural activity in עולם האמת is לימוד. The nightly סדר of קריאת שמע על המיטה, the Arizal on ושים חלקנו בתורתך, the ישיבה של מעלה — all of it presupposes that the נשמה learns Torah once released from the body's daily distractions. The illiterate man's tragedy is that he arrived in the place where the soul's natural activity is לימוד, and had made himself structurally unable to participate. The nightly סדר aligns our conscious mind with what the נשמה is about to do anyway. To skip the סדר is to release the נשמה into the ישיבה של מעלה without preparation.

II. Where We Left Off — The Gemara and רבינו חננאל's Girsa

Last week's שיעור established the מקור of קריאת שמע על המיטה in the גמרא at the end of מסכת ברכות[1]: "הַנִּכְנָס לִישׁוֹן עַל מִטָּתוֹ..." When a person enters his bedroom to sleep, the גמרא outlines the seder: first, קריאת שמע (at minimum the first פרשה, from שמע ישראל until the end of והיה אם שמוע); then the ברכה of הַמַּפִּיל.

A specific textual detail worth naming: רבינו חננאל[2] emends the גירסא of the גמרא to include ויאמר — the third פרשה of קריאת שמע, the one that contains מצות ציצית. This is the widespread מנהג today. There is a structural anomaly here: nighttime is not the זמן of the mitzvah of ציצית, so on the mitzvah-level ויאמר does not obviously belong. But the purpose of קריאת שמע על המיטה is not to fulfill מצות ציצית — the purpose is שמירה מן המזיקין. Since the full קריאת שמע contains רמ״ח (248) words, corresponding to the רמ״ח איברים of the human body, reciting it in its entirety provides שמירה covering every אבר. Rabbeinu Chananel's addition of ויאמר rounds out the count and grants full-body protection. The letter-count logic is why we say the whole thing even though the mitzvah-of-ציצית logic would not require it.

III. The Three Functions of הַמַּפִּיל

After קריאת שמע, the גמרא instructs the recitation of הַמַּפִּיל: בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱלֹקֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם הַמַּפִּיל חֶבְלֵי שֵׁינָה עַל עֵינָי וּתְנוּמָה עַל עַפְעַפָּי... Rabbi Ephrati broke out three distinct structural functions the ברכה is doing at once:

A) הודאה על מתנת השינה

The most obvious function: gratitude for the gift of sleep. הקב"ה gave us a physiological mechanism that lets us shut our eyes, still the body, restore the נפש, and wake ready for the day's עבודה. That is a gift on the level of מנהגו של עולם — the structure of the universe. The ברכה acknowledges it.

B) תקוה להשכמה

Second: the hope of waking. Rabbi Ephrati stopped to make the point explicit. Do not take for granted that you will wake up tomorrow. A Yid connects to הקב"ה through recognizing that every breath comes from Him — and the moment of surrendering consciousness at night is the moment when the fragility of it all is most visible. The תקוה of waking, embedded in הַמַּפִּיל, is a small structural אמונה-act: "I am not guaranteed the morning; I am hoping for it, from Him."

C) בקשת שמירה מחלומות רעים

Third: protection from bad dreams and destructive thoughts. The mind wanders as sleep approaches. חלומות רעים and הרהורים רעים are real halachic categories — the גמרא elsewhere treats bad dreams as spiritual events requiring התעוררות. Rabbi Ephrati flagged an underappreciated contemporary practice: hataavas chalom — the formal procedure for "reversing" a bad dream, printed in every ArtScroll siddur, requiring three people to say חלמא טבא חזאי in response.

The Reb Chaim in the Kehillah Rabbi Ephrati preserved a small personal detail from his own daily שיעור in Flatbush. There is a Yid — Reb Chaim — who comes to the daily קם-shiur every morning at 75 or 80 years old. Every day after the shiur, he asks Rabbi Ephrati for three people to be with him for a hataavas chalom. "I always ask him what did you dream last night. He says: 'None of your business.' But he always asks for hataavas chaloimos." The point Rabbi Ephrati was making: the halachic category of חלומות רעים is not archaic. There are still Yidden who take dreams seriously enough to walk into a בית מדרש every morning and organize a three-person response. The nightly protection-clause of הַמַּפִּיל is the pre-emptive version of what Reb Chaim's daily hataavas is doing after the fact.

IV. The Central Problem — הפסק and the Nature of הַמַּפִּיל

The ברכה concludes with שם ומלכות: בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה', הַמֵּאִיר לְעוֹלָם כֻּלּוֹ בִּכְבוֹדוֹ. This is a full, formal ברכה instituted by חז"ל. Which raises the sharp question: why do so many contemporary Yidden refrain from reciting it with שם ומלכות?

The answer is a structural halachic problem — הפסק. The פוסקים raise the standard principle: one does not make a ברכה if the act the ברכה is on may not immediately follow. You do not make המוציא unless you are certain you are about to eat bread. Similarly, how can one recite הַמַּפִּיל — a ברכה on sleep — without a guarantee of immediately falling asleep? What if you need to speak to your spouse, use the restroom, wait ten minutes for sleep to come? If sleep does not follow immediately, the ברכה risks becoming ברכה לבטלה. This concern drives the major machloikes of the אחרונים.

V. The Chayei Adam's Position — הַמַּפִּיל is ברכת שבח

The חיי אדם[3] — ר' אברהם דנציג — opens the analysis by addressing a related question first. Should one recite הַמַּפִּיל before a daytime nap? He rules not. During the day the possibility of not falling asleep is real, and the risk of a ברכה לבטלה is too high. Do not make הַמַּפִּיל for a daytime שינת קבע.

But this raises the obvious follow-up: if daytime naps are subject to the לבטלה concern, why is nighttime any different? A person may lie down at night and not fall asleep for an hour, or need to talk to his rebbetzin, or need the bathroom. Why is the halacha for nighttime not the same as the halacha for daytime?

The Chayei Adam's answer is a striking piece of structural לומדות. At night, הַמַּפִּיל is not a personal ברכה on this individual's sleep. It is a ברכת השבח on the universal institution of sleep — מנהגו של עולם — praising הקב"ה for having created a world in which humanity as a whole rests at night and rises renewed for עבודת ה'. The daytime nap, by contrast, is not part of the universal institution — it is this person's personal sleep. So daytime הַמַּפִּיל would be a ברכת הנהנין on personal benefit, and the לבטלה concern applies. Nighttime הַמַּפִּיל is a ברכת השבח on the cosmic phenomenon, and personal wakefulness does not undo the ברכה.

Who Was the Chayei Adam? Rabbi Ephrati took a moment to anchor the Chayei Adam biographically because the shittah is such a foundational one and the man behind it is not as widely known as the sefer. ר' אברהם דנציג — 1748–1820 — lived in Vilna and was a close מחותן of the Vilna Gaon. He was a גאון עולם in his learning but — critically — he was a businessman, not a formal rav or rosh yeshiva. He earned his living as a wealthy merchant traveling on business. Rabbi Ephrati preserved the Chayei Adam's own line from his introduction: "I used to travel, and I always had my שולחן ערוך with me, and I gathered all my notes and put out a sefer." The Chayei Adam wrote what became the most popular practical-halacha sefer for בעלי הבית in modern history — and did so as a בעל הבית himself. His halachic authority carries the ballast of both his למדות and his lived experience with practical questions.

VI. The Mishnah Berurah's Sharp Disagreement

The משנה ברורה[4] — in his commentary on או"ח סימן רל"ט and especially in the ביאור הלכה ד"ה סמוך למיטתו — pushes back on the Chayei Adam sharply. The Chofetz Chaim's argument: the לשון of the ברכה itself is decisively personal. The words are הַמַּפִּיל חַבְלֵי שֵׁינָה עַל עֵינַי וּתְנוּמָה עַל עַפְעַפָּי... וִיהִי רָצוֹן... שֶׁתַּצִּילֵנִי. Every clause is in the singular. Every noun is "my eyes," "my eyelids," "my saving." The ברכה is textually inseparable from the individual reciting it.

You cannot, the Chofetz Chaim argues, take a ברכה whose entire text is written in the first-person singular and reframe it as a communal universal-institution שבח. The syntax will not bear it. Therefore הַמַּפִּיל is a ברכת הנהנין for the personal benefit of sleep, and the הפסק concern applies with full force. If interruption is likely — if the person is not confident he will fall asleep quickly and remain asleep — he should not recite הַמַּפִּיל with שם ומלכות. This is the ground of the widespread contemporary מנהג to omit שם ומלכות, or to omit the ברכה entirely, when anticipating delay.

VII. Rav Asher Weiss — Is Sleep Even a הנאה?

הרב אשר וייס[5] takes a further structural step that pressures both the Chayei Adam and the Mishnah Berurah frames. His question: on what basis do we assume sleep is a הנאה at all in the halachic sense?

The תוספות בברכות מ"ג ע"ב[6] discusses ברכות on ריח — smell. Why does one make a ברכה on smelling בשמים? Because something physically enters the גוף. The molecules of the fragrance enter through the nose. So the categorical parallel to food (which also enters the גוף) is preserved, and a ברכת הנהנין is warranted. Tosafos codifies the principle: ברכות הנהנין are on things that enter the גוף.

Rav Asher Weiss applies this to sleep. Sleep does not enter the גוף. It is not consumed the way food, drink, or even fragrance is consumed. It is a biological necessity that happens to the גוף, not something the גוף takes in. On that structural analysis, הַמַּפִּיל cannot be classified as a ברכת הנהנין at all, regardless of the singular לשון. It is a ברכת השבח or a ברכת הודאה — praising הקב"ה for the institution of sleep, or thanking Him for the daily gift of it.

If Rav Asher Weiss is right, the הפסק pressure diminishes substantially. The concern that shaped the Mishnah Berurah's caution loses its structural teeth. This does not fully resolve the machloikes — Rav Asher Weiss himself acknowledges that the Mishnah Berurah's לשון-argument is not to be dismissed lightly — but it opens a serious pathway for those who want to recite הַמַּפִּיל with שם ומלכות while accepting that they may not fall asleep instantly.

VIII. The Sequence Question — הַמַּפִּיל and קריאת שמע

A related structural question that the מגן אברהם[7] takes up: does קריאת שמע come before or after הַמַּפִּיל? Sidurim vary on the order. What is at stake?

The answer flows directly from the position on the previous machloikes. If הַמַּפִּיל is a ברכת הנהנין (Mishnah Berurah), one must avoid any הפסק between the ברכה and the sleep it activates. Say קריאת שמע first, then הַמַּפִּיל immediately before sleep, and then sleep — with no verbal or physical activity in between the ברכה and the sleep. If הַמַּפִּיל is primarily ברכת שבח (Chayei Adam), the הפסק rules are much more lenient. One may say הַמַּפִּיל and then say קריאת שמע afterward, as a צורך התפילה — the קריאת שמע extends the moment rather than interrupting the ברכה, especially if שם ומלכות was omitted. בדיעבד, even the stringent side may not classify the intervening קריאת שמע as a real הפסק.

The practical output for most contemporary siddurim: הַמַּפִּיל comes at the end, after the פרשיות of קריאת שמע and after most of the surrounding nightly תפילות, so that the ברכה sits as close as possible to the moment of sleep itself. This aligns with the Mishnah Berurah's structural priority. Some קהלות nonetheless preserve the older order per the Chayei Adam / earlier ראשונים.

Not after חצות — the מקובלים's Position Continuing last week's discussion: the מקובלים have a strong tradition — followed by the Ben Ish Chai, the Kaf HaChaim, and the broader Sefardic-kabbalistic mesorah — that הַמַּפִּיל should not be recited after חצות. The kabbalistic-halachic calculus of the second half of the night differs from the first half in ways that affect the ברכה. Rabbi Ephrati reiterated last week's note that the Kaf HaChaim himself reversed his personal practice on this — for many years he did not say הַמַּפִּיל after חצות following the מקובלים, then upon reviewing the standard פוסקים he found no poskim make this distinction, and he began saying it. But the מקובלים's practice remains widely observed in Sefardic communities. Combined with the hefsek concerns, this is another reason many contemporary Yidden either omit שם ומלכות or omit the ברכה entirely.

IX. The Heart of the Nightly סדר — הריני מוחל

Rabbi Ephrati transitioned from the technical machloikes on הַמַּפִּיל to what he called the heart of the nightly סדר — and named this section as the most spiritually weighty part of the entire שיעור. Many siddurim print at the start of קריאת שמע על המיטה a declaration of forgiveness:

הֲרֵינִי מוֹחֵל וְסוֹלֵחַ לְכָל מִי שֶׁחָטָא כְּנֶגְדִּי, בֵּין בְּגוּפִי בֵּין בְּמָמוֹנִי בֵּין בִּכְבוֹדִי... וְלֹא יֵעָנֵשׁ שׁוּם אָדָם בְּסִבָּתִי.

Structurally: before sleep, we perform חשבון הנפש. It is natural at that quiet moment to recall the day's slights — the driver who cut you off, the colleague who spoke down to you, the family member whose words stung. But this is not the state in which we send our נשמה up to הקב"ה. If we arrive at the ישיבה של מעלה with unresolved anger, we invite מדה כנגד מדה — Heavenly prosecution for our own עבירות. Forgiving the day's transgressors preemptively is a self-protective act as much as a generous one.

The Zohar's Story — ר' אבא and the Double Miracle

The מקור of the practice is in the זהר הקדוש, פרשת מקץ[8]. The Zohar tells of רבי אבא — one of the primary תלמידי חכמים of the Zohar's cast — seeing a man asleep by the roadside. A poisonous snake approached the man. Miraculously, a branch fell from a nearby tree and killed the snake before it reached him. Then, moments later, the stone mound the man was sleeping against collapsed — had he remained sleeping in that spot for another minute, he would have been crushed. A double נס: rescued from the snake, then rescued from the collapse.

ר' אבא approached the man and asked what זכות he had that granted him double miracles. The man answered simply: "Any time someone hurts me, I try to appease them and to forgive. If I cannot appease them right away, every single night before I go to sleep I declare: רבונו של עולם, אני מוחל לכל מי שפגע בי — בְּלֵב שָׁלֵם." Ribono Shel Olam, I forgive everyone who hurt me — with a full heart.

ר' אבא wept at the encounter. He declared the man worthy of the double miracle — and noted that in a certain sense the man's practice surpasses even Yosef HaTzadik's famous forgiveness of his brothers in בראשית פרק נ' פסוקים י"ט–כ"א[9]. Yosef forgave a specific set of brothers for a specific documented wrong. This man forgives every Jew, every single night, without exception. The scope of the daily forgiveness is broader than even Yosef's dramatic one-time מחילה.

Sincerity Is Not Optional

Rabbi Ephrati flagged the critical caveat. The forgiveness only works if it is sincere. The pasuk in תהלים ק"א פסוק ז'[10]: דֹּבֵר שְׁקָרִים לֹא יִכּוֹן לְנֶגֶד עֵינָי. One who speaks falsely cannot stand before My eyes. If you recite הריני מוחל while clinging to the day's resentment, the formula does nothing. Worse — it may register as a spoken lie that itself needs teshuvah. The nightly declaration is not a mechanical rite; it is a discipline of the heart. If the heart cannot yet forgive a specific incident, the honest move is either to leave that incident out or to work through it before returning to the formula.

The Pele Yoetz's Sharp Warning

The פלא יועץ[11] issues an equally important warning in the other direction. Never harm another person while assuming: "He will forgive me tonight when he says הריני מוחל." Hurting another creates two distinct obligations: (a) the victim's duty to forgive; and (b) the offender's duty to actively seek מחילה and to appease the person he harmed. The victim's nightly declaration does not absolve the offender of the second duty. Even if a Yid you wronged forgives you every night with הריני מוחל, you still owe him a proper apology and a proper attempt at appeasement. The two duties are structurally independent.

The practical output: if you have not been saying הריני מוחל, add it — sincerely. And if you have wronged someone during the day, do not lean on his nightly recitation to discharge your own obligation.

X. ושים חלקנו בתורתך — The Nightly ישיבה של מעלה

Another fascinating request in the bedtime תפילות: וְשִׂים חֶלְקֵנוּ בְּתוֹרָתֶךָ. The נודע ביהודה — in his צל"ח על ברכות דף ה' ע"א[12] — famously asks the sharp question: why are we asking for a portion in Torah right before sleep? This is the moment when we are about to stop learning for the night. What sense does the request make at this timing?

The האר"י ז"ל[13] answers with a kabbalistic teaching that transforms the meaning of the request. When a Yid sleeps, his נשמה ascends to the ישיבה של מעלה and continues learning Torah there. The body sleeps; the נשמה attends yeshiva above. On that framework, the request ושים חלקנו בתורתך is exactly right at bedtime — it is the request that our חלק in the world's Torah learning should continue through the night as our נשמה sits in the yeshiva above.

This connects back to the opening mashal. The illiterate man in גן עדן was structurally excluded from the very activity his נשמה was created to perform. Every night, the same activity is available to every Yid — the נשמה ascends to a yeshiva where the tuition is free and the חבורה is unmatched. The nightly seder is our preparation for attending.

XI. The מהר"ם of Rothenburg's Prison Seder

The מהר"ם מרוטנבורג[14] — R' Meir ben Baruch, one of the greatest German ראשונים of the 13th century — spent the last years of his life imprisoned in the tower of Ensisheim in Alsace, where he had been detained by King Rudolf I in a scheme to extract ransom from the Jewish community. The מהר"ם famously refused to allow the community to pay the ransom, understanding that acceding would establish a precedent that would endanger every future gadol. He died in prison after seven years of captivity.

During those years, תלמידים visited the מהר"ם and preserved his practices. Among what they preserved is his own nightly קריאת שמע על המיטה סדר. The seder is deliberately concise — likely reflecting both the constraints of prison life and the מהר"ם's characteristic economy. It is:

  1. The first פרשה of קריאת שמע (שמע ישראל... ואהבת... והיה אם שמוע)
  2. הַמַּלְאָךְ הַגֹּאֵל אֹתִי מִכָּל רָע יְבָרֵךְ אֶת הַנְּעָרִים... — בראשית מ"ח פסוק ט"ז[15]
  3. לִישׁוּעָתְךָ קִוִּיתִי ה' — בראשית מ"ט פסוק י"ח[16]
  4. יֹשֵׁב בְּסֵתֶר עֶלְיוֹן בְּצֵל שַׁדַּי יִתְלוֹנָן — the whole of תהלים פרק צ"א
  5. השכיבנו (without חתימת ברכה — see section XIII below)
  6. ברכת כהנים

The מהר"ם's seder is the source for much of what we still say. Contemporary siddurim expand on it substantially (המפיל, מימיני מיכאל, הריני מוחל, ושים חלקנו, more פסוקים and תהלים), but the structural spine — first פרשה, המלאך הגואל, לישועתך, יושב בסתר, השכיבנו, ברכת כהנים — is preserved from the מהר"ם's cell.

XII. Halachic Framework — When and How Much

A structural halachic point that flows from last week's Rabbeinu Tam / Rashi discussion:

If a person davened מעריב early (before צאת הכוכבים) and has not yet fulfilled ובשוכבך in its proper זמן, the משנה ברורה rules he must say the full קריאת שמע with all three פרשיות at bedtime, because the bedtime recitation is now serving as his actual mitzvah-of-קריאת שמע. This is the Rashi-line position from last week — plag-Maariv does not discharge the ובשוכבך obligation on the Rashi frame, so the bedtime Shema must complete it.

If a person already davened מעריב within זמן קריאת שמע properly (after צאת הכוכבים with the full ברכות), לכתחילה it is still better to say the entire קריאת שמע at bedtime for the שמירה function. But בדיעבד, the מהר"ם of Rothenburg's practice — only שמע ישראל and ואהבת — is sufficient.

XIII. השכיבנו at Night Without חתימת ברכה

A halachic detail with genuine practical import. The טור[17] records that הרא"ש said השכיבנו at bedtime without the חתימת ברכה — that is, without opening or closing the paragraph with ברוך אתה ה'. Many אחרונים codify the same practice: one should not recite a full ברכה-format outside its fixed מקום (which is in מעריב, immediately after אמת ואמונה). Rather, say the body of השכיבנו as a תפילה — השכיבנו ה' אלקינו לשלום, והעמידנו מלכנו לחיים... — but omit the ברכה frame.

This preserves the שמירה quality of the השכיבנו words (the paragraph is structurally the שומר עמו ישראל language) while avoiding the halachic problem of a ברכה-formula in an inappropriate structural location. Yidden who use the standard nightly siddur pages should be aware that השכיבנו in that context is a שיר, not a ברכה. Read it accordingly.

XIV. ברכת כהנים at Night — The שישים גיבורים

Why does the מהר"ם's seder — and the widespread nightly practice — include ברכת כהנים at bedtime? Sefarim[18] explain the connection through a beautiful pasuk-remez. שיר השירים ג' פסוק ז'[19]: הִנֵּה מִטָּתוֹ שֶׁלִּשְׁלֹמֹה, שִׁשִּׁים גִּבֹּרִים סָבִיב לָהּ מִגִּבֹּרֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל. Around Shlomo HaMelech's bed stood sixty warriors, from the גיבורי ישראל, guarding him.

The sefarim count the letters of ברכת כהנים and find the count to be exactly sixty: יְבָרֶכְךָ ה' וְיִשְׁמְרֶךָ. יָאֵר ה' פָּנָיו אֵלֶיךָ וִיחֻנֶּךָּ. יִשָּׂא ה' פָּנָיו אֵלֶיךָ וְיָשֵׂם לְךָ שָׁלוֹם. Sixty letters — sixty גיבורים. The pasuk-count of the ברכה corresponds structurally to the count of the warriors around Shlomo's bed. On this reading, saying ברכת כהנים at bedtime places sixty spiritual גיבורים around your own bed for the night's watch. Every letter is a warrior. The peace and protection of the ברכה literally surrounds the sleeper.

XV. מימיני מיכאל — The Four מלאכים

The beloved תפילה invoking the מלאכים by name: מִימִינִי מִיכָאֵל, וּמִשְּׂמֹאלִי גַּבְרִיאֵל, וּמִלְּפָנַי אוּרִיאֵל, וּמֵאֲחוֹרַי רְפָאֵל, וְעַל רֹאשִׁי שְׁכִינַת אֵ־ל. Its essence: the Yid retires under the שכינה, encircled by four archangels, each with a specific structural role.

Rabbi Ephrati flagged the ארגמן acronym that codifies the practice. The word ארגמן — the purple-scarlet dye referenced across the Torah in the context of משכן / בגדי כהונה — is read as a ראשי תיבות of the four angels: אוריאל, רפאל, גבריאל, מיכאל, נוריאל (or אוריאל in place of one of them, depending on the tradition). The א at the start additionally represents אלוף העולם — הקב"ה Himself. The word ארגמן, on this reading, encodes the entire ring of divine protection around the sleeping Yid: הקב"ה above, four מלאכים circling the four directions.

XVI. Practical Guidance

The שיעור closed with a summary of practical guidance. Rabbi Ephrati emphasized that the point of the entire treatment is not scholarly — it is that this is every Yid's practice, every single night, and even small improvements compound.

Open a siddur nightly. Do not go from memory alone. The physical act of opening the siddur creates the moment of transition from the day's noise to the nightly setup.

Say at minimum שמע ישראל — and, where circumstances permit, the full קריאת שמע with all three פרשיות for the רמ״ח-word protection.

Include the core פסוקים and תפילות: המלאך הגואל, לישועתך קיויתי, יושב בסתר, השכיבנו (without חתימת ברכה), ברכת כהנים, and מימיני מיכאל.

Regarding הַמַּפִּיל: endeavor to say the instituted ברכה. If you fear interruption — conversation with your spouse, a bathroom trip, difficulty falling asleep — recite הַמַּפִּיל without שם ומלכות. This honors the חז"ל institution while avoiding the ברכה-לבטלה concern. This is the Mishnah Berurah's approach and is the widely-accepted default for the contemporary uncertain-sleeper.

Be meticulous with הריני מוחל — and mean it. If you have not been saying it, add it. And do not lean on other people's nightly recitation to absolve your own duty to seek their forgiveness when you have wronged them.

The arc of the שיעור in three movements. (i) The mashal of the two illiterates as the pastoral opening, then continuation of last week's structural setup — the Gemara in ברכות ס' ע"ב, רבינו חננאל's girsa-addition of ויאמר, and the three functions of הַמַּפִּיל (הודאה, תקוה, בקשת שמירה) with the aside on the daily hataavas-chalom practice of Reb Chaim in Flatbush. (ii) The central machloikes on הַמַּפִּיל — Chayei Adam's ברכת השבח framing (with the biographical detail of the חיי אדם as businessman-מחותן-of-the-Gra) against the Mishnah Berurah's ברכת הנהנין argument from the singular לשון, plus Rav Asher Weiss's structural push from Tosafos on ריח that sleep is not a הנאה of the גוף at all. The Magen Avraham on sequence. The מקובלים on after-חצות. (iii) The heart of the seder — הריני מוחל, the Zohar's story of ר' אבא and the double נס, the surpassing of Yosef's forgiveness, the Pele Yoetz's warning that the offender's duty is independent. ושים חלקנו בתורתך and the Noda B'Yehuda / Arizal on the ישיבה של מעלה. The מהר"ם of Rothenburg's prison-seder as the structural spine. השכיבנו without חתימה per הרא"ש. ברכת כהנים's sixty letters as the שישים גיבורים around Shlomo's bed. מימיני מיכאל with the ארגמן acronym. And practical guidance — open a siddur, at minimum shema, הַמַּפִּיל without שם ומלכות if unsure, and הריני מוחל sincerely.

מראה מקומות

  1. גמרא ברכות ס' ע"ב — הנכנס לישן על מטתו. — The Gemara at the end of מסכת ברכות outlines the structural סדר for one preparing to sleep. First: קריאת שמע (at minimum the first פרשה, from שמע ישראל until the end of והיה אם שמוע). Then: the ברכה of הַמַּפִּיל. The full ברכה text: בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱלֹקֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם הַמַּפִּיל חֶבְלֵי שֵׁינָה עַל עֵינָי וּתְנוּמָה עַל עַפְעַפָּי... בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' הַמֵּאִיר לְעוֹלָם כֻּלּוֹ בִּכְבוֹדוֹ. This is the structural anchor for the entire sugya of קריאת שמע על המיטה and הַמַּפִּיל. ↩
  2. רבינו חננאל on ברכות — girsa-addition of ויאמר. — רבינו חננאל בן חושיאל (10th–11th century Kairouan, North Africa) — one of the earliest and most authoritative גאונים-era commentators on the Talmud. His commentary is quoted extensively by רש"י, the ראשונים, and the Rif. His girsa-emendation of the Gemara in Berachos to include ויאמר (the third פרשה, containing מצות ציצית) is the source of the widespread contemporary מנהג to say the full קריאת שמע at bedtime. The structural justification: even though nighttime is not the זמן of ציצית, the שמירה function requires the full רמ״ח-word count, and the third פרשה contributes to that count. ↩
  3. חיי אדם — the ברכת שבח framing of הַמַּפִּיל. — ר' אברהם דנציג (1748–1820, Vilna) — author of חיי אדם (halacha of daily practice) and חכמת אדם (halacha of יורה דעה). A close מחותן of the Vilna Gaon and a wealthy merchant who wrote from his lived experience with practical questions. His treatment of daytime שינת קבע and nighttime הַמַּפִּיל draws the structural distinction: daytime הַמַּפִּיל is a personal ברכת הנהנין and subject to לבטלה risk; nighttime הַמַּפִּיל is a ברכת שבח on the universal institution of sleep (מנהגו של עולם) and does not carry the same הפסק pressure. The framework is discussed in חיי אדם's treatment of דיני קריאת שמע שעל המטה. Exact siman not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  4. משנה ברורה אורח חיים סימן רל"ט; ביאור הלכה ד"ה סמוך למיטתו — the ברכת הנהנין framing. — The Chofetz Chaim's decisive pushback on the Chayei Adam. The core argument: the לשון of the ברכה is entirely in the singular — הַמַּפִּיל חבלי שינה על עיני ותנומה על עפעפי, ויהי רצון... שתצילני. Every clause is personal-singular. This textual structure is incompatible with a ברכת שבח framing on a universal institution. Therefore הַמַּפִּיל is a ברכת הנהנין for the personal benefit of sleep, and הפסק concerns apply with full force. Practical psak: if interruption is anticipated or sleep is uncertain, do not recite הַמַּפִּיל with שם ומלכות. The Mishnah Berurah's position is the widely-accepted contemporary default for most kehillos. ↩
  5. הרב אשר וייס — structural pushback on ברכת הנהנין framing itself. — Rav Asher Weiss (contemporary ראב"ד of the Beth Din of ירושלים and Rosh Kollel of Machon Minchas Osher) — a leading contemporary Ashkenazic posek. His treatment of הַמַּפִּיל presses the structural question one level deeper: on what basis is sleep even classified as a הנאה in the halachic-brachah sense? Drawing on תוספות בברכות מ"ג ע"ב (see fn6), Rav Asher argues that ברכות הנהנין are structurally on things that enter the גוף — food, drink, even fragrance (which enters through inhalation). Sleep does not enter the גוף; it is a biological state that happens to the גוף but is not consumed by it. On that framing, הַמַּפִּיל cannot be a ברכת הנהנין regardless of the singular לשון, and it must be classified as a ברכת שבח or ברכת הודאה. The full argument is developed in Rav Asher's published shiurim on the sugya. ↩

His treatment of הַמַּפִּיל appears in his general shiurim on Berachos and in his responsa collection מנחת אשר. Verify the specific citation before referencing in a formal halachic context. (Checked via Sefaria API 2026-07-23: מנחת אשר and Rav Asher Weiss's published shiurim are not indexed in Sefaria's corpus; the specific citation is outside Sefaria coverage.)
  6. תוספות ברכות מ"ג ע"ב ד"ה דאמר קרא — ברכות הנהנין require entry into the גוף. — The Tosafos discussion on why we make a ברכה on ריח. Tosafos establishes the structural principle: ברכות הנהנין are on things that physically enter the גוף. Food enters via the mouth; drink enters via the throat; even fragrance enters via inhalation (molecules of scent enter the nasal passages). This categorical parallel is what qualifies these three as הנהנין-worthy. Rav Asher Weiss (see fn5) applies this categorically: sleep does not enter the גוף, so it fails the Tosafos criterion and cannot be classified as הנהנין. ↩
  7. מגן אברהם on the sequence of הַמַּפִּיל and קריאת שמע. — The Magen Avraham (ר' אברהם גומבינר, 17th c. Poland) addresses in his commentary on the השכיבנו-related simanim of או"ח the question of whether הַמַּפִּיל comes before or after קריאת שמע in the nightly seder. The output depends on how one classifies הַמַּפִּיל: if ברכת הנהנין (Mishnah Berurah), no הפסק between the ברכה and sleep — קריאת שמע comes first, then הַמַּפִּיל, then sleep. If ברכת שבח (Chayei Adam), the הפסק rules are lenient — one may say הַמַּפִּיל first and then say קריאת שמע afterward. Exact siman in Magen Avraham not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  8. זוהר, פרשת מקץ — the story of ר' אבא and the double נס. — The Zohar's story of רבי אבא encountering a man sleeping by the roadside who received a double miracle: (a) a branch fell and killed a poisonous snake approaching him, and (b) moments later the stone mound he was sleeping against collapsed, which would have crushed him had he remained there. R' Abba's investigation revealed the man's practice: nightly forgiveness of all who had wronged him with בלב שלם. The Zohar frames this forgiveness as surpassing even Yosef HaTzadik's forgiveness of his brothers (בראשית נ') — Yosef forgave one set of brothers for one wrong; this man forgives every Yid every night for everything. The מקור of the nightly הריני מוחל practice as we have it in siddurim traces back to this Zohar passage. Exact page in the Zohar not specified in the שיעור; the passage is in the פרשת מקץ section of the Zohar's continuous commentary on ספר בראשית. ↩
  9. בראשית פרק נ' פסוקים י"ט–כ"א — יוסף's forgiveness of his brothers. — The pesukim at the end of ספר בראשית where Yosef, after Yaakov's death, reassures his brothers who fear he will now take revenge. אַל תִּירָאוּ... וְאַתֶּם חֲשַׁבְתֶּם עָלַי רָעָה, אֱלֹקִים חֲשָׁבָהּ לְטֹבָה... וְעַתָּה אַל תִּירָאוּ, אָנֹכִי אֲכַלְכֵּל אֶתְכֶם וְאֶת טַפְכֶם, וַיְנַחֵם אוֹתָם וַיְדַבֵּר עַל לִבָּם. Yosef's forgiveness is the paradigmatic act of forgiveness in תנ"ך. The Zohar's story about ר' אבא frames the anonymous man's nightly forgiveness as, in a certain sense, surpassing even this — because the scope is universal-every-night rather than one-time-one-set-of-brothers. ↩
  10. תהלים ק"א פסוק ז' — דובר שקרים לא יכון לנגד עיני. — The pasuk from דוד המלך: one who speaks falsely cannot stand before My eyes. The pasuk is the halachic-hashkafic ground for the requirement that הריני מוחל be sincere. A recitation of the forgiveness formula while clinging internally to the day's resentment is not merely halachically ineffective — it is a spoken lie in a moment of standing before הקב"ה. The pasuk elevates the sincerity requirement from good-practice to structural halachic necessity. ↩
  11. פלא יועץ — the offender's independent duty to seek מחילה. — The Pele Yoetz (ר' אליעזר פאפו, early 19th c. Bulgaria) — a classic musar work organized alphabetically by topic. His treatment of מחילה establishes the structural independence of the two duties in an offense: (a) the victim's duty to forgive, and (b) the offender's duty to actively seek מחילה and to appease the person he wronged. The victim's nightly recitation of הריני מוחל does not discharge the offender's duty. An offender who leans on "he'll forgive me tonight" as an excuse for not seeking apology is failing an independent halachic obligation. The Pele Yoetz's framing is a foundational text in contemporary halachic-musar treatments of interpersonal repentance. ↩
  12. נודע ביהודה — צל"ח על ברכות דף ה' ע"א — question on ושים חלקנו בתורתך at bedtime. — ר' יחזקאל לנדא (the Noda B'Yehuda, 1713–1793, Prague) — one of the great ראבי"דים of the 18th century, author of the שו"ת נודע ביהודה and the ציון לנפש חיה (צל"ח) commentary on selected מסכתות of shas. His question in the צל"ח on Berachos 5a: why do we ask for a portion in Torah at bedtime, when this is precisely the moment we are ceasing to learn for the night? The question is a classic Noda B'Yehuda opening — pressing on an apparent structural incongruity in the liturgy — and it opens the way for the Arizal's kabbalistic answer (see fn13). ↩
  13. The האר"י ז"ל — the ישיבה של מעלה answer. — The Arizal's teaching that when a Yid sleeps, his נשמה ascends to the ישיבה של מעלה and continues learning Torah there. The framework transforms the meaning of the bedtime request ושים חלקנו בתורתך: the request is not for continued learning while awake (which is about to end), but for continued share in the world's Torah learning while the נשמה attends yeshiva above. The teaching is developed across multiple Lurianic texts and preserved in the standard published editions of the Arizal's writings (עץ חיים, שער הכוונות). Exact citation not specified in the שיעור. ↩
  14. The מהר"ם מרוטנבורג's nightly seder — preserved by תלמידים from his cell in Ensisheim. — ר' מאיר בן ברוך מרוטנבורג (c. 1215–1293) — one of the greatest Ashkenazic ראשונים of the 13th century, rebbe of the Rosh, the Mordechai, and much of the following generation of Ashkenazic פוסקים. Imprisoned in the tower of Ensisheim in Alsace by King Rudolf I of Germany as part of a scheme to extract ransom from the Jewish community. The מהר"ם refused to allow the community to pay, understanding that acceding would establish precedent endangering every future gadol. He died in prison after seven years of captivity; his body was ransomed and buried in Worms decades after his death by the Alexander Wimpfen family in exchange for a place next to him in the beis hakvaros. During his imprisonment, תלמידים visited and preserved his practices, including his nightly seder for קריאת שמע על המיטה. The seder's spine — first פרשה of shema, המלאך הגואל, לישועתך קיויתי, יושב בסתר, השכיבנו without חתימה, ברכת כהנים — is the source of much of what appears in contemporary siddurim. ↩
  15. בראשית מ"ח פסוק ט"ז — המלאך הגואל אותי מכל רע. — The pasuk from Yaakov's blessing of Menashe and Ephraim: הַמַּלְאָךְ הַגֹּאֵל אֹתִי מִכָּל רָע יְבָרֵךְ אֶת הַנְּעָרִים וְיִקָּרֵא בָהֶם שְׁמִי וְשֵׁם אֲבֹתַי אַבְרָהָם וְיִצְחָק וְיִדְגּוּ לָרֹב בְּקֶרֶב הָאָרֶץ. The pasuk invokes the malach who redeemed Yaakov from all harm. It sits early in the nightly seder as a request for the same protective malach's presence during the night ahead — and is central to the מהר"ם of Rothenburg's prison-seder tradition. ↩
  16. בראשית מ"ט פסוק י"ח — לישועתך קויתי ה'. — The pasuk from Yaakov Avinu's blessing of Dan: לִישׁוּעָתְךָ קִוִּיתִי ה'. Yaakov interrupts his blessing to Dan to interject this cry for divine salvation. The traditional understanding: Yaakov saw the future of Dan (including Shimshon's eventual downfall) and cried out for the ultimate ישועה. The pasuk's placement in the nightly seder frames sleep itself as a moment when we await ה"s ישועה — the daily rescue from the night's dangers, and metaphorically the ultimate rescue from all peril. ↩
  17. טור אורח חיים סימן רל"ה — הרא"ש's practice of השכיבנו at night without חתימה. — The Tur (ר' יעקב בן הרא"ש, c. 1269–c. 1343) records his father the הרא"ש's practice of reciting השכיבנו at bedtime without opening or closing the paragraph as a formal ברכה. Many אחרונים codify the same practice: one should not recite a full ברכה-format outside its fixed structural מקום (which is in מעריב, immediately after אמת ואמונה). The words of השכיבנו — with their emphasis on שומר עמו ישראל — carry their שמירה function without needing the ברכה frame. Siman רל"ה in the Tur covers the relevant halachos of מעריב and its accompanying nightly practices. ↩
  18. במדבר רבה י"א:ג' — "ששים גבורים סביב לה — אלו ששים אותיות שבברכת כהנים" (ר' שמלאי). — The primary chazal-mekor for the derasha equating the sixty warriors of שיר השירים ג' פסוק ז' with the sixty letters of ברכת כהנים is במדבר רבה פרשה י"א סימן ג', in the extended דרשה of ר' שמלאי on the פרשה of ברכת כהנים. On the phrase ששים גבורים סביב לה, the midrash states explicitly: אלו ששים אותיות שבברכת כהנים — these are the sixty letters of the Priestly Benediction. The same דרשה continues that even one who dreams a sword is cutting his thigh should rise early to hear ברכת כהנים and no harm will come to him — grounding the nightly-שמירה application. The specific letter-count of exactly sixty depends on standard orthographic conventions (יְבָרֶכְךָ... וְיִשְׁמְרֶךָ / יָאֵר... וִיחֻנֶּךָּ / יִשָּׂא... וְיָשֵׂם לְךָ שָׁלוֹם); different counting systems produce slightly different totals, and the "sixty" is preserved by the count assumed by the Midrash Rabbah itself. ↩
  19. שיר השירים ג' פסוק ז' — שישים גיבורים סביב לה. — The pasuk from שיר השירים: הִנֵּה מִטָּתוֹ שֶׁלִּשְׁלֹמֹה, שִׁשִּׁים גִּבֹּרִים סָבִיב לָהּ מִגִּבֹּרֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל, כֻּלָּם אֲחֻזֵי חֶרֶב מְלֻמְּדֵי מִלְחָמָה, אִישׁ חַרְבּוֹ עַל יְרֵכוֹ מִפַּחַד בַּלֵּילוֹת. The pasuk describes the sixty warriors surrounding Shlomo HaMelech's bed, each armed and battle-trained, on watch against the night's dangers. The traditional מפרשים and מקובלים read the sixty as corresponding to the sixty letters of ברכת כהנים (see fn18), producing the structural parallel that saying ברכת כהנים at bedtime places sixty warriors around one's own bed. The pasuk's specific phrase מפחד בלילות — "from fear of the nights" — makes the nighttime parallel explicit. ↩
